## What makes hers hers

Pulled only from patterns actually visible in [[Library/Examples/meta-copy]].

- **Title tags pair her name with a plain-language role, not a keyword-stuffed phrase.** "Alyssa Coleman | Slow Marketing for Service Pros" and "Alyssa Coleman | Your Left-Brained Strategic Partner."
- **Meta descriptions state the tension and the payoff in two short sentences, at conversational register even at this length.** "Running your biz can be hard, but it doesn't have to be. Our slow marketing systems help online business owners spend more time offline (without sacrificing sales)."
- **Even at meta-copy length, a personality line survives.** "Meet the productivity strategist who's wicked good at helping creatives get back to their creativity" keeps "wicked good," a spoken idiom, inside SEO copy most brands would flatten.
- **Product meta descriptions name the mechanism directly, not the outcome alone.** "The automated sales and marketing department designed to help soft CEOs scale their business using only email and pinterest" names the two channels by name rather than saying "a proven system."
