## What makes hers hers

Pulled only from patterns actually visible in [[Library/Examples/sales-pages/boh-sales-page]].

- **Section headers run as short, stacked, all-lowercase or all-caps fragments, not full sentences.** "Is the current vibe . ." then "your business is running entirely on / front of house energy." reads like slide text, not paragraph copy.
- **A recurring bolded refrain reappears verbatim across the page as a structural beat.** "Your business model is too high maintenance. / Re-calibrate before it costs you your business" and "You don't need MORE (content, leads, time), you need a better system!" both repeat three times across the page, unchanged, marking section boundaries.
- **A checklist contrast block: "here's how to know you're stuck" vs. "now let's compare it to."** Each line starts with a checkmark or icon and states a plain fact, not an abstraction.
- **The origin story is told mid-page, not at the top**, dropped in after the offer has already been pitched once: "Once I had a real baby, it's like a veil was lifted."
- **A branded metaphor system runs the whole page** (kitchen, house, menu, mise en place) and every phase of the curriculum is named inside that metaphor: "Phase 1: The House Specials," "Phase 2: The Warm Plate Method," "Phase 3: Evergreen Traffic Pantry," "Phase 4: Soft Sales System."
- **FAQ answers are written as if answering a specific friend's objection**, opening with "Same." or "Yes. But here's the plot twist" rather than a neutral FAQ tone.
- **Testimonials are dropped in raw, unedited, with just a first name.** No framing sentence introduces them.
