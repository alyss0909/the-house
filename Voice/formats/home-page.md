## What makes hers hers

Pulled only from patterns actually visible in [[Library/Examples/home-page]].

- **Opens with an all-caps positioning line before any greeting.** "SLOW MARKETING FOR CEOS WITH BETTER THINGS TO DO" sits above "Come over to the soft side," doing the work a tagline usually does, but stated flatly rather than styled as a slogan.
- **A strikethrough correction inside the welcome line reframes the whole page's promise.** "cozy systems to make ~~running your business~~ your life a hell of a lot easier."
- **A running food and hospitality metaphor structures the whole page**: cookies, a menu, "THE CLASSICS," "OUR SECRET FAMILY RECIPE," a closing line about "creatives who'd rather be making dough."
- **A table used as a layout device for a four-item lifestyle list**, each cell a full sentence about what life looks like once the system works, not a bullet fragment.
- **Section headers run as banners repeating a phrase for emphasis**, e.g. "HUSTLE CULTURE IS OVERCOOKED // HUSTLE CULTURE IS OVERCOOKED // HUSTLE CULTURE."
- **Ends on a one-line brand signature, not a CTA.** "Alyssa Coleman / Slow marketing for the creatives who'd rather be making dough."
