## What makes hers hers

Pulled only from patterns actually visible in the examples above.

- **Opens on a relatable scene, not a pitch.** "You know those people who just… make things?" starts [[Library/Examples/sales-emails/silent-killer-of-good-work]] before the offer is named at all.
- **Emoji used as punctuation and pacing, not decoration.** "🥨 Back of House is that business model. 🥨" bookends the sentence like a stamp.
- **Bolds the belief shift, not the feature.** "It's that you have HIGH STANDARDS." is bolded mid-paragraph, sitting the reader in a self-image rather than a product claim.
- **Steps are numbered and named like a system, with an emoji per step.** STEP 1: BUILD, STEP 2: AUTOMATE, STEP 3: GROW, STEP 4: SELL, each with its own emoji (🐌 💌 📌 🧸).
- **The reply-word CTA is casual and cased like a text message.** "Reply Ghost your Feed Tmrw >>" and "Reply manual and I'll send you the link" both read like a friend texting a link, not a marketer issuing a directive.
- **A PS repeats the CTA in fresh words**, never a verbatim copy-paste of the main CTA line.
- **Trailing dots for a held pause.** "It's … not your BEST work.." uses the dot trail exactly where a sentence would otherwise just end.
