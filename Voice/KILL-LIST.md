# Voice Kill List

This is the editing pass, not the writing guide. Write toward her real examples in [[Voice/formats]] first. Then run a finished draft against this checklist before it ships. Source: [[Hermes/ANTI-AI]], built from Alyssa's own journal words, not from AI inference about her.

## Rule priority, when rules collide

1. Be accurate.
2. Be clear.
3. Be specific.
4. Sound human.
5. Style only when it improves the sentence.

Cited from [[Hermes/ANTI-AI]].

## The negative parallelism ban (the big one)

Never reject one frame and replace it with another. One hit anywhere in a draft, including headlines, covers, and slide headers, means rewrite the passage. The banned shapes, cited from [[Hermes/ANTI-AI]]:

- "This isn't X. This is Y."
- "Not X. Y."
- "Forget X. This is Y."
- "Less X, more Y."
- "Not only X, but also Y."
- "It's not just about X, it's about Y."
- "X? No. Y."
- "Stop thinking X. Start thinking Y."
- "X is dead. Y is the future."
- "The question isn't X. The question is Y."
- "You don't need X. You need Y."
- "X is overrated. Y is what matters."
- "While X might seem right, Y is actually..."
- "Sure, X works. But Y is where the real..."
- "X gets all the attention, but Y is what actually..."

Also watch for the sneaky setups: "Most people think X...", "The common assumption is X...", "Conventional wisdom says X...", "People focus on X..."

**Sole carve-out**: a line quoted verbatim from her real voice or transcripts may keep a banned shape if it already exists in the source. Never generate a new one in any of these shapes, in any register, ever. If a draft is near any banned shape, run this check more than once.

**Fix rule**: delete the rejected half, state the positive claim directly. "It is not about the prompt. It is about the context" becomes "Context controls the output."

## Punctuation checklist

- [ ] Zero em dashes anywhere in the draft. Grep for "—" and confirm the count is 0.
- [ ] Spaced hyphen " - " and trailing dots (" .. ") are fine, em dashes are not.
- [ ] Period density is low; dot trails are frequent, roughly one dot trail per two hard periods in narrative or confession register. Structural, labeled lists are exempt.
- [ ] No staccato runs of short punchy sentences except one, at most, placed at the single emotional peak of the piece. Long sentences are the default in every register.
- [ ] No "its not this its that" construction anywhere, including headers and hooks.
- [ ] Prose is not aggressively choppy or adverb-free. That flatness is its own AI tell. Her style is caps, stretched vowels, run-ons, dots, and asides, not minimalism.
- [ ] Zero "-ing significance clauses" ("...creating a sense of...", "...highlighting the importance of...").
- [ ] No recycled signature devices from her documented winners (a strikethrough gag, "(on purpose)," "Why I burned down...") reused as a new template. Her hits are evidence of taste, not a formula to repeat.

## Banned vocabulary checklist

- [ ] "Quiet" / "quietly": total ban, her direct order. Zero, in any register.
- [ ] Her bot's banned list: ditch, hustle, hamster wheel, transform, "take your business to the next level," "step into," "lean in," crushing it, boss babe, aligned action, magnetic, quantum leap, dream life, gremlin, goblin, "the quiet part nobody talks about," "I hope this finds you well."
- [ ] Standard LLM vocabulary: delve, realm, harness, unlock, tapestry, paradigm, cutting-edge, revolutionize, intricate, showcasing, crucial, pivotal, meticulously, vibrant, unparalleled, underscore, leverage, synergy, innovative, game-changer, testament, groundbreaking, foster, enhance, holistic, garner, pioneering, unleash, versatile, transformative, redefine, seamless, optimize, robust, empower, streamline, elevate, effortless, data-driven, insightful, proactive, visionary, disruptive, reimagine, unprecedented, intuitive, dynamic, immersive, supercharge, interplay, captivate.
- [ ] Copulatives to replace: "serves as," "stands as," "represents a," "boasts," "aims to," "seeks to." Use is, has, uses, gives instead.

## Dead openings and transitions checklist

- [ ] Cut "In today's...", "It is important to note", "Let's dive in/explore/unpack", "At the end of the day", "Nobody is talking about", "Most people don't realize."
- [ ] Cut "Furthermore," "Additionally," "Moreover," "That said," "With that in mind," "On top of that."

## AI pattern checklist

- [ ] No puffery ("a pivotal moment," "a major shift"). State the fact and let the reader judge the weight.
- [ ] No shallow rule-of-three padding. If she doesn't know what to say, AI defaults to three. Use 1, 2, or 4 items, and let one item break the pattern. A deeply developed three-act structure with real scenes is not a violation; a real example lives in her Reason nO1/nO2/nO3 email.
- [ ] No false ranges ("from X to Y") when the middle can't be named.
- [ ] No elegant variation, saying the same thing again with a different word for style's sake.
- [ ] No meta commentary that announces the writing itself.
- [ ] At most one personified dread object ("quietly dying," "silently killing"), and only if played for comedy. The body ban on "silent/quiet" family stands regardless.
- [ ] No concept-label transitions ("The experts call it: X"). She pivots by discovery, in fresh words each time.

## Final pass, run every time

1. Cut throat-clearing first sentences.
2. Vague to specific.
3. Remove fake importance.
4. Check for repeated sentence shapes.
5. Grep the draft for em dashes. Must be 0.
6. Count staccato runs (Word. Word. Word.). Near zero.
7. Search negative parallelism across sentence boundaries and inside headers, hooks, and subjects.
8. Delete rejected-frame constructions.
9. Delete analogies that don't earn their place.
10. Cut endings that only repeat the point already made.
11. If the point is made, stop.

All items above are cited from [[Hermes/ANTI-AI]]. That file holds the full context, the adjudication history, and the register-by-register calibration notes behind each rule. Read it in full when a draft sits close to a line here and the call isn't obvious.
