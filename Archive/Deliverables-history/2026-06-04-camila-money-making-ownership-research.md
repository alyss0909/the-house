# Camila Money-Making Ownership Research

## Executive summary

Camila should not own broad operations yet. She should own narrow revenue systems with clear inputs, automations, trackers, and measurable outcomes.

Best ownership lanes:

1. Podcast pitching and guest-conversion system
2. Workshop/webinar operations and follow-up system
3. Checkout, cart recovery, and buyer-flow QA
4. Lead tracker and fast follow-up dashboard
5. Student activation, testimonial, and referral system
6. Evergreen Blog & Pin production pipeline
7. Collaboration / affiliate / partner opportunity tracker
8. Second Brain and saved-content intake pipeline
9. Rush Order client delivery tracker

The operating principle: AI/Larry/Pax/Wren provide strategy, criteria, and templates. Camila owns the workflow, tracker, verification, follow-up, QA, and reporting.

## Evidence base

- Thinkific frames online education as a scalable revenue driver and names customer engagement, retention, and revenue as core outcomes for course businesses.
- Kit positions creator automations around welcome funnels, launches, course funnels, segmentation, abandoned cart reminders, and evergreen revenue.
- Baymard's checkout research lists a 70.22% average cart abandonment rate and shows many abandonment causes are operationally fixable.
- Edison Research's 2025 podcast report shows podcasts have broad reach, premium listener demographics, and strong ad receptivity.
- IAB reports creator advertising more than doubled from 2021 to 2024, and brands cite identifying the right creators as a top challenge.
- Bain's loyalty research argues retention has large profit leverage.
- Ahrefs argues evergreen content works only with ongoing maintenance, which is an operational commitment.

## Ranked ownership lanes

### 1. Podcast pitching and guest-conversion system

**Fit:** Very high  
**Money path:** visibility -> authority -> email list -> calls/sales -> BOH/Rush Order/private offers  
**Why Camila:** market research, lead generation, CRM, follow-up, outreach automation.

**She can own entirely:**
- podcast opportunity tracker
- show research and contact verification
- episode/theme notes
- fit scoring using criteria from Pax/Larry
- pitch preparation using Wren-approved templates
- follow-up schedule
- status reporting
- podcast-specific landing page/CTA checklist

**Not hers:**
- final positioning
- final pitch voice
- deciding which shows are strategically worth Alyssa's time when the fit is unclear

**Daily output:**
- 3 to 5 opportunities added or updated
- follow-ups due today completed or flagged
- top opportunity flagged for Alyssa when relevant

**Weekly output:**
- 10 to 15 researched opportunities
- 5 to 10 pitches prepared or sent
- reply/follow-up tracker updated

**Monthly output:**
- response rate, booked appearances, best niches, best angles, and next-month target list

## Daily / weekly / monthly jobs Camila could own

### Daily

- Check lead/support inbox for sales-relevant messages.
- Update lead, pitch, partner, and workshop trackers.
- Complete due follow-ups.
- QA any active links/checkouts before promos.
- Capture and label saved content inputs.
- Flag "Alyssa decision needed" items.

### Weekly

- Podcast pipeline: 10 to 15 qualified shows, 5 to 10 pitches prepared/sent.
- Partner pipeline: 10 targets researched, 3 to 5 pitches prepared/sent.
- Workshop pipeline: registrants, reminders, replay follow-up, and hot replies updated.
- Blog & Pin pipeline: 2 to 5 pieces moved forward.
- Checkout QA: one active offer or funnel tested.
- Student activation: stuck students, wins, testimonial opportunities summarized.
- Second Brain intake: weekly packet ready for Larry/Wren.

### Monthly

- Revenue leak audit: checkout, broken links, abandoned cart, tags, buyer flow.
- Outreach report: podcast/partner pitches, replies, bookings, best topics.
- Workshop report: registrations, show-up, replay, clicks, purchases/leads.
- Student success report: activation, support themes, testimonials, referrals.
- Content pipeline report: topics shipped, assets scheduled, opt-in links checked.
- "What Camila should own next" recommendation based on what worked.

## Best first 30-day experiment

Run two owned systems only:

1. Podcast pitching system
2. Checkout and buyer-flow QA

Why:
- Both are measurable.
- Both map directly to Camila's profile.
- Both can make or recover money.
- Neither requires Camila to own brand strategy or creative judgment.

Success criteria after 30 days:

- Podcast tracker has at least 40 researched shows.
- At least 15 pitches prepared or sent.
- Follow-ups are scheduled.
- BOH/Rush Order/live offer buyer flows have been tested.
- Broken links, missing tags, checkout issues, or abandoned-cart gaps are documented.
- Alyssa feels less mental load, not more.
