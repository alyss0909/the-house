The 4 Posts That Could Bring You Traffic All Year

arc approved as teaching layerCTA: comment Pinterest → Pin Planner
  1. Cover: title + hook
  2. Introduce the concept: 4 solid pieces on a search engine can carry your whole year of traffic
  3. Proof: pins don't expire, they accumulate; content on Pinterest keeps driving traffic for months or years
  4. Step 1: pick your four signature topics, the four things you want to be known for
  5. Step 2: write ONE really good piece per topic, the post that answers what your dream client is already typing in
  6. Step 3: add a simple upgrade to each (checklist, template, prompt) so readers become subscribers
  7. Step 4: keep pinning them fresh: new image + new title on a post you already have, so 4 posts feed months of daily pins
  8. CTA: comment Pinterest → Pin Planner

Sources: 3.1 Bread and Butter Content · 3.3 The Batch · Slowly Viral Class

Option A

The Only 3 Pins You Need To Grow Your List With Buyers

  1. Cover: title + hook
  2. Introduce the concept: three pins, each with one job, are all it takes to fill a list with buyers
  3. Proof: Pinterest users walk into the store with a list, mid-buying-decision and already searching; a hundred email subscribers outperform a thousand followers
  4. Pin 1, the search pin: built on keywords your dream client actually types (pull them straight from the Pinterest search bar)
  5. Pin 2, the answer pin: points at your one best page, the one that makes her think "she gets exactly what I'm dealing with"
  6. Pin 3, the opt-in pin: points at a freebie only your right-fit person would want, so it filters the list before anyone's on it
  7. How it compounds: variations of these three = months of daily pins from things you already have
  8. CTA: comment Pinterest → Pin Planner

Sources: Slowly Viral Class · 3.1 Bread and Butter Content

Option B

How To Get Found On Pinterest Without Posting Every Day

  1. Cover: title + hook
  2. Introduce the two types of visibility: feed-based (Instagram, reach expires daily) vs index-based (Pinterest, content is stored and resurfaced)
  3. Proof, stacked with real Pinterest stats: 38% of viral pins are over two years old; pins accumulate instead of expiring; one page can become 200 pins and ~73k visitors a year
  4. Step 1: search your topic in the Pinterest search bar; the suggestions that pop up ARE your keywords
  5. Step 2: put those keywords everywhere: profile, board titles, pin titles, descriptions (naturally, don't stuff)
  6. Step 3: keep making new images: a fresh pin is a new image + new title on the same link, Pinterest reads it as brand new
  7. CTA: comment Pinterest → Pin Planner

Sources: Slowly Viral Class · 3.3 The Batch
