# Carousel Angle Proposals — 4 Weekly Topics

Repeatable angle types, not one-off concepts. Each one is a format column that can run fresh content through the same structural engine. All angles are designed to create a curiosity gap the cover slide cannot close alone.

---

## Week 1 — Email Marketing (Alyssa Style)

`The Myth Autopsy` — Takes a widely-believed email advice rule and dismantles it with actual data or client evidence — best archetype: Data Argument

`The Before/After System Map` — Two-scene contrast: what their email back-end looks like without a system vs. what it looks like when the Warm Plate Method is running — best archetype: Story/POV

`The Permission Slip` — A specific thing her audience feels guilty about not doing in email (sending less often, not A/B testing, having a small list) reframed as a strategic choice — best archetype: Manifesto

`The Single Rule` — One counter-intuitive principle about email that runs against what most marketing educators teach, unpacked over 7–8 slides — best archetype: Data Argument

`The Anatomy` — Breaks down one real email (a Simmer, a welcome sequence email, a promo email) into its working parts — what each section does and why — best archetype: Step Framework

`The Comparison` — Email vs. another channel (social, podcast, SEO) measured on a specific dimension: maintenance time, lead quality, longevity of content — best archetype: Data Argument

`The Sequence Story` — A client (or Alyssa's own) business ran on zero email infrastructure; here's what changed when the welcome sequence went live — best archetype: Story/POV

`The Slow Burn Argument` — Why compounding email beats high-volume posting over any 12-month window, using the dollar-cost averaging / library vs. slot machine framing — best archetype: Trend Report

## Week 2 — Pinterest Marketing (Alyssa Style)

`The Search Engine Reframe` — Pinterest is not a social platform; here is the specific mechanical difference that changes everything about how you use it — best archetype: Data Argument

`The Traffic Pantry Tour` — Walks through Alyssa's actual Pinterest content infrastructure (pillars, pin types, keyword logic) without framing it as a tutorial — best archetype: Step Framework

`The Longevity Data` — Uses real numbers (38% of viral pins are 2+ years old, etc.) to show why Pinterest content compounds while Instagram content disappears — best archetype: Data Argument

`The Platform Swap` — Personal decision to move time/energy from Instagram to Pinterest: what she stopped, what she built, what changed in leads — best archetype: Story/POV

`The Batch Logic` — Makes the case for batching 70 pins in a single session vs. creating daily; the math and the mental load argument — best archetype: Step Framework

`The Dead Account Comeback` — What happens when an inactive Pinterest account gets a keyword-first audit and the Slow Pinterest system applied — best archetype: Story/POV

`The Content Multiplier` — Shows how one piece of content (a blog post, a freebie, a podcast ep) generates X months of Pinterest pins through The Batch — best archetype: Trend Report

`The Beginner's Honest Map` — What you actually need to start Pinterest traffic (stripped of everything she says you can skip) — best archetype: Product Reveal

## Week 3 — Back Office Systems

`The System Reveal` — Pulls back the curtain on one specific system Alyssa runs in her own business (client onboarding, content scheduling, launch planning) with the actual components named — best archetype: Step Framework

`The Automation Myth` — One thing people think automation handles that it actually doesn't, and one thing it handles that people don't realize — best archetype: Data Argument

`The Hidden Time Cost` — Names a common back-office task (inbox management, social scheduling, proposal sending) and shows what it actually costs in hours per year — best archetype: Data Argument

`The Stack Breakdown` — The actual tools Alyssa uses, what each one does, and — critically — what she dropped and why — best archetype: Trend Report

`The One-Decision Rule` — Argues that most service pros are making the same low-stakes decision 40 times a week; here's the system that removes that decision entirely — best archetype: Manifesto

`The Soft CEO Week` — What a week actually looks like when back office systems are running: what she does, what she doesn't do, what happens in the background — best archetype: Story/POV

`The First Domino` — The single system she would build first if starting over, and why it unlocks everything else — best archetype: Step Framework

`The Client Experience Map` — Traces the full client journey from DM to delivered project and marks every point where a system either exists or should — best archetype: Step Framework

---

## Week 4 — Soft Productivity

`The Anti-Routine Routine` — What her actual day looks like — not the idealized version, the real version with ADHD and a daughter and a clay studio — best archetype: Story/POV

`The Guilt Inventory` — Lists the things her audience feels guilty about not doing (batching content every week, answering emails same-day, having a morning routine) and systematically gives permission to stop — best archetype: Manifesto

`The Deep Work Architecture` — How she protects creative time structurally, not motivationally — no "discipline" required, just system design — best archetype: Step Framework

`The Rabbit Hole Defense` — Makes the case that wasting time on a hyperfixation is productive; the creative work that came out of her most "unproductive" periods — best archetype: Manifesto

`The Energy Map` — Not a schedule — an energy audit. When her brain does what kind of work, and how the calendar is built around that rather than against it — best archetype: Step Framework

`The ADHD Reframe` — Named as a feature: the specific ways ADHD shows up in how she runs her business and why she stopped trying to work around it — best archetype: Manifesto

`The Offline Argument` — What happens to the business when she's genuinely offline for a week. Not a fear hook — a proof point — best archetype: Data Argument

`The Rest as Strategy` — Reframes rest not as recovery from work but as the prerequisite for the creative work that actually makes the business money — best archetype: Manifesto
