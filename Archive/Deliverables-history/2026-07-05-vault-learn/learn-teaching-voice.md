## 1. The teaching moves

These are the named, reusable moves she runs when teaching a real thing. Each one is a pattern a smaller model can execute directly.

### Move 1: Mundane-scene-first framing (the domestic on-ramp)

**Definition:** Enter every topic through a small, specific, slightly absurd domestic scene that carries *conflict or loss* — never through the concept. The scene becomes the mechanism's metaphor, and the pivot is always confessed out loud ("I couldn't help but see the parallels"), never pre-announced.

Verbatim examples from three different emails:

> "K, so I have this problem, [FIRST NAME GOES HERE]. **My daughter HATES getting her hair washed.** like, she goes full CAT IN WATER MODE." — *gentle-parent-your-business-model* (bath meltdown → launch amnesia)

> "**_I cried into my fridge this week, [FIRST NAME GOES HERE]._** I MIGHT have been in my luteal phase… but i swear that had nothing to do with it 🙃" — *your-list-called-theyre-hungry* (full messy fridge → full cold email list)

> "So, a few years ago I **mapped out an entire automated offer funnel on a napkin** during a red eye flight to LA... Buuuut somewhere between wheels down and room service, it disappeared. 🫠 **Gone.**" — *when-delta-stole-my-business-idea* (lost napkin → ideas need a system, not memory)

**When/why she deploys it:** It buys attention before the reader knows they're being taught, and it makes the eventual diagnosis feel *discovered* rather than pitched. Hard constraints from [[Studio/Analysis/simmer-opening-forensics]]: the scene must pass ≥2 of {conflict, loss/stakes, absurdity, emotional charge}; the pivot to business must land by ~35% of body; the metaphor must yield ≥2 distinct business insights and be rich enough to joke about ("(Okay I swear I'm done with the bath metaphors)"). A static object of desire (a thing she merely wants) is auto-rejected — wanting is not conflict.

### Move 2: The restaurant/kitchen metaphor as *naming system*, not decoration

**Definition:** Food/hospitality language is structural — it names offers, phases, departments, and CTAs. It does NOT run through the middle of arguments. Metaphor density peaks at section names, product architecture, and CTAs; body copy mid-argument drops it.

> "**MEET YOUR BACK OF HOUSE DEPARTMENTS: OFFER DEPARTMENT — The Signature Dish** — Concoct a menu of email ready offers so irresistible, 'yes chef' is the only option" — *boh-sales-email-pinterest-plan*

> "the freebie is ladies night: There's free margaritas every Thursday. For the girls, it's no commitment... Now we say, hey, by the way, the app menu's on the table and chips and guac are half price tonight. This is an obvious yes" — *1.4 Double Yes Freebie Transcript* (an extended set piece — legal ONCE per lesson as a teaching centerpiece)

> "# O1: Pinterest Traffic Kiosk ... # O2: Email Reception Desk ... # O3: (Soft) Sales Register" — *when-delta-stole-my-business-idea* (the free "manual" itself gets hospitality names)

**When/why:** The metaphor makes the system *memorable and repeatable* — students can "tell their sister about it." Per STYLE.md §4, one extended-stretch moment per piece max; FAQ/pricing/tech zones go metaphor-free.

### Move 3: Belief-shift-before-mechanics (Symptoms → Diagnosis → Prescription)

**Definition:** Never teach the how until the reader believes her explanation of *why the problem exists*. She dismantles the reader's current diagnosis first, then the method arrives as relief.

> "People will not buy if they don't believe the diagnosis." — *2.1 Warm Plates Sequence (via boh-teaching-index-phases-0-2)*

> "**But here's what I've learned:** most people **DONT** actually want to go viral... **When I dig deeper, what they really want is to finally stop worrying about WHERE THEIR NEXT CLIENT IS COMING FROM**" — *slowly-viral-webinar-invite* (belief shift delivered before a single tactic)

> "You do not have a lead problem... What you most likely have is a conversion problem." — *Slow Marketing Plan Class (via boh-teaching-index-phases-3-5)*

**When/why:** This is the load-bearing move of every conversion event and welcome sequence: "1) Disrupt industry norms ('why the old way is BS') 2) Validate their nightmare & dream 3) Reveal the new way (your philosophy) 4) Teach the method (back it up) 5) Inspire action" (*4.1 Lazy Launch*). The pitch should "feel like a relief — oh, there is something that does this. Not that you're now starting to persuade them" (*1.2 Signature Dish Transcript*).

### Move 4: Go one layer deeper (the symptom of the symptom)

**Definition:** Never stop at the surface pain everyone else markets to. Name the hyper-specific downstream consequence — the thing that actually happens in their kitchen, calendar, or bank app.

> "Maybe it's that they're cancelling their kid's sports camp. Ouch, that hurts. Maybe it's avoiding opening their bank statement because they know it's not going to be good... That's so hyper specific that they're like, shit, she gets me, okay?" — *1.2 Your Signature Dish Transcript*

> "do you have a room in your house where you store stuff and hope nobody goes into? Are you so tired of the mental load every time you walk into a room and think oh god, I should deal with that pile." — *1.2 Your Signature Dish Transcript* (professional organizer example)

> "_So you create shitty posts and half assed emails and graphics you don't love._ 🫠 But you're doing it. It's … not your BEST work.. **But it's fine because… at least it's done.**" — *silent-killer-of-good-work* (the same move applied in a live sales email)

**When/why:** "These are things that make people actually buy. Just saying you have clutter is so obvious." The deeper layer is also where the pain stays *structural, never personal* — and can even be elevated into a compliment: "**It's that you have HIGH STANDARDS.**" (*silent-killer*).

### Move 5: Name the framework, then repeat it forever

**Definition:** Every reusable idea gets a sticky proper name (Bacon and Butter Rule, Double Yes, Fridge Test, BFF Test, Hawaii, Right Time Offers, Sign Off Specials, the 84 Combinations), and the name is repeated across lessons, emails, and sales pages until it's internal vocabulary.

> "is this Bacon and butter or am I giving oatmeal right now?" — *1.2 Signature Dish Transcript* (she teaches students to interrogate their own draft with the named rule)

> "Would my person wanna print this out and put it on their fridge?... Would they screenshot it? Would they put it in their notes app?... These are things that pass the fridge test." — *1.4 Double Yes Freebie Transcript*

> "i'd install **'right time offers'** in your email strategy so people can buy when THEY'RE ready, not just when you're selling (aka sales come in between promos)" — *boh-sales-email-pinterest-plan* (course vocabulary reused verbatim in a sales email)

**When/why:** Names turn one-time insights into portable tools and make the paid system feel like a coherent world. When a new model coins a concept, it should name it in her register (playful, food/hospitality-coded, concrete) — but per ANTI-AI.md, never recycle her documented winners as templates; "her hits are evidence of taste, not templates."

### Move 6: Real-numbers-as-proof, delivered mid-sentence with personality

**Definition:** Specific, often odd numbers from her own business or real research, embedded in a sentence — never a "FACT:" stat dump. Numbers name the enemy or prove the mechanism.

> "**_💳 Why: Email converts 3x better than social media._** _Plus, the ROI: A cool $42 for every $1 spent._" — *boh-sales-email-pinterest-plan*

> "So I figured out how to build something **that generates leads for YEARS**, instead of just churning out content that lasts (at best) for 19.4 hours." — *slow-marketing-plan-webinar-invite*

> "This $37 product paid for my maternity leave" / "We know that once someone makes one purchase from you, the data says they're 70% more likely to purchase from you a second time" — *1.5 Perfect Apps / 1.4 Double Yes transcripts*

**When/why:** The numbers do the disruption work so she never has to argue from vibes: "Always names the enemy precisely with fresh, memorable stats before offering the alternative" (*boh-teaching-index-phases-3-5*, webinar moves). Odd numbers beat round ("$12,212 coupon," "5848 tabs," "1,400 pins").

### Move 7: Compress the build-up into one quotable line ("Translation:")

**Definition:** After a long teaching stretch, she lands a single plain-spoken compression — often literally headed "Translation:" or "TLDR:".

> "### Translation: # Email Turns Your Pinterest Traffic Into Paying Clients" — *boh-sales-email-pinterest-plan*

> "The full system in one sentence: Pinterest drives traffic to your best evergreen content, which pre-qualifies visitors and sends them to your email list, where you consistently sell your signature offer." — *Slowly Viral Class*

> "Virality is a casino. Pinterest is dollar-cost averaging." — *Slowly Viral Class*

**When/why:** The compression is the fridge-test artifact — the line the student quotes to a friend. It also manages overwhelm: "That's a system, guys. That's it." / "It's not that deep."

### Move 8: Permission structure + overwhelm deflation

**Definition:** She explicitly scopes what to do now vs. ignore, gives permission to move fast on tactics and slow on foundations, and deflates the task's perceived size at the exact moment students would spiral.

> "Everybody out here is trying to overcomplicate what an actual launch is. It's not that deep. I've done more than 30 launches in my own business and been behind the scenes in thousands." — *4.1 Lazy Launch Transcript*

> "Things you don't have to worry about yet are figuring out what your tripwire is or writing your tripwire sales page... Just decide on your freebie, create it and start thinking about how you're going to actually sell it on your opt-in page. All right? Go on and get your first yes. Bye." — *1.4 Double Yes Freebie Transcript*

> "I would rather you send one crappy email than no email that week. Get it out the door." — *2.3 Simmer (via teaching index)*

**When/why:** Contrast-managed: "take your time here" on philosophy/foundations, "don't overthink this... it's gum" on tactical execution. The permission IS the product promise (a business that doesn't eat you), so the teaching voice must model it.

### Move 9: The worked example from her own real copy, annotated line by line

**Definition:** Instead of hypotheticals, she walks through her own named, top-performing artifact and narrates what each line is doing psychologically.

> "Let's actually go through an example together... this is one of my top performing emails, maybe my top performing email of all of my sequences ever." — *2.1 Warm Plates Transcript (via teaching index)* — she then annotates: opens with a relatable quote ("Omg, I've been SO busy"), a vulnerable detail pulled from discovery-call language ("I ate lunch at my desk"), a belief shift ("I wasn't being productive at all"), a future-pace ("we took the scenic route").

> "That's a real story from a real person and that's something that I'm gonna tell my friend about" — *1.2 Signature Dish Transcript* (the credit-card-points story, used to teach outcome-language)

> "Chappell Roan was playing Pink Pony Club in delis before she changed THIS BRANDING TECHNIQUE and then she headlined Coachella with the same song." — *scriptbook.md* (Pop Culture Proof — a named, reusable proof device)

**When/why:** Proof-by-artifact builds trust the same way her confession openers do — she shows the receipts instead of claiming authority. A model teaching "as Alyssa" should quote her real examples, never invent composite case studies.

### Move 10: Trial-close questioning (phrase pain as questions, collect yeses)

**Definition:** Pain points are phrased as questions the reader answers "yes" to internally — never as accusations. The sale is a stack of small agreements before the offer appears.

> "I don't want you to say you suck at Branding... You feel like you don't have the branding touch? Question mark? We don't want to be calling them out at this point... We want to be instantly getting a yes as much as possible." — *1.2 Your Signature Dish Transcript*

> "You ever wish for that, [FIRST NAME GOES HERE]? …that someone would just hand you THE book." — *when-delta-stole-my-business-idea*

> "🥲 Is it you? 🥹 Is it them?" — *i-fixed-your-email-list*

**When/why:** "Russell Brunson calls these trial closes. You want them being like, yes, Before they ever even get to the button." The question form keeps pain structural and preserves the reader's dignity.

## 2. How she solves real problems in examples

The arc is always the same five stations: **reader pain → named problem → her mechanism → tiny win → gap toward the offer.** Three end-to-end traces:

### Trace A — *gentle-parent-your-business-model* (Simmer)

1. **Reader pain (mirrored through her scene):** The bath fight that resets every night — "as soon as bath time rolls back around it's like **NONE OF THAT EVER HAPPENED** 😑 **We're back at square one.**" Then the pivot to the reader's version: "the **launch would land, I'd sell spots (!!) I'd breathe again**. 🦗 ...but then…crickets... Not because the launch flopped. But because people FORGOT. And **I had no system to remind them.**"
2. **Named problem:** She gives the recurring pain a headline the reader has literally asked themselves: "# why does it feel like I'm always starting over?"
3. **Her mechanism (diagnosis first):** "Well, here's why: **the internet is one giant attention sinkhole.** 🫠 **If your best stuff isn't living in assets, emails, pinterest, or a sequence... it just evaporates 😬🫧**"
4. **Tiny win:** The reframe itself is the win — the reader now knows the launch didn't flop and they aren't broken; the *storage medium* was wrong. (Note: in short Simmers the tiny win can be purely diagnostic.)
5. **Gap toward the offer:** "# ☝️ But I've got the fix (for the business part at least) **Inside** [**_Back of House_**]**, we build EVERGREEN, email-first systems that:** Keep your audience warm (between promos)..." then real scarcity + the magic-word CTA: "**We've got one spot left this month.** 📌 _Reply_ **'SHAMPOO'** _and I'll send you the link_."

### Trace B — *boh-sales-email-pinterest-plan* (sales email)

1. **Reader pain:** "So many ppl I run into lately are **Pinterest-curious**, but not quite sold (fair), or just wondering if it'll work if they have **NO presence on pinterest** right now."
2. **Named problem → her mechanism as a full free plan:** "**If i were starting from ZERO in 2026 & wanted to get all my clients from pinterest** # Here's Exactly What I Would Do..." — then four complete steps (Audit my Offers → Prep for Pin Traffic → Clone Myself → Send Better Emails), each with a *why* backed by numbers ("it can drive traffic to your list for months (or even years) after posting").
3. **Tiny win:** The reader leaves with a real, followable plan — "(and maybe just follow this plan if YOUR starting from scratch 👇)". The TLDR restates it as theirs: "**If you were my client, [FIRST NAME GOES HERE], this is the exact system I'd build for you 👇**"
4. **Gap toward the offer:** The plan is complete at strategy level but enormous at execution level, and she names the shortcut: "# I've spent the last year perfecting this automated strategy so I can package it up and deliver it to you on a silver platter. [**🛎️ Make Your Reservation >>**]" — the departments, templates, bots, weekly calls, and "**turns 4 of your best posts into 1,400 fresh pins in under a minute**" are all execution machinery for the plan she just gave away.

### Trace C — *i-fixed-your-email-list* (Soft Sunday, the maximal value issue)

1. **Reader pain, validated before redirected:** "**your email list looks solid on paper.** The numbers are good, or getting there. 😅 But **there's a sneaky chunk of subs who haven't opened an email in months and it's nagging at the back of your mind** and you're wondering wtf you should do about it."
2. **Named problem (belief shift):** List Envy is declared OUT — "people have **clung** to the idea that 'bigger is better'... 👀 Mmmm…maybe not." with three concrete reasons (deliverability, "You Foot the Bill," ego).
3. **Her mechanism:** Re-engagement Rituals — prune + re-engage, with the counter-intuitive core: "cutting cold subscribers doesn't mean you're giving up on them 🥹. You're simply _prioritising_ the ones who want to hear from you."
4. **Tiny win (fully executable, free):** "**🕵️‍♀️ DAY ONE — Find Cold Subscribers**... **✍️ DAY TWO — Re-Engagement Campaign**... **✂️ DAY THREE — Prune the List**" plus seven re-engagement email types WITH writing prompts, plus "[_To make it easier, I'll let you have one of my automation templates 🫶._]" and a free Notion plan.
5. **Gap toward the offer:** Only after the full gift: "👀 Did something about me just handing you my automation make marketing feel more doable? **I do this for all email sequences and newsletters within Back of House**... _I've got room for 2 more people to get feedback... Reply AUTOMATION and I'll send the link_." The offer is the *scaled, personalized* version of the thing the reader just experienced once for free.

**The generalized arc rule:** the offer must arrive as *the obvious way to keep having the experience the email just gave you* — never as a subject change. Per [[Studio/Analysis/soft-sunday]]: "The product is the action you take after you're convinced — not the reason for the email."

## 3. Email anatomy

Two distinct templates. Do not blend their slot logic.

### Template A — Sales email (promo / launch / invite)

| Slot | Mechanics | Verbatim example |
|---|---|---|
| **Subject line** | Job-based (see [[Studio/Analysis/subject-line]]): for action, sharper not softer — anti-advice, I-FIXED, deliverable nouns, odd dollar amounts. ≤40 chars. | `🙅‍♀️ DON'T "grow your list" do this instead…` (5.42% CTR, #1 of 351) · `The silent killer of GOOD work. (pls avoid!⚠️)` |
| **Preview text** | Second subject line doing a *different* job — urgency or register-extension, never summary. | "a call to arms for the creative w high standards" — *silent-killer* |
| **Pre.S / open** | Either a Pre.S. that front-loads the deal for skimmers, or a market observation/provocation. Never "Today I want to..." | "**Pre.S.** _We're installing a Back of House System for 10 entrepreneurs... get your buns to this page & use code ORDERUP to **save $500** before friday_" — *boh-sales-email* |
| **Story / belief shift** | The reader's current diagnosis dismantled; contrarian reversal with receipts. | "**And after we get comfortable, they sometimes reluctantly admit ….** **'I just wanna go viral'** ... **but I know they mean it.**" then "fast, viral spikes ALMOST NEVER turn into real engagement growth (they tend to reverse right after the spike 🤡)" — *slowly-viral-webinar-invite* |
| **Value drop (the free plan)** | A complete strategic skeleton, numbered, each step with a why + a number. | "## 📈 Step O1: Audit my Offers ... **Pinterest drives the traffic, but your inbox does the closing.** 📈" — *boh-sales-email* |
| **Translation / TLDR** | One-line compression + restate the plan as theirs. | "**TLDR:** **If you were my client, [FIRST NAME GOES HERE], this is the exact system I'd build for you 👇**" |
| **Offer block** | The product as the execution machinery, named in metaphor architecture (departments/menu), concrete deliverables, price + coupon + real deadline. | "**EMAIL DEPARTMENT — The Servers** — Clone your best marketer (you) and automate your sales with our proven recipe for done-once emails... [**🌶️ LET'S COOK >>**] _(coupon valid till friday)_" |
| **CTA(s)** | Multiple, escalating; reader's inner monologue or food-world, never commands. | "[**🛎️ Make Your Reservation >>**]" · "[**👻 Ghost your Feed Tmrw >>**]" |
| **PS** | Re-pitches with a FRESH angle (a bonus, tomorrow's call, a segment-specific hook) — never repeats. | "**PSST:** ☎️ **YOUR FIRST CALL IS TOMORROW [FIRST NAME GOES HERE]!** ☎️ ..._if you join today you can literally have my eyes on your business before lunch._" |
| **Footer** | Fixed brand block + honest segment opt-out during promo weeks. | "Designed by a reformed girl-boss (hi! It's me, Alyssa)... 🤎 **Lean into your anti-social side**: [Join Back of House.]" · "_If you're not interested, click here and you won't receive any more enrollment emails for this round. (don't worry, you'll still get our regularly scheduled Soft Sunday emails)._" |

### Template B — Simmer / nurture email (weekly, single-topic, 300–600 words)

| Slot | Mechanics | Verbatim example |
|---|---|---|
| **Subject line** | Register collision or confession, lowercase, ≤40 chars; business payoff may be named to pre-pay a late pivot. | "my existential crisis & pinterest." · "let me gentle parent your business model." |
| **Preview text** | Pre-loads the turn so the domestic scene has runway. | "this one starts in the bath and ends in your bank account." |
| **Open (mid-scene)** | Diary cold-open with conflict, [FIRST NAME] in line 1–2 mid-sentence. | "whats up [FIRST NAME GOES HERE], I just finished re-organizing my closet (& NOTHING was sparking joy 🫠)." |
| **Story (≤35% of body)** | The scene fights, loses, or loops; comic texture accumulates. | "so, my compromise is to create **these little uniforms instead 💅**, summer Alyssa lives in linen pants and a button up, while winter Alyssa goes for (stretchy) trousers and a cozy knit 🤎." |
| **Named pivot** | The parallel is confessed, not announced — fresh words each time. | "**😏 And all this pinterest-ing got me thinking about the marketing version of this:**" · "it **reminded me of exactly how I used to feel after a launch** 🙈" |
| **Reframe / value drop** | One belief shift + 1–2 usable tactics, each sourced. | "**But that doesn't mean you reinvent your offer.** 👏 i repeat. 👏 NO NEW OFFERS. 👏" then "**1) 🍎 Back to School:** Bundle your offer with a 'structure' bonus. **Think:** calendars, workflows, checklists... _(Source: The Promo Planner (Notion Edition))_" |
| **Offer (woven, not announced)** | "That's why I built..." — the product resolves the exact tension the story raised. | "That's why I built the [**Seasonal Promo Planner**] a Notion dashboard designed to turn your one signature offer into four fresh, done for you promo campaigns" |
| **CTA** | Right Time Offer mechanics: reply-with-a-magic-word + real scarcity, or a coupon with a personality reason. | "📌 _Reply_ **'SHAMPOO'** _and I'll send you the link to join us inside Back of House_" · "[**use code LUKE at checkout and get 50% off**] 🔥 because sometimes you need strong coffee and a good deal" |
| **PS** | Carries the offer one more time with a new proof point; the metaphor gets its exit joke here. | "**PS.** The algorithm can't remember your best post from last week. My Pinterest funnel still brings in leads from 2021. [Reply 'shampoo']..." |
| **Footer** | The "BTW If your business feels too loud rn" three-offer menu + brand block. | "🥖 _**Plan Your Month Softly: Soft Ceo Sessions**_ 🥖 ... ☁️ _**Steal my Notion Planner**_ ☁️ ... 🛎️ _**Automate your Business' Back of House**_ 🛎️" |

**Soft Sunday variant** (the multi-section newsletter) adds the fixed section furniture — Pep Talk (🫶...🫶), House Report (WHAT'S IN/WHAT'S OUT), Nerding Out On, Bake Break, Time Luxuries, Creep My List, and the non-negotiable Check In ritual: mood binary → anchor word ("This week I'm going with: **🤎 Restore 🤎**") → "_'my week would be a success if the only thing I did was ________________.'_" → "Wishing you a cozy, soft Sunday 🤎". Full slot law is in [[Studio/Analysis/soft-sunday]]; don't re-derive it.

**The core structural difference:** in a sales email the CTA is the point of the email and appears multiple times; in a Simmer/Soft Sunday the CTA is what you do *after* you got the point, appears once or twice, late. Formula from the course itself: "Story → Point → Value Drop → (optional) Right Time Offer... Not every simmer email needs an offer — most should just end on value." (*2.3 Weekly Simmer*)

## 4. The snippet-selection rule

**This is the section to get right.** Smaller models fail in both directions: they either dump the whole method (nothing left to buy) or gatekeep everything (the free content is useless and trust never forms). Alyssa's actual practice is neither. Derived from comparing what she publishes free against what BOH sells:

### The operational heuristic

**Give away the diagnosis and the skeleton. Sell the reps, the assets, and the eyes.**

Concretely, a snippet is SAFE TO TEACH FREE when it is:
1. **The full belief shift / diagnosis** — always free, at full strength. "People will not buy if they don't believe the diagnosis" — so the diagnosis IS the marketing. She never waters it down.
2. **A complete strategic skeleton** — numbered steps, the whole shape of the system, including the why and a real number per step. The reader must be able to genuinely act on it ("maybe just follow this plan if YOUR starting from scratch 👇").
3. **One fully-executable tiny win** — usable in under ~30 minutes, no purchase required (the 3-day list cleanup; one free automation template; two seasonal layering tactics with a fill-in-the-blank script: "_For [limited time], you'll also get [bonus stack or incentive] so your [desired transformation] pays you back long after [the offer] ends._").
4. **Anything that makes the remaining work *legible but large*** — the free plan reveals scale ("1,400 unique pins," "25 pre-written email prompts," a year of sequences). Understanding it is free; doing it by hand is the pain the offer removes.

A snippet is HELD FOR PAID when it is:
1. **Volume assets** — swipe files, template banks, prompt libraries, the things that collapse execution time. "The **actual swipe vault** I use to write these right time offer emails (including Over 50 ready to roll email prompts, 80 subject lines, 60+ postscript CTAs and plug-and-play segment strategies) is inside [Back of House] 😏."
2. **The named sub-mechanics that make the system repeatable** — she'll say the rules exist and how many there are, but not what they are: "I have **3 rules** I follow when planning an email-ready offer suite, we'd cross check those" (*boh-sales-email* — the three rules themselves are Phase 1 content).
3. **Her eyes** — reviews, feedback, custom application: "Submit your emails, offers, and strategy for **direct feedback from me**" / "_I've got room for 2 more people to get feedback on their sequence before Friday._"
4. **The automation/tooling that removes the labor** — the Pin Planner, the bots, "The PIN Keymaster... **You show up once. It keeps showing up for you**."
5. **Raw implementation detail that would bore or overwhelm in email anyway** — "I wouldn't dare put real math in this email" (*i-failed-formula-for-growth*, linking a spreadsheet instead). Framework depth in the email; implementation depth in the linked asset or product. Per [[Studio/Analysis/soft-sunday]]: "the Soft Sunday teaches the concept and gives the structure; the lead magnet, product, or linked resource contains the implementation."

### The doctrine behind it, in her own words

> "Useful does not lead to conversions. There are tons of free, helpful things on the internet... This is not a random act of kindness. I'm sorry, but it's strategy. We wanna think of this as giving them the shot and then selling the chaser. **We are here to get them to want the next thing in your funnel.**" — *1.4 Double Yes Freebie Transcript*

> "there's a difference between giving away a free margarita mixing class and the history of tequila for free and just giving the people what they want." — *1.4* (i.e., free ≠ comprehensive; free = exactly the wanted thing)

> "It should be so good that if someone else was offering it, they might even pay for it, but you've made it free... We want them thinking by the end of this — wait, if this is free, what else do they know?" — *1.4*

> The tripwire bar: "Would I feel low-key nervous about giving this away so cheap because it's that good? If yes, it's probably perfect." Plus: solve "without teaching" (solve, don't educate), usable within 10–30 minutes. — *1.5 The Perfect Apps*

> "We wanna solve a problem that they know they have now, and then reveal the problem that they don't see yet... it really should feel like getting an appetizer that, as soon as it hits the table, you're like, damn, I didn't even realize how hungry I was." — *1.4* (the free thing must OPEN a gap, not close all of them)

### Concrete given-away / held-back comparisons

**Comparison 1 — the Delta "manual" email.** GIVEN AWAY: the entire three-part system at cliff's-notes depth, each with its why: "choose one evergreen freebie & create 3 pins for it using keywords your audience is **actually** searching for (not just a catchy hook)"; "Start with a belief shift or NEW philosophy they need to hear... Keep it short, story focused & persuasive"; "install '**right time offers**'... Plus, the ROI? A cool $42 for every $1 spent." HELD BACK: the swipe vault (50 prompts, 80 subject lines, 60+ PS CTAs, segment strategies) and the live review spots. The reader can start today; BOH makes it fast, plentiful, and reviewed.

**Comparison 2 — the Pinterest plan sales email.** GIVEN AWAY: the exact 4-step architecture, the first 3 welcome-sequence email types ("One that shifts their belief and shows them my philosophy (and why it's different) / A real convo I've had with a client (they can relate to) / A big AHA moment from my method (a reframe they didn't know they needed)"), and the strategic why of each step. HELD BACK: the "3 rules" for an email-ready offer suite (named, counted, not stated), the sales page templates, the written sequences themselves, the Pin Generator that "turns 4 of your best posts into 1,400 fresh pins in under a minute," and weekly calls. Note the pattern: *email types* are free; *the written emails* are paid.

**Comparison 3 — the I-FIXED Soft Sunday.** GIVEN AWAY: an unusually complete gift — 3-day plan, 7 re-engagement email types with prompts AND example copy ("_Example Subject Line: 'Have I done something wrong? 😢' or 'are we in a fight'_"), one real automation template, a free Notion hub. HELD BACK: "**I do this for all email sequences and newsletters within Back of House** ... Templates for: 💌 **Welcome** sequences 🔥 **Weekly** simmer emails with right time offers 💵 **Promo** emails" — i.e., the *category* is demonstrated once for free; the *complete library across every sequence type* is the product. She then explicitly converts the feeling into the pitch: "👀 Did something about me just handing you my automation make marketing feel more doable?"

**The one-line test for a smaller model:** *After reading the free thing, the reader should be able to explain the whole system to their sister AND start step one today — but doing all of it well, fast, and repeatedly should obviously require the paid thing.* If the free draft leaves the reader unable to act, you held back too much. If the paid thing has no remaining job (no volume assets, no personalization, no automation, no feedback), you gave away too much.

## 5. Do-not list

Anti-patterns conspicuously ABSENT from her real work, each with the verbatim counter-example of what she does instead. (Vocabulary and punctuation bans live in [[Hermes/ANTI-AI]] — these are the *teaching/selling shape* bans.)

**1. Topic-first openings ("Today I want to talk about email list health...")**
Never appears. Every email opens mid-scene or mid-confession.
> INSTEAD: "Did i ever tell you about the time **I almost quit my business because of delta.**" — *when-delta-stole-my-business-idea*

**2. The pre-announced metaphor ("Let me use a fridge analogy...")**
The metaphor is discovered on the page and confessed after the fact, then explicitly retired before the teaching.
> INSTEAD: "🍳 As it happens, I was also working on a [NEW **3 day email list revival plan**] and I couldn't help but see the parallels." ... "(they're missing you like you missed the chili paste in the side drawer - lol ok im done with the food thing.)" — *your-list-called-theyre-hungry*

**3. Generic listicle teaching ("5 tips for better email marketing")**
Her numbered content always carries a personal frame and a stake; a subject line that summarizes content is "a table of contents, not a subject line" (*voice-profile*).
> INSTEAD: "**I failed statistics in university [FIRST NAME GOES HERE] … twice**" → then and only then, the three growth formulas — *i-failed-formula-for-growth* (the failure story is what earns the formulas)

**4. Symmetric AI antithesis ("It's not X. It's Y.") and clean reframe shapes**
The one clean instance in the corpus lives in the email Alyssa herself flagged as too-AI ("Re-commerce **isn't just** a business strategy. It's a mindset." — *me-i-need-ideas*, flagged). Her real contrast is asymmetrical, softened, split across lines with a beat.
> INSTEAD: "Not because the launch flopped. / But because people FORGOT. / And **I had no system to remind them.**" — *gentle-parent* (three lines, a beat between each, caps carrying the emphasis)

**5. Hedged, both-sides teaching ("You might want to consider possibly...")**
She commits with doctrine-level force, then softens with permission — never the reverse.
> INSTEAD: "**But that doesn't mean you reinvent your offer.** 👏 i repeat. 👏 NO NEW OFFERS. 👏" — *my-existential-crisis-and-pinterest*; and when she must scope a claim she does it with lived precision, not hedging: "Don't get me wrong, **social media works wonders for some people**... but it **just doesn't work for me (and my life) 🙈**" — *why-i-burned-down-my-business*

**6. Fake urgency and manufactured scarcity**
No countdown timers on evergreen offers; every deadline in the corpus is a real coupon expiry, real spots, or a real call tomorrow.
> INSTEAD: "**We've got one spot left this month.**" — *gentle-parent* · "_I've got room for 2 more people to get feedback on their sequence before Friday._" — *when-delta* · the doctrine itself: "If someone can just join next quarter, they'll join next quarter... 'I'm going to open it again at this time, but this coupon and this bonus are expiring right now.'" — *4.1 Lazy Launch*

**7. Motivational-poster pep talk ("You've got this!! Believe in yourself!")**
Her pep talks are specific visions with named metrics, warmth qualified through parentheticals ("my business besties (you)").
> INSTEAD: "**🔮 My big sis vision for you in 2026 is to have a list of warm leads, high open rates, real engagement, legit click rates, and consistent sales.** **You deserve it, babe. 💖**" — *i-fixed-your-email-list*

**8. Shame-based pain sections ("Stop making excuses. You're doing it wrong.")**
Pain is structural (the system/algorithm/model failed), and can even elevate the reader.
> INSTEAD: "**And you're definitely not doing it wrong**, but the truth is that most of us service providers, coaches and creatives were taught 'how to show up online'" — *slow-marketing-plan-webinar-invite* · "**It's that you have HIGH STANDARDS.**" — *silent-killer-of-good-work*

**9. Cold product drops ("By the way, I also sell a course.")**
Every offer in the corpus appears only after the content that makes it the obvious next step, usually via "That's why I built..."
> INSTEAD: "Once you know how to reframe your core offer for each, you don't need new jeans, you just need [the right layering]. --- That's why I built the [**Seasonal Promo Planner**]" — *my-existential-crisis-and-pinterest*

**10. Comprehensive-course-in-an-email (over-teaching)**
She never does implementation depth in email — framework depth in the send, execution in the linked asset or product, stated openly.
> INSTEAD: "I wouldn't dare put real math in this email" (links the spreadsheet) — *i-failed-formula-for-growth* · "_(dw - I didn't leave you out to dry with this. I've got specific ideas and examples below)_" — *i-fixed-your-email-list*

**11. Command CTAs ("Buy Now" / "Sign Up" / "Click Here" / "Learn More")**
Zero instances. CTAs are the reader's inner monologue, food-world, or a magic reply word.
> INSTEAD: "[**🛎️ Make Your Reservation >>**]" · "[**👻 Ghost your Feed Tmrw >>**]" · "_Reply_ **'SHAMPOO'**" · "[**📌 Say No More >>**]"

**12. Teaching without receipts (arguing from vibes)**
Every enemy she names comes with a stat or a lived number; every method claim comes with her own results.
> INSTEAD: "_Don't just take my word for it, according to this 2025 study in Scientific Reports,_ _**viral posts rarely lead to real long-term engagement growth…**_" — *slowly-viral-webinar-invite* · "My Pinterest funnel still brings in leads from 2021." — *gentle-parent PS*

**13. The tidy, evenly-weighted lesson (AI's balanced three thin bullets)**
Her structures are lopsided on purpose — one step gets a soapbox, one gets a joke, one gets two sentences. Per ANTI-AI.md the rule-of-three ban targets shallow enumeration, not deeply developed structures ("Reason nO1/nO2/nO3" each ran 300–500 words with its own scene). If every item in your list is the same length and temperature, it will read as machine output.
> INSTEAD: compare the four steps of the *boh-sales-email* plan — Step O1 is two sentences with a withheld rule-count, Step O2 is a bulleted mini-tutorial with a why-block, Step O3 is a three-email brief, Step O4 gets the joke ("**(Alt title: make email your sugar daddy)**").
