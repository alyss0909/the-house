## What over-performed, and why

**Threat-payoff and Paradox covers win views and shares.** The two highest-view posts (Jan 20: 132K, Mar 3: 57K) both use a payoff riding the SAME cover line — a reframe-shaped promise or a flat threat statement — never a second-person question. Jan 29 stacks Paradox three separate times in one deck and still holds together because each paradox resolves a specific, named shame (laziness, SEO intimidation, Pinterest "addiction").

**Save-rate rewards the "gap left open" teaching move, not volume of information.** Jan 15 (5,781 saves, the top save count) delivers 8 slides of pure trend analysis with the freebie appearing only at the very end, earned by the argument rather than interrupting it — textbook one-nuance-then-gap. Jan 20 (4,070 saves) does the identical move: "what's shifting" analysis first, "what to do instead" only after full agitation.

**Pure identity content (no teaching, no data) outperforms education on share ratio.** Jan 22 ("Wasting Time") and Feb 19 ("Sugar Daddy") both post shares and saves competitive with or exceeding the educational posts, despite carrying zero framework and (in Jan 22's case) no CTA until an empty-logo slide. The "I" litany structure and the visual-joke cover do emotional work that out-converts explanation. This validates FTG as a full lane, not filler (Fable failure mode #9).

**The offer is always earned, never interrupts.** Across every top post, the CTA/chat-word slide is the LAST slide (or second-to-last with a repeat), never mid-deck as a hard pitch. Jan 29 seeds the offer three times but always as an aside ("comment PINTEREST" in handwritten script), never the headline claim.

**Personal-testimony ("Why I Swapped X For Y") is a repeatable structural winner**, not just a formula on paper — Feb 24 uses it directly and lands strong comment counts (251, highest of the Pinterest-lane posts), confirming the "and not but" rule from [[Deliverables/2026-07-05-social-skills-repo-intake/extracted/hook-patterns-map]] in practice.

## Which patterns to bet on for the 6 posts (decision)

- **Bet on:** threat-payoff / paradox cover-hooks that ride the same-line reframe; earned-CTA-at-the-end structure; FTG as a real lane with its own "I" litany mechanic; personal-testimony format for one slot.
- **Do not force:** carousel gymnastics onto W3/W4 slots that already have real teaching in Soft Sunday/email/BOH source (per MAP §3 ruling) — those lanes should teach from their real source, not manufacture a fake carousel arc.
- **Avoid:** numbered "mistakes" listicle shape (never appears in the top 9); second-person cover questions (zero instances in the top 9 — every winning cover is either first-person, a flat claim, or an audience-identity label).
