## EMAIL MARKETING

**[The Double Yes Flip]** — shows why the freebie has one job (predict the purchase), not twelve — and why "helpful content" that doesn't do that job is quietly costing you sales — best archetype: Data Argument

**[Warm Plate Method Walkthrough]** — the welcome sequence most people skip: documentary structure (symptoms → diagnosis → prescription) applied to automation so it sounds like a human wrote it — best archetype: Step Framework

**[Action Training 101]** — the open/click habit your subscribers build before the sales email ever lands; why the sale works or doesn't before you send a single promo — best archetype: Manifesto

**[Email vs. Social Brain]** — subscribers in "checking mode" are in a fundamentally different psychological state than someone scrolling; this changes everything about what you say and when — best archetype: Data Argument

**[The Lazy Launch Checklist]** — the actual prerequisites (300–500 hot leads, high engagement signals) before a 5-email sequence is worth running; the list most people skip straight past — best archetype: Trend Report

**[Right-Time Offers Explained]** — the soft CTA woven into the Simmer for the person ready to buy right now — not the one waiting for the next promo; why skipping it is leaving money in the pot — best archetype: Story/POV

**[What the Simmer Actually Does]** — the weekly email is not a newsletter; it's a Flavor → Story → Value Drop → soft ask machine — and why that specific sequence is the one that compounds — best archetype: Step Framework

**[The Fake Urgency Autopsy]** — countdown timer on an evergreen offer: the one thing that kills trust faster than not emailing at all; what real urgency looks like instead — best archetype: Provocation / Data Argument

## PINTEREST MARKETING

**[Bread & Butter Content Explained]** — four cornerstone posts per content pillar, each with a content upgrade routing to the list; the asset you make once and leverage forever — best archetype: Step Framework

**[Slow Pinterest vs. Fast Pinterest]** — why keyword-first, set-it-and-forget-it outperforms daily posting; the 38% stat and what it means for how you spend a Tuesday — best archetype: Data Argument

**[The Batch in Plain English]** — 70 pins per URL, 6 months scheduled in one session; what that actually looks like and why it only has to happen once per content pillar — best archetype: Step Framework

**[Metadata Stacking Breakdown]** — the 7 places keywords live (profile name, bio, board titles, board descriptions, pin titles, pin descriptions, image overlay text); missing even three of these is why pins underperform — best archetype: Trend Report

**[Pinterest Is Not Social Media]** — what changes when you treat it like a search engine hybrid instead of Instagram; the psychology difference that makes daily posting irrelevant — best archetype: Manifesto

**[The Hot Take Pin Formula]** — "why [common belief] is actually making [problem] worse": how to write a pin that earns saves from cold audiences who have never heard of you — best archetype: Story/POV

**[Stacking Assets vs. Feeding the Machine]** — one Bread & Butter post outlasts 90 days of daily content; the compounding math on evergreen vs. trend-chasing — best archetype: Data Argument

**[Bridge Builder Structure]** — the "three things that changed everything for my clients" format: how your 3-step process becomes the content, not the caption to the content — best archetype: Step Framework

## BACK OFFICE SYSTEMS

**[The Email-First Offer Suite]** — why everything — the offer, the welcome sequence, the Simmers, the promotional periods — connects to email as the center of gravity; what breaks when it doesn't — best archetype: Manifesto

**[The Cozy Business Plan, Demystified]** — buyer persona + pricing formula (income goal divided by clients divided by program length) + funnel decisions in one snapshot; the plan that fits on one page — best archetype: Step Framework

**[Launch Amnesia and How to Fix It]** — starting from scratch every quarter because the system didn't exist to catch what worked last time; the Quarterly Rhythm System as the cure — best archetype: Story/POV

**[The Quarterly Rhythm System]** — seasons have buying psychology; one signature offer reframed four times is not laziness — it's the only repeatable marketing that compounds — best archetype: Data Argument

**[List Growth Math, Shown]** — the exact backward calculation: income goal → clients needed → leads needed → conversion rate tracked; why guessing this number is the most expensive thing a service provider does — best archetype: Data Argument

**[Feeding the Content Beast vs. Stacking Assets]** — what front-of-house energy only looks like at 18 months; what back-of-house infrastructure looks like at 18 months; side by side — best archetype: Story/POV

**[What "Email First" Actually Means for Your Offer Suite]** — the principle that your entire offer architecture revolves around email is not a preference — it's what determines whether the automation does anything useful — best archetype: Manifesto

**[The Automated Marketing Department Frame]** — what you're actually building when you build back-of-house systems: not content, not strategy — the department that runs sales and marketing while you're doing anything else — best archetype: Product Reveal

## SOFT PRODUCTIVITY (THE SOFT CEO)

**[Hustle Culture Is Overcooked]** — the marquee line as a carousel: what "cooked" actually looks like for a service provider who is great at delivery and bad at saying no to the machine — best archetype: Manifesto

**[The Soft CEO Profile]** — not a type, a situation: introverted, perfectionistic, creative, divergent thinker who is brilliant at the work and under-systemized in the back end; why the fix is structural — best archetype: Story/POV

**[Please Retire the Word "Value"]** — a walk through every piece of marketing advice that hides behind "value" and what she says instead; specific, named, actually useful — best archetype: Provocation / Data Argument

**[The 3-Day Work Week, Numbered]** — the specific systems (not vibes) that cut 10 hours per week; what was eliminated, what was automated, what was handed off; in order — best archetype: Step Framework

**[Strategic Slowness, Defined]** — doing less on purpose as a compounding long game, not a rest day; why stopping daily posting made the business grow; the math — best archetype: Data Argument

**[What the ADHD Brain Actually Needs from a System]** — not less stimulation — a container that catches everything so the 5848 tabs can stay open; why standard productivity advice fails creative divergent thinkers — best archetype: Story/POV

**[Let Social Media Be a Creative Outlet Again]** — the permission slip most service providers need to hear and the system that makes it possible to stop relying on it for lead generation — best archetype: Manifesto

**[Front of House vs. Back of House Energy]** — the original diagnosis: a business running entirely on DMs, comments, livestreams, and daily posting with nothing in the back running on its own; what the alternative infrastructure looks like — best archetype: Data Argument
