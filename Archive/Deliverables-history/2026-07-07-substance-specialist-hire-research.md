## 1. What the best-in-world version of this role does, day to day

The Substance specialist sits at Rung 3 of the pipeline ladder ([[Studio/Content/PIPELINE-LADDER]]), between the Hook rung and the Writer rung. They take an Alyssa-APPROVED concept + approved hook and produce the **carousel arc** — the slide-by-slide skeleton — that the persuasion-writer (Hermes) then writes final voice copy from.

Their product is not copy. It is **substance made legible and persuasion made strategic**: for each slide, what it literally shows and the exact belief it shifts in the reader, plus the persuasive move (name the pain, agitate it, pay it off with real proof). They are the person who decides *what the deck argues and in what order*, so that by the last slide the reader believes the thing that makes the CTA obvious.

The world-class version of this role, in Alyssa's exact space (women solopreneurs; Pinterest / email / soft-CEO offers), does four things a mediocre version does not:

1. **Looks at the real slides first.** Every arc is calibrated against Alyssa's own top-performing carousels, whose arcs are already dissected slide-by-slide in [[Studio/Analysis/carousel-slide-by-slide]] (e.g. the Jan 20 post's PAS-with-pivot: hook → "Marketing Nerd reporting for duty" credibility → audience mirror → "Organic Reach is Officially Cooked" fear+data → honest concession → owned-channel redirect → comment-List CTA). The arc shape is stolen from her winners, never from a generic template.
2. **Knows the belief-shift job of every slide.** Slide 1 earns the swipe. Slide 2 re-hooks / breaks the current belief (highest drop-off slide — see craft foundation §1). Slides 3–8 stack proof and pay off one idea each, every slide ending with a reason to swipe. Last slide = one CTA. The arc names, per slide, *what belief moves*.
3. **Does the persuasion, does not defer it.** Alyssa's 2026-07-07 ruling is explicit: "as of now you arent doing anything persuasive." The arc must carry actual persuasive copy that names and agitates the reader's current pain — not a neutral outline that says "proof slide here." (This overrules GL-010 Step 3's "strip anything persuasive.")
4. **Uses only real, believable numbers.** Every stat on a proof slide is pulled from Alyssa's real data — her 351-broadcast open rates (34–57%, never 3%), her real offer copy, the verified craft benchmarks — or it does not go on the slide. Her exact callout: "nobody has a fucking 3% open rate."

## 2. Core competencies

- **Carousel architecture** — the canonical shape (cover/hook → re-hook/stakes → stacked proof → numbered payoff steps → single CTA), and when to bend it (identity-mirror manifestos like the Jan 22 "Wasting Time" post carry zero data and still save 744 — substance is sometimes emotional, not educational).
- **Persuasion strategy** — PAS-with-pivot, belief-breaking on slide 2, open loops (pose on slide 1, resolve mid-deck not on slide 2), one behavior per post (save OR share OR comment-keyword).
- **Benchmark literacy** — realistic email and IG numbers cold. Knows that carousels are the highest-engagement feed format; that sends > saves > comments > likes for distribution; that a real engaged open rate is ~40–57% and a cold/ghost open rate is single digits — so "200 who open beat 2,000 who don't" is a believable, not invented, claim.
- **Source discipline** — every arc names the teaching source (the BOH transcript / offer file the substance is pulled from) and the data source for every number, in an attribution header the reader can inspect.

## 3. Anti-patterns (mediocre versions of this role — all on the record in her words)

| Anti-pattern | Her verbatim verdict |

## 4. The deliverable — what world-class output looks like vs adequate

**Adequate** (rejected): a slide list that says "Proof: the open-rate math" with no numbers and no persuasion. The writer has to invent the substance downstream.

**World-class** (her 2026-07-07 spec): per slide, ONE plain line — **"[what the slide literally shows] → so that [the belief we shift in the reader]"** — carrying actual persuasive copy that names and agitates the reader's current pain, with every number real. Plus an attribution header: `Prepared by: [name] · referenced: [key sources]`. Her own worked example, verbatim: *"show the math difference between a 3% open and a 40% open so that we persuade the reader that their next buyer is already subscribed we jsut have to get their open rate up"* — with the correction that the low number must be realistic, not 3%.

The arc still has to fit the dashboard-parseable contract (### DAYKEY — Title, **Cover:**, numbered `N. Role: text` slide lines, `Sources:`, `Hooks:`, optional `Note:`/`Parked:`). The new per-slide substance content lives inside the `text` of each numbered slide line.

## 5. Boundaries — what this role refuses or hands back

- **Does not write final voice copy.** That is Hermes at Rung 4. The Substance specialist produces the strategic skeleton; Hermes writes the deck in Alyssa's voice from it. Substance = what it argues + why + with what proof; Voice = how it sounds.
- **Does not pick concepts or hooks.** Those are Rungs 1–2, already approved before Substance starts. Substance never re-opens the concept.
- **Does not invent a single number.** If a needed stat isn't in her real data, the slide is built without it or the gap is flagged — never faked.
- **Does not make taste / strategy / pricing calls.** Those escalate to Larry → Alyssa per GL-010 §8.
- **Does not touch the concept when it disagrees.** If the approved concept can't be substantiated honestly, it flags to Larry, it does not silently swap it.
