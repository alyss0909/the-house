# B1-R2 — Seed 1, Email edu (W1 Mon)

**Cover eyebrow:** FREEBIES, ACTUALLY
**Cover line:** The Smoothie Marketing Problem.

**Slide 2 — open loop**
I've been building freebies (and watching other people's freebies flop) long enough to see the exact same mistake every time.. and it's not the topic.

**Slide 3 — agitate**
Most freebies just collect dust in your four later folder. Not because they're ugly. Because they were never built to do the one job a freebie actually has.

**Slide 4 — value 1**
YOUR KID DOESN'T KNOW SHE'S LOW ON MINERALS.
She just wants something sweet. So you blend the spinach into the smoothie she already asked for. That's the whole method. Solve the felt problem on the surface, solve the real one underneath it - and she never has to know spinach was involved.

**Slide 5 — value 2**
"Useful" doesn't convert. I used to think the most generous freebie was the most detailed one. It's not. You need a churros level of desire built in, not a nutrition pamphlet.

**Slide 6 — value 3, THE FRIDGE TEST**
Would she print it and put it on the fridge. Screenshot it. Save it in her notes app for later. Send it to a friend. If the honest answer is no to all four, it's not a freebie yet, it's a PDF.

**Slide 7 — value 4**
Think of the freebie as ladies night, not the whole relationship. Low stakes, one fun yes, margaritas before the real menu comes out. The tripwire's the app menu. We're not getting into that this week.

**Slide 8 — aha**
Here's the part nobody says out loud. If your free thing is THIS good, the only question left in her head is: what does she charge for.

**Slide 9 — CTA**

# B2-R2 — Seed 2, BOH offer (W3 Wed)

**Cover eyebrow:** FOR SERVICE PROS BUILDING AN OFFER SUITE
**Cover line:** I Built My Launch From Leftovers.

**Slide 2 — open loop**
Not a metaphor. I went looking for a new launch sequence and found out I'd already written half of it, months ago, for something else entirely.

**Slide 3 — agitate**
If people need to DM you, watch your stories, or hop on a call just to understand what you're selling.. it's not ready for an email-first approach yet. That's not a content problem. That's a clarity problem.

**Slide 4 — value 1**
Q1: what's the amazing outcome someone gets after working with you. Not the feature. The after. Say it in one sentence or it's not clear enough yet.

**Slide 5 — value 2**
Q2: what's your client scared they'll HAVE to do to get that outcome. This is the fear sitting right next to the yes - name it before she does.

**Slide 6 — value 3**
Q3 + Q4, the deep ones: what does she think disqualifies her, and what's actually keeping her up at 3am. Get uncomfortable here. Almost nobody knows this part about her.

**Slide 7 — value 4**
An offer suite should be easy to re-offer. Not a full launch every time, not repetitive either - just... available. That's the whole rule.

**Slide 8 — aha**
People think they already know the problem. What you're actually doing is showing them they've been fighting the wrong battle the entire time.
