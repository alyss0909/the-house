# C1-R2 — Seed 1, Email edu (W1 Mon)

**Eyebrow:** FOR PEOPLE WHO ARE SICK OF THEIR OWN FREEBIES
**Cover:** I Stopped Trying To Sell Spinach.

**Slide 2**
I've built freebies for four years and watched most of them go straight into the four later folder..

Here's the one thing I changed that actually moved the needle.

**Slide 3**
Most freebies fail for the same reason. They're either too vague, too overwhelming, or not specific enough to solve one real problem.

They collect dust. Not because people don't want help. Because the thing you made doesn't feel like THEIRS.

**Slide 4**
This comes back to the spinach in the smoothie.

Your kid doesn't know they're exhausted because they're low on minerals. They just know they want something sweet.

So you give them the smoothie. And somewhere in there, so is the spinach.

Your freebie is the smoothie. The transformation you're actually selling is the spinach.

**Slide 5**
Useful does not lead to conversions.

I know that sounds backwards, but stacking more tips into a PDF isn't the win. You need bio-psychology, not bullet points, you need to create the CRAVING first.

**Slide 6**
The Fridge Test.

Would they print it and put it on their fridge. Would they screenshot it. Would they send it to a friend, or bring it up with their therapist.

If the honest answer is "they'd close the tab," it's not done yet.

**Slide 7**
Think of your freebie as the margarita on ladies night, not the whole meal.

It's the low-stakes yes. The one that makes the SECOND yes (the app menu, the thing you actually sell) feel obvious instead of scary.

**Slide 8**
Here's the part nobody tells you.

If your freebie is genuinely good, someone should look at it and think "wait.. they gave this away?"

That reaction isn't a bonus. That's the whole freebie doing its job.

# C2-R2 — Seed 2, BOH offer (W3 Wed)

**Eyebrow:** FOR OFFERS THAT STILL NEED A DM TO MAKE SENSE
**Cover:** I Built My Launch From Leftovers.

**Slide 2**
I've diagnosed this exact pattern across probably 40 client offers now, and it's never the price.

There's one belief-gap sitting underneath almost every offer that isn't converting.

**Slide 3**
If someone needs to DM you, watch your stories, or hop on a call just to understand what you're selling..

it's not ready for an email-first approach yet. That's not a them problem. That's an offer problem.

**Slide 4**
So I built my last launch out of four questions, not four new assets.

What outcome do they actually get. What are they scared they'll have to do to get it. What do they think disqualifies them. What's keeping them up at 3am that almost nobody else knows about.

**Slide 5**
Question one and two are where the real gap lives.

They want the outcome. They're terrified of the cost they THINK it takes to get there (the calls, the overwhelm, the learning curve). Name that gap out loud and half your sales page writes itself.

**Slide 6**
Question four is the one people skip.

Not "inconsistent revenue." Get deeper than that. What's the actual 3am thought. Cancelling the kid's sports camp. Watching a competitor's launch and feeling sick. That's the thing your offer is really answering.

**Slide 7**
And your offer should be easy to re-offer. Not a full relaunch every time, not repetitive either.

If you have to reinvent the whole thing every quarter, the offer was never actually finished.

**Slide 8**
Here's the belief shift, the one I keep coming back to.

People think they know their problem. Your job isn't to introduce a new solution.

It's to show them they've been fighting the wrong battle the whole time.

Every offer I've ever fixed came down to the same four questions, not a rebrand, not new funnels. Just actually answering what's keeping your person up at 3am.
