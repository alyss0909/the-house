### Item X

**Eyebrow:** FOR PEOPLE WHOSE FREEBIE IS COLLECTING DUST

**Cover:** I Stopped Trying To Sell Spinach.

**Slide-by-slide:**
1. **Cover** — headline above. Footer: BACK OF HOUSE. Prop: warm-toned still life, silver tray, a green smoothie in a mason jar next to wilted spinach, condensation on the glass.
2. **Credibility / open loop** — "I've Made A Lot Of Freebies Nobody Downloaded." Body: I've built freebies for my own list and for clients for years, and here's the thing nobody tells you.. most of them don't work. Not because they're not useful — because useful isn't the job.
3. **Agitate** — "Your Freebie Is Collecting Dust." Body: most freebies are either too vague, too overwhelming, or just not specific enough — so they sit in someone's "for later" folder, unopened, doing nothing for either of you.
4. **Value 1** — "The Smoothie Isn't The Point. The Spinach Is." Body: a kid doesn't know they're exhausted because they're low on minerals, they just know they want something sweet. So you give them the smoothie and sneak the spinach in where they can't taste it. Solve the felt problem on the surface, diagnose the real one underneath.
5. **Value 2** — "Useful Does Not Lead To Conversions." Body: I know that sounds wrong. But a freebie packed with information isn't the same as a freebie someone actually wants. You need the craving first — the churros, not the nutrition label.
6. **Value 3** — "Would They Put It On Their Fridge?" Body: would they screenshot it, save it in their notes app, send it to a friend? That's the only test that matters — not "is this helpful," "is this something they'd keep."
7. **Value 4** — "Your Freebie Is Ladies Night. Not The Whole Menu." Body: it's the margarita, not the app menu — low stakes, easy yes, builds momentum for the next one. The freebie isn't supposed to close the sale, it's supposed to get them to say yes again.
8. **Aha** — "If This Is Free, What's The Paid Thing Like?" Body: it should be so good that if someone else was charging for it, they'd pay. You just made it free. That's not generosity for its own sake — it's proof of concept, served before they even ask. Prop: burgundy-door visual shift marking the CTA doorway.
9. **CTA** — "Comment List." Body: and I'll send you the exact re-engagement email strategies I'm using right now to revive cold subscribers — free, in Notion.

**Caption opener:**
```
k so real talk — i've made SO many freebies over the years that nobody downloaded 🥲

turns out useful isn't the job. the job is: solve the thing they think they want, sneak in the thing they actually need underneath it
```
