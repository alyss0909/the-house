"You Wrote Five Emails And Zero Of Them Have A Plot." / Body: "most welcome sequences are just.. an about page, a testimonial, a 'here's what I offer,' stitched together bc someone told you a list needs nurturing. nobody asked what the reader is supposed to BELIEVE by email five that she didn't believe by email one. so she reads three, forgets you exist, and there's your open rate."
4. "A Welcome Sequence Is A Belief Arc, Not A Highlight Reel." / Body: "if your signature offer had a documentary, what would the audience think about it in the opening scene, and what would they think by the closing shot. that gap - the distance between those two beliefs - is the actual job of your welcome sequence. not 'get to know me.' not 'here's my offer.' one belief, moved on purpose."
5. "Name The Start-State Before You Write A Single Email." / Body: "what does she believe walking in. probably something like 'I just need to post more' or 'I haven't found the right template yet.' write THAT down first. every email after it either chips at it or replaces it - nothing in the sequence is allowed to just sit there being nice."
6. "Name The End-State Second." / Body: "what do you want her to believe by the last email. not 'buy this.' a BELIEF. something like 'my back end can hold this without me babysitting it.' the offer becomes the obvious next scene once she believes that - you're not selling, you're rolling credits."
7. "Every Email In Between Is A Scene, Not A Slide." / Body: "a scene moves the plot. so before you hit send on email 3 ask: does this move her one inch closer to the end-state belief, or is it just 'content' I felt obligated to include bc a template told me to. if it's the second one, cut it."
8. "The Documentary Was Never About You." / Body: "I kept trying to make my welcome sequence sound impressive. turns out the whole time the audience wasn't watching me - they were watching whether THEIR OWN problem got solved onscreen. the second I wrote the arc from her belief instead of my highlight reel, the sequence finally had a plot."
