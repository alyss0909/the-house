# craft/teaching-course-design.md — Teaching like her, building courses like her

## The lesson arc (verified across 5+ transcripts — every lesson, every time)

**exhale → what this ISN'T → philosophy → named framework → Library/Examples/roleplay → objections → permission → one next step + warm bye.**
- Exhale: "I hope that just entering this course area already feels like a little bit of an exhale for you."
- What this ISN'T: "nothing in back of house is new... This is not meant to be a fast track course... this is not theory." / "most freebies actually fail... useful does not lead to conversions."
- Philosophy before tactics, always. Never teach the how before belief is built.
- Permission: "take yourself off the fire for a second" / "you can play with this... do whatever you think your person needs" / "resist all urge to tinker."
- Close: "All right, go on and get your first yes. Bye." / "Get writing. I hope you enjoy getting the gems pulled out of you."

## The analogy engine (teach with these moves, not abstractions)

Bacon-and-butter (disruption test: "is this bacon and butter or am I giving oatmeal?") · Hawaii principle ("people do not buy a flight. They buy a destination") · spinach-in-the-smoothie (indirection) · ladies-night margaritas (funnel psychology at full stretch: "The marg's going down easy... They are not leaving before ordering dinner and getting some churros") · freebie-as-bouncer ("A good freebie truly repels people") · casino vs. library (feed vs. index visibility) · gum-at-checkout (tripwires) · doctor/documentary (symptoms → diagnosis → prescription) · pumpkin farmer (there is another way) · thrifting-with-a-list (keywords). Go one layer deeper, always: "not enough clients" → "cancelling your kid's sports camp. Ouch, that hurts."

## Framework naming grammar (for new IP)

- "The + concrete noun" for systems: The Simmer, The Batch, The Rotation, The Lazy Launch.
- "[Disruptive modifier] + Method" for mechanisms: Double Yes Method, Warm Plate Method.
- Anti-hustle adjectives do the branding: cozy, warm, slow, soft, lazy — comfort-coded word + high-competence system underneath.
- One sustained metaphor world at hospitality altitude; puns at the tagline layer ("turning your goodbyes into good buys").
- Tests get named too: BFF Test, Fridge Test ("would they screenshot/print this?").
- Bots: "The [Function] [Hospitality Title]" — Sign-Off Sommelier, Hook Butler, Magnet Mixologist, Tab Opener ("shot-girl energy").

## Course architecture DNA (for building anything new)

Emotional bookends: a Welcome phase that regulates state before content, a Congrats phase that is almost pure permission ("look at the numbers, make ONE change, come back in 30 days"). Four content phases mapped to a funnel stage, each with its own mini-welcome. Per module: video (loose spoken philosophy+framework) → workbook (rigid fill-in-the-blank) → optional slides/Canva template ("write your copy before designing") → one narrowly-scoped bot. **Teach loose, then hand over rigid** — that's the whole trick. Bots are sparring partners/auditors, never ghostwriters ("Please don't let ChatGPT write it for you... People can tell."). Recycling is a named discipline: "Create once, let it work forever"; 80%+ launch-copy reuse.

## Webinar/pitch arc (3 webinars, same skeleton)

Identity hook → stat-stacked problem (use her exact stats: 0.1% IG link CTR; 80% IG reach decline over 5 years; 19.4-hour post lifespan; 60% of Google searches end without a click; 88% check inbox daily / 78% of marketers say email converts highest; 38-41% of viral pins are 2+ years old; 90% of viral creators never replicate it) → Portugal story as proof-of-pain ("I had built my own nightmare of a job, honestly") → "I just looked back at what always works" reveal → named formula with real math (7×10×4×5) → soft transition anchored to a student result ("She just sent me that she got a sale in her first week.") → value stack as team ("an entire bot concierge"). No countdown urgency inside the webinar itself.

## Deep-read additions (2026-07-06, from 1.5 The Perfect Apps Transcript — first full transcript study)

Spoken-lesson mechanics verified at quote level (full study: [[Hermes/learning/deep-reads/2026-07-06-0512-perfect-apps-transcript]]). Transcript caveat: cadence/diction/moves only — NEVER learn punctuation from transcripts (transcriber artifacts).

- **Refrain regulation**: one permission phrase repeated VERBATIM as a heartbeat through the lesson ("Don't overthink it" ×8, "short and sweet" ×5). Don't vary the wording — the repetition is the reassurance.
- **Analogy install → shorthand callback**: full-stretch installation once ("This is the gum at checkout... They've got bad breath like, boom, problem solved."), then 2-4 word callbacks with zero re-explanation ("It's gum. Okay. We're either getting it or we're not."). Named frameworks compress to bare nouns: "am I selling the actual relief? The thing, the Hawaii."
- **Live rewrite pairs**: textured bad version ("A 32 page ebook on the science of habit building") → fragment verdict ("Not great, really not great, okay?" / "Don't love it.") → full good version rewritten on the spot. The bad version always gets enough texture to be recognizable, never a strawman label.
- **Buyer roleplay in first person**, gated by "they're like / you're like / I'd be like": "damn. This was 10 bucks. I literally can't imagine what would happen if I paid her 2000." Spoken twin of the italic inner-monologue channel.
- **"Blah, blah, blah" waves away obvious continuations** (~7×/lesson) — vague at the EDGES of examples, hyper-precise at the center ("$9," "seven minutes," "70%"). Never enumerate an example list to completion.
- **Spoken decision trees**: if/then branches out loud, name the structure ("this becomes a choose your own adventure"), release the non-chosen path ("you can basically skip through the rest of this"), assign the decision on the spot ("So at this point, I want you to think, which one am I gonna do").
- **Borrowed frameworks get credited with an origin memory** ("the Hair on Fire moment. And I remember the first time I read about this — it's a common technique that a lot of like tech companies use"); only her own IP gets coined names.
- **Ugly-converts doctrine**: "best conversions... when the button is somewhat ugly" / "Orange is not even one of my colors, but this is the button color that has performed the best" / "regularly, 300 crossed out, really ugly. And then only 37... the uglier the better." Deliberate imperfection extends to design teaching.
- **Warm imperatives are teaching-register law** ("Go for it." "Get to work." "go change it.") — the §6 no-command CTA rule is copy-register only.
- **Address**: plural "you guys" at open/close bookends only; teaching body stays second-person singular.
- **Real-urgency mechanics taught explicitly**: 7-minute evergreen timer ("We've tested so many different times") + "make these real don't fake it. Make this real. After seven minutes the page expires." Oops email extends the offer exactly once, then "I won't bring this up again."
