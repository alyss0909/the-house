# craft/subject-lines.md

The voice: "raccoon in a cashmere sweater" — short + casual + slightly emotionally loaded + a tiny bit wrong. Rule zero: **readers open for Alyssa-the-person, click for Alyssa-the-strategist.** One job per line.

## Patterns that win (real numbers)

1. **Confession/vulnerability** (opens): "I failed |🍦Soft Sunday" (44.98%, 341 clicks) · "Why I burned down a perfectly good business (on purpose)" (56.08% — the ONLY line to win opens AND clicks; study it: confession + contradiction layering) · "oopsie, I've been gatekeeping?" (48.85%) · "k embarrassing story from my 20s" (45.71%).
2. **Faux-logistics/fake-reply** (opens; warm lists, event reminders only): "re: tmrw's meeting" (57.35% all-time) · "re: tmrw's planning appointment [🥖 Soft Ceo Session]" (45.6% at 8,380) · "you coming?" (76.7% to segment) · "tmrw: your whimsical february" (51.31%).
3. **Deliverable-inside** (clicks): "[PLAN INSIDE] clock out by summer" · "[replay inside] Slowly Viral + $1k off BOH" (60.17%, 18.5% click) · "ur (not)NEW march plan inside" (49.55%, 496 clicks). Weighted nouns that carry clicks: plan, checklist, fix/fixed, replay, class link, template, swipe, coupon, invite, steps. "Inside" alone = nothing.
4. **Anti-advice reframe** (clicks): `🙅‍♀️ DON'T "grow your list" do this instead…` (#1 all-time clicks: 1,209).
5. **Personified objects**: "Your list called, they're hungry 👀" (44.15%, 349 clicks).
6. **Soft Sunday wrapper**: "[2-5 word weird/emotional phrase] |🍦Soft Sunday" — the wrapper carries brand equity; the front phrase still pulls the weight.
7. **Segment intimacy** (small lists): "you up? free custom bot delivery 🍔" (60%) · "You good, {NAME}?" (57.76%) · "💌 Here, take what worked" (57.81%, 32.8% click).

## Mechanics

Lowercase and txt-speak are deliberate ("ur," "tmrw," "k," "u want?") and cost nothing. Odd/specific numbers beat round ("$12,212 coupon"). Food metaphor at seasoning level, not every send ("how to make mermaid money," "NO empty calories (just free treats) 😏," "🛎️ Your late night combo's in the window"). {NAME} personalization in logistics lines. **Preview text = second subject line doing a DIFFERENT job** — add context/urgency/personalization, never summarize, never explain the joke.

## Never

**Exceed 40 characters before the wrapper** (2026-07-05: "all subj lines are too long nobody in email marketing or copywriting professionals would approve that"). Winners run 8-30 chars; only a Why-I narrative line may run long, never sales/launch. **Norah's name in a subject line, ever** (2026-07-05: family texture = body copy only). Summarize the content ("5 tips for better email" = table of contents). Over-explain (format+outcome+container in one line). Long subjects on sales emails. Urgency without a story. Fake-reply on cold lists (trust-burn). Off-register randomness (worst performer: "Stoner Math" 22.4% — a register outlier, keep as contrast case).
