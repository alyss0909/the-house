# craft/offer-development.md — Making new offers she would actually sell

An offer idea passes only if it survives her own judgment pattern: **Verdict → What IS useful → What to skip → Action.**

## The fit tests (all must pass)

1. **Channel fit**: serves the email → Pinterest → IG-carousel machine. Not LinkedIn, not video-first, not daily-posting-dependent (her Blotato verdict: "wrong platform for Alyssa's workflow").
2. **Thesis fit**: helps the buyer give a shit about their specific thing by removing marketing load. If it adds daily management, it's out ("automation promises that still require daily management" = taste disgust).
3. **Ecosystem fit**: rehearses a BOH phase skill in miniature (email products ↔ Phase 2, Pinterest ↔ Phase 3, promo ↔ Phase 4) or extends the Soft CEO identity lane. Satellites feed the flagship.
4. **No duplication**: "Skip. We've already built this, better." Check BUSINESS.md map first — the low-ticket shelf is already crowded ($9-$97: 10 products).
5. **Audience fit**: established-but-under-systemized creatives whose #1 barrier is fear of not following through — offers need built-in completion mechanics (dashboards, DFY templates, bots, VIP-day formats), not more information.
6. **Format fit** (her repeats): Notion dashboard · prompt/swipe pack with a specific count (55 prompts, 44 ideas, 80 lines) · VIP/intensive day · recurring free event → tripwire · automation-as-product.

## Where the real structural room is (evidence, not invention)

- **The $333 → $2,000 canyon.** The 90 Minute Reset sits alone mid-ladder; nothing bridges to flagship. (Rush Order at $2,999 targets the done-with-you gap ABOVE BOH — it doesn't solve the mid-ladder.)
- **The DWY demand is documented**: survey barrier list includes "Prefer done-for-you or hands-on help."
- **Crowding**: two $37s and two $19s — consolidation candidates before new low-ticket.
- Three products marked needs_edits before anything new ships in their lanes (A Year of Emails, Biz Tracker, Daily Profit Activities).

## Naming + pricing register

Name per the grammar in craft/teaching-course-design.md (restaurant world for BOH-lane, "Soft" for identity-lane, time-compression, revival/heat for email-lane). One-liner = outcome + anti-effort clause. Price: odd/specific beats round in promos ("$12,212 coupon"); "Regularly $X, today $Y" for tripwires; scarcity small and real ("Only 2 spots").

## Hard stops

Never propose: **anything involving weekend work — hers OR the buyer's** ("i would NEVER work on a weekend this offer is against everything i stand for as a soft ceo," 2026-07-05, killing "The Reheat Weekend"; no weekend intensives, weekend challenges, always-on formats, or delivery that eats a buyer's weekend) · anything requiring daily posting or trend-chasing · virality mechanics · "passive income" framing without the setup-work truth · partnerships with bro-launch/manosphere energy ("I'm not selling them to the manosphere") · a product whose CTA/chat word you'd have to invent · pitching anything Geriatric/Sunset/idea-stage as live.
