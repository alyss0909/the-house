# craft/emails-soft-sunday.md

Soft Sunday = the weekly identity/permission newsletter. "The weekly productivity report for daydreamers, creative thinkers, ADHDers, and anybody who loves a good check-in." Fixed ritual skeleton — the sections are non-negotiable, the content inside them breathes.

## The skeleton (every email)

1. **Ritual tagline** then **🫶 The Pep Talk** — opens as scene/confession/blurt, NEVER a topic statement. "I cried into my fridge this week..." / "I failed statistics in university [NAME] … twice." Longest storytelling of any format lives here — 4-8 sentence paragraphs allowed.
2. **✍️ The House Report** — the teaching. Bold = skim path (3-6 bolds/200 words; if only bolds are read the argument must hold). Products appear only AFTER the teaching earns them, never cold.
3. **🤓 Nerding Out On** — genuine current obsession with real texture ("trained to audit offer pages... red pen in hand").
4. Optional: Time Luxuries / Just Upgraded / On The Calendar.
5. **🍦 The Check-In** (non-negotiable close): mood question → anchor word bookended by the same emoji twice → ☁️ "the one thing" fill-in → "Wishing you a cozy, soft Sunday 🤎."

Section headers use the bracket format: emoji — _italic description_ — same emoji.

## Rules with teeth

- **The pivot must be discovered, not announced.** Spend 2+ paragraphs in the personal scene, then name it: "I couldn't help but see the parallels." The flagged too-AI email failed exactly here (concept-label transition after a thin mirror).
- **One non-inventable personal detail per email minimum**: luteal-phase mention, grandma's china, two hundred jars of tomato sauce. Never a lifestyle-brand vignette.
- **Anchor words are atmospheric, never productivity-speak**: "magic," "grounded," "Restore" — never "productive," "organized."
- **No jokes during the vulnerable beat.** "I had truly lost the plot." stands alone; humor returns the next line.
- **Strategy lists are the danger zone.** If teaching multiple items, every item carries texture or a self-aware aside — a clean label+description list is the #1 too-AI tell (see examples/bad/).
- **Food metaphor**: entry points and Check-In only; exit it out loud mid-teaching ("lol ok im done with the food thing").
- **CTAs**: lowercase, conversational, once or twice, late. Reply-mechanics ("Reply STATS," "Reply AUTOMATION") with real limited availability behind them.
- **Subject**: "[weird/emotional 2-5 word phrase] |🍦Soft Sunday". Confession beats topic: "I failed," "I feel sick about this," "Why I burned down a perfectly good business (on purpose)" — the only subject in her history to win both opens AND clicks.

## Register

Big-sis warm: "Ok, I know growing your email list is on your mind (proud of you for that)." Emotional reframes of technical topics: "💫 You deserve a community 💫 ... behind those subs are people, not just numbers." Boldest brand moves live here: "🔥 So we burned it all down. (yep, even the profitable parts)."

## Deep-read additions (2026-07-05, from "Why I burned down a perfectly good business")

- **Slash-cascade opener** — an alternate Pep Talk entry alongside scene/confession/blurt: stack synonymous phrases with slashes to name a shared cultural moment before narrowing to a direct question. "New year / new me / new goals / new vision / new life loading / vibes that are swirling in the air right now — Am i right?"
- **Rhetorical-question ladder** — 3-4 stacked questions building recognition before she names her own answer, used when teaching a concept rather than opening on a single scene.
- **Per-section pivot, not per-email** — in multi-part teaching emails (e.g. numbered reasons), each sub-section gets its own discovered pivot phrase ("So we burned it all down" / "So, project simplify was born" / "That's when I decided to make social media optional"), not one pivot for the whole piece.
- **Open question — bold density in long-form origin-story emails**: this document runs 15-20+ bolds/200 words in the House Report, well above the documented 3-6 ceiling.
