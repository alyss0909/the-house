## The job of the post

Make the reader feel:

> "I already know this in my soul, but I forgot I was allowed to live like this."

The post should feel like a tiny permission slip for creative business owners who are tired of turning every thought, hobby, idea, and season of life into output.

It is usually selling, softly, the same deeper thing:

**more space, better ideas, less frantic marketing, a business that keeps working while they go be a person.**

---

## Core style

The writing should feel like:

* a note to self
* a saved screenshot
* a line from a private notebook
* a tiny verdict
* a thought overheard mid-walk
* Sofia Coppola business girlie meets low-maintenance CEO

It should be **short, specific, emotionally familiar, and slightly funny without trying to be funny.**

---

## The magic formula

Most strong lines do one of these things:

### 1. Reclassify something "unproductive" as part of the work

Examples:

> closing the app
> is part of the work

> wasting time
> is part of my process

> collecting little things
> counts as research

Formula:

**[soft/unproductive thing] + counts as / is part of / helps**

---

### 2. Give permission without sounding like advice

Examples:

> it's ok to stop
> before it's perfect

> i don't need a reason
> to take my time

> i'm giving myself permission
> to change my mind

Formula:

**i don't need...**
**it's ok to...**
**i'm allowed to...**

But keep it grounded. No therapy poster voice.

---

### 3. Name the identity they want to return to

Examples:

> the summer i remember
> i like making things

> for the version of me
> who got excited before she got strategic

> interesting people
> notice things

Formula:

**for the version of me who...**
**the summer i...**
**people who...**

This works because people repost identities faster than tips.

---

### 4. Make the business point without saying the business point

The surface line might be about closing the laptop, shells, summer, notes apps, or wandering off.

The hidden business message is usually:

> My ideas deserve a better system than daily posting.

Examples:

> my ideas
> can be easier to find
> than i am

> some ideas
> need a system
> not a spotlight

> the summer i give
> my best thoughts
> a longer shelf life

## Cadence rules

Use short lines.
Let the line breaks do the work.

Best rhythm:

> first line = familiar image
> second line = quiet reframe
> third line, optional = tiny twist

Examples:

> leaving it alone
> helps

> thoughtful decisions
> age better

> this doesn't need
> to become productive

No big explanation. No lesson. No polished quote-machine energy.

---

## Tone rules

The tone should be:

* calm
* sly
* intimate
* specific
* lightly absurd
* emotionally restrained
* business-adjacent, not business-bro

It should never feel like:

* motivational content
* a productivity hack
* an affirmation deck
* Pinterest beige therapy
* "5 ways to optimize your creativity"
* a sales page cut into tiny pieces

---

## What makes one good

A good "girls post" line usually has at least 2 of these:

* Someone would repost it to say "me."
* It sounds obvious after you read it.
* It gives relief, not hype.
* It protects the reader's creative life.
* It makes slowness feel intelligent.
* It makes offline life feel strategic.
* It has one concrete detail: app, laptop, notes app, shell, domain, inbox, rabbit hole, archive, summer, slushie, phone, Pinterest.

Concrete detail = cute little hook.
Abstract truth = reason it lands.

## Best structures to reuse

### "X counts as Y"

> collecting little things
> counts as research

> resting
> counts as planning

> saving it
> counts

### "X is part of Y"

> closing the app
> is part of the work

> wasting time
> is part of my process

### "I don't need..."

> i don't need
> a full life rebrand
> to start the year right

> i don't need
> to use every idea immediately

### "It's ok to..."

> it's ok to stop
> before it's perfect

> it's ok
> to leave this unfinished

### "The summer I..."

> the summer i get my ideas
> from real life again

> the summer i wander off
> and call it research

### "For the version of me..."

> for the version of me
> who bought the domain name on a whim

> for the version of me
> who got excited before she got strategic

## The deeper thesis to keep returning to

Every post should orbit one of these beliefs:

* Good ideas need space.
* Slow is often smarter.
* Not every idea needs to become content immediately.
* Business should protect your creativity, not eat it.
* Your archive is not a mess. It's raw material.
* Being offline can be part of the system.
* The best marketing often starts as noticing.
* One good thought can do more than daily posting.

Tiny mantra:

> protect the idea
> give it somewhere to go
> then go live your life

That's the skill.
