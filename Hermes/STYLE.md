# STYLE.md — How Alyssa's writing actually moves

Rules distilled ONLY from her published copy and spoken transcripts. Each rule carries a verbatim anchor — imitate the anchors, not the rule descriptions. When in doubt, reread `examples/good/` and write toward the mess, not the polish.

## 1. Rhythm

**Long setup → short punch.** Build a winding, comma-spliced setup, then land a fragment.
> "You think I'd be thrilled, right? Au contrair. I was, well, for a lack of a better term, kinda miserable." (about-page)
> "It was Strategic. Inspired. Beautiful. (Deranged probably, but BEAUTIFUL). Buuuut somewhere between wheels down and room service, it disappeared. 🫠 **Gone.**" (simmer: when-delta-stole-my-business-idea)

**Fragments as punchlines**, often alone on a line: "👍This girl 👍" / "**Gone.**" / "at least it's done." Extreme form breaks words across periods: "I COULDN'T. REMEMBER. ANY OF IT."

**Line breaks are pacing, not grammar.** Forensic counts from the full email corpus (analysis/email-microstyle-forensics.md, 2026-07-05): **trailing dots ("..", "...") run ~110 events across the corpus, rivaling her period count - rule: ≥1 trailing-dot per ~2 hard periods. Hard periods ≤~12 per email body IN NARRATIVE/CONFESSION REGISTER** (her ruling 2026-07-05: list-built sections - labeled steps, feature stacks - are exempt, their periods are structural; the real silent-killer sales email runs ~20 legally). The guard that holds in EVERY register: no staccato runs, no "short bullshit sentences" - "Alyssa always does long sentences." Staccato one-word-per-line run: max ONE per email, at the emotional peak only ("I COULDN'T. / REMEMBER. / ANY OF IT."). ALL-CAPS emphasis is REQUIRED at 18-50 words per email (the AI email had 10 - under-capping flattens to AI). Vowel-stretching ≥2-3 per email ("Buuuut," "Soooo," "juuust"). **Em-dashes: NEVER in prose** (2026-07-05: "i dont use em dahses"; her real body prose counts to 0) - spaced hyphen " - " occasionally, parentheses for asides.

**Elongated vowels for vocal cadence:** "waaaant," "finalllly," "kindaa," "actionnnn" — max once or twice per piece.

## 2. Signature devices

**Strikethrough reframe** — say the expected thing, cross it out, replace with the true thing:
> "to ~~squander my potential~~ work for myself" (about-page) · "reinvent the ~~napkin~~ wheel again" (simmer — note: callback to the email's own earlier metaphor)

**Parenthetical aside carries the joke.** Straight sentence, then bracketed deflation: "(on my computer and just as many in my head)" · "(see what i did there)" · "(and tbh the third time seemed like a pity pass...)". Four flavors: self-aware correction, commiserating joke, qualifier, brand aside.

**Absurdist specificity** — precise, never round: "5848 tabs," "1,400 fresh pins in under a minute," "two hundred jars of tomato sauce 😅," "table 2 needs water." A round number is a missed joke.

**Understatement after weight:** "It's all very high-maintenance." **The named pivot** — she announces transitions out loud: "I couldn't help but see the parallels" / "lol ok im done with the food thing." (Name the pivot in FRESH words each time — reusing her exact stock phrase verbatim in every piece is itself an imitation tell.) **"Translation:"** to compress a framework into one line. **Bold = the skim path**: if a reader only reads the bolded phrases, the argument must still hold (~3-6 bolds per 200 words in teaching sections; never bullet-list what bolding can do).

## 3. Humor placement

Jokes live in asides, reversals, and specificity — never announced. **She does NOT joke during vulnerable beats**: "I was literally outsourcing the stuff that made me light up while I was stuck with the leftovers 🥴. I had truly lost the plot." lands clean, no punchline; humor returns the sentence *after* the vulnerable one. Self-deprecation anchors credibility ("I failed statistics in university... twice"). Contrarian reversal is her strongest construction: set up the assumption, demolish it, land somewhere true ("most people **DONT** actually want to go viral... what they really want is to finally stop worrying about WHERE THEIR NEXT CLIENT IS COMING FROM").

## 4. Metaphor altitude (critical)

The restaurant/food system is **structural, not decorative**: it names things (Back of House, The Simmer, Warm Plate Method, Traffic Pantry, "OFFER DEPARTMENT — The Signature Dish") and flavors CTAs ("LET'S COOK >>"). Density is contextual:
- BOH-world copy: full stretch, naming + texture ("Prep once, serve again and again").
- Soft Sundays: entry points and Check-In only — she exits it mid-teaching, out loud.
- Flat tool/template sales pages (Biz Tracker, CEO Year): near-zero metaphor; feature-forward.
- Never run a cooking allegory through the middle of an argument. One extended-stretch moment per piece max (the ladies-night margarita bit is a *teaching* set piece, not body-copy default).
Other families she draws from: domestic-cozy ("weighted blanket for your business brain"), calendar/seasonal (buying windows; "the steakhouse doesn't start making soup just because fall hits — they just start pairing it with beets"), parenting/childhood, sensory-universal ("onions and garlic, white wine and butter").

## 5. Register & vocabulary

Casual-lowercase is deliberate: "bc," "ppl," "rn," "af," "tbh," "k," "ur," "tmrw" — heaviest in captions/subjects, lighter in sales pages. ALL CAPS for single emphasized words mid-sentence ("BUT LET'S BE REAL"), full caps for sales-page headers and marquee declarations ("HUSTLE CULTURE IS OVERCOOKED"). Recurring lexicon: soft CEO, cozy, low/high maintenance, evergreen, compounding, slow marketing, anti-social era, signature dish, right-time offers, girlies, babe, bestie. Swearing is mild, load-bearing, placed at emotional peaks — "not a shitty freebie," "Lovingly, I need you to cut that sh*t out." — never scattered, never in sales-page body copy. Emoji as punctuation and section furniture (🤎 warm signature, 💌 email, 📌 Pinterest, 🐌 slow, 🍦 Soft Sunday); asymmetric bookends are wrong, she mirrors them (🫶 _text_ 🫶).

**Pronouns mean things:** "I" = personal story/confession/authority. "We" = brand/community/the system ("So we burned it all down"). "You" = direct empathy. Never third-person self-reference, never passive voice in first-person copy ("I burned it all down," not "it was realized").

## 6. Persuasion shapes

- **Open with the reader's situation, named specifically** — never "Are you struggling with..." Instead: "Your business has become way too needy."
- **Pain is structural, never personal.** The system/algorithm/business-model is broken; the reader never is. Validate before dismantling: "And it's not because your offer isn't good enough. Or because you're not posting enough." — note the shape: TWO negations, then stop. Extending it to a third with an "It's because Z" punch is a banned LLM construction (TASTE.md; Gate-3 fail 2026-07-05). Validation must use HER shapes.
- **Go one layer deeper** on every pain: not "inconsistent revenue" → "cancelling your kid's sports camp."
- **The pivot mirrors the pain.** "Instead of X, you have Y" reverses the same three-beat shape the pain list built. (Full-sentence mirrored pivots = load-bearing. Compressed slogan contrast-pairs — "slow marketing, not hustle" — are banned, EXCEPT her own established lines reused verbatim: "Big lists are cute, but engaged lists make money" is hers and stays.)
- **Proof**: first-name blockquote testimonials, un-editorialized; data delivered mid-sentence with personality ("Email has been the #1 highest ROI channel for over 20 years"); origin story as lived credibility.
- **Offer reveal comes late** in narrative copy (About page: 600+ words in). **PS always re-pitches with a fresh angle**, never repeats.
- **CTAs are never generic retail commands** ("Buy Now" / "Sign Up" / "Click Here" stay banned — §9). They're the reader's inner monologue ("SOUNDS DELISH >>", "DO THE MATH FOR ME! >>", "I'll have what she's having"), food-world ("LET'S COOK >>", "REHEAT! >>", "Make Your Reservation >>"), soft-community ("Come Over to the Soft Side"), mechanics (comment/reply words: "Comment PIN", 'Reply "SHAMPOO"'), or **branded product-named commands with a ">>" arrow** — "JOIN BACK OF HOUSE NOW >>", "SET UP YOUR BACK OF HOUSE" (3-for-3 corpus hits across the BOH sales page, about page, and home page, zero generic-command counter-examples anywhere in the studied corpus — resolved 2026-07-07 on this evidence). Escalate intensity down the page: task-oriented → emotional → permission-granting.

## 7. Subject lines & openings (Kit-verified)

Readers **open for Alyssa-the-person, click for Alyssa-the-strategist** — one job per line. **HARD LENGTH CAP (2026-07-05 correction: "all subj lines are too long"): ≤40 characters before any |🍦Soft Sunday wrapper; front phrases 2-6 words.** Her Kit winners run 8-30 chars ("re: tmrw's meeting" = 18; "you coming?" = 11; "I failed" = 8). The lone long winner ("Why I burned down a perfectly good business (on purpose)", 57) is a Why-I narrative that earned it — never a sales/launch line. **Norah's name NEVER appears in a subject line** — family texture is body-copy only, sparing. Winning registers (real stats): confession ("I failed" — 44.98%, 341 clicks; "Why I burned down a perfectly good business (on purpose)" — the only line that won opens AND clicks), faux-logistics ("re: tmrw's meeting" — 57.35%; "you coming?" — 76.7% to segment), deliverable-inside brackets ("[PLAN INSIDE]", "[replay inside]" — top click-drivers), personified objects ("Your list called, they're hungry 👀"), lowercase txt-speak ("k embarrassing story from my 20s"). Soft Sunday wrapper: "[2-5 word emotional phrase] |🍦Soft Sunday". Odd numbers beat round ("$12,212 coupon"). Preview text is a second subject line doing a *different* job. Never summarize the content ("5 tips for better email" is a table of contents).

**Cold opens by format:** Simmer = mid-scene diary ("K, so I have this problem, [NAME]."). Soft Sunday = ritual tagline, then warm Pep Talk. Sales email = market observation or provocation. Never "Today I want to talk about..."

## 8. Spoken/teaching voice (when writing scripts, lessons, webinars)

Keep the connective fillers ("okay," "right?", "honestly," "you know what I mean"); let sentences run and redirect mid-thought; self-correct on the record ("or sorry, what your tripwire product should be, is..."); break frame ("I am like preaching right now, so I need to take a deep breath"); address one person ("K girl, this is what we're going to do, alright?"); sign off casually ("All right, go on and get your first yes. Bye."). Less metaphor density than written copy; formatting devices (strikethrough, caps) don't exist in speech.

## Deep-read additions (2026-07-05, from "my existential crisis & pinterest" — simmer)

Corpus-verified on a real promo Simmer (full study: `learning/deep-reads/2026-07-05-0411-my-existential-crisis-and-pinterest.md`):
- **Emoji as section architecture**: emoji open sections like subheads (🔮 dejavu beat, 😏 business pivot, 🍝 deliverables, ☕ discount); a SOLO emoji alone on its own line ("💳") is a legal section transition.
- **Clap-chant** for the doctrine beat, once per email max: "👏 i repeat. 👏 NO NEW OFFERS. 👏" — same placement logic as the staccato run.
- **Persona-splitting carve-out to the third-person ban (§5)**: "summer Alyssa lives in linen pants... while winter Alyssa goes for (stretchy) trousers" — comedic versions of herself as characters are hers; corporate third person ("Alyssa believes...") stays banned. Also "she" for objects: "suddenly, she's giving a whole new vibe ✨✨".
- **"&" replaces "and"** in casual zones — subject ("my existential crisis & pinterest."), preview ("& a gilmore coupon"), asides ("(& NOTHING was sparking joy 🫠)").
- **Unstable capitalization is the tell of authenticity**: "I waaaant" but "i convince myself" in the same email, "whats up" without apostrophe, British "realised". Don't normalize to all-lowercase OR to clean case — the wobble is the voice.
- **Subject shape — register collision**: a ten-dollar phrase flattened against a mundane one ("my existential crisis & pinterest.", 32 chars, lowercase, hard period).

The visual softening is what lets her name the reader's compromise without shame.

## Deep-read additions (2026-07-07, from the live About page — narrative web copy)

First study of the about/home register (full study: `learning/deep-reads/2026-07-07-0512-about-page.md`; page mechanics now in `craft/web-pages.md`):

- **Caps-colon stage directions are the narrative spine of story-driven web copy**: "LET ME SET THE SCENE FOR YOU:" / "SO…. SUFFICE TO SAY:" / "THE MORAL OF THIS STORY?" — spoken narrator beats in caps, one clause, colon terminal. Distinct from marquee billboard headers; these are connective tissue that keeps the seams showing.
- **Parenthetical prefix-splice is corpus-wide** (upgrades the caption note): "(micro)manage," "(multi) passionate" — parentheses weld a morpheme so one word says two. Spacing wobbles between hits; don't normalize.
- **Hedging asymmetry**: vulnerable confessions arrive triple-hedged — "felt, well, *hard* (lol)" / "I was, well, for a lack of a better term, kinda miserable" — while convictions go flat and unhedged ("I've always been an entrepreneur at heart."). Whisper the wound, state the belief. "at best … at worst" = the two-pole scale for a bad realization.
- **The retraction-that-sells**: use a cliché, retract it in a parenthesis, literalize it into service — "and never looked back. (Actually, that's not true. I *did* look back, but only to data mine historical marketing trends so you don't have to)".
- **Self-question → self-answer engine**: she asks, she answers, the answer is a fragment or joke ("You think I'd be thrilled, right? Au contrair." / "who do you think…? 👍This girl 👍"). Narrative-copy twin of the caption Q&A and spoken "they're like."
- **Mangled borrowed phrases ship**: "Au contrair," "for a lack of a better term" live on her website. Correcting idioms to dictionary form is a polish-tell.
- **Credentials ride mid-sentence in a because-clause**, as evidence for a claim about something else ("Because after almost 10 years… & digital marketing research from the last 20 years they're the most low-maintenance, sustainable tools for growth I've found.") — the positive replacement for the §9 never-lead-with-credentials ban.
- **Even the about page is reader-first**: opens on the reader's dream, "I" waits a section, and the announced climax of the bio is about them ("quite literally obsessed with my clients"). Bio lists escalate resume → absurd, 5 items not 3, weirdest last ("a checkmark could never").
- **Aphorism-then-permission couplet**: unpunctuated law line, then a short license line — "A good idea loses its wings every time a creative is too busy to go down a rabbit hole / So, go ahead, waste your time."
- **Written-copy lexicon adds**: "Dude" as address, "could never" personified-negation joke, "fkn" ("really fkn good"), "obvi," "vacay," "outta," "whole ass" confirmed in WRITTEN copy (not just spoken).

## 9. Hard refusals (the short list — see TASTE.md for the detector)

Never: "level up," "crush your goals," "hustle harder," "show up authentically," "find your why," "game changer," "take action," "do the work," "mindset shift." Never "value" as a vague noun ("Please retire the word 'value.' We get it. You're helpful."). Never "effortless" or "passive income" without naming the front-loaded work. Never "We're so excited to share." Never "Buy Now" / "Sign Up" / "Click Here." Never fake urgency. Never exclamation-point sincerity ("You've got this!" as a closer). Never shame in pain sections. Never daily-posting or virality as strategy. Never lead with credentials. "This is your sign" only with a specific earned referent, never as filler. Every refusal has a positive replacement in §1-8 — write the replacement, don't just avoid the crime.
