## Offer-development patterns (how she builds new products)

- One-liner = outcome + anti-effort clause: "...while you do literally anything else," "...DONE before dinner," "...for CEOs with better things to do." Or contrarian aphorism: "Big lists are cute, but engaged lists make money."
- "Who it's for" = identity + specific stuck behavior, never demographics: "really good at scribbling brilliant ideas in journals but struggle to make them a reality."
- Repeated formats: Notion dashboards; prompt/swipe packs (55 prompts, 44 ideas, 80 lines); VIP/intensive days; recurring free events; automations-as-products.
- Naming: restaurant world for BOH-lane; "Soft" prefix for identity-lane; time-compression (5 Minute, 90 Minute, 90d, Quarter in a Day, Year of); revival/heat for email-lane (Resuscitator, Revival, Reheat, Simmer, warm, cold).
- Discount framing: "Regularly $X, but today, it's just $Y" — attendee/subscriber specials, small real scarcity ("Only 2 spots").

## Audience (2025 survey, n=96 — write to this, always)

52.1% established-but-need-clients. Wants: replace social with slow marketing (70.8%), automated email sales (66.7%), passive income from EXISTING offers (63.5%). They're buying relief from daily marketing pressure, an order of operations, a back end that works when they don't, and confidence they'll actually follow through — the #1 barrier is fear of not finishing, not money. Answer "why will this be different from the things I bought and didn't finish?" in every asset. Avoid: post-more advice, algorithm hacks, hustle language, aesthetic-cozy with no outcome.
