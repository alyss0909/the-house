# Deep read — Back of House sales page (flagship)

First deep read of a sales page, and it's the flagship — the page `craft/sales-pages.md`
already uses as its canonical skeleton. So the 12 beats were known going in; this study
hunts what the skeleton does NOT capture: line-level devices, register quirks, and the
places the page contradicts the brain.

Capture note: the vault copy is a text scrape — stray spaces before punctuation
("Someone who just gets it .", '" ORDERUP "') almost certainly mark where bold/italic
spans lived on the real page. Don't imitate the spaces; read them as emphasis markers.

## New patterns found

### 1. The coined-term architecture (coin → footnote → FAQ)
She invents an audience name and then builds page furniture around it. The CTA block
"Designed for the time connoisseur*" carries an actual asterisk-footnote —
"a CEO who knows the value of an intentional pace." — and the term later gets its own
FAQ entry ("Q: What is a Time Connoisseur? They're the entrepreneurs I made this course
for... They don't just manage their time, they savor it"). The full block (marquee +
footnote + CTA) repeats 3x down the page as a refrain unit. This is "coin, don't
describe" (Cycle 4) operating at page scale: the coined term becomes structural.

### 2. Reader-voice objection headers, mid-page
Beyond the end-of-page FAQ, she plants full sections whose HEADERS are the reader
talking: "wait not another "system" alyssa" (lowercase, no punctuation, addressing her
by name) and "So I'll never post on social? Won't that look bad?" The objection is
voiced AS the reader, then answered in her most rant-adjacent register on the page
("The buzzword has been stretched so thin it barely means anything anymore. After years
in productivity (where my literal job is to "systematize") I have to set the record
straight!!!"). The FAQ is the clinical version; these mid-page blocks are the warm one.

### 3. "Same." — the one-word commiseration answer
FAQ: "Q: This sounds great, but I don't trust myself to actually finish it." → answer
opens with "Same." One word, then the fix ("That's why Back of House isn't about
learning, it's about setting up."). This is the audience-truth barrier (fear of not
following through, biggest purchase barrier per the 2025 survey) answered by ADMITTING
SHE SHARES IT before solving it. Commiserate-first FAQ answers are a repeatable move.

### 4. No dead zones — boilerplate gets voice
The anti-piracy legal block flips mid-warning: "DO NOT PIRATE THIS COURSE... BUT YOU
DON'T HAVE TO BECAUSE going in with a friend is a biz-bestie-love-language." Then a real
course-share policy ("email us after purchase and we'll set you and your bestie up to
BOTH have logins") and the aside "(and don't worry we won't judge your netflix accts)".
Even the Canadian pricing note is "🍁 Canadian Cuties, we've got you." Rule: legal,
logistics, and payment copy are brand moments, never template filler.

### 5. Emoji as the verb between clauses
"Pinterest drives the traffic 🤝 Email does the converting." — the handshake IS the
conjunction. Same family as the solo-emoji section dividers found in the simmer read,
but here the emoji sits mid-sentence doing grammatical work. Also one mid-prose clap in
the origin story ("That's when I knew: something had to change. 👏 I decided to
literally SCRAP EVERYTHING") — a single clap, not the full clap-chant.

### 6. Personified proof (email is "she")
Data delivered with the object personified: email "has outlived every social platform
that's ever tried to kill it... she remains a stable, research-backed marketing channel
that continues to drive engagement and conversions (aka THAT girl)." Confirms the
simmer finding ("suddenly, she's giving a whole new vibe") in sales-page register:
"she" for beloved objects/channels, played warm, one per zone.

### 7. Section-level TLDR blocks as the skim path
Every curriculum phase closes with "🤎 TLDR:" + a 2-3 sentence outcome summary ("By the
end of this phase, you'll have a lean offer suite that pre-sells itself"). This extends
the bold-skim-path rule (STYLE §2) to section architecture: a skimmer reading only the
🤎 TLDRs gets the full promise arc. Note the TLDRs sell the destination (Hawaii), while
the steps above them describe the flight.

### 8. Register lexicon extensions
- "my dude" as reader address: "(this is your life's work my dude, how do you want to
  experience that?)" — alongside babe/bestie/girlies.
- Body/cycle humor in feature copy: "so you're always stocked with fresh leads (even
  during luteal phase 🫠)".
- Title-stack with a joke third item: "Productivity Strategist, Automation enthusiast,
  and antisocial accomplice since 2017" — the lopsided-list rule applied to her own bio.
- "FINISH THIS COURSE ACTUALLY MODE: ACTIVATIED" — mode-activation gag (typo included).
- Hospitality gesture opening the bio: "Grab a cocoa and a seat. You're definitely not
  alone."

### 9. Comparison lists WITHOUT villain formatting
The "stuck in high-maintenance marketing" list and the "low-maintenance model" list
both use ✅ checkmarks. She does not do ❌-vs-✅ theater; the sentence content carries
the contrast ("You can't take a break without losing momentum." vs "Your best content
works for years, not hours."). Emoji-bulleted feeling lists (🥱📉🕳️ pains → 🛎️🧸☁️
wants) do the same job with texture instead of X-marks.

### 10. Typos survive on the flagship
"Your Siganture DIsh.", "MODE: ACTIVATIED", "CLONE YOUR BEST MARKERTER", "Instagram
get's all the attention". The deliberate-imperfection value (TASTE: "the typo that
proves a human") holds even on the highest-stakes page she owns. Drafts polished to
zero-defect read less like her, not more.

## Contradictions / corrections (flagged to inbox, not applied)

1. **Countdown timer on the flagship evergreen page.** "👆 USE COUPON CODE " ORDERUP "
   BEFORE THE TIMER RUNS OUT TO SAVE $500" — TASTE disgusts list has "Fake urgency;
   countdown timers on evergreen offers" and STYLE §9 says "Never fake urgency." Her own
   page runs a timer. Possibly an evergreen-funnel timer she considers "real" (a genuine
   personal deadline per subscriber), possibly pre-doctrine copy. Needs her ruling before
   Hermes ever writes one.
2. **"quietly" twice in her own published copy** ("a digital Back of House quietly
   working in the background"; "the introverted indie duo of online business, quietly
   pulling in leads") vs the 2026-07-05 TOTAL BAN ("never use word quiet"). Page predates
   the ban. Assumed reading: ban governs new generation; her old copy is grandfathered
   like the banned shapes. Confirm, and confirm she doesn't want the live page scrubbed.
3. **Primary CTA is a command.** "JOIN BACK OF HOUSE NOW >>" / "JOIN NOW! >>" dominate
   the page, while STYLE §6 states "CTAs are never commands." The craft file already
   catalogs JOIN as legal, so the real rule is narrower than STYLE's phrasing: generic
   retail commands (Buy Now / Sign Up / Click Here) are banned; branded, product-named
   commands with the ">>" arrow are house style on sales pages. Needs a scoping edit to
   STYLE §6 — flagged rather than edited since it corrects an existing rule.

**Banned-shape corpus hits (covered by her 2026-07-05 standing ruling — grandfathered
verbatim source, never templates; recorded for judge calibration, no new flag):** this

eyeballs" (sneaky setup, used as beat 4's launchpad); "the question isn't: What's

(#2-adjacent); "This is not about endless tweaking! it's about long-term compounding"
(#6). Sales-page marquee register clearly tolerates contrast shapes far beyond email
register. Per the ruling: reuse hers verbatim lightly, generate zero new ones.

## Rules extracted (DO / DON'T)

DO:
- Coin the audience term, footnote it with an asterisk, refrain it, FAQ it.
- Give boilerplate (legal, payment, geo) the same voice as the hero copy.
- Voice objections as reader-spoken headers mid-page; save the clinical version for the FAQ.
- Open the hardest FAQ (follow-through fear) with "Same." — commiserate, then solve.
- Close every curriculum section with a 🤎 TLDR that sells the destination.
- Let one emoji do grammatical work per zone (🤝 as conjunction, 👏 as beat).
- Keep testimonials verbatim-messy ("worth its weight in gold. I only wish I had
  signed up sooner!") — never trimmed to slogan length.
- Use identical checkmarks on both sides of a comparison; let content carry contrast.

DON'T:
- Don't ❌-vs-✅ a comparison table. Don't round the value-stack numbers into one tidy
  bundle price ($40/$50/$100/$150 line items are the texture).
- Don't sterilize typos and capitalization wobble out of a draft.
- Don't generate contrast/reframe marquees even though this page is full of them —
  grandfathered source only, per her standing ruling.
- Don't put a timer, "quietly," or a generic retail command (Buy Now) in ANY new draft
  while the three inbox flags above are unadjudicated.
