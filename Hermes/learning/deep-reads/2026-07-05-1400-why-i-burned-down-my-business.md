# Deep read: "Why I burned down a perfectly good business (on purpose)"

First run of the deep-voice-study task — no prior INDEX, so this is also the
source document for the subject-line exception already cited in `STYLE.md` §7
("the only line that won opens AND clicks"). This study goes past the subject
line into full body-copy structure.

## New patterns found

1. **Slash-cascade opener.** The Pep Talk opens not with a scene but with a
   rapid-fire stack of synonymous phrases separated by slashes, mimicking
   internet meme cadence, before pivoting to a direct question:
   > "New year / new me / new goals / new vision / new life loading / vibes
   > that are swirling in the air right now — Am i right?"
   This is a documented alternate Pep Talk opener alongside the
   scene/confession/blurt already in `craft/emails-soft-sunday.md` — a
   recognition-cascade that names the reader's mental noise before the real
   question lands. Note the wait — this uses an em dash mid-cascade ("right
   now — Am i right?"), the single em dash found in the whole document (see
   Contradictions below).

2. **Rhetorical-question ladder before the reveal.** Four stacked questions
   build recognition before she names her own answer:
   > "think about the last time you felt this way when was it? And how long
   > did you stay swept up in it? What pulled you out of it? How long do you
   > usually stay in this creative space before that spark starts to go
   > away?"
   This is a distinct teaching-entry technique from the single "named pivot"
   move already documented — a ladder, not a single question, used specifically
   to open a Pep Talk before any personal scene is offered.

3. **Stylized "Reason nO1 / nO2 / nO3" numbering** — lowercase "n" + capital
   "O" + digit, used consistently for all three headers. A diction/formatting
   quirk not yet logged anywhere in STYLE.md; worth treating as a possible
   one-off house style for numbered teaching lists rather than a universal
   rule (only one document sampled so far).

4. **Fire/growth metaphor cluster, distinct from the four families already
   logged** (food, domestic-cozy, calendar/seasonal, parenting/childhood,
   sensory-universal). This document leans on burning-down/pruning/spark
   imagery as its own throughline:
   > "There were days I couldn't tell if I was pruning or burning it all to
   > the ground."
   > "2025 is really feeling like a spark that's ready to catch. I've spent
   > months tending to what's next."
   Given the email's entire subject and premise is "burned down," this may be
   occasion-specific (a single extended metaphor built for this piece) rather
   than a recurring family — flag for confirmation on a second fire/rebuild
   themed email before promoting it to a permanent family in STYLE.md §4.

5. **Raw personalization placeholder left in the source**: `[FIRST NAME GOES
   HERE]` appears inline mid-copy, not a merge tag. Operationally useful —
   confirms these example docs are working drafts/sent-copy pulls, not
   scrubbed final templates. Not a voice pattern, just a note for future
   deep-reads: expect occasional draft artifacts like this in the examples
   folder and don't mistake them for a real habit.

## Confirmations of existing brain

- **No jokes during the vulnerable beat** (STYLE.md §3): the line "*I had
  truly lost the plot.*" stands completely alone as its own sub-header/
  blockquote, no punchline before or after it in the same beat — humor only
  resumes in the next paragraph's numbered list. Exact match to the rule.
- **PS/late CTA re-pitches with a fresh angle** (STYLE.md §6): the email's
  actual offer CTA doesn't appear until ~800 words in, and the Check-In
  section closes with a *second*, differently-framed CTA ("Psst: I've spent
  the last year perfecting a slow marketing system...") rather than repeating
  the earlier "click here to be the first to see how it works" line.
- **Pronoun discipline** (STYLE.md §5): "I" carries every confession/origin
  beat ("I realized," "I knew I needed to change two things"); "we" appears
  exactly at the brand-pivot moments ("So **we** burned it all down," "**we**
  completely restructured the offer suite"). Clean split, no drift.
- **Named pivot per section**, not just once per email: each of the three
  reasons gets its own discovered-pivot phrase ("So we burned it all down,"
  "So, project simplify was born," "That's when I decided to make social
  media optional") — confirms the pivot move operates at the sub-section
  level in longer teaching emails, not only once per piece.
- **Elongated vowels within the STYLE.md cap**: "Buuuut," "haaad," "eeeease"
  — three instances in ~1,700 words, consistent with the "max once or twice
  per piece" guidance being closer to a soft ceiling than a hard one in a
  longer-format email.
- **Check-In skeleton matches `craft/emails-soft-sunday.md` exactly**: mood
  question → anchor word bookended by the same emoji twice ("🧚‍♀️ magic
  🧚‍♀️") → "the one thing" fill-in → CTA. One-to-one match, no drift.

## Rules extracted

**DO**
- Open a Pep Talk with a slash-cascade of synonymous phrases when the topic
  is a shared cultural moment (new year, etc.) before narrowing to a direct
  question — an alternate to the scene/confession opener already documented.
- Stack 3-4 rhetorical questions in a ladder to build recognition before
  revealing the personal answer, when the email is teaching a concept rather
  than opening on a single scene.
- Give each sub-section of a multi-part teaching email its own discovered
  pivot phrase, not one pivot for the whole piece.
- Close with a second, differently-angled CTA distinct from the offer CTA
  used mid-email — never repeat the same line twice.

**DON'T**
- Don't assume the 3-6-bolds-per-200-words ceiling applies to long-form
  origin-story Soft Sundays without more samples — treat as provisional
  pending a second long-form read (flagged to inbox, not resolved here).
- Don't treat a three-act structure with deep, textured sections as a
  "rule of three" violation — the ban targets shallow parallel bullets, not
  developed narrative acts.
- Don't silently canonize the "it's not just about X, it's about Y" hit
  found here as permission to use the shape again — it's logged as a
  contradiction for Alyssa to rule on, not a green light.
