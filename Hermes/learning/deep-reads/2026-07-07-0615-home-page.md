# Deep-read: the live home page (alyssacoleman.ca)

Second study in the about/home register (first was `2026-07-07-0512-about-page.md`). Read in full — it is short (108 lines) but structurally dense: seven distinct sections, each with its own device.

## New patterns found

**1. Marquee ticker header (ticker truncation as a device).**
> "HUSTLE CULTURE IS OVERCOOKED // HUSTLE CULTURE IS OVERCOOKED // HUSTLE CULTURE"

The phrase repeats with `//` dividers and is cut off mid-repeat on the third instance — visually mimicking a scrolling marquee/news-ticker that the reader's eye catches mid-scroll. This is a section-header device that doesn't exist anywhere in STYLE.md or the sales-page skeleton. Distinct from the "marquee text = standalone billboard declaration" note already in `craft/sales-pages.md` — that note covers a single caps line; this is a *repeated, truncated* caps line functioning as a design element, not prose. Home-page-specific so far (n=1).

**2. Inline strikethrough swap (no beat, stays in the clause).**
> "cozy systems to make ~~running your business~~ your life a hell of a lot easier"

STYLE.md's strikethrough rule is documented as "say the expected thing, cross it out, replace with the true thing" with a beat between (its anchors: "~~squander my potential~~ work for myself"). This instance is tighter — the struck word and its replacement sit inside one continuous clause with no punctuation break, and the sentence still parses if you delete the strikethrough entirely ("cozy systems to make your life a hell of a lot easier"). It's a subtler, in-line version of the same device: the strikethrough softens what could read as a jab at "running a business," not a two-beat joke. Worth distinguishing "beat" strikethrough (about-page) from "inline" strikethrough (home-page) as two flavors of the same tool.

**3. Two-line yearning-then-obstacle setup landing on a bolded one-line diagnosis.**
> "Want clients to discover your work online.. / but don't want your creativity smothered by "consistency"? / **You might need slow marketing**"

This is a persuasion shape not yet in STYLE §6: two short unbolded lines (desire, then the thing blocking it) followed by a single bolded sentence that names the diagnosis/product-category directly. Different from the fragment-verdict device (teaching register, short and unbolded) — here the "verdict" is a full sentence and it's bold, functioning as the section's thesis statement. Also note the scare-quotes on "consistency" — mocking the buzzword, same device as `craft/sales-pages.md`'s "feed the machine" / "renewal fees" note, new corpus hit with a new word.

**4. Full-sentence italics as a register shift, not single-word/phrase emphasis.**
> "You've been hustling to land better leads and make more sales. *But it's becoming next to impossible to keep up.*"

STYLE §3/§8's italic rule (confirmed across two prior deep-reads) is inner-monologue/confession, usually a short phrase. Here an entire independent clause is italicized to mark a tonal drop from momentum to strain. Same channel, longer unit — worth noting italics can carry a full sentence when the beat is a mood shift rather than an aside.

**5. Menu-board table as a benefit list.**
A markdown table with `:---:` alignment, one row, four cells, no header:
> "| That's when making both 10k and pasta from scratch become just another Tuesday. | When you stop having to sacrifice the dream of a 4-day workweek (because it's now your reality). | ... |"

Laying parallel "when this becomes normal" benefit lines out horizontally, like a menu board, is a formatting device unique to this page in the corpus so far. It's the food metaphor operating at the *layout* level, not just the language level — "shorter menus" (STYLE §4/§9 wordsmithing note) made literal.

**6. The unexplained proper-noun list item.**
Inside the "non-exhaustive list of the little things I'm making time for":
> "Cutting my bangs (again) / Becoming a clay artist / Agatha. / Sourcing scrap leather for my journal making hobby"

"Agatha." sits alone as a complete list item — a proper noun (a pet, per `SOUL.md`) with a hard period and zero explanation. It's funnier for being unglossed; the reader either knows who Agatha is or doesn't, and either way it reads as an insider aside rather than a gap. This is a sharper version of "absurdist precision" (TASTE.md) — not just a specific number or detail, but a specific *name* dropped with total confidence that context is optional.

**7. Unifying pun as the sign-off.**
> "Slow marketing for the creatives who'd rather be making dough."

"Dough" closes the page punning on both money and the literal bread/cooking metaphor that's been running the whole page (cookies, recipe, menu, Nonna). Ending a piece by resolving the extended metaphor into a single pun is a closing device not yet logged — distinct from the about-page's aphorism-then-permission couplet close.

**8. "STEAL" joins the acquisition-verb lexicon.**
> "CTA: STEAL MY SYSTEMS [Link to Free Resources]"

Extends the caption-corpus note (`swipe/snag`, never grab/download) with a third acquisition verb, confirming the family is broader than the two already logged.

## Confirmations of existing brain

- **Zero em-dashes.** Confirmed across the full page — extends the about-page's "longest-standing live copy" zero-count to a second page.
- **Caps-colon narrator device travels to home page.** "IF THERE'S ONE THING I KNOW ABOUT YOU IT'S THIS:" is the same stage-direction shape as the about-page's "LET ME SET THE SCENE FOR YOU:" — confirms this is a cross-page device, not About-page-specific.
- **Two-negation validation shape, second corpus hit.** "And it's not because your offer isn't good enough. / Or because you're not posting enough." — matches STYLE §6's rule verbatim (two negations, then stop, no third "it's because Z" punch). Good second real-source confirmation of the approved shape.
- **List escalates to the absurd, weirdest last, and lands in bold.** "...or **finally making it to the 2nd floor of that antique mall.**" — same escalation logic as the about-page bio list, applied here to a lifestyle list. Bold used to land the punch, which is a new detail (about-page used a plain fragment, not bold, for its escalation punch).
- **Restaurant metaphor as structural naming, full stretch (BOH-world register).** "THE CLASSICS," "OUR SECRET FAMILY RECIPE" — confirms STYLE §4's "full stretch, naming + texture" register call for BOH-adjacent copy.
- **CTAs are inner-monologue/food-world, never commands**, arrows as CTA punctuation (`>>`, `-->`) — confirmed again ("SOUNDS DELISH >>", "Lets Cook -->", "Marketing Your Nonna Would Approve of >>").
- **Deliberate imperfection ships at high-visibility pages.** "Lets Cook" (missing apostrophe) on a live homepage CTA — same class as the about-page's "Au contrair" and the BOH sales page's "Siganture DIsh."

## Contradictions / corrections

- **A genuinely broken/unbalanced parenthesis ships live**: "cozy email + pinterest marketing to (finally) do the things you've been dreaming of since you bought your domain name)." — the trailing `)` has no matching open-paren (the only opened paren, "(finally)", already closed itself). This isn't a stylistic wobble like the spacing ones already logged as load-bearing (`(micro)manage` etc.) — it's a plain unclosed/orphaned parenthesis, likely a copy-paste or editing artifact rather than a deliberate voice choice. Flagging to inbox rather than silently treating it as evidence for "typos are voice" — worth Alyssa confirming whether this is a live bug on the page (worth fixing) versus something Hermes should read as further proof of ship-anyway culture. Do not generate new "helpful" typos in imitation of this; the existing typo-tolerance rule already covers deliberate misfires, this one's ambiguous.

## Rules extracted (DO / DON'T)

**DO:**
- Use a repeated, truncated marquee-ticker line as a section header when you want a billboard moment with scroll-motion energy (n=1, home-page register — don't over-generalize to email yet).
- Let strikethrough soften a phrase inline, mid-clause, with no beat, when the joke is small (contrast with the bigger "beat" strikethrough for confessional reveals).
- Build a two-line yearning+obstacle setup and land it on ONE bolded diagnosis sentence, when introducing a category/positioning claim at the top of a page.
- Let italics carry a full sentence, not just a phrase, when marking a mood drop (momentum → strain).
- Drop an unglossed proper noun into an absurdist list and let it stand alone with a period — don't explain who/what it is.
- Close a metaphor-heavy piece by resolving the whole running metaphor into one final pun.
- Lay parallel benefit lines out as a borderless table/menu-board when the food metaphor should operate visually, not just verbally.

**DON'T:**
- Don't assume italics = short phrase only; check whether the unit is a mood-beat (can be a full sentence) or an inner-voice aside (stays short).
- Don't treat every parenthesis irregularity as intentional voice — an orphaned/unbalanced parenthesis with no matching open is more likely a live typo than a device; flag rather than imitate.
- Don't reuse the exact marquee-ticker construction verbatim (imitation-tell risk per ANTI-AI.md) — the ticker-truncation *mechanism* is reusable, the specific line ("HUSTLE CULTURE IS OVERCOOKED") is hers.
