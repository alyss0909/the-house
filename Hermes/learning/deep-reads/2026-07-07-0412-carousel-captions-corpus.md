# Deep read — the carousel caption corpus

First caption-format study. Note on method: [[Hermes/craft/captions-carousels]] already cites this file as a source, so this pass hunted specifically for what the earlier mining MISSED — caption-register micro-mechanics rather than deck structure. Thirteen captions read in full; every pattern below is quoted.

## New patterns found

### 1. The whisper caption — volume scales inversely with the visual
When the deck or image carries the whole joke, the caption goes nearly silent and NEVER re-explains. The Guest Cheque quote post (Mar 10) runs a five-word caption, total:

> "guys, i think i figured it out"

No CTA, no personality clause, no vibe paragraphs — the caption is the *reaction* to the image, not a summary of it. Jan 22's manifesto deck gets two short lines ("here are some gentle reminders for soft ceos doing things differently this year 🤎") and a signature. Meanwhile education/offer captions run 5-8 paragraphs. Rule: caption length is set by how much work the visual already did, and a caption that restates the slides is a tell.

### 2. Faux-disclaimer openers — announce the sin, commit it anyway
Two clean instances of an opener shape the brain didn't have:

> "Not to be dramatic... but i think it's time to break up with social" (Feb 14)
> "Sorry but Feb is the new Jan." (Feb 1)

The disclaimer ("not to be dramatic," "sorry but") is fake — she does the dramatic thing in the same breath. It's the written twin of her transcript self-aware rant flag ("I'm definitely on one"). Distinct from throat-clearing because the disclaimer IS the joke.

### 3. Reader-roleplay Q&A with the "Oh, it's only..." reveal
Feb 1 stages the reader's question mid-caption and answers it with a deflated humble-brag:

> "What's that? I hear you say
>
> Oh, it's only the all in one notion HQ to map out your entire year, month, and week so you can just show up as the employee of the month that you are and follow your (own) plan."

This is the caption-register twin of the transcript buyer roleplay ("they're like...") and the sales-email italic inner voice. "Oh, it's only the..." = mock-casual product reveal. Also note "employee of the month that you are" — the solo-business joke where the reader is both boss and employee.

### 4. The one-word parenthetical insert
Beyond the aside-that-carries-the-joke (STYLE §2), captions run a tighter micro-device: a SINGLE word dropped into brackets mid-phrase, deflating or intensifying without breaking stride:

> "a genuine waste of (precious) time" (Jan 20)
> "It's aesthetic, evergreen, and (actually) sustainable." (Feb 19)
> "follow your (own) plan" (Feb 1)
> "my reengagement automation (free)" (Jan 15) · "inside the (free) Pin Planner" (Jan 29)

"(free)" is the canonical price tag — lowercase, bracketed, tossed off, never a FREE! headline in caption prose (the CTA *slide* gets the one earned "(FREE!)"). Feb 1 even welds the bracket on with no space: "in one clear(but cozy) space" — the typo-adjacent weld survives publication.

### 5. Real urgency = a lowercase weekday inside the gift sentence
Her real-urgency doctrine at caption scale: the deadline is a named day/time, folded mid-sentence into the CTA, often kissed with "xx" — never its own countdown line:

> "revive that email community before friday xx" (Jan 15)
> "I'll send you the details for my upcoming pinterest class (this thursday!!) 📌" (Feb 24)
> "comment VIRAL to watch before the replay (& secret menu bonus) comes down at midnight xx" (Mar 7)

### 6. Asterisk-italics for the inner voice
IG has no italics, so the sales-email italic channel (reader's inner monologue) shows up as asterisk wraps:

> "daily visibility even on your slow days, creative days and *don't talk to me* days" (Mar 7)

### 7. Trailing giggle particles and the leading-ellipsis punchline
Clause-end softeners the brain hadn't logged: "I need the whole month to just kinda warm up lol." (Feb 1) · "i did not hold back the inner nerd this time hehe" (Mar 7) · "to re-heat your community, pronto." (Jan 20). And the punchline that arrives as its own paragraph opening WITH the ellipsis (not trailing from the previous line):

> "...that turns 4 pieces of content into daily traffic, while you do
>
> …literally anything else" (Jan 29)

### 8. Acquisition verbs: swipe / snag / send-you
CTA verbs across all 13: "comment PIN to **swipe** the slow marketing strategy" (Jan 29, again Feb 6), "comment PIN to **snag** my pinterest planner" (Feb 14), "Comment X and **i'll send you**..." (Jan 15, Feb 19, Feb 24, Mar 3). Zero instances of "grab," "download," "get my," or "access."

### 9. The sign-off is a variable slot, not a fixed "xx/xo"
Four distinct sign-off behaviors in the corpus: bare "xx" (Jan 15, Jan 29) · "xo" (Feb 19, Feb 24) · signed like a letter, "xx / Alyssa" (Jan 22 manifesto) · themed persona sign-off when the caption runs a conceit: "love, your secret business admirer" (Feb 14 Valentine breakup caption) · and NO sign-off at all on several offer captions (Feb 1, Feb 6, Mar 3 — Mar 3 uses a solitary "." on its own line as the spacer before hashtags). The conceit-matched sign-off is the strongest find: when the caption world-builds, the signature stays in-world.

### 10. Exclamation marks are legal in captions — but only on logistics
Caption register runs hotter than email: "🚨 Changes you should know! (pt1)" · "& QUICKLY!!" (Mar 3) · "(this thursday!!)" · "So this is your nudge!" (Jan 15). Every instance is attached to news/logistics/urgency or an aside — never to sincerity ("You've got this!" remains nonexistent). The double-bang "!!" lives inside parentheses or after caps, playing excitement for comedy.

### 11. Lexicon additions
- **"silly little" as affectionate diminutive**: "so YOU can get back to being a silly little creative 🤎" (Jan 20).
- **"is giving X (vibes)"**: "The algorithm is giving desperate ex vibes" (Feb 14) — confirms and extends the simmer's "she's giving a whole new vibe."
- **"COLD AF"** (Jan 15) — caps + af welded.
- **"wont get em"** (Jan 15) — dropped apostrophe AND "em" for "them"; don't clean these up.
- **"gaslit us into chalking everything up to 'another algorithm update'"** (Mar 3) — scare quotes as the eye-roll device, confirmed in caption register.

## Rules extracted (DO / DON'T)

DO:
- Scale the caption to the visual: whisper (≤10 words, zero CTA) on quote/FTG images, full 5-8-paragraph build on education/offer decks. Never restate the slides.
- Open with a faux-disclaimer that commits the sin it disclaims ("Not to be dramatic... but", "Sorry but").
- Stage the reader's question and answer with the mock-casual reveal ("What's that? I hear you say / Oh, it's only the...").
- Deflate with one-word parentheticals: "(free)", "(actually)", "(precious)", "(own)".
- Put real deadlines lowercase mid-sentence: "before friday xx", "(this thursday!!)", "comes down at midnight xx".
- Use *asterisk wraps* for the inner-voice channel; "lol"/"hehe"/"pronto" as clause-end softeners, sparingly.
- Pick acquisition verbs from: swipe, snag, "i'll send you".
- Match the sign-off to the caption's world (persona sign-off on conceit captions; "xx Alyssa" on signed manifestos; nothing at all is legal on offer captions).

DON'T:
- Don't re-explain the deck in the caption — the caption frames vibe or reacts, the slides teach.
- Don't headline "FREE!" in caption prose — the bracketed lowercase "(free)" is the price tag.
- Don't use grab/download/get/access as CTA verbs.
- Don't attach exclamation marks to sincerity — logistics and comic excitement only.
- Don't normalize her welded brackets, dropped apostrophes ("wont get em"), or unstable i/I — the wobble ships.
- Don't put a countdown construction anywhere; urgency is a named weekday inside the gift sentence (standing no-timers hold continues pending the ORDERUP inbox ruling).
