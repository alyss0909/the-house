# AI Team Hiring Plan

## Read this first

This is not a generic "hire a marketing team" plan. The vault already has an active writing brain ([[Hermes/README.md]]), an in-progress Content OS, a Ready Queue, a Signals Index, a Second Brain, design agents, app/dev agents, automation, and structural database/vault roles.

So the first hire is not "email writer" or "social media manager." Hermes already owns final copy in Alyssa's name. The real first gap is the person who turns all the moving parts into a weekly operating rhythm: launches, workshops, lead follow-up, inboxes, client check-ins, checkout QA, partner/podcast pipeline, and the decision list Alyssa needs to see.

The team should be built around Alyssa's actual week:

- socials
- emails
- launches
- research
- sales and offer suite
- client work
- lead magnets
- ads
- workshops, replays, and follow-up
- opt-in pages, sales pages, and other web assets
- funnel audit / big-picture conversion
- admin, inboxes, and follow-up

## Departments

### 1. Operating Room

Owns the week. Keeps the machine from becoming another place Alyssa has to manage.

Jobs:
- weekly command center
- inbox triage
- lead tracker
- workshop registration, reminder, replay, and follow-up ops
- client check-in tracker
- checkout/buyer-flow QA
- "Alyssa decision needed" list

This department should be boring in the best way. Its output is fewer dropped balls.

### 2. Revenue and Offer Suite

Owns the shape of what is sold and how the pieces ladder together.

Jobs:
- offer map
- product ladder
- lead magnet fit
- launch thesis
- pricing/positioning conflicts
- sales page logic
- upsells, bumps, tripwires, and rush-order paths
- funnel audits

This is not just "sales." It is offer architecture plus conversion logic.

### 3. Content OS

Owns the production system, not just posting.

Jobs:
- monthly theme calendar
- concept pitching
- Hermes briefs
- email to carousel
- carousel to pins
- content packaging into Notion
- designer handoff
- performance feedback loop

Hermes writes. Content OS makes sure Hermes is writing the right things from the right sources at the right time.

### 4. Launch and Workshop Studio

Owns campaign windows and live/replay conversion.

Jobs:
- launch calendar
- asset checklist
- registration page
- emails, socials, ads, reminders, replay page
- hot reply escalation
- post-launch debrief

This should be one seat at first, not five. Your vault shows workshops, replays, launch emails, and pages are connected.

### 5. Client and Student Success

Owns the experience after someone says yes.

Jobs:
- onboarding
- asset collection
- weekly check-ins
- transcript review
- support themes
- testimonials/referrals
- Rush Order delivery tracker

This matters because [[weekly-client-check-ins]] is already a habit and Rush Order is an active launch idea.

### 6. Growth and Distribution

Owns traffic and experiments.

Jobs:
- Pinterest / evergreen search
- ads
- podcast and partner outreach
- affiliate/collab tracker
- performance reporting
- source quality by channel

This department should not be allowed to create random volume. It serves the offer suite.

## What not to hire first

### Do not hire a generic email writer first

Hermes is already the high-sensitivity clone writer. A generic writer would either sand down Alyssa's voice or create another review burden.

Better first move: hire/AI-build the Offer Architect and Content OS Showrunner around Hermes.

### Do not hire a generic social media manager first

The social problem in the vault is not "someone should post more." It is source selection, repurposing, weekly themes, CTAs, ManyChat words, design handoff, and performance feedback.

Better first move: Content OS Showrunner, then Social/Pinterest Producer.

### Do not hire ads before offer/page/follow-up are stable

Ads are a multiplier. The vault has funnel ideas, but also gaps: offer ladder, price conflicts, chat words, and page QA. Fix those first.

### Do not let admin absorb revenue ops

"Replying to inboxes yadda" is not the same as warm-lead follow-up, client success, checkout QA, or workshop conversion. Admin can assist, but a Revenue Ops Lead owns the revenue-critical version.

## Core business engines

This is the version that uses Alyssa's actual Second Brain. The business is not "marketing, sales, ops." The business runs on a few repeatable engines:

- **Email engine:** Simmer, Soft Sunday, sales emails, subject lines, welcome sequences, sign-off specials, oops emails, right-time offers.
- **Workshop engine:** monthly workshop topics, registration pages, pre-workshop forms, live teaching, replay follow-up, offer pitch, workshop-to-content reuse.
- **Social/content engine:** competitor remixes, hooks, carousels, stories, captions, pins, content from emails, content from workshops.
- **Offer engine:** small offers, freebies, bumps, tripwires, BOH, Rush Order, seasonal promos.
- **Ads engine:** weekly ad copy/design tests and reporting.
- **Vault engine:** weekly sweep that turns saved knowledge into emails, workshops, posts, offers, and pages.

## The master recurring rhythm

If the whole team existed, the business rhythm would look like this:

### Daily

- Revenue Ops runs the command center.
- Admin clears routine noise.
- Active launches/workshops/ads get checked.
- Client next-actions are visible.
- Content intake is routed.
- Hot leads are escalated.

Business result: Alyssa starts the day with a short decision list instead of a fog bank.

### Weekly

- Revenue Ops runs the weekly business review.
- Content OS runs the weekly content batch.
- Client Success runs weekly check-ins.
- Workshop Producer runs promo/replay follow-up when active.
- Launch Manager runs the launch room when active.
- Social/Pinterest moves approved assets through distribution.
- Performance Reporter posts the weekly snapshot.

Business result: the week closes with shipped assets, followed-up leads, visible numbers, and fewer unresolved threads.

### Monthly

- Offer Architect reviews the suite and gaps.
- Content OS plans the editorial calendar.
- Web/Funnel Producer reviews conversion friction.
- Workshop Producer reports workshop performance.
- Paid Growth reviews funnel economics.
- Client Success reports success/proof themes.
- Revenue Ops runs revenue leak audit.

Business result: the business improves one layer at a time instead of just producing more stuff.

### Quarterly

- Offer Architect resets the offer map.
- Content OS resets the market/content thesis.
- Launch Manager builds seasonal promo calendar.
- Growth Lead recommends channel allocation.
- Research Lead updates positioning/category view.
- Revenue Ops reviews capacity and delegation.

Business result: Alyssa gets a real CEO-level review: what to sell, what to stop, what to scale, what to simplify.
