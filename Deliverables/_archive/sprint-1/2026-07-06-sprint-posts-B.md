**Slide 2 — Open loop / credibility**
> **I Storyboard My Welcome Emails Like A Documentary.**
>
> I'VE REVIEWED MY NOTES (& MY OWN WELCOME SEQUENCE) AND HERE'S THE THING NOBODY TELLS YOU ABOUT THE FIRST FEW EMAILS A NEW SUBSCRIBER GETS FROM YOU.. THEY'RE NOT A LIST OF FACTS. THEY'RE A MOVIE. AND MOST OF US NEVER ASK WHAT THE OPENING SHOT IS.

**Slide 3 — Agitate**
> **You Wrote Five Emails And Zero Of Them Have A Plot.**
>
> most welcome sequences are just.. an about page, a testimonial, a "here's what I offer," stitched together bc someone told you a list needs nurturing. nobody asked what the reader is supposed to believe by email five that she didn't believe by email one. so she reads three, forgets you exist, and there's your open rate.

**Slide 4 — Value 1 (the worked example, documentary's one allowed stretch)**
> **If Your Offer Had A Documentary, Email One Is The Opening Shot.**
>
> if your signature offer had a documentary, what would the audience think about it in the opening scene, and what would they think by the closing shot. that gap - the distance between those two beliefs - is the actual job of your welcome sequence. take my own 5 Minute Newsletter sequence: email one opens on "I don't have time to cook," email five closes on "I already know what's for dinner." nobody in between is selling anything. they're just moving the belief, one email at a time, the way a documentary moves you from confused to convinced without you noticing the cuts.

**Slide 5 — Value 2 (lopsided, one line, the confession)**
> **I Didn't Know My Own End-State Until Someone Asked Me.**
>
> a friend read my welcome sequence and asked "what do you want them to believe by email five." I didn't have an answer. I'd written five nice emails and zero beliefs.

**Slide 6 — Value 3 (mid-thought correction, teaching)**
> **Every Email Either Chips At The Old Belief Or Replaces It.**
>
> so, start-state first - what does she believe walking in, probably "I just need to post more" or "I haven't found the right template yet" - actually wait, that's the wrong order. name the END-state first, the belief you're building toward, then work backward to where she's starting from. builds faster that way. either direction gets you the arc, but backward is less guessing.

**Slide 7 — Value 4 (teaching, plain declarative)**
> **A Scene Moves The Plot. A Slide Just Sits There.**
>
> before you hit send on email 3, ask if it moves her one inch closer to the belief you need by email five, or if it's just "content" a template told you to include. cut the ones that just sit there.

**Slide 8 — Aha (adds, doesn't restate)**
> **The Boring Detail Is What Made Email Three Convert.**
>
> not the documentary metaphor, not the belief arc theory - the one line in email three where I mentioned I burn toast on purpose so the smoke alarm doesn't feel personal anymore. that's the email people replied to. the arc got them there. one dumb true detail is what made them hit reply.

**Slide 9 — CTA**

**Slide 3 — Agitate**

>

**Slide 4 — Value 1 (teaching)**
> **Freshness Is A Combination Problem, Not A Content Problem.**
>
> a fresh pin is a new unique combination of image + title + description + URL + board. never new content on its own. you can reuse every single asset you already have as long as the COMBINATION hasn't been seen before.

**Slide 5 — Value 2 (teaching)**
> **Not All Metadata Is Weighted The Same.**
>
> a new URL + image is valued much higher than just a new file name. renaming your same old pin isn't recombining, it's cosplaying as new. the platform can tell the difference even when your eyes can't.

**Slide 6 — Value 3 (teaching, the wardrobe device)**
> **Mix It Like A Capsule Wardrobe.**
>

**Slide 7 — Value 4 (lopsided, one line + aside)**
> **The Pin You Made In March Still Fits.**
>
> you just never tried it with a different board. (a historian would've noticed that pattern months ago, but you don't need to be one, you just need one new board.)

**Slide 8 — Aha (adds a new consequence, direct claim)**
> **Your Seven Links Are Doing The Work Of Seventy.**
>
> that's fourteen hundred pins pulling traffic from seven pieces of content you already made. the math was always there. you just never opened the closet.
