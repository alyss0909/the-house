## Draft 1

Hey [name],

Can i tell you what to do this week? i promise it comes from a loving place.

Most of the people i work with are ~idea people~. Big thinkers, fast starters, usually three tabs deep into the next shiny thing before the last one's even finished (hi, it me too ☁️). You've got a running list of Marketing Things You Should Do, and somehow it never gets any shorter. And the thing i hear most from this crowd is basically: just tell me the ONE move.

Okay. Here's the one move: email your list.

Not four separate marketing tasks. One email. Because a good newsletter does more than "keep in touch." It warms people up AND makes sales in the same send, which means it quietly does your nurturing, your engaging, and your selling all at once. Do it enough and you slowly stop having "a list" and start having a little community of people who open everything you send, get how you think, and are already halfway to buying before you even ask. It's honestly the lowest-maintenance, highest-return thing in my whole business, and the quiet magic of it is that it keeps selling for you between launches, so you're not white-knuckling every single promo.

THE ONE THING:
✅ Pick one small moment from your week
✅ Say what it actually made you think about
✅ Point the ready folks toward your offer, gently

It takes maybe 20 minutes, and you don't have to invent anything clever. You just need one true little thought and somewhere to put it. Write it right now by filling in this:

> Subject: something a lil funny
> Something i've been chewing on lately: [the thought rattling around]
> I used to think [old belief], now i think [new belief]
> Which matters because [your reader's real problem]
> So here's one tiny thing to try today: [simple action]
> And if you want the whole thing, i made [offer] >> [link]

That's the entire marketing to-do list for the day, babe. Set a timer, fill in the blanks, and hit send before another idea wanders in and steals you. No overthinking, no perfect draft, just sent.

Copy, paste, done 💌
