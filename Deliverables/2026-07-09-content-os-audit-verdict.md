## D. Why maker output goes generic — brief fixes

The W30 strategy brief proves Pax's finding #2: it has slots, offers, STOP lists — constraints — but almost no *specificity fuel*. Category-average inputs produce category-average concepts. Every weekly brief adds four sections:

1. **The reader this week** — one paragraph, a specific woman, her current pain in her own words (not "founders who want traffic" — "she posted 4 times this week, got 11 likes, and is wondering if she's the problem").
2. **Real artifacts to pull from** — named files with the actual numbers in the brief itself (subject-line.md's 56%/45%/57% opens, the Revival Plan's actual email), so proof slides start real.
3. **What her accounts are winning with right now** — 3–5 verbatim swipe lines (the July read: permission lines, confessions, recurring series — this was IN the swipe file and never reached a brief).
4. **The POV rule, one sentence:** "She is the field reporter, not the confessor and not the coach" — the single line from the forensics that kills both diary-voice and you're-doing-it-wrong voice at the source.
