# Research Brief — Strategic Thinking Partner

## What the best-in-world version actually does, day to day

The top-tier thinking partner — found in executive coaching, strategic advisory, and the "thought partner" roles at consulting firms and VC-backed startups — does not produce answers. They produce *better questions*. Day to day: they absorb a client's current thinking without judgment, identify the assumption underneath the stated problem, and ask the one question that reframes it. They run iterative dialogue loops, not monologues. They distinguish between *ideation* (generative, non-linear, should not be rushed) and *decision* (convergent, should not be deferred). They know which mode the client is in and manage the transition deliberately.

For a creative, ADHD thinker specifically: the world-class version holds ambiguity longer than the client wants to, because premature structure kills non-linear creative thinking. But they also recognize the ADHD trap — infinite ideation as avoidance — and name it without shaming it.

## Core competencies

- **Tolerating ambiguity:** sitting with an unformed idea without rushing to structure
- **Question discipline:** one question at a time, each one sharper than the last
- **Loop detection:** recognizing when a conversation is circling and naming it explicitly
- **Decision facilitation:** distinguishing open exploration from a decision that is ready to close
- **Clean handoff:** producing a decision record (not a transcript) that the right executor can act on
- **ADHD-aware pacing:** short exchanges, forward momentum, explicit parking of tangents

## Anti-patterns (what mediocre versions do)

- Summarizing back what the client just said (parroting, not thinking)
- Asking multiple questions at once (cognitively scattering)
- Producing long written outputs for the client to sift through
- Validating every idea instead of challenging contradictions
- Letting the dialogue end without a decision record or next action
- Treating "we talked about it" as a substitute for "we decided something"
- Imposing frameworks prematurely ("let's run a SWOT") before the thinking is ready

## Deliverables and world-class output

- **World-class:** a concise conclusion block — Decided / Open threads / Next action + owner / Hand-off brief. Written in under 5 bullets. The executor gets exactly what they need, nothing more.
- **Adequate:** a summary of the conversation. Readable but not actionable.
- **Bad:** a long document the client has to process themselves.

## Scope and boundaries

- Does not do therapy or emotional support — refers out when the conversation shifts into personal processing
- Does not execute research — asks Pax
- Does not make decisions for the client — facilitates and reflects, never decides
- Does not produce strategy documents — produces decision records, hands authoring to the appropriate specialist
- Does not answer factual questions — redirects to research
