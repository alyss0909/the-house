## 3. The Key Element tree — the architecture as she built it

Two branches, matching Key Elements/INDEX.md's framing ("the load-bearing walls of your life... four to seven files, no more"): **Business** (7 files) and **Life** (4 files) = 11 total.

### Business/ (`parent_key_element: business` except business.md itself)

- **[[business]]** — the meta layer. "The big-picture stuff that doesn't belong in a specific child area — overall strategy, model thinking ('reverse funnel,' 'triple yes offer suite'), positioning decisions, audience insights." Explicitly *not* the execution layer for the children below. Holds Team/Ops sub-notes (hiring, ops assistant, contracts).
- **[[emails]]** — "Everything to do with email in my business — weekly simmer sends, right-time offers, Soft Sundays, sequences." Good = "A back of house for my list — emails written once that run and convert long after I send them." Explicitly excludes launch copy/sales pages (→ promos) and the products sold about email (→ offers).
- **[[socials]]** — IG, Pinterest, content frameworks, ManyChat, scheduling. Good = "a batched, systematic content flow where Pinterest does the evergreen heavy lifting, Instagram has a vibe without demanding my whole life." Excludes paid ads (→ promos).
- **[[promos]]** — launches, campaigns, ad strategy, waitlists, seasonal sales, affiliates, webinar angles — "The machinery of making a sale happen." Excludes weekly email send (→ emails) and offer details themselves (→ offers).
- **[[offers]]** — the products themselves: idea development, naming, positioning, pricing, course outlines. "This is about the offer itself, not how I sell or deliver it." Feeds the entire Offers/ folder (section 4 below).
- **[[clients]]** — 1:1 and done-for-you client work: onboarding, deliverables, welcome pages. Current clients listed by name: Alexandria Abramian, Gillian Flynn, Lara Kimmerer, Maude Trottier. Excludes group offers/courses (→ offers).
- **[[brand-world]]** — "The world I'm building — who I am, who I speak to, what I stand for, and how it all looks and sounds." "What this covers," "What good looks like," "What I am ignoring" all left blank in the body — the file is mostly a related-notes shelf (15 `ideas` entries tracing brand-phrase development from 2023 to 2026, e.g. "set the table, less hustle more flow," "hot honey, secret sauce," "escape artist, time connoisseur").

### Life/ (`parent_key_element: life` except life.md itself)

- **[[life]]** — top of this branch. "My home, parenting Norah, who I'm becoming, and the rhythms and recipes that hold it all together." Good = "Present, not overwhelmed, and intentional. A life that feels like mine, not a set of tasks to get through." Explicitly excludes anything work-related.

Calm, curious, gentle but boundaried. Having what I need — the ideas, the scripts, the traditions — before the moments demand it." ## 5. The person — synthesized from these files only

**What she's building toward:** A business that runs without her constant presence — the recurring phrase across Key Elements and Offers is some version of "while I do literally anything else." [[business]]'s "what good looks like" names it directly: "slow marketing, compounding evergreen assets, low-maintenance revenue." [[emails]] wants "emails written once that run and convert long after I send them." [[socials]] wants Pinterest to do "the evergreen heavy lifting" so Instagram doesn't have to "demand my whole life." The flagship offer, Back of House, is literally named for this: "The automated sales & marketing department for CEOs with better things to do." She is not building a bigger hustle — she's building a system that lets her step back from the front of the business ("front of house energy," per the BOH sales copy) without the business stopping.

**What she protects:** Family time and a soft-CEO boundary around how she works, not just what she sells.
- [[life]]'s "what good looks like": "Present, not overwhelmed, and intentional. A life that feels like mine, not a set of tasks to get through."

- The offer suite's own brand identity leans the same way — "Soft CEO," "goodbye girlboss," "productivity strategist for service providers entering their post-girlboss era," "office hours," "coworking sessions... your sacred CEO appointment." The audience data in BUSINESS.md backs this as more than personal taste: her own survey (n=96) shows the #1 barrier her buyers feel is "fear of not finishing, not money," and she is explicitly told to avoid "hustle language."
- Concretely, the Norah birthday project (the most fully fleshed-out, fully actionable file in all of `My Life/`) and the parenting Key Element's Montessori-and-scripts orientation show where her actual attention outside business currently goes.
