# THE CAROUSEL PATTERN CODEX
## Machine-usable replication guide for Alyssa Coleman's IG carousels

> **Who this is for:** any model (including lower-capability LLMs) writing new carousels in Alyssa's system WITHOUT re-reading the raw sources. Everything load-bearing is quoted verbatim here. If a line is in quotes, it is her actual language — reuse the *move*, not the exact line, unless building a direct variant.
>
> **Taxonomy (locked, from CALENDAR.md):** Theme ∈ {Email, Pinterest, Productivity, BoH Systems, Sales} × Post type ∈ {Education, Offer, For The Girls}. Anything more specific (Manifesto, Trend Report, Data Argument, Step Framework, Webinar RSVP) is a free-text "Content role," never a Theme or Post type.

## CARD 01 — 2026-01-15 "2026 Slow Marketing Trends (Part One)"

- **Slot:** Email × Education (Week 1 Email · "Edu Carousel + Freebie")
- **Slides:** 10
- **Hook mechanic (slide 1):** *Editorial Series Title + Surrealist Still Life.* Headline: **"2026 Slow Marketing Trends"** with handwritten "Part One" and subhead "FOR ONLINE SERVICE PROVIDERS, COACHES & CREATIVES". No promise, no question — the post is framed as journalism, and "Part One" is a built-in retention engine. Visual: red rotary telephone + ballet flat on a silver serving tray (deliberately incongruous scroll-stopper).
- **Slide-2 mechanic:** *Title Card repeat.* Slide 2 restates the series name/audience with rearranged layout — a masthead for viewers who arrive mid-scroll. No new information; brand anchoring only.
- **The spine (10):** 1. series cover → 2. series title card → 3. credentialed thesis ("Not to be dramatic but things are changing 👀" + "I'VE REVIEWED MY NOTES (& INDUSTRY REPORTS)") → 4. trend 1 intro (named concept: "The AI Kiss of Death") → 5. trend 1 implication ("It's Not —Just— The Em Dash") → 6. trend 1 silver lining ("Nuance is Making a Comeback") → 7. trend 2 intro ("Great News for (cool) Small Businesses") → 8. trend 2 action w/ bait-and-switch joke ("Use This Prompt" → "**JUST KIDDING.**") → 9. offer reveal ("Tiny Email Lists Are All The Rage This Year" / "THIS IS YOUR SIGN") → 10. freebie CTA ("Comment Template").
- **CTA mechanics:** Chat word **TEMPLATE** → Cold Subscriber Automation + re-engagement email template (free). On-slide ask: "Comment Template — AND I'LL SEND YOU MY EXACT COLD-SUBSCRIBER CLEANING AUTOMATION AND RE-ENGAGEMENT EMAIL TEMPLATE (FREE!)". Caption ask: "🎁 Comment TEMPLATE for my reengagement automation (free) and revive that email community before friday xx". The freebie is *shown* on the final slide as a hand-drawn automation flowchart served on the silver tray.
- **Unmistakably Alyssa:** "Not to be dramatic but" (brand-signature opener) · "I'M ALL FOR BEING CRINGE IN PUBLIC BUUUT..." · "CHAT-WRITTEN" (her coinage for GPT content) · "MAKE THEIR MONEY ONLINE **BUT DON'T WANT TO LIVE ONLINE**" (the audience mirror) · "IT'S TIME TO WEAN OFF GPT WRITTEN EMAILS BABE (IT'S HURTING CONVERSIONS)" · "NATURALLY SOUNDING MORE LIKE YOU.. (IMAGINE THAT?!)" · "(SEE OUR COLLECTIVE SUBSTACK ADDICTION FOR PROOF OF THIS ONE)". Serving-tray prop system: every slide serves one metaphorical object.
- **Metrics:** 64,295 views · 70,011 reach · 4,957 likes · 2,659 shares · 955 follows · 523 comments · **5,781 saves** (highest saves in dataset).

# PART 2 — CROSS-CUTTING LAWS

These hold across ALL exemplars. Violating one produces content that is visibly not-Alyssa.

## L1. Slide counts
- Range: **7–11 slides** for carousels (7, 8, 8, 9, 9, 9, 10, 10, 10, 11, 11). Mode: **9–10**. Single-image posts exist only in the FTG lane.
- 10–11 slides only when the structure earns it (two 3-slide trend blocks; a full compressed sales page). 7–8 when the argument is one clean arc.
- Headlines: **4–8 words** typical, 2–5 on step/CTA slides; outliers earn their extra words rhetorically ("even if you DID go viral"). Body: never more than ~3 short paragraphs; most slides carry 1–2 lines.

## L2. Where the offer appears
- **Never before ~60% of the carousel has run.** Education posts: offer in final 1–2 slides only. Offer posts: product may be *revealed* early (02-06 slide 3) but the *ask* still concentrates at the end.
- **Three-layer CTA architecture:** (1) early seed on slide 2–3 in body copy or handwriting ("REPLY PIN to snag my Pinterest Planner & keep reading"), (2) mid-deck signal without a hard ask (embedded product card, named concept), (3) full reveal + comment trigger on the last 1–2 slides.
- **Footer seeding:** the product/brand name rides every slide's footer ("BACK OF HOUSE" ×10 on 03-03) so the reveal never feels cold.
- **The chat word is always thematically fused to the carousel's world:** TEMPLATE→list cleaning, LIST→list revival, PIN→Pinterest, VIRAL→Slowly Viral (ironic mirror of the anti-viral argument), YESCHEF→restaurant world, CLOCK→clock-in world, CEO→CEO planner. Chat words are verbatim or they don't exist — never invent one.
- **No panic CTAs.** Soft deadlines only ("before Friday," "comes down at midnight"). The ask is "come with me" / "let's make it happen," never "act now."

## L3. Teaching depth vs. gap-opening
- She teaches **complete, usable systems** in the free content (the 4-step Pinterest plan, the Q2 monthly plan, keyword instructions with real UI screenshots). The reader could leave and implement. That completeness is what earns follows and saves.
- The **gap** is never withheld information — it's *execution infrastructure*: the template, the automation, the planner, the class, the coaching. "Here's the whole strategy; the product is the container that does it for you / with you." (03-01 gives the full Q2 plan, then sells the dashboard that catches it; 02-19 teaches all 4 steps, then RSVPs you to the funnel walkthrough.)
- Ratio rule: **problem/education always outweighs pitch ≥ 3:1 in slide count.** Never fewer than 3 slides building the problem before the alternative appears.
- Every carousel has exactly one **emotional validation slide** (mid-deck, warmer register, zero CTA): "and well, exhausting" · "IF YOU FEEL TETHERED TO THIS SINKING ORGANIC MARKETING SHIP" · "IF KEEPING UP WITH THE CONTENT MACHINE HAS YOU EXHAUSTED". This slide earns the right to make the offer.
- **Villain assignment, never reader blame:** "social platforms tend to reward volume over value" · "we've been tricked into making their platforms better" · "the content machine has you exhausted." Guilt is converted into righteous motivation.

## L4. Structural differences by Post type
| Dimension | Education | Offer | For The Girls |
|---|---|---|---|
| Job | Open the theme week, grow cold audience | Convert the theme week | Friday brand-world, relationship maintenance |
| Spine | Trend report or step framework; freebie CTA at end | Education front-load → product demo → staged CTA; OR story/POV → pivot → webinar RSVP | Litany of identity statements in a single repeated visual container; question or sign-off close |
| Data/proof | Heavy (stats, screenshots, citations) | Product transparency (real Notion screenshots, testimonials) | ZERO data, zero tactics, zero framework |
| CTA | Free resource chat word | Product/webinar chat word, coupon/deadline optional | None on slides; soft or no chat word in caption |
| Metrics it wins | Saves, non-follower reach, follows | Comments, shares, conversions | Saves-per-reach, follower love (66.8% follower views on 03-07) |
| Register | "Smart friend explaining a trend" | Same, plus "here's the inside of the thing" | Intimate, first-person, devotional |

## L5. The dual-voice system (visual + verbal)
- Every carousel runs two typographic voices: **display serif** (burgundy/wine or icy blue) = the authoritative claim; **handwritten script** = the aside, the whisper, the CTA, the correction. "The serif makes her credible. The handwriting makes her human." Both must be present.
- Handwritten layer jobs seen in the wild: series label ("Part One"), circled year, softener ("and well, exhausting"), CTA ("Comment PINTEREST"), conspiratorial note ("IT REALLY IS AS GOOD AS IS SOUNDS"), credibility bomb ("yes, i literally coded a pinterest app for you"), correction ("(not new ideas)").
- **Props are arguments:** serving tray (insights served), coffee cup (private thoughts), butter/bread (bread-and-butter evergreen content), doily heart (valentine), cherries (Slowly Viral indulgence), guest cheques/bells/croissants (BOH restaurant world). Never decorative — always metaphorical.
- **Deliberate imperfection is a feature:** "PERMISSIOIN", "RESUSITATION", "walkthough", "AS GOOD AS IS SOUNDS" all shipped. Do not sand the voice down to polish; do not fake typos either — just don't over-correct the human texture.
- Voice markers to deploy (sparingly, 1–3 per carousel): extended spellings ("BUUUT...", "EEEVERYTHING", "VERRRRRY", "kindaa", "actionnnn"), parentheticals carrying all nuance, "babe"/"girls"/"girlies" community address, one exclamation point per carousel max, "Not to be dramatic but", "My advice:", "This is your sign", asterisk emphasis (*actually*), text-speak drops ("4 U", "em").
- **What never appears:** urgency/countdown/scarcity language, hashtag walls on slides, cluttered slides (one point per slide), selling before 60%, stock photography, polished AI-looking gloss, direct audience blame.

## L6. Caption laws
- Caption restates the argument in 2–5 short lines, adds ONE emotional beat the slides didn't have, then the chat-word ask, then hashtags (~20–30, same base stack: #alyssacolemanca #slowmarketing #softceo ... plus topic tags).
- Sign-offs: "xx", "xo", "xx Alyssa", "love, your secret business admirer".
- Emoji as bullet markers in offer captions ("📅 ✅ 💻 📌 💅" for the Pin Planner stack; "📌 💌 📈" for the flipped model).
- FTG captions can be one line ("guys, i think i figured it out").

# PART 3 — THE 3 ARCHETYPAL SPINES

Reusable slide-by-slide templates synthesized from the real posts. Each slide: **JOB** + a verbatim example line from a real post to calibrate against. Slide counts flex ±1; keep the order.

## SPINE A — EDUCATION (9–10 slides)
*Synthesized from 01-15, 01-20, 01-29, 02-10. Persona: industry analyst / marketing nerd sharing what she's seeing.*

1. **COVER — series/promise hook.** Editorial title naming a trend, year, or maximalist promise; audience subhead; series label if applicable. — *"2026 Slow Marketing Trends" / "How To Get All Your Clients From Pinterest This Year"*
2. **FRAME — title card OR credential + early CTA seed.** Repeat masthead for mid-scroll arrivals, or establish research basis and drop the first soft trigger. — *"I'VE REVIEWED MY NOTES (& INDUSTRY REPORTS)... WITH CLIENTS WHO MAKE THEIR MONEY ONLINE BUT DON'T WANT TO LIVE ONLINE" · "REPLY PIN to snag my Pinterest Planner & keep reading"*
3. **THESIS / AUDIENCE MIRROR.** Name who this is for and the shift happening, with self-aware drama. — *"Not to be dramatic but things are changing 👀" · "A LITTLE MORE GROUNDED, A LITTLE MORE ANALOG, AND A LOT LESS DEPENDENT ON THE ALGORITHM"*
4. **PROBLEM / TREND 1 — named concept.** Brand the observation; assign the villain to the system. — *"The AI Kiss of Death (& what that means for tiny audiences)" · "Organic Reach is Officially Cooked."*
5. **IMPLICATION.** Widen the problem; make the stakes credibility/energy, not just tactics. — *"Even when we can't name what feels off, we feel it ...and subconsciously discount the creator's credibility."*
6. **SILVER LINING / CONCESSION PIVOT.** Deliver the relief beat honestly; steel-man the counterargument if needed. — *"THE GOOD NEWS? AUDIENCES ARE NATURALLY GRAVITATING TOWARDS THINGS THAT FEEL REALLY NUANCED!" · "(OK YES, YOU CAN STILL SUCCEED ON SOCIAL... BUT YOU'LL HAVE TO PUT YOUR BACK INTO IT.)"*
7. **TREND 2 / STEP BLOCK.** The practical system, one point per slide, metaphor-named steps or numbered steps, personal evidence. — *"I'M SEEING THIS WITH MY OWN CLIENTS EVERY SINGLE DAY!" · "Pinterest loves fresh pins, not just fresh content"*
8. **ACTION SLIDE (densest copy allowed).** The "what to do instead" payoff, possibly with a joke beat. — *"Use This Prompt — JUST KIDDING. IT'S TIME TO WEAN OFF GPT WRITTEN EMAILS BABE"*
9. **OFFER BRIDGE.** Connect the analysis to the freebie as the natural first step; permission language, not pressure. — *"IF YOU'VE BEEN PUTTING OFF 'CLEANING' YOUR LIST THIS IS YOUR SIGN."*
10. **FREEBIE CTA.** Two-word header = the instruction; show the freebie; frame as gift. — *"Comment Template — AND I'LL SEND YOU MY EXACT COLD-SUBSCRIBER CLEANING AUTOMATION... (FREE!)"*

## SPINE B — OFFER (9–11 slides)
*Synthesized from 02-06, 02-19, 02-24, 03-01, 03-03. Two sub-modes: DATA/DEMO (03-03, 02-06) and STORY/POV (02-24, 02-19). Shared skeleton:*

1. **COVER — provocation or searchable promise.** Bold claim, personal swap story, provocative joke, or seasonal countdown. Product name may already ride the footer. — *"Organic Marketing Is Dying" · "How To Make Pinterest Your Sugar ~~Daddy~~ Mama" · "There are 80 Days Until Summer"*
2. **VALIDATE — belief mirror or receipts.** Either mirror the audience's current belief, or open the evidence file immediately. Preview the structure if steps are coming. — *"Most people think to get more sales you just need more eyeballs" · "Reach Is Down (way down)" · "here's my 4 step plan to leverage Pinterest (even if you're starting from scratch)"*
3. **AGITATE / EVIDENCE.** 1–3 slides of pressure lists, data grids, myth-busting. Translate every stat into human consequence. — *"pressure to show up, pressure to go viral, pressure to create more" · "FOR EVERY 10K VIEWS ON AN IG POST ONLY 10 PPL WILL CLICK A LINK"*
4. **VILLAIN / EMOTIONAL VALIDATION (the hinge — no CTA here).** Name the system as the culprit; name the feeling. Warmer register, script font allowed. — *"IT'S CLEAR WE'VE BEEN tricked into making their platforms better" · "It's all very high maintenance... and well, exhausting"*
5. **PERSONAL PIVOT.** First-person turn; "I" arrives with weight. — *"That's why I flipped my marketing model" · "SO I STARTED LOOKING FOR something more low maintenance"*
6. **SOLUTION / METHOD BLOCK (2–4 slides).** Named framework label repeated across slides; benefits with specific numbers; product shown from the inside (real screenshots, features as reader outcomes); testimonials styled as found screenshots; mid-deck seed of the chat word in handwriting. — *"THE SLOW MARKETING PLAN: Pins Last 1600x Longer" · "Been avoiding Notion because you don't know where to start? I've got you." · "This is the strategy that's baked into Back of House"*
7. **CLOSING ARGUMENT / IDENTITY PAYOFF.** Problem restated as diagnosis + the identity mirror saved for engaged readers. — *"If your content disappears the moment you stop posting... that's not a system." · "THIS IS FOR THE 'I WANT MY MARKETING TO RUN IN THE BACKGROUND' GIRLS"*
8. **OFFER REVEAL.** Name the product; annotate the deliverables on a mockup; soft deadline or price anchor if real. — *"meet your business' Back of House ... JOIN US BEFORE FRIDAY" · "IT'S ON SALE RN FOR ~$39~ $19"*
9. **COMMENT-TRIGGER CTA.** Chat word in the carousel's own metaphor language; collaborative framing. — *"comment yeschef AND LET'S MAKE IT HAPPEN!" · "comment 'viral' to rsvp" · "Comment Clock ... CLOCK IN NOW, & LET FUTURE YOU COLLECT LATER"*
10. **(Optional) BRAND CLOSE — no CTA.** Sign-off or emotional bookend that trusts the content; drives resonance comments. — *"xx Alyssa" · "READY TO GET YOUR NEXT CLIENTS FROM PINTEREST?" + "i've got your back"*

## SPINE C — FOR THE GIRLS (9–11 slides, or 1 image)
*Synthesized from 01-22, 02-14, 03-07, 03-10. Persona: the reader's own inner voice, steadied.*

1. **COVER — provocation or artifact.** A guilt-loaded phrase owned proudly, or a seasonal format declaration, inside ONE fixed visual container (cup, doily, cheque) that will repeat on every slide.
