Her voice rules used as the filter for every pattern below (all from [[Hermes/craft/captions-carousels]]):
- Under 7 words on covers
- Payoff mandatory (fix / threat / confession / series stake / joke)
- No reframe shape on covers
- No second-person "you/your" on covers
- Period or nothing, never a question mark
- Never recycle a documented winner's exact construction
- Weird beats formula — a coined concept beats a pattern-matched winner

## (a) Patterns She Already Runs

Cited from her own 7-formula system in [[Hermes/craft/captions-carousels]]:

1. **The Paradox** — taboo behavior + legitimizing reframe, period close ("Wasting Time Is Part of My Process.") — overlaps with create-viral-content's "Before/After Compression" and Blotato's "Contrarian" categories, but hers ends flat, never as a reframe-resolution (the reframe lives on interior slides, not covers).
2. **Bold Outcome Promise** — time-anchored claim, no stacking "every/all" ("How To Get All Your Clients From Pinterest This Year") — direct overlap with Blacktwist's "Bold Claim" and Blotato's "Speed/Effortless How-To" category, but hers avoids the generic-superlative version those libraries lean on.
3. **Personal Testimony** — "Why I Swapped X For Y," consequence kicker never concession — overlaps with Blacktwist's "Story Opener" and Blotato's "Transformation/Before-After," but she requires first-person + "and" not "but."
4. **Trend Alarm (3-punch)** — fear+validation → symptom → cliffhanger, hinge word "but" — closest external cousin is create-viral-content's "Prediction + Stakes" pattern ("I think X is the [year] [category] that [outcome]") and Blotato's Urgency category, but hers is structurally a 3-beat sequence, not a single line.
5. **Reframe Question** — "What's Actually Shifting in 2026 (And What To Do Instead)" — overlaps with Blacktwist's "Question" and title-formulas' "The Myth Buster" ("Is X actually Y?"), but hers rides a parenthetical payoff on the SAME cover, which none of the external libraries specify as a rule.
6. **Audience Identity** — "The Pinterest Marketing Plan for Beginners" — direct overlap with Blacktwist's "Best patterns: Audience Identity" concept and Blotato's "Audience Callout" category (#57-63), same mechanic (name the exact reader), same caution (don't beginner-splain in body).
7. **Visual Joke/Strikethrough** — screenshot-worthy before copy is read, once-per-cycle-max — no direct external equivalent found in any of the four source libraries; this appears to be a genuinely distinctive mechanic in her corpus (closest cousin is create-viral-content's thumbnail-humor guidance, but that's a video-thumbnail device, not a text/typography device).

**Convergence finding:** every external hook library independently reinvents something close to her existing 7 formulas. None of the four sources contain a hook FORM she doesn't already have a version of. The value in these repos is not new hook shapes — it's testing discipline and taxonomy, covered in (b) below.

## (b) Genuinely New Mechanics Compatible With Her Voice Rules

These are process/testing mechanics, not hook text, filtered to what survives her cover rules unmodified:

1. **The first-3-words test** (Blotato viral-hooks, also implicit in Blacktwist) — read the first 3 words alone, ask if they create curiosity/surprise/pull independent of the rest of the sentence. This is compatible because it's a filter, not a shape — apply it to any of her 7 formulas as a pre-Maitre-d' triage. Example: her real cover "Organic Marketing Is Dying" passes this test cleanly ("Organic Marketing Is" already creates alarm before the sentence resolves).
2. **"Could this stand alone as a tweet?" as a standalone-strength check** (Blacktwist hook-writer, Blotato viral-hooks) — a portable test for whether a cover hook needs the rest of the deck to make sense, or earns attention on its own. Compatible with the payoff-mandatory rule since a strong standalone line usually already has an implicit payoff.
3. **The keyword-density split: algorithmic-reach words vs. emotional-appeal words are different words in the same hook** (TikTok hook-forensics method) — a genuinely new analytical lens. Her hooks already do this instinctively ("chat-written," "champagne goggles worn off" = emotional-appeal register; "Pinterest," "marketing," "2026" = reach register) but she doesn't currently separate the two layers when reviewing a draft cover. Worth a Maitre d' checklist line: does this cover have BOTH a searchable/topical word and a coined/emotional word?
4. **"Vary hook category across a batch"** (Blotato viral-hooks, content-repurposer-sms) — an explicit anti-formula rule at the BATCH level, not just the per-hook level. Compatible with — and reinforces from a new angle — her existing "never recycle a documented winner" rule. If Test Kitchen generates 5 concepts, this adds: don't let all 5 covers share one formula (5 Personal Testimonies) even if none of them individually copies a past winner.
5. **Negative superlatives outperform positive by a measurable margin (Outbrain, cited in create-viral-content)** — NOT a license to adopt "worst/never/mistakes" clickbait framing (that violates her taste), but a data point that supports her existing use of THREAT payoffs ("...Is Dying") over purely aspirational ones. Useful as evidence backing an existing choice, not a new mechanic to add.
6. **The "So What" bridge test applied to a hook specifically** (marketingskills copywriting/copy-editing) — after drafting a hook, ask "so what?" once. If the cover doesn't imply an answer is coming, it's not a hook, it's a label. Compatible because it's a diagnostic question, not a template.
7. **Eyebrow-as-series-stake vs. eyebrow-as-filing-slug** (already partly hers, but the create-viral-content title-formulas category "The Year Call" — "[Concept] is the [year] [category] that [outcome]" — sharpens WHY a dated/series eyebrow works: it creates a timeframe + FOMO + self-identification in one line). Useful as a rationale to cite when explaining to Alyssa why an eyebrow needs a stake, not just a label.
8. **Odd/small numbers test better than round or large ones** (Blotato Receipt category note: "Odd numbers and small numbers test best," create-viral-content's "5,781 saves, not 6k" instinct already matches this) — confirms, doesn't add to, her existing "round numbers where the real data has odd ones" ban. Cite as corroborating evidence, not a new rule.

## (c) Patterns to BAN (engagement-bait, platform-wrong, or voice-violating)

| Pattern (source) | Why banned |
|---|---|
| **Second-person cover hooks** — Blacktwist's "Empathy" pattern ("If you're struggling to stay consistent..."), Blotato's Question Hook category (#64-71: "Are you still...", "Have you ever felt..."), title-formulas' "How to X even if [objection]" | Direct violation of her Rule 4 — no you/your on covers. These belong in body copy or CTAs only, never the cover. |
| **Reframe-shaped covers** — Blotato's "Reframe" category isn't named as such but several entries are reframes-in-one-breath ("Your 9-5 isn't killing your dreams. Wasting your 5-9 is." from Blotato post-writer's hook patterns), title-formulas' "The Wrong Approach" ("X is wrong — here's why") | Violates Rule 3 — a reframe resolves itself in the same breath, leaving nothing to swipe for. Her reframes live on interior/closing slides only. |
| **Generic curiosity-gap engagement bait** — Blotato's "Curiosity Gap" category #34-42 ("Here's what nobody tells you about X," "I can't believe no one told me this"), Blacktwist's plain "Question" pattern with no specific stakes | These are the exact shape of "slop leak" content ANTI-AI.md and TASTE.md exist to catch — vague curiosity with no un-inventable detail, would fail the texture floor and the "label + description" tell instantly. |
| **"Change my mind" / "What do you think?" / explicit engagement-bait CTAs treated as hooks** — create-viral-content explicitly bans these too (rare point of full agreement across sources) | Already banned in her system implicitly via the CTA rules in [[Hermes/craft/captions-carousels]] (comment-word CTAs framed as gifts, never commands) — external sources confirm this is bad practice everywhere, not just for her. |
| **Negative-superlative clickbait framing** ("X mistakes destroying your Y," "Warning: X is killing your Y" — Blotato Negative Frame category #19-27, title-formulas' "The Warning") | The MECHANIC (threat payoff) is fine and she already uses it ("...Is Dying"); the SPECIFIC SHAPE (numbered mistake-list framed as a countdown to failure) is a generic listicle-fear hybrid that reads as bought-template, not her voice. Ban the shape, keep the underlying threat-payoff logic. |
| **TikTok-style "wait for it" / "POV:" spoken-hook conventions** (Blacktwist TikTok guidance, TikTok hook-forensics corpus) | Platform-wrong — these are video-audio hook conventions for a platform/format (talking-head video) Alyssa doesn't run. Zero applicability to static carousels or captions. |
| **Pinterest-style pure-SEO keyword-stuffed titles** ("Small Kitchen Organization Ideas for Renters (No Drilling Required)" — Blacktwist Pinterest guidance) | Platform-wrong for her current primary platform (Instagram-first carousels); would also violate her "coined concept beats pattern-matched winner" rule since SEO-title formulas are the definition of pattern-matched. |
| **Astrological-authority / bold-claim hybrid hooks and "call and response" hooks sampled from TikTok corpus** (`hook-astrological-authority-bold-claim.md`, `hook-call-and-response.md`) | Niche-wrong (astrology, relationship-drama, Jarvis/AI-narration content) — sampled TikTok patterns skew heavily toward food, relationship-repair, and entertainment niches with non-English original-language content; near-zero transferability to a coaching/business audience even before voice-rule filtering. |
| **"Most people think X, they're wrong" reverse-hook when used WITHOUT a real wedge** (Blotato post-writer pattern #10, marketingskills' "Unpopular opinion:" framing) | The mechanic itself is fine (she runs something similar in her audience-belief-mirror device, explicitly protected in the Round-1 root-cause notes as a real construction), but the external versions invite genericized contrarianism with no actual wedge behind it — only usable when there's a real, source-backed opinion, never as a template fill-in. |

## Practical: Pitch-Station Checklist — 5-8 New Hook Moves (Alyssa-Safe Rewrites)

For Test Kitchen to run BEFORE Maitre d', each rewritten to pass her cover rules (≤7 words, no reframe, no second-person, payoff mandatory, period-or-nothing):

1. **First-3-words triage** — Before pitching a cover, isolate the first 3 words and ask: does this alone create alarm, curiosity, or a claim? 
   *Fail example:* "Here's what I learned" → *Pass rewrite:* "I burned the funnel." (confession payoff, first-person, under 7 words)

2. **Dual-layer keyword check** — Every cover needs one reach-word (topical/searchable) and one coined/emotional word. 
   *Generic:* "Marketing Trends for 2026" → *Alyssa-safe:* "The Tiny List Effect." (coined term carries the emotional layer; "list" + implied marketing context carries reach)

3. **Batch-variety gate** — If Test Kitchen pitches 5 concepts, no more than 2 may share a hook formula (Paradox, Bold Outcome, Personal Testimony, Trend Alarm, Reframe Question, Audience Identity, Visual Joke). 
   *Enforcement note:* flag at pitch stage, before Mean Alyssa scoring, so batch homogeneity doesn't reach the gate.

4. **Threat-payoff without the countdown-listicle shape** — Use the fix/threat/confession/series/joke payoff categories, never a numbered "X mistakes" framing. 
   *Generic:* "5 Content Mistakes Killing Your Reach" → *Alyssa-safe:* "Organic Marketing Is Dying." (threat payoff, no number, no listicle shape, matches her real Mar 3 winner)

5. **Series-stake eyebrow, not filing-slug** — When building a multi-part concept, the eyebrow must qualify the audience or name a stake, never a slug. 
   *Generic:* "content ideas, pt. 2" → *Alyssa-safe:* "FOR SOLO SERVICE PROVIDERS / Part Two"

6. **So-what bridge test on the cover alone** — After drafting, ask "so what?" once, out loud. If nothing answers, it's a label, not a hook. 
   *Fail:* "New Year, New Marketing" (states a fact, no implied answer) → *Alyssa-safe rewrite:* "I Killed My Content Calendar." (implies a coming explanation, first-person, period close)

7. **Standalone-tweet test, adapted to her period-close rule** — Could this line, alone, with no deck behind it, land as a complete thought? If yes, it's strong enough for a cover. 
   *Weak:* "What's Actually Shifting in Marketing" (needs the parenthetical to land) → note: her real version of this ("...And What To Do Instead") already fixes this by riding the payoff on the same cover — the test confirms her existing construction rather than replacing it.

8. **Odd/small-number discipline on any cover using a number** — Never round for cover copy; use the real odd number even if it's less clean. 
   *Generic:* "Nearly 6,000 People Saved This" → *Alyssa-safe:* "5,781 People Saved This." (matches her existing anti-round-number rule, reinforced by external Receipt-category evidence that specific numbers outperform round ones)
