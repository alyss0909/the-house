Generated 2026-07-06 by Larry/Pax.

Scope: last 9 months only, from 2025-10-06 onward. This is intentionally narrower than the broad content/social inventory. It excludes old 2024 material and pure external repo hook dumps so the useful Content OS signal can surface.

## What This Map Is For

Use this when building or refining the Content OS. It gathers the notes that answer:

- What does Alyssa want content to do?
- What should the content machine make repeatedly?
- What should be automated, delegated, or turned into skills?
- What visual/social examples has Alyssa saved because they feel right?
- Which external tools/repos are references, not source?

Source boundary: journal notes are Alyssa source. Second Brain and Deliverables files are derived maps or team work. External repos/tools are reference material only.

## Core Thesis That Surfaced

The Content OS is not mainly a hook generator. It is a system for turning Alyssa's best thinking into durable, funnel-forward content, especially through email, Pinterest, carousels, and recurring social formats.

The strongest local pattern is:

1. Alyssa has an anchor idea or email.
2. The system identifies the funnel job.
3. The idea becomes a carousel, pin layer, caption, email variant, or recurring post format.
4. The piece points back to a freebie, low-ticket offer, or BOH teaching pathway.
5. Alyssa reviews taste/strategy, not every mechanical transformation.

## What This Means For Content OS

### Keep

- Email and Pinterest as the strategic base.
- Instagram carousels as a repurposing/output lane, not the whole strategy.
- Repeatable formats: guest check, calendar dates, cup quotes, slow marketing/Pinterest, For The Girls.
- One anchor idea/email generating multiple outputs.
- Outlier-content analysis before batch creation.
- Skill design with examples, evals, memory, and clean trigger rules.
- AI as hands, Alyssa as head/heart.
- Visual direction as part of strategy, not decoration.

### Avoid

- Old 2024/early 2025 notes unless a specific gap requires them.
- Treating external repo hooks as Alyssa source.
- Letting hook libraries drown out Alyssa's own operating notes.
- Installing tools just because the concept is interesting.
- Building for LinkedIn/X/video when Alyssa's live pattern is email -> carousel -> Pinterest/email ecosystem.
