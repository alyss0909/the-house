## STEP 0 — Outlier Analysis (her own saved technique)

Looking at the top-9 in `PKM/Second Brain/examples/Carousels/captions.md`, the over-performers break their own archetype's expected metric in a specific, learnable way:

1. **01-20 ("What's Actually Shifting")** should have been a save-magnet like 01-15 (same trend-report archetype, same week) but instead it won on *views + follows* (132,758 views, 856 follows — the dataset's best raw reach) while saves came in lower than 01-15's. The break: it dropped the neutral "let's look at data" framing for a first-person emotional confession mid-caption — "so YOU can get back to being a silly little creative" — which pulled non-followers in on identity, not utility. A trend post over-performing on reach instead of saves means the COVER did algorithm work the body copy didn't need to do.
2. **02-19 ("Sugar Mama")** is filed as a Pinterest offer post but wins on *cold shares* (3,038, the dataset's #1) — a metric usually reserved for provocation/manifesto content, not product-reveal posts. The break: the visual joke (strikethrough "Daddy"→"Mama") made it screenshot-worthy before anyone read a word of the pitch, so the SHARE happened pre-comprehension. A product post outperforming as a share-magnet means the joke did the distribution work the offer normally has to earn.
3. **01-22 ("Wasting Time for the Girls")** has literally no CTA word and still moved 397 shares against only 93 follows and 21 comments — an FTG post that should be a quiet corner of the grid instead posted the dataset's cleanest share:follow ratio. The break: it's structured as a repeated permission litany ("I don't need / I can still / I'm giving myself"), which reads as something the follower forwards to a specific friend, not something they engage with themselves.
4. **Pattern across all three:** the over-performing metric always came from a DIFFERENT slide than the one doing the "primary job." 01-20's cover did the reach; 02-19's slide 1 visual did the share; 01-22's repeated first-person spine did the share without a CTA at all. The lesson for this batch: don't just hit the expected archetype→metric pairing — build one slide or line into each pitch that could plausibly over-perform its own lane, and name which slide that is in the concept.
