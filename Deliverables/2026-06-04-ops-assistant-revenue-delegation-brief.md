# Ops Assistant Revenue Delegation Brief

## Executive summary

For Alyssa's business, Camila's highest-leverage weekly work should be the operational layer that turns strategy into money: lead follow-up, launch execution, content repurposing, email/Kit hygiene, client/student support, project tracking, and performance reporting.

The job is not "random admin." The job is to protect Alyssa's CEO/creator time, keep revenue assets moving, and make sure no sales, launch, client-success, or content opportunity quietly goes stale.

## Key findings

1. **Lead response and follow-up should be operationalized. Confidence: High.** Harvard Business Review's lead-response research found many companies respond too slowly to online inquiries; for Alyssa, Camila should triage support/inquiries, flag high-intent leads, and make sure every warm lead gets a timely human next step.

2. **Executive/admin support is revenue-relevant when it protects strategic time. Confidence: High.** McKinsey reports that effective time allocators receive strong scheduling/allocation support, and gives the example of an assistant making sure the CEO's calendar reflects strategic priorities.

3. **Project/launch management is the bridge between strategy and revenue. Confidence: Medium-High.** Asana's project-management guidance emphasizes owners, deadlines, resources, risk tracking, and reporting. Course-launch operators serving coaches/consultants describe launch management as coordination of timelines, funnels, emails, bonuses, webinars, sales tracking, and team sync.

4. **Kit/email operations are a direct revenue lane, not back-office work. Confidence: Medium.** Kit positions creator email automation around hands-off revenue, personalized automations, segmentation, launches, upsells, re-engagement, and abandoned-cart reminders. Vendor ROI claims should be treated cautiously, but the strategic pattern is consistent with creator/course business practice.

5. **Client/student success protects and expands revenue. Confidence: Medium.** Bain's loyalty research shows retention can have outsized profit impact. For Alyssa, Camila's support of BOH students and 1:1 clients should focus on early wins, reduced friction, testimonial capture, referral cues, renewal/upsell signals, and fewer dropped balls.

## Recommended ownership lanes for Camila

- Revenue follow-up and inbox triage
- Weekly project command center
- Launch/campaign execution tracker
- Kit/email/list hygiene and scheduling
- Content repurposing and designer handoff
- Client/student success follow-up
- Monthly marketing performance report
- Internal documentation/course material upkeep
- Alyssa calendar/priority protection
