## What Alyssa Corrected

### 1. The plan was too hire-first

The original plan asked Alyssa to approve hires before the team had clearly shown:

- who does what,
- what each person reads,
- what each person produces,
- what "good" means,
- how Mean Alyssa tries to kill work at every rung,

- where Blotato/social-repo mechanics live,

**Correction:** build the job, sources, SOP, gate, and handoff first. Then Nolan hires onto that.

### 2. Trend Scout must be concrete

Alyssa asked for exact operational detail, not "trend vibes."

Current truth:

- There is a **monthly Trend Scout scrape Claude Routine** visible in the Claude app.
- It is not a Codex automation or vault script.
- It should not be rebuilt from scratch.
- It does need to be documented in the vault and connected to WS-011.
- A separate **weekly Trend Scout brief** still needs to exist or be confirmed.

Trend Scout's job is not "find trends" generically. It should deliver:

- what top-performing trusted accounts are doing now,
- winning covers / hooks / first slides,
- slide-structure patterns,
- stale or overused mechanics,
- performance signals,
- direct Mean Alyssa ammo,
- and opportunity notes for Alyssa's offers.

### 4. Concept pitch needs two layers

Alyssa liked the creative-brief research summary:

> audience, objective, key insight, single message, desired behavior, deliverable, and approval path; plus business goals, audience data, trends, competitors, and measurable behavior.

But she also clarified the concept can be Alyssa-readable and simple:

> "a post about cold subscribers and the email to send to fix your open rate in 3 days" - make it funny because it breaks the belief that you need more leads or views; give them something they can do right now to heat up their list; CTA List Revival freebie.

**Correction:** the backend concept brief can carry the formal fields, but Alyssa sees the plain concept.

### 5. Substance vs Writer is the key split

This is the biggest correction.

Substance is not "the argument" in a vague way. Substance is:

> find and organize Alyssa's real teaching so the post is actually worth something.

Substance should answer:

- What does Alyssa actually teach about this?
- Where does she teach it?
- What module, transcript, template, example, offer file, journal note, or framework backs it?
- What is Alyssa's philosophy here?
- What is Alyssa NOT saying?
- What does the reader want?
- Which part of BOH or another offer does this tease?
- What fact, example, template, or mechanism backs point 2?
- Would someone pay $5 for this post if it were sold as a solution?
- Would this post make someone want the linked offer?

Writer's job is different:

- take the approved concept + hook + Substance Dossier,
- make it work as carousel copy,
- make it sound like Alyssa,
- use carousel/copywriting frameworks to shape the delivery,
- but **not** rediscover the real teaching from scratch.

### 6. Cassius cannot be retested under the old standard

Alyssa's critique to preserve:

> the example from Cassius was not persuasive, not based on what Alyssa teaches, not buyer-psychology-aware, not following carousel best practices, not following direct response, not interesting, not useful, not like Alyssa's carousels, and not like top-performing trend examples.

Root issue Alyssa named:

> Cassius did not metabolize Alyssa's thoughts. He was trying to get the job done.

**Correction:** if Cassius is re-tested, he is re-tested on the new Substance Dossier standard, not on the old "make an arc" standard.

## Proposed SOPs / Internal Skills

Next available SOP number at time of writing: SOP-033.

### SOP-033 - Run Trend Scout Brief

**Default owner:** Mack for automation wiring; Trend Specialist/Suki for interpretation.

Inputs:

- Claude Routine monthly scrape output.
- `Team Inbox/pitches/trusted-sources.md`
- `Team Inbox/pitches/swipe/YYYY-MM-swipe.md`
- weekly macro platform sources listed in trusted-sources.
- recent Alyssa grid / covers if relevant.

Output:

- `Team Inbox/pitches/trend-briefs/YYYY-Www-trend-brief.md`

Must include:

- what is working now,
- what is stale,
- top cover mechanics,
- top slide-structure patterns,
- examples from trusted accounts,
- opportunity notes for Alyssa,
- Mean Alyssa ammo,
- any scrape gaps.

Pass:

- Suki and Concept can use it without re-scraping or guessing.

Fail:

- generic "trends" without real examples, or anything copied as voice.

### SOP-034 - Write Concept Pitch Brief

**Default owner:** Concept Specialist / Suki.

Inputs:

- Weekly Trend Scout Brief.
- Suki Weekly Strategy Brief.
- weekly theme / slots / offers.
- `Hermes/FUNNEL.md`
- `Hermes/MAP.md` section 3.
- `Hermes/BUSINESS.md` / offer files as needed.
- `Team Inbox/pitches/taste-ledger.md`
- Alyssa source files for the topic.

Output:

- 10+ concept candidates internally.
- Survivors shown to Alyssa as plain-language concepts.
- Goal: 3 Mean-Alyssa-surviving concepts per M/W/F slot before Alyssa chooses.

Backend concept fields:

- audience moment,
- objective,
- key insight,
- single message,
- desired behavior,
- offer/freebie tie,
- trend signal,
- Alyssa teaching/source clue,
- why it breaks a belief,
- approval path.

Alyssa-facing shape:

- "A post about [reader problem] and [specific solution/angle], funny/sharp because [belief it breaks], gives them [do-right-now payoff], CTA [freebie/offer]."

Pass:

- actual idea, not topic; stranger understands it; offer fit is real; source path exists.

Fail:

- "email marketing mistakes" style topic, generic template, top-9 regurgitation, unsupported promise.

### SOP-035 - Run Hook Bench

**Default owner:** Hook Specialist / Suki.

Inputs:

- Alyssa-approved concept.
- Weekly Trend Scout Brief.
- current swipe covers.
- `PKM/Second Brain/analysis/carousel-hook-forensics.md`
- `Deliverables/.../repo-alignment-audit.md`
- Blotato/social repo mechanics as adapted process only.

Use:

- first-3-words test,
- hook = 50% score,
- batch variety,
- payoff required,
- no self-resolving reframe,
- one reach word + one emotional/coined word,
- "so what?" test.

Output:

- many hook options internally.
- 3 Mean-Alyssa-surviving hook options per approved concept for Alyssa to choose.

Pass:

- cover line has a real reason to swipe and matches the approved concept's teaching.

Fail:

- sounds like a hook library, resolves itself, generic guru phrasing, no payoff, wrong trend signal.

### SOP-036 - Build Substance Dossier

**Default owner:** Substance Specialist / Suki.

Inputs:

- Alyssa-approved concept.
- Alyssa-approved hook.
- Suki brief.
- exact Alyssa source files.
- BOH/module/transcript/template/offer files as applicable.
- `Hermes/FUNNEL.md`
- Alyssa carousel forensics.
- real data sources if claims/numbers appear.

Output:

- Substance Dossier, not final copy.

Dossier must include:

- what Alyssa actually teaches,
- where she teaches it,
- her philosophy / not-philosophy,
- reader desire and objection,
- source quotes or source pointers,
- useful examples/templates/facts,
- what the post gives away,
- what the post teases about the offer,
- $5 test,
- offer-desire test,
- recommended slide teaching sequence.

Pass:

- someone would pay $5 for the post as a useful solution, or want the linked offer because the post teased something genuinely valuable.

Fail:

- "feels like Alyssa would teach this" without actual sources; generic advice; no reader payoff; no offer desire; no proof.

### SOP-037 - Run Mean Alyssa Content Gate

**Default owner:** Suki / Larry; reusable by every content rung.

Inputs vary by rung.

Must use:

- taste ledger,
- trend brief,
- Alyssa real-source proof,
- Blotato/social repo review mechanics,
- line-by-line kill format,
- subtract-points scoring where useful,
- control calibration when judging voice/AI-likeness,
- "what would Alyssa immediately call bullshit on?"

Output:

- killed list with reasons,
- survivors,
- exact fix instructions where repair is allowed,
- "send to Alyssa" only after gate passes.

Pass:

- nothing reaches Alyssa that the team could have killed or answered internally.

Fail:

- Alyssa has to correct role confusion, source gaps, generic content, or fake confidence.
