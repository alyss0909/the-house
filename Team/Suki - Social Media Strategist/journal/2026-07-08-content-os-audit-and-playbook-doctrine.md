# What I learned activating on the Content OS

**Durable insight for the next Suki instance — read before any Content OS work.**

## Deliver plain, not dense
My first audit report was rejected verbatim: *"the most confusing thing ive ever read."* Alyssa reacts fast to plain talk and gets lost in structure — tiers, §-refs, rung-numbers, file paths in prose. She is a smart founder who does not live inside this system's naming. **Talk to her like a friend over coffee:** what's working (2–3 things), what's broken and why it matters to *her* business, and the ONE decision you need — with your honest rec. Save the detailed structure for the build docs, not the conversation. Every maker output she sees should pass the plain-English test.

## The spine was already good — my job is to sharpen, not re-pour
The pitch loop, LOOK-before-writing, approve-at-the-outline, the taste ledger, map-not-digest — all sound and paid for in rejections. The real leaks were: top-of-funnel job-collapse (the writer secretly doing idea + hook, the same failure that caused the hollow-arc rejection), and the craft skills sitting unloaded by any maker. Fix those, don't rebuild the foundation.

## Her real target is comments, not shares
Everything optimizes toward a keyword comment → ManyChat → list/coupon → sale. Shares are a lever that widens the funnel, never the goal. Trend reports are the one save-first exception. Build every ranking rule (great hook, great last slide, the gate's ammo) toward the comment, or you bake in the wrong bar.

## Bring her fresh stimulus, filtered
She explicitly does NOT want her hooks to inbreed on her own top 9. Keep a swipe file of real working-now covers as **mechanic** input (never copied text), rebuilt in her voice — plus a widened set of her own winners. Fresh input, her filter, always.

## She gates hiring and system wiring
Nothing gets researched or hired until she reviews the plan in detail. Spec the gap; let HR (Nolan) hire. Never collapse spec + hire into one seat — that was the old bottleneck.
