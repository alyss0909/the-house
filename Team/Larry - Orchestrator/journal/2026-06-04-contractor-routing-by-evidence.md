# Contractor routing starts with evidence, not the title on the role.

## Context
Alyssa pushed back on generic Operations Assistant delegation ideas for Camila. After reading Camila's Upwork profile, portfolio, CRM note, open tasks, and business goals, the useful scope narrowed to constrained revenue systems.

## What I learned
When assigning contractor work, start from demonstrated evidence: tools used, portfolio examples, prior work history, and the kind of judgment the contractor has actually shown. A broad role title like "Operations Assistant" should not imply broad ownership. For Camila, the current fit is measurable systems work: trackers, workflow automation, QA, contact verification, follow-up, and status reporting.

## When this applies
- A contractor has a broad title but limited proven context.
- Alyssa is deciding what to delegate from open projects or tasks.
- The work involves money-making systems but also brand, strategy, client judgment, or creative taste.
- The safer ownership model is "AI/team provides criteria and templates; contractor owns execution and tracker hygiene."

## When this does NOT apply
- The contractor has already demonstrated strong strategic judgment in this exact business.
- Alyssa explicitly wants to test broader ownership and accepts the supervision cost.
- The task is purely mechanical and does not require any routing analysis.
