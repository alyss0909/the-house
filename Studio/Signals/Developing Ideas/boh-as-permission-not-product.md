# BOH as Permission, Not Product

## What keeps showing up

A subtle but significant reframe keeps surfacing in how Alyssa talks about BOH: it's not primarily a course, it's permission. Permission to stop chasing the algorithm. Permission to be the niche obsessive. Permission to build the thing that runs while you're doing what you actually love. The product mechanics (Pinterest + email + automation) are the how. But the emotional payload — what people are actually buying — is the freedom to opt out of the performance hamster wheel.

This connects directly to the `middle-ground-entrepreneur` signal but is specifically about how BOH gets *positioned* and *sold*, not just what Alyssa believes.
