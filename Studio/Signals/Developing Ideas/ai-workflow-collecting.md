## What I think you're actually saying

> I think the collecting itself is the message. You saved eleven AI workflow resources in four days, and when the team actually sits down with one of these, the answer has been "we already built this" three times in a row. So the saves are probably not shopping. They look more like reassurance checks: is my system keeping up, is there something out there I am missing. If that is right, the useful move is not to process the backlog one by one. It is to give the saves a standing lane: each one gets a fast two-line verdict against the system we already have (does it add a capability the vault lacks, yes or no), and anything that keeps surviving that check becomes a build task. That would turn a growing pile into a heartbeat check on the system, and it would also quietly prove or disprove the "our system already beats the external tools" pattern with real data. There may also be teaching material in here later: your students feel this exact same firehose, and "how to check a shiny AI tool against the back of house you already have" sounds like a BOH lesson.
