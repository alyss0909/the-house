**Mandatory load before writing any Simmer:** [[Studio/Analysis/Frameworks/the-postmaster-bot]] + [[Studio/Analysis/Frameworks/bingeable-newsletter-knowledge-file]] (HER OWN bot + knowledge file for this exact format, handed over 2026-07-05: "learn it WELL") + all 3 real simmers.

The Simmer = the weekly nurture email. HER canonical 5-part framework (The Postmaster, verbatim hers):

| Part | Length | Job | Common mistake (her list) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hook (WRITTEN LAST) | 1-2 lines | open a loop | generic curiosity-bait |
| Story | 2-4 short paragraphs | pull them in - recent, specific, REAL | lacks specific detail |
| Takeaway | **1-3 sentences MAX** | make the point ONCE | over-explained, preachy |
| Value | 3-5 items or short paragraphs | earn their open (retention insurance) | surface-level tips |
| Offer (optional) | 1-2 lines, reply-word | find ready buyers, right-time not all-time | sales-y pivot |
| Sign-off | 1 line | leave them smiling | "warm regards" |

**Metaphor callbacks — her ruling 2026-07-05 (REVERTS the old "stays in the STORY" rule, which came from our analysis, not her source):** "puns are totally fine... I pun tag it, I use a little wink... It's when you're treating the reader like a child who needs the metaphor extended instead of layering on top of it, being silly, playful, fun, coming at it from a new angle." → Layer / callback / pun-tag / wink / new-angle after the takeaway = HERS ("two ways to layer (see what i did there)", "(ok last outfit pun)"). Heavy-handed child-splaining extension of the allegory mid-argument = AI, still banned. Teaching sentences stay literal; callbacks do texture work.
**Metaphor role-consistency check (the cupcake lesson, her verbatim 2026-07-05):** the same image cannot be the SOLUTION in the story and the VILLAIN in the takeaway ("the you get what you get... solved the problem [in the story]. But in the Zoom note and the takeaway... is actually the villain. So the metaphor kind of like lost its way."). Verify the image plays one role end to end before shipping.
Offer rotation: same offer, different note each time (feature / client result / fresh angle / bonus) - a chef describing tonight's special.

## The shape

1. **Cold open mid-scene, diary register, zero preamble:** "K, so I have this problem, [NAME]." / "My daughter HATES getting her hair washed. like, she goes full CAT IN WATER MODE." / "Did i ever tell you about the time I almost quit my business because of delta."
2. **Stay in the scene** — 2+ paragraphs of story with asides and specificity before any business content. The mess is the charm: "It was Strategic. Inspired. Beautiful. (Deranged probably, but BEAUTIFUL)."
3. **Name the pivot out loud** — the discovered parallel, never a concept-label: "I couldn't help but see the parallels." Not "Marketers call this X."
4. **One lesson, 2-3 tactics max**, short paragraphs, bold as skim path.
5. **Right-time offer mechanic** (her invention — use it): "That's exactly why I created [offer]. Right now, anyone who joins gets [specific thing]... just comment/reply '[word]' and I'll send everything over." Reply-CTAs need genuine scarcity behind them ("we've got one spot left this month").
6. **Sign-off + PS with a fresh angle:** "**PS.** The algorithm can't remember your best post from last week. My Pinterest funnel still brings in leads from 2021."
7. Fixed footer block: 3 cross-sell links + brand tagline.

## Register

Personal-scale metaphors mapped tightly to ONE marketing lesson (bath time → launch amnesia; thrift store → buying windows; stolen napkin idea → documentation). Lowercase comfort, elongated vowels ("waaaant"), self-aware aspirational gaps ("I waaaant to be a Steve Jobs type with one outfit on repeat, but I keep finding myself at the thrift store.. 🙃"). CTAs: 'Reply "SHAMPOO"' / 'Reply "manual"' — the word comes from the story, not the product name, when possible. Email REPLY words are a mailto mechanic she invents fresh per send from the story (SHAMPOO = the bath scene) — free to create. Only IG COMMENT words are Notion product facts that must never be invented. Don't confuse the two.

## Hard guards (2026-07-05, corpus-measured — a draft failing ANY of these does not ship)

1. **Pivot to business by 35% of body** (real corpus: Delta ~1%, bath ~29%, closet ~34% = the ceiling). Extend toward ~50% ONLY if pre-paid by an early signal: subject/preview promising business ("...& pinterest" / "ends in your bank account") or conflict in line one. A static scene earns zero extension - pivot by ~30%.
2. **Scene-stakes test: the seed must score ≥2 of 4** - conflict / loss-or-stakes / absurdity / emotional charge. Bath = 3 (fight + neighbor absurdity + charge). A wanted object = 0/4 and is the same failure as her flagged too-AI email (static wishlist). Wanting is not conflict; the reader needs to watch her FIGHT something.
3. **Metaphor-nuance test, all 3 required**: (a) yields ≥2 distinct mapped insights (bath = reset + compassion-with-boundary + assets-vs-evaporation); (b) carries an internal tension mirroring the business tension (compassion vs boundary); (c) a self-aware exit joke is writable ("okay I swear I'm done with the bath metaphors"). One flat 1:1 mapping = "too obvious and un-nuanced" (her verdict, 2026-07-05).
Full evidence: [[Studio/Analysis/simmer-opening-forensics]].

## Don'ts specific to Simmers

No multi-topic emails. No teaching before the story earns it. No food-metaphor unless the story IS food. Subject line = the story's hook, lowercase-casual ("let me gentle parent your business model."), never the lesson's title.
