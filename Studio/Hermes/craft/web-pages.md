Load with: SOUL, STYLE, TASTE + [[Library/Examples/about-page]] and/or [[Library/Examples/home-page]].

Split out of [[Hermes/craft/sales-pages]] on 2026-07-07 (mechanical reorg, no content change) once two real pages confirmed this register is genuinely distinct: about/home pages are **told stories or marquee positioning**, not the pain→system→offer argument a sales page runs. Don't force the 12-beat sales-page skeleton onto these.

## About-page register (told story)

Full study: [[Hermes/learning/deep-reads/2026-07-07-0512-about-page]].

- **Story skeleton**: reader's-dream question headline → instant kinship ("**Dude, we must be soulmates.**") → hedged confession ("running my business felt, well, *hard* (lol)") → bio list (5 items, resume → absurd, weirdest last) → the announced climax hands the page back to the reader ("BUT MAYBE THE SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT THING... I'm quite literally obsessed with my clients") → scene-setting of the crisis → burn-it-down pivot → rabbit-hole reveal (email + Pinterest) → offer ~600+ words in → moral addressed to the reader ("We went down that rabbit hole so you can go down yours") → aphorism-then-permission couplet → soft positioning close ("Consider this a *soft* place for your ideas to land").
- **Section transitions are caps-colon stage directions**, not headers: "LET ME SET THE SCENE FOR YOU:" / "AT THE END OF THE RABBIT HOLE:" / "MEET: THE SYSTEM OF ALL SYSTEMS" / "THE MORAL OF THIS STORY?" — the narrator announces every beat out loud.
- **Verse pacing ships on web pages**: clause stacks broken across lines ("When I flat out dreaded going back to work, / it was … new information at best, kind of concerning at worst."), four-dot ellipses ("SO…."), spaced mid-sentence ellipsis. Grammar wobbles and idiom misfires ("Au contrair") stay — don't correct them.
- **Credentials only ever mid-sentence in a because-clause** ("Because after almost 10 years... they're the most low-maintenance, sustainable tools for growth I've found.") — evidence for a tool claim, never a bio flex.
- **Hedging asymmetry**: vulnerable confessions triple-hedged ("felt, well, *hard* (lol)"); convictions stated flat, unhedged.
- **The retraction-that-sells**: retract a cliché in a parenthesis, literalize it into service ("(Actually, that's not true. I *did* look back, but only to data mine historical marketing trends so you don't have to)").
- **Positioning one-liners are audience-coded, lowercase-casual**: "The automated sales and marketing department for ceos with better things to do."

## Home-page register (marquee/top-of-funnel)

Full study: [[Hermes/learning/deep-reads/2026-07-07-0615-home-page]].

- **Shorter, section-by-section** — each section is its own device, not one continuous narrative arc.
- **Marquee ticker header**: a caps line repeats with `//` dividers, truncated mid-repeat on the last instance ("HUSTLE CULTURE IS OVERCOOKED // HUSTLE CULTURE IS OVERCOOKED // HUSTLE CULTURE") — a scrolling-ticker illusion, distinct from a single billboard line.
- **Bolded one-line diagnosis** closes a two-line yearning+obstacle setup ("Want clients to discover your work online.. / but don't want your creativity smothered by "consistency"? / **You might need slow marketing**").
- **Two flavors of strikethrough**: the documented "beat" version (cross out, pause, replace) and a tighter **inline** version with no beat, inside one continuous clause ("cozy systems to make ~~running your business~~ your life a hell of a lot easier"). Inline for small low-stakes softening; beat version for confessional reveals.
- **Italics can carry a full sentence** (not just a phrase) for a mood-drop: "You've been hustling to land better leads and make more sales. *But it's becoming next to impossible to keep up.*"
- **Menu-board table as benefit list**: parallel benefit lines as a borderless table — the food metaphor at the layout level, not just diction.
- **The unexplained proper-noun list item**: a bare name, period, zero gloss ("Agatha.") — funnier unexplained.
- **Closing pun resolves the running metaphor**: "Slow marketing for the creatives who'd rather be making dough."
- **Acquisition-verb lexicon**: "STEAL" joins swipe/snag (never grab/download).
