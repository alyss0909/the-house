# craft/email-to-carousel.md — Turning any email into carousel(s)

The transformation that powers the future relay. Derived from her actual pairs, not theory.

## The rule, step by step

1. **Split, don't summarize.** One email yields 1-3 carousels — one per sub-claim. The Predictions Soft Sunday became pt1 (email trends → Cold Subscriber Automation) AND pt2 (organic dying → List Resuscitator). A carousel that tries to hold the whole email is a failed carousel.
2. **Thesis sentence → hook**, recompressed under 15 words, register dropped to lowercase-casual: the email's "organic is dying" argument became "2026 Predictions 🔮 ... 🫠 We've been saying 'ORGANIC IS DYING' for years, but I really think this is the year..."
3. **Teaching middle → slides.** One idea per slide, bold skim path. The caption never repeats the slides — it frames the mood and the stakes.
4. **Personal story compresses to one clause or drops.** What survives email→carousel is the pattern-recognition and the reader-identity mirror ("make their money online but don't want to live online"), not the anecdote. The anecdote belongs to the email.
5. **Email CTA → comment-word CTA**, matched to the SLOT's product, framed as gift: "🎁 Comment LIST for my reengagement emails (free) in notion to re-heat your community, pronto." Chat word from BUSINESS.md or "manual selection needed."
6. **Slot assignment decides the cut**: Monday = the teachable tip inside the email + its freebie. Wednesday = the same idea angled toward the paid product (still educational). Friday = the identity/permission residue of the email ("ur (not)NEW march plan" SS → "if your champagne goggles have worn off..." gentle-reminders FTG carousel, CTA-less, BOH waitlist seed).
7. **Register conversion**: email's "K, so" diary → caption's internet-native lowercase; add "xx/xo"; hashtag block; elongated vowels welcome; scarcity converts to soft deadline ("before friday").
8. **BOH deepening check**: if the idea needs a stronger teaching point, pull ONE concept from the module map (craft/teaching-course-design.md §map) — a single framework beat (Fridge Test, Hawaii principle), never a full lesson. Nuance, not curriculum.

## Placeholder rule

When a chat word is "manual selection needed," do NOT print a guessed placeholder word in the draft ("comment PROMO..."). Write the CTA sentence with an explicit blank: `comment [CHAT WORD — manual selection needed] ...` — a plausible-looking placeholder WILL get shipped by accident.

## Failure modes

Summarizing the whole email (violates rule 1) · keeping email-length sentences on slides · carrying the personal story into the caption · CTA word that doesn't match the slot product · Friday cut with a sales CTA · beginner-fying the teaching to fit slides (cut scope instead).
