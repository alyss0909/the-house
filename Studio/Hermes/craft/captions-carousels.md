## Cover rules (Gate-3 v2 failure autopsy, 2026-07-05 — "literally not even a social media hook")

Verified against all 12+ real covers ranked by performance:
0. **WEIRD BEATS FORMULA (2026-07-05: "'on purpose' is a little too ai.. something more weird or unexpected like 'delete your leads, quick' or 'the tiny list effect'").** A coined concept ("the tiny list effect," "Stoner Math," "chat-written") or an unexpected command with wrong-register punctuation ("delete your leads, quick") beats any pattern-matched winner. **NEVER recycle a construction from a documented winner** - "(on purpose)," "Why I burned down," the strikethrough gag - reusing her hits IS the imitation tell.
1. **Under 7 words (her stated cap, 2026-07-05).** Older winners ran to 10 with a parenthetical payoff; new covers obey 7.
2. **A payoff is MANDATORY on the cover** - one of five: fix ("...And What To Do Instead"), threat ("...Is Dying"), confession ("Why I..."), series stake ("Part One"), or joke (Sugar ~~Daddy~~ Mama). No payoff = not a hook.
3. **NEVER a reframe shape on a cover.** Her reframes ("that's not a system") live on interior implication/closing slides only, after stakes are built. A reframe resolves itself in the same breath, so there's nothing left to swipe for.
4. **Talk TO the reader (her ruling 2026-07-06, overrides the old no-"you" rule: "they need to induce curiosity they need to tlak TO a reader they need to hint at a result or a loss aversion").** Every hook passes three checks: (a) opens a curiosity gap it does not close, (b) addresses the reader directly — second-person "you/your" is now LEGAL on covers, or identity-label/first-person-mirror where it lands harder, (c) hints at a result the reader wants or a loss she's already taking. Her older winners were first-person/identity-label; that's history, not the cap — 2026 hooks lead with the reader's stake.
5. **Period or nothing at the end, never a question mark** (questions belong on CTA/engagement slides).
6. **The eyebrow names a series-with-a-stake or qualifies the audience** ("ONLINE BUSINESS PREDICTIONS / Part One"; "FOR ONLINE SERVICE PROVIDERS, COACHES & CREATIVES") - never a filing slug ("email things, pt. 1" failed exactly here).
7. **Problem before solution, always**: ≥3 slides build the problem before any alternative appears; the offer is earned after ~60% of the deck. A cover that resolves is a closing argument in the wrong seat.

## THE flow (Alyssa's canonical spine, stated verbatim 2026-07-05 — default for every deck)

1. **Cover: big hook, main clause UNDER SEVEN WORDS** (overflow only as a parenthetical that is itself a second hook).
2. **Slide 2: secondary hook that creates an open loop.** The credibility/audience-mirror line IS this slide when it promises a payoff and withholds it ("I've reviewed my notes & industry reports.. here's what I'm seeing" = authority + unanswered question, one slide, one job).
3. **Slide 3: agitate their problem.**
4. **Slides 4-7: value, ONE idea per slide - and value means TEACHING** (2026-07-05: "the 'value' has no teaching USE boh and all products"). Every value slide teaches a real, nuanced piece of her actual material: a BOH micro-lesson (via `Studio/Analysis/boh-teaching-index-*.md`), a named method (Double Yes, Fridge Test, The Batch, Grow/Nourish/Offer), or a real product mechanic. Generic observations are not value. **Per-slide architecture: BIG catchy headline first (the skimmer path - reading only headlines must deliver the argument), then the explaining body text underneath.**
5. **Slide 8: something big - a massive perspective shift or aha moment.** This is a CONTENT reversal (bait-and-switch, villain reveal, "JUST KIDDING", the closing-argument reframe) - NOT the visual interrupt.
6. **Slide 9: CTA.** The full-bleed visual world-shift is a separate device that marks the CTA doorway (S8→S9), never the last-slide content itself.

Archetypes (manifesto/FTG, story/POV) may flex the middle, but a deck that skips the open-loop slide 2, the agitation, or the slide-8 aha does not ship. **For The Girls / manifesto posts have their own dedicated writing skill — load [[craft/for-the-girls-posts|craft/for-the-girls-posts.md]] before writing that lane** (it flexes hardest: permission-slip litany, often no CTA slide, close on an open question). Build the deck to the argument within this spine; don't pad, don't reorder without a real-deck precedent.

**Slide 1 (hook/cover).** One headline, almost never over 10 words. Real covers: "2026 Slow Marketing Trends" (4 words + year) · "What's actually shifting in 2026 (And What To Do Instead)" (10 words) · "Wasting Time Is Part of My Process." (6 words, full stop — a statement, not a confession). A handwritten "Part One" / "Part Two" top-corner label is her series device — it drove Jan 20 to 132,758 views partly on Jan 15's momentum. Multiple cover variants (personality-forward vs. SEO-worded) are normal; that's a repin/Pinterest consideration, not indecision.

**Slide 2-3 (credibility or audience mirror).** Trend/data posts open with the credibility line — quote it, never paraphrase: *"I'VE REVIEWED MY NOTES (& INDUSTRY REPORTS) LAST YEAR AND MARKETING IS SHIFTING QUICKLY IN 2026. HERE'S WHAT I'M SEEING IN MY OWN BUSINESS AND BEHIND THE SCENES WITH CLIENTS WHO MAKE THEIR MONEY ONLINE BUT DON'T WANT TO LIVE ONLINE."* This exact phrase (or near-verbatim) appears in Jan 15, Jan 20, Jan 29, and Mar 3 — it is the brand's 9-word audience-definition and the reason non-follower reach runs 87-93% on trend posts. Manifesto posts (Jan 22) skip credibility entirely and open straight into a first-person permission-slip litany: "I Don't Need a Full Life-Rebrand to Start the Year Off Right." — the repeated "I don't need / I can still / I'm giving myself" construction across slides is a structural spine, not filler.

**Body slides.** One idea per slide, selective bolding as the only skim path — never bullets in body copy (freebie lists in captions are the one place bullets belong, see below). Real body beat: Jan 15 slide 4, *"I'M ALL FOR BEING CRINGE IN PUBLIC BUUUT... THERE'S SOMETHING ABOUT SPOTTING A **CHAT-WRITTEN** CAPTION PASSED OFF AS SOMEONE'S OWN INSIGHT THAT FEELS **UNSETTLING** NOW RIGHT?"* — note the elongated "BUUUT," the tag-question close, and the coined term "chat-written" doing brand-voice work a generic phrase couldn't.

**Pattern-interrupt slide (~75-80% through, never last).** Every analyzed carousel has exactly one slide that visually breaks the deck's own template to announce the CTA zone is coming. Jan 15 slides 1-8 live in cream/vintage-tray world; slides 9-10 switch to a full-bleed burgundy door — "a complete visual world shift... the visual palette change signals: we've arrived somewhere." Jan 22's pattern-interrupt is a wordless slide: the BOH logo fills the cup with only a footer, "WAITLIST OPENING SOON" — after seven slides of "I" statements, an empty cup is the loudest possible break.

**CTA slide (final).** Short headline (Jan 15 slide 10: "Comment Template," 2 words) plus one line stating exactly what they get: *"AND I'LL SEND YOU MY EXACT COLD-SUBSCRIBER CLEANING AUTOMATION AND RE-ENGAGEMENT EMAIL TEMPLATE (FREE!)"* — the only all-caps exclamation in that entire carousel; it earns the emphasis by being the one moment that deserves it. For The Girls posts often have **no CTA slide at all** — Jan 22 closes on an open question ("What Are Your January Reminders?"), and that's correct, not a miss.

## Hooks (from social-hooks.md — the 7 formulas she actually runs)

1. **The Paradox** — taboo behavior + legitimizing reframe, ends on a period: "Wasting Time Is Part of My Process."
2. **Bold Outcome Promise** — "How To Get All Your Clients From Pinterest This Year" (time anchor, no "every"/"all" stacking).
3. **Personal Testimony** — "Why I Swapped Instagram For Pinterest" — kicker is a consequence ("and"), never a concession ("but").
4. **Trend Alarm (3-punch)** — fear+validation → observable symptom → cliffhanger, hinge word "but": Mar 3's "Organic Marketing Is Dying" drove 491 follows, the dataset's best follow-rate, on exactly this shape.
5. **Reframe Question** — "What's Actually Shifting in 2026 (And What To Do Instead)" — "actually" implies the common answer is wrong; the parenthetical payoff rides the same cover.
6. **Audience Identity** — "The Pinterest Marketing Plan for Beginners" — "beginners" is the highest-intent search word, used deliberately, not as a slip into beginner-splaining the body copy.
7. **Visual Joke/Strikethrough** — "How To Make Pinterest Your Sugar ~~Daddy~~ *Mama*" — screenshot-worthy before any copy is read; Feb 19 hit 3,038 shares, the dataset's #1. Use once per content cycle max, reserved for cold-reach plays.

Archetype → metric, from real performance: trend report → saves (Jan 15: 5,781 saves); manifesto/identity mirror → shares with **zero CTA** (Jan 22: 397 shares, no comment word); provocation/visual joke → cold shares (Feb 19: 3,038); data argument + fear hook → follows (Mar 3: 491); story/POV → comments (Feb 24: 251).

## Caption craft (verbatim anchors from the real top-9)

1. **Hook line, under 15 words, casual/emoji-seasoned**: "🚨 Changes you should know! (pt1)" · "2026 Predictions 🔮" · "Pinterest is the internet version of 'where the locals eat'".
2. **2-4 short paragraphs of vibe-framing that do NOT repeat the slides**: "Lately i've been thinking less about how things look and more about how they feel, and it seems like the industry as a whole agrees. 🐌"
3. **Personality clause** before the ask: "so YOU can get back to being a silly little creative 🤎".
4. **Freebie list variant** (the one place bullets belong): "inside the (free) Pin Planner: 📅 DFY content calendar / ✅ pinterest workflow + checklist / 💻 proven content styles Pinterest loves / 📌 content reposition plan / 💅 cute pin templates" — note the list breaks its own parallel structure on the closing line, "…literally anything else," which is the texture floor requirement, not decoration.
5. **Comment-word CTA framed as a gift, never a command**: "🎁 Comment TEMPLATE for my reengagement automation (free) and revive that email community before friday xx." **Comment/chat words are product facts, never invented** — the canonical list (TEMPLATE, LIST, PIN, CEO, CLOCK, YESCHEF, VIRAL...) lives in `BUSINESS.md`; check it before writing any CTA. For The Girls posts frequently carry **no CTA word at all** (Jan 22: "CTA word: none") — that absence is correct.
6. **Sign-off** "xx" or "xo" + 20-30 hashtag block, core set (#alyssacolemanca #slowmarketing #softceo) mandatory on education/offer captions. **Flexible on FTG/seasonal posts** (resolved 2026-07-07): Feb 14 ships 4 hashtags with no core set at all, signed in-persona instead ("love, your secret business admirer") — that's the FTG/seasonal boilerplate slot, not a one-off.

Register: lowercase-native, seasonal hooks welcome ("if your champagne goggles have worn off and your new year goals are side-eyeing february,"), extended metaphor as flirtation works ("The algorithm is giving desperate ex vibes... love, your secret business admirer").

## The texture floor (anti-"label + description") — RAISED after Tournament Round 1 (2026-07-05, 8/9 caught)

**~1 un-inventable detail per VALUE SLIDE, harvested from source** — a real number, a named micro-scenario, a coined term like "chat-written," "champagne goggles worn off." The old floor (≥2 details per deck) was an order of magnitude below her real density and judges caught every deck built to it. Clean parallel "label + description" value slides = the assembled-ness tell that killed candidates instantly; her real decks are lopsided, one item always breaks the pattern. Same rule for freebie-list captions: the parallel bullet structure must break at least once with personality.

## Round-1 root-cause rules (2026-07-05 — every judge converged on these, architecture-independent)

1. **Headline-shape ban**: reframe/negative-parallelism shapes are banned in HEADLINES, covers, and slide headers, not just prose — "Bouncer, Not Welcome Mat" / "Old Way, New Way" as a cover = instant catch. (Carve-out per her ruling: verbatim from her voice/transcripts only.) Her real covers never reframe (corpus-verified, all 14).
2. **Metaphor-layering cadence**: ONE extended-stretch moment per deck max; after it, only layered callbacks/pun-tags/winks (her adjudication-#3 ruling). Running the food/story metaphor across every value slide = metaphor-as-wallpaper (TASTE Disgust) and got every Round-1 candidate flagged.
3. **Per-slide texture floor** (above) is checked slide-by-slide at the gate, not per-deck.
Watch-for: gate-trained judges over-penalize her REAL slide-2 audience-belief-mirror device ("Most people think..." near-verbatim from corpus) — real-source constructions are approved (J1's Round-1 false positive on the real Feb 24 deck).

## Deep-read additions (2026-07-07, full caption-corpus pass — micro-mechanics the first mining missed)

Full study: [[Hermes/learning/deep-reads/2026-07-07-0412-carousel-captions-corpus]]. All quoted from the real top-13.

1. **The whisper caption — length scales inversely with the visual.** When the image/deck carries the joke, the caption goes near-silent and reacts instead of explaining: the Guest Cheque quote post's ENTIRE caption is "guys, i think i figured it out" (5 words, no CTA). Education/offer decks earn the full 5-8-paragraph build. A caption that restates the slides is a tell.
2. **Faux-disclaimer openers**: announce the sin, commit it anyway — "Not to be dramatic... but i think it's time to break up with social" / "Sorry but Feb is the new Jan." The disclaimer IS the joke, distinct from throat-clearing.
3. **Reader-roleplay Q&A + mock-casual reveal**: "What's that? I hear you say / Oh, it's only the all in one notion HQ..." — the caption twin of the transcript buyer roleplay. Also "employee of the month that you are" (solo-biz boss/employee joke).
4. **One-word parenthetical inserts**: "(precious)", "(actually)", "(own)" — and "(free)" is the canonical price tag, lowercase and bracketed in prose; the CTA slide gets the one earned "(FREE!)". Her welded bracket "clear(but cozy) space" ships as-is.
5. **Real urgency = a lowercase weekday inside the gift sentence**, never its own line: "before friday xx" · "(this thursday!!)" · "before the replay (& secret menu bonus) comes down at midnight xx".
6. **Asterisk-italics carry the inner voice** ("*don't talk to me* days"); giggle particles "lol"/"hehe"/"pronto" close clauses, sparingly; the punchline can arrive as its own paragraph opening WITH the ellipsis ("while you do / …literally anything else").
7. **Acquisition verbs: swipe, snag, "i'll send you"** — zero corpus instances of grab/download/get my/access.
8. **The sign-off is a variable slot**: "xx" · "xo" · signed "xx / Alyssa" (manifesto) · in-world persona sign-off when the caption runs a conceit ("love, your secret business admirer") · or nothing at all (several offer captions; Mar 3 uses a solitary "." line as the hashtag spacer). When the caption world-builds, the signature stays in-world.
9. **Exclamation marks are legal in caption register — logistics/comedy only**: "Changes you should know! (pt1)", "& QUICKLY!!", "So this is your nudge!" — never sincerity-cheerleading.
10. **Lexicon**: "silly little creative" (affectionate diminutive), "is giving desperate ex vibes," "COLD AF," "wont get em" (dropped apostrophe + "em" — don't clean it up).

## Never

Beginner-splaining outside an Audience Identity hook that calls for it on purpose. CTA on a manifesto/FTG post unless the source shows one. Sales-voice on Monday/Friday slots. Inventing a comment word — always check `BUSINESS.md`. Paraphrasing the credibility line. Round numbers where the real data has odd ones (5,781 saves, not "nearly 6k"). A pattern-interrupt slide placed last — it never is, in any real deck. Re-explaining the deck in the caption. CTA verbs grab/download/get/access. "FREE!" in caption prose (bracketed lowercase "(free)" is the price tag).
