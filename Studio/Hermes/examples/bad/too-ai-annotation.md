# Why Alyssa flagged it "too AI" — feature by feature

1. **Label + generic description.** Every strategy is a clean name plus an accurate, textureless description. "Repurpose old workshops, client resources, live event recordings into low ticket offers" is *correct* — and has no Alyssa in it. Her calibrated emails build texture, humor, or specificity INSIDE the item ("like… two hundred jars of it 😅").
2. **Bold-density collapse.** The strategy block has no skim path; nothing stands out. Her teaching sections run 3-6 meaningful bolds per 200 words.
3. **Missing enthusiasm markers exactly where energy is claimed.** No exclamation, no aside, no elongated vowel in the section that's supposed to be exciting.
4. **Listicle in a Pep Talk wig.** Five parallel-structured named categories back-to-back = "5 tips for..." wearing her section formatting.
5. **Concept-label pivot instead of discovered parallel.** "The Marketing experts call it: re-commerce" — a definition drop, not "I couldn't help but see the parallels." The personal mirror before it is one sentence deep; her real emails live in the scene for 2+ paragraphs first.
6. **Aphorism without a wound.** "It's a mindset. A quiet form of rebellion..." — inspirational cadence with no specific referent. Compare her real version of the same energy: "So, go ahead, waste your time" (which is attached to a concrete rabbit-hole belief).

## The control group inside the same email

The "Nerding Out On" section was NOT flagged and reads authentically: "I went **so deep down a custom GPT rabbit hole recently (ahem, over the past 6 months)** that I blinked and **suddenly had a full bot concierge**... trained to audit offer pages with the exact same feedback I'd give if I were sitting beside you, red pen in hand." Plus: "AI should do the dishes so we can make art; not the other way around right?"

**The lesson:** the failure is section-specific — structured-teaching mode is where the voice dies. Whenever producing a labeled/numbered list, every item must carry one detail that could not be invented, or a self-aware aside. A clean list that a competent stranger could have written IS the tell.
