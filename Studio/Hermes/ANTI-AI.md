## Her punctuation corrections (2026-07-05, outrank everything)

- **Em-dashes (—): ZERO.** "i dont use em dahses." Corpus-verified: 0 in her real body prose. She uses a spaced hyphen " - " occasionally, ".." trails, and parentheses for asides. (Stylometry research agrees: 6-12 em-dashes/1000 words = LLM; 0-2 = human.)
- **Period density: low, dots high — register-scoped (her ruling 2026-07-05: "B is the accurate rule").** Corpus rule: trailing dots ("..") ≥ 1 per 2 hard periods; ≤~12 hard periods in NARRATIVE/confession register. List-built sections (labeled steps, feature stacks) are exempt — their periods are structural. But: "make sure that we're not doing staccato runs. We're not doing short bullshit sentences. Alyssa always does long sentences." Staccato max one, at the emotional peak; long sentences are the default in every register.
- **"its not this its that": banned in all forms**, including headers and hooks ("It's not personal. They just forgot." / "Isn't Cold. It's Just Full." both failed). Her covers NEVER use reframes - verified across all 12+ real carousel covers.
- **Beware over-correction**: aggressively choppy, adverb-free prose is its own AI template. The fix is never "less style," it is HER style: caps, stretched vowels, run-ons, dots, asides.
- **Trailing "-ing significance clauses"** ("...creating a sense of...", "...highlighting the importance of..."): the single strongest stylometric LLM tell in research. Zero.
- **The imitation tell (2026-07-05)**: recycling a construction from her documented winners ("(on purpose)", "Why I burned down...", the strikethrough gag) reads as AI pattern-matching. Her hits are evidence of taste, not templates. Coin something weird instead ("the tiny list effect").

## Dead openings and transitions (never)

"In today's...", "It is important to note", "Let's dive in/explore/unpack", "At the end of the day", "Nobody is talking about", "Most people don't realize" · "Furthermore," "Additionally," "Moreover," "That said," "With that in mind," "On top of that."

## AI patterns to avoid

- Puffery ("a pivotal moment," "a major shift") — state the fact, the reader judges weight.
- **Rule of three** — AI lists 3 when it doesn't know what to say. Use 1, 2, or 4. Her lists are lopsided; one item always breaks the pattern.
- False ranges ("from X to Y") when you can't name the middle.
- Elegant variation — say the same thing with the same word again.
- Meta commentary — never announce the writing.
- Personification-for-dread stacks ("quietly dying," "silently killing"). One personified object max, played for comedy.
- Concept-label transitions ("The experts call it: X") — she pivots by discovery, in fresh words each time.

## Deep-read additions (2026-07-05, from "Why I burned down a perfectly good business")

**Rule-of-three clarification**: a real email built its entire teaching spine on three numbered reasons ("Reason nO1/nO2/nO3"), each a 300-500 word section with its own scene, problem, and named pivot. Don't block a draft for using three well-textured sections.

## Final pass checklist (run every time)

1. Cut throat-clearing first sentences. 2. Vague → specific. 3. Remove fake importance. 4. Check repeated sentence shapes. 5. Grep the draft for "—" (must be 0). 6. Count staccato runs (Word. Word. Word.) — near-zero. 7. Search negative parallelism ACROSS sentence boundaries and in headers/hooks/subjects. 8. Delete rejected-frame constructions. 9. Delete analogies that don't earn their place. 10. Cut endings that only repeat the point. 11. If the point is made, stop.
