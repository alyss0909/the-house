# Subject Lines

Her real subject lines, with performance data, live in [[Hermes/craft/subject-lines]] (Kit-verified, confidence HIGHEST — 351-broadcast analysis). There is no dedicated subject-line folder in [[Library/Examples]]; the subject lines themselves are her words (tier-1) and travel with the emails they belong to in [[Library/Examples/sales-emails]], [[Library/Examples/simmers]], and [[Library/Examples/soft-sundays]].

Read [[Hermes/craft/subject-lines]] in full before writing a subject line. Voice: "raccoon in a cashmere sweater" — short, casual, slightly emotionally loaded, a tiny bit wrong. One job per line: **readers open for Alyssa-the-person, click for Alyssa-the-strategist.**

## What makes hers hers

Pulled from the real Kit-verified winners cited in [[Hermes/craft/subject-lines]].

- **Confession/vulnerability opens, and the one line that won both metrics.** "I failed |🍦Soft Sunday" (44.98% opens, 341 clicks) and "oopsie, I've been gatekeeping?" (48.85%) open cold on an admission. "Why I burned down a perfectly good business (on purpose)" is the only line in her history to win opens AND clicks — confession stacked with contradiction.
- **Faux-logistics and fake-reply lines, warm lists and event reminders only.** "re: tmrw's meeting" (57.35% all-time), "you coming?" (76.7% to segment). Never on cold lists — that burns trust.
- **Deliverable-inside brackets are the click engine.** "[PLAN INSIDE] clock out by summer," "[replay inside] Slowly Viral + $1k off BOH" (60.17% open, 18.5% click). The weighted nouns are plan, checklist, fix/fixed, replay, class link, template, swipe, coupon, invite, steps. "Inside" alone, with no noun, converts nothing.
- **Personified objects and segment intimacy read like a text from a friend.** "Your list called, they're hungry 👀" (44.15%, 349 clicks); "you up? free custom bot delivery 🍔" (60% on a small segment); "You good, {NAME}?" (57.76%).
- **The Soft Sunday wrapper is a container, not the hook.** "[2-5 word weird/emotional phrase] |🍦Soft Sunday" — the front phrase still has to carry the weight; the wrapper only carries brand equity.
- **Lowercase and txt-speak are deliberate and cost nothing.** "ur," "tmrw," "k," "u want?" Odd, specific numbers beat round ones ("$12,212 coupon" over "$12k coupon").
- **Preview text does a different job than the subject line** — it adds context, urgency, or personalization, never a summary and never an explanation of the joke.

## Hard length cap and the never-list

- **≤40 characters before any wrapper**, front phrases run 2-6 words. Her 2026-07-05 correction, verbatim: "all subj lines are too long nobody in email marketing or copywriting professionals would approve that." Winners run 8-30 characters; only a Why-I narrative line may run long, and never on a sales or launch send.
- **Norah's name never appears in a subject line.** Her verbatim ruling, 2026-07-05: family texture is body-copy only. This is a hard boundary, not a style preference.
- Never summarize the content ("5 tips for better email" reads as a table of contents, not a hook).
- Never over-explain — format, outcome, and container all in one line is the tell.
- Never run a long subject on a sales or launch email, even if the Why-I exception above would otherwise apply.
- Never use urgency without a story behind it.
- Never use the fake-reply pattern on a cold list — it burns trust on a list that hasn't earned the intimacy register yet.
- Off-register randomness is a real risk, not just a style miss: "Stoner Math" (22.4%) is the worst performer in the corpus and stays in the record as the contrast case, not a template to copy.
