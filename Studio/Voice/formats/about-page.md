## What makes hers hers

Pulled only from patterns actually visible in [[Library/Examples/about-page]].

- **Opens on a question aimed straight at the reader's own contradiction, not a personal introduction.** "Dreaming of late-night creative sessions by candlelight, but instead you're burning the candle at both ends just trying to keep your business running?" then answers her own hook with "Dude, we must be soulmates."
- **A numbered personal-facts list mixes the trivial and the telling, with a strikethrough gag inside it.** "I got my dream job right outta university, and promptly left 3 months later to ~~squander my potential~~ work for myself."
- **Misfired idioms and spacing wobbles stay uncorrected on purpose.** "Au contrair. I was, well, for a lack of a better term, kinda miserable" and "(micro)manage" both ship as she wrote them. Per [[Hermes/ANTI-AI]], these are load-bearing voice devices, not typos to clean up.
- **Section headers act as scene-setting stage directions in all caps.** "LET ME SET THE SCENE FOR YOU:" and "MEET: THE SYSTEM OF ALL SYSTEMS" both function like a narrator cue, not a headline.
- **The pivot from personal story to offer happens gradually across several short sections, never in one hard turn.** The Portugal story, the burn-it-down decision, and the Pinterest-and-email discovery each get their own beat before "THE SYSTEM OF ALL SYSTEMS" appears.
- **Zero em dashes across the entire live page**, the longest-standing confirmation of the em dash ban in the corpus.

## The full story skeleton

Full detail in [[Hermes/craft/web-pages]]. The ordered spine a writer needs, not isolated bullets: reader's-dream question headline → instant kinship ("Dude, we must be soulmates.") → hedged confession → the 5-item bio list, resume to absurd, weirdest last → an announced climax that hands the page back to the reader → scene-setting of the crisis → the burn-it-down pivot → the rabbit-hole reveal (email + Pinterest) → the offer, 600+ words in → a moral addressed to the reader → an aphorism-then-permission couplet → a soft positioning close.

- **Verse pacing ships on web pages.** Clause stacks break across lines, four-dot ellipses ("SO…."), and a spaced mid-sentence ellipsis all appear. Grammar wobbles and idiom misfires stay uncorrected — imitate imperfections that ship readable mess; don't generate broken punctuation as a device.
- **Credentials only ever land mid-sentence, inside a because-clause** — evidence for a tool claim, never a bio flex.
