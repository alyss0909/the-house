## What makes her captions hers

Pulled only from patterns actually visible in the captions in [[Library/Examples/Carousels/captions]].

- **Caption opens with a hook line, often a confession or a provocation, before any teaching starts.** "Sorry but Feb is the new Jan." and "Not to be dramatic... but i think it's time to break up with social" both open cold, no preamble.
- **Comment-word CTA is a single all-caps word, always named explicitly.** "Comment TEMPLATE," "Comment LIST," "comment PIN," "Comment VIRAL," "Comment YESCHEF." This is the mechanism the whole content system runs on: comment keyword to ManyChat to list.
- **Exclamation marks attach only to logistics or comic excitement, never to a sincerity line.** "& QUICKLY!!" and "(this thursday!!)" both mark real urgency about timing, not emotional insistence.
- **A closing signature varies by mood, never fixed.** "xx", "xx Alyssa", "xo", "love, your secret business admirer" all appear depending on the register of that specific post.
- **One real "Less X, more Y" and one real "Less content, better leads" appear as grandfathered source hits** (2026-01-15 and 2026-02-24 captions). These stay exactly as written. Per [[Hermes/ANTI-AI]], this shape is never generated fresh in a new caption; only her verbatim originals may be reused, lightly.
- **Metrics prove save-and-comment behavior drives the format, not just reach.** The 2026-01-20 caption pulled 4,070 saves and 856 follows off a single comment-word CTA, confirming the mechanism works before the copy gets touched.

## What makes her covers work

Full forensic detail lives in [[Hermes/craft/captions-carousels]] and [[Studio/Analysis/carousel-hook-forensics]] — read them before writing a cover, don't write from this summary alone.

- **A coined concept beats any pattern-matched winner.** "The tiny list effect," "Stoner Math," "chat-written" — an unexpected command with wrong-register punctuation ("delete your leads, quick") outperforms a recycled construction. Her 2026-07-05 ruling: "'on purpose' is a little too ai.. something more weird or unexpected." Never recycle a construction from a documented winner ("(on purpose)," "Why I burned down," the strikethrough gag) — reusing her own hits is the imitation tell, not a way to honor them.
- **Under 7 words** is her stated cap (2026-07-05). Older winners ran to 10 with a parenthetical payoff; new covers obey 7.
- **A payoff is mandatory on the cover** — one of five: fix ("...And What To Do Instead"), threat ("...Is Dying"), confession ("Why I..."), series stake ("Part One"), or joke (Sugar ~~Daddy~~ Mama). No payoff means it isn't a hook.
- **Never a reframe shape on a cover.** Her reframes ("that's not a system") live on interior or closing slides only, after stakes are built — a reframe resolves itself in the same breath, so there's nothing left to swipe for. This is a cover-specific, stronger version of the negative-parallelism ban in [[Studio/Voice/KILL-LIST]].
- **Talk TO the reader — her 2026-07-06 ruling overrides the old no-"you" rule.** Verbatim: "they need to induce curiosity they need to tlak TO a reader they need to hint at a result or a loss aversion." Second-person "you/your" is legal on covers now. Every hook opens a curiosity gap it doesn't close, addresses the reader directly, and hints at a result she wants or a loss she's already taking.
- **Period or nothing at the end, never a question mark.** Questions belong on CTA/engagement slides.
- **Problem before solution, always** — at least 3 slides build the problem before any alternative appears; the offer is earned after roughly 60% of the deck.

## THE flow (her canonical spine, stated verbatim 2026-07-05)

The default architecture for every deck, cited in full in [[Hermes/craft/captions-carousels]]:

1. **Cover** — big hook, main clause under seven words (overflow only as a parenthetical that is itself a second hook).
2. **Slide 2** — a secondary hook that creates an open loop. The credibility/audience-mirror line IS this slide when it promises a payoff and withholds it.
3. **Slide 3** — agitate the problem.
4. **Slides 4-7** — value, one idea per slide, and value means teaching. Her 2026-07-05 correction: "the 'value' has no teaching USE boh and all products." Each value slide teaches something real — a named method, a BOH micro-lesson, a real product mechanic — never a generic observation. Per-slide architecture: a big catchy headline first (the skimmer path), the explaining body underneath.
5. **Slide 8** — something big, a massive perspective shift or aha moment. A content reversal, not the visual interrupt.
6. **Slide 9** — the CTA. The full-bleed visual world-shift marks the CTA doorway between slides 8 and 9; it is a separate device, never the last slide's content itself.

Archetypes (manifesto/FTG, story/POV) may flex the middle, but a deck that skips the open-loop slide 2, the agitation, or the slide-8 aha does not ship.

**For The Girls posts have their own dedicated writing skill** — load [[Hermes/craft/for-the-girls-posts]] before writing that lane. It flexes hardest: a permission-slip litany, often no CTA slide, closing on an open question rather than a comment-word ask.

## The texture floor

**Roughly one un-inventable detail per value slide**, harvested from source — a real number, a named micro-scenario, a coined term. Clean, parallel "label + description" value slides are the assembled-ness tell; her real decks are lopsided, one item always breaking the pattern. The same rule applies to freebie-list captions: the parallel bullet structure has to break at least once with personality.
