## What makes hers hers

Pulled only from patterns actually visible in the examples above.

- **Opens with a specific, almost throwaway memory, not a topic sentence.** "Did i ever tell you about the time I almost quit my business because of delta." reads like she is mid-conversation already.
- **The story runs long before it turns toward the offer.** [[Library/Examples/simmers/when-delta-stole-my-business-idea]] spends roughly two thirds of the email on the napkin story alone.
- **One word per line for emotional emphasis, then a return to normal prose.** "I COULDN'T. / REMEMBER. / ANY OF IT." breaks the sentence into a staccato run at the single emotional peak of the email, then the prose goes back to long sentences right after.
- **Self-aware asides in parentheses or tildes.** "the ~here's how it actually works behind the scenes~ file" and "(Deranged probably, but BEAUTIFUL)" both puncture her own sentence mid-thought.
- **A numbered "manual" or "cliff notes" section reframes the teaching as found material, not authored advice.** "O1: Pinterest Traffic Kiosk," "O2: Email Reception Desk," "O3: (Soft) Sales Register."
- **Multiple offers stacked at the end with food/hospitality language tying them together.** Soft Ceo Sessions, Soft Ceo Monthly, and Back of House are each introduced with a matching emoji and a one-line pitch, not a hard sell.

## Hard guards (corpus-measured, 2026-07-05 — a draft failing any of these does not ship)

Full evidence in [[Hermes/craft/emails-simmer]] and [[Studio/Analysis/simmer-opening-forensics]].

- **Pivot to business by 35% of body.** Real corpus: the Delta story pivots at ~1%, the bath story at ~29%, the closet story at ~34% — the ceiling. Extend toward ~50% only if a subject/preview line or the opening conflict already pre-paid it. A static scene earns zero extension.
- **Scene-stakes test: the seed must score at least 2 of 4** — conflict, loss-or-stakes, absurdity, emotional charge. Wanting something is not conflict; the reader needs to watch her fight something. A flat wanted-object opening scores 0/4 and is the same failure as her own flagged too-AI email.
- **Metaphor-nuance test, all three required**: the image yields at least 2 distinct mapped insights, it carries an internal tension mirroring the business tension, and a self-aware exit joke is writable from it. One flat 1:1 mapping is "too obvious and un-nuanced" — her verdict, 2026-07-05.

## The promo variant

Confirmed by her ruling, 2026-07-05, verbatim: "I think it's version B. Most simmer emails are simple one or two line replies, but sometimes I'll write a simmer email that's more of a promo." Two modes exist, not one:

- **Nurture** (the default above) — a 1-2 line offer, reply-word CTA.
- **Promo** — legal only on a flagged promo week, never the default. Runs a full offer block at roughly 40% of the email: a bridge ("That's why I built [product]"), 4 emoji-bulleted deliverables with odd counts, a bolded outcome line, and a discount CTA with link only (no reply-word). The discount code is themed to the email's pop-culture bit.

## Metaphor callbacks

Her 2026-07-05 ruling reverses the earlier "metaphor stays in the story" draft rule: puns, winks, and layered callbacks after the takeaway are hers — "It's when you're treating the reader like a child who needs the metaphor extended instead of layering on top of it, being silly, playful, fun, coming at it from a new angle." Teaching sentences stay literal; callbacks do the texture work. Heavy-handed, child-splaining extension of the metaphor mid-argument is still the AI tell.

**Metaphor role-consistency check** (the cupcake lesson, her verbatim 2026-07-05): the same image cannot be the solution in the story and the villain in the takeaway. Verify the image plays one role end to end before shipping.
