## Design principles (the post-mortem, encoded)

1. **Look before writing.** The writer must have SEEN real carousels that week — Alyssa's actual slide images and live top posts from the niche. Text about carousels is not carousels.
2. **Concepts are simple promises.** Real carousel concepts read like "How To Get All Your Clients From Pinterest This Year" — a direct promise + who it's for. The magic is execution, not concept cleverness. If a concept needs a "mechanism" explained, it's dead.
3. **Short instruction, fresh context.** New maker context each week. No rule stacks — checklist-driven writing produces checklist-shaped writing.
4. **Taste belongs to Alyssa.** Larry's gate is mechanical only (real citations, funnel-fit on meaning, chat words verified, no duplicates). "Is it good" is answered by her reacts, which accumulate in the taste ledger.
5. **Cheap to react to.** One line per concept. No hooks, no annotations at pitch stage.
6. **Nothing downstream is produced until upstream is approved.** Concept → Hook → Substance → Writer, each gated, each approved before the next begins.

## Cadence — work-ahead buffered (Alyssa, 2026-07-08)

Trend + strategy insights run **Sunday**. **Monday**, Alyssa reviews concepts for a **future publish week (W+1 or further)** — the pipeline is forward-buffered, never scrambling for the week it's in, and the team may run several weeks ahead. The monthly scrape ([[trend-scout-monthly-routine]], next run **2026-08-01**) feeds the swipe file; the weekly Trend Brief re-slices it and flags `[working ahead — verify at ship]` when built past the scrape's freshness window.

|---|---|---|---|---|

| 2 | Suki Weekly Strategy Brief → **slot-offer lock** (M=free / W=product / F) | [[SOP-038-suki-weekly-strategy-brief]] | — | Alyssa gets a 2-line skim (goal + first approval) |
| 3 | Concept pitch (10+ → survivors as plain-language concepts) | [[SOP-034-write-concept-pitch-brief]] (Concept Guy) | [[SOP-037-run-mean-alyssa-content-gate]] concept gate | **Alyssa picks** |

| 6 | Writer (Hermes) turns approved substance into carousel copy in her voice | Hermes ([[GL-011-substance-arc-contract]] §2 boundary) | SOP-037 voice/social gate | **Alyssa final approval** |

**Step 1 is a HARD GATE — the chain cannot start without it.** No concepts (SOP-034) are generated until a real Trend Scout Brief exists for the week. **The brief's PRIMARY source is what Alyssa's Layer-1 trusted accounts are actually posting RIGHT NOW** — the swipe file / scrape read for the TOPICS and ANGLES her people are winning with (not just cover-format structure). **Generic marketing-blog / web trends are NOT the trend source** — they produce the evergreen filler she rejects; web is only ever for verifying one specific fact a concept needs, never the signal itself. If the brief is missing or thin, **Larry HALTS and flags it to Alyssa before any downstream rung runs — never silently proceeds.** (Origin: 2026-07-08 — concepts came out generic because Step 1 was skipped, then mis-sourced from generic web instead of her trusted accounts. The signal is HER niche, read from HER sources — e.g. the July swipe showed soft-life/offline/freedom confessions massively out-performing tactical how-tos.)

Every react is appended verbatim by Larry to [[Studio/Content/taste-ledger]] (date, item, verdict, her words) — the ledger loads into every future SOP-034 run, so the system learns her taste instead of guessing it.

## What this workflow explicitly does NOT do
- No rubric stacks at pitch stage (retired for concepting; mechanical lint still applies at production)
- No decks before her concept approval (touchpoint #1 is the pitch sheet)
- No copy relays between agents; revisions return to the SAME context via SendMessage, never a fresh patcher
- Larry never declares work "ready" on procedural passes — a sheet is "ready for reacts," nothing stronger
