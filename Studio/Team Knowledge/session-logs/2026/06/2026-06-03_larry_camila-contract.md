# Camila formal contract — draft, refine, and recurring task research

## Context

Alyssa came in with a prior freelance contract (written for a creative marketing assistant) and wanted to adapt it for Camila's actual role: Operations Assistant, right-hand/CEO support, ops + content. The session covered full contract editing, clause-by-clause refinement, and a Pax research pass to surface recurring task ideas for Camila from the PKA.

## What we did

- **Larry** adapted the original contract template to Camila's role — swapped title, opening framing, responsibilities, and availability language throughout.
- **Larry** iterated on availability language through multiple rounds until landing on the right tone (co-working framing, not punitive framing).
- **Larry** made three targeted additions: invoice daily breakdown language, WhatsApp comms clause, kept Ownership of Work clause.
- **Pax** ran a 40+ file deep read across PKA (projects, goals, offers, habits, key elements, journal May–June 2026) and surfaced 7 non-obvious recurring task candidates grounded in real open threads.
- **Larry** synthesized Pax's findings and presented to Alyssa. No task confirmed before session close.

## Decisions made

- **Title:** Operations Assistant (not Creative Marketing Assistant, not Executive Assistant)
- **Co-working window:** Tuesdays 10:30am–3:30pm EST — synchronous, "online and engaged," explicitly framed as co-working not just availability
- **Remaining hours:** 5 hrs flexible throughout the week; daily email check-in M–F
- **Invoice language:** "total hours with a daily breakdown" — two words added to existing clause
- **Ownership of Work:** Kept in despite ops-heavy role (Camila creates copy, Notion DBs, course materials — all owned by Alyssa)
- **WhatsApp:** Named as primary comms channel; expectation to update progress and flag blockers actively
- **Governing law:** Ontario, Canada — unchanged from template
- **Contract sent:** Alyssa confirmed contract sent to Camila end of session

## Insights

- The word "expected" in contract availability language reads as a warning shot, not a professional standard. Better framing: define the window by what it's *for* ("our co-working hours for synchronous projects, real-time feedback, and direction") and note that availability is "part of the role" — same message, zero aggression.
- When researching recurring tasks for a contractor, the PKA's open threads and non-negotiables lists are more useful than generic role templates. Pax found 7 grounded ideas in 40 files; the first round of generic ideas was rejected immediately.

## Realignments

- First batch of recurring task suggestions (content batching/scheduling, Kit email formatting, Pinterest repurposing, support@ inbox triage, BOH student check-ins, monthly marketing report) was rejected in full. Alyssa said "meh, don't wanna do any of those." Pax research pass surfaced better options — but none confirmed before close.
- "Expected" flagged as too aggressive in availability language — replaced with co-working framing across multiple drafts.
