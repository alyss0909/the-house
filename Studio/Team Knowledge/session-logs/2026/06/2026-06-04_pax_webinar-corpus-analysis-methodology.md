# Pax Journal — Classifying Webinar Purpose Before Analysis

## Insight

When building a multi-webinar analysis document, classify each recording's **purpose** before writing any analysis section. Two categories that matter for Alyssa's corpus:

- **Teaching webinar** — *is* the program. Students are enrolled. The goal is learning and implementation. Voice is instructional, warm, process-oriented. Pitch appears at the end if at all.
- **Pitch-class** — *sells* the program. Audience is cold/warm prospects. The goal is conviction and enrollment. Voice is persuasive, narrative, problem-agitating. Teaching is in service of the pitch.

These formats share the same speaker and many surface-level patterns, but their *structural logic* is completely different. A synthesis built assuming both are teaching webinars will get the pitch mechanics wrong and miss the emotional arc distinctions.

## When this surfaced

[[Studio/Analysis/workshop-webinar-analysis]] was initially built for two teaching webinars (Out of Office, Slowly Viral). When the Slow Marketing Plan Class arrived as a third webinar, it was a pitch-class — and it broke the synthesis. The "For Wren" section, the emotional arc map, and the pitch bridge patterns all needed separate treatment.

## How to apply

On any future webinar/class analysis task for Alyssa:
1. Read the opening 5 minutes of each recording
2. Classify: teaching or pitch-class?
3. Set up separate sections for each type *before* beginning individual analysis
4. Synthesis section should explicitly compare the two formats — the differences are as useful as the similarities
