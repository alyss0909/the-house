## Decisions made

- **About page and home page deserve their own full analysis files, not just coverage in writing-style.md.** They teach completely different things: the about page is the personal brand blueprint and trust architecture; the home page is the cold-conversion machine and brand welcome mat. A single general writing-style file cannot do justice to both.
- **The copy team member is an AI specialist (Wren), not a human hire.** All the human-hire research from Pax and Nolan remains valid as framing — the sensibility requirements, scope split, and wrong-person signals translate directly to agent design decisions.
- **Wren's Don'ts are hard-coded inside the shim, not just referenced.** If they live only in writing-style.md, a Level 1 quick task might not load them. They're always in context because they're always in the operating contract.
- **Wren reads the Second Brain. Never writes to it.** Patterns worth capturing get flagged to Larry; Larry decides what gets filed.
- **Subject line analysis filed at v1 (preliminary).** Directionally correct; v2 will be built once the top performers data (2-year history) arrives and merges with the preliminary patterns.
- **The "raccoon in a cashmere sweater" phrase is now the canonical internal descriptor for Alyssa's subject line personality.** Filed as the north star in subject-line-analysis.md.

## Insights

- **The about page and home page teach fundamentally different things.** The about page teaches trust-building before selling — the reader finishes it in a state of relief and recognition, not urgency. The home page teaches cold-conversion compression — the brand argument in its most stripped-down form. Treating them as one category ("website copy") misses the point of each.
- **"Come over to the soft side" + cookies-on-stovetop is doing more work than any headline on the sales page.** The home page has to earn trust with strangers in seconds; the sales page works with warm traffic. The home page opening sequence is therefore the highest-stakes copy on the site — three sentences disarming, positioning, and promising.
- **"Agatha." — a period, no explanation — is one of the most instructive lines in the entire Second Brain.** It appears in the Time Connoisseur list on the home page. The fact that it works without explanation is the lesson: Alyssa trusts her reader, doesn't over-explain, and lets specificity do the intimacy work. This applies across every format she writes in.
- **The trap brief (with embedded fake scarcity) is the single best evaluation screen for an AI or human copy hire.** Alyssa's urgency doctrine is explicit — "Your urgency must be real" (Lazy Launch transcript). Any writer (AI or human) who executes fake urgency without flagging it is wrong for this brand. Worth building into Wren's first real test.
- **The Second Brain is now genuinely usable as a copy system.** With the routing map, 5 analysis files, real examples, Wren's operating contract, and me.md — a cold session can now produce on-brand copy by following the protocol. This was not true before today.
