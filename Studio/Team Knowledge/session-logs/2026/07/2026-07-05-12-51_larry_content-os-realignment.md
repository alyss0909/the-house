# Content OS Realignment

## Context

Alyssa challenged the team's handling of the Content OS calendar evidence. The team had been over-indexing on whether exact grid examples existed instead of solving for a source-grounded content system.

## What we did

- Larry verified that the vault contains more evidence than the carousel grid: Soft Sundays, Simmers, sales emails, sales pages, journal riffs, webinar material, Kit performance chunks, and Team Inbox captures.
- Larry created [[Deliverables/_archive/content-os/content-evidence-system]] as the operating rule for how the Content OS should use local evidence, external pulls, and cross-format examples.

## Decisions made

- **Question:** Should a missing exact carousel example block a content slot?
  **Decision:** No. A missing exact example triggers a build recipe: closest post-type precedent, matched teaching source, closest voice source, verified CTA rules, and clearly marked assumptions.

- **Question:** Should For The Girls be forced to match the weekly business theme?
  **Decision:** No. For The Girls is a loose identity/mood layer. It can attach to a month or moment by resonance, such as monthly affirmations in a Productivity week or "for the version of me who..." with One Good Thought.

- **Question:** Should Apify be the first move?
  **Decision:** No. Local vault sources come first. Apify or another external pull is useful only when the actual published artifact is missing locally.

## Insights

- The Content OS needs an evidence table, not a brittle grid. The grid should route theme, post type, offer, and emotional lane; the evidence table should supply real examples and confidence.
