# Carousel Example Gap Map

## Context

Alyssa asked Larry and Wren to look at the Second Brain through the lens of her monthly repeating carousel rhythm, themes, and offer map. She shared a planning grid with Monday education/freebie, Wednesday offer, and Friday For The Girls posts across four monthly themes: Email, Pinterest, BOH Systems / Slow Marketing, and Productivity.

## What we did

- Larry routed the content-pattern read to Wren.
- Wren loaded the Second Brain compass, voice profile, carousel examples index, carousel captions, carousel slide-by-slide analysis, carousel framework, Wren memory, signals index, yes-pattern, and relevant offer files.
- Larry identified the likely durable gap: the Second Brain has the rhythm, offer files, and several strong carousel examples, but it still needs example coverage by monthly slot and conversion intent.

## Decisions made

- **Question:** What should the team remember about the carousel system?
  **Decision:** Future carousel planning should not only ask "what topic?" It should ask which slot it fills, which offer/freebie it points to, what proof or example it uses, and whether it is high-value education, offer-led education, or brand-world relationship content.

## Insights

- The strongest existing carousel evidence is concentrated in Email, Pinterest, BOH Systems, trend/data arguments, and For The Girls identity posts.
- Productivity is under-observed relative to the monthly grid. The vault has productivity offers, but fewer proven carousel examples showing how productivity posts should teach, sell, and maintain the Soft CEO identity.
- The missing layer is an example matrix, not necessarily more theory: examples of captions and carousels that teach something high-value while naturally pointing to a specific offer or freebie.
- The standing signal `email-to-content-repurposing-system` is directly relevant: after each email or Soft Sunday is filed, Wren should be able to propose two carousel angles from it.

## Realignments

- Alyssa explicitly asked to remember this: the team needs to know what examples are missing for the carousel situation, including productivity-area posts, captions that do specific jobs, promo examples for specific offers, and high-value posts that still lead toward an offer.
