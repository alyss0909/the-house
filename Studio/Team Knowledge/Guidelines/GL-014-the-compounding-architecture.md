# GL-014 — The Compounding Architecture

The house does not just store knowledge; it compounds it. Every future decision — new workflow, new agent, new file, new tool, new automation — is tested against this Guideline before it ships. The proof behind the law: the 2026-07-11 autopsy ([[2026-07-11-memory-autopsy-and-compounding-rules]]) showed that the only subsystem that ever got smarter was the one whose lessons landed in files a later task was FORCED to load.

## The trigger word: **compound**

When Alyssa says **"compound"** (alone or in any sentence), the acting agent immediately:
1. Re-reads this Guideline.
2. Audits the current work against the five laws below and states, in one short block, where it complies and where it leaks.
3. Fixes any leak in the same session, or files it as an open thread with an owner.
It is a one-word course-correction — she should never need more words than that.

## The five laws (every decision passes all five)

**1. No capture without a forced consumer.**
Anything worth writing down names WHO reads it and WHEN, mechanically — on the boot path (NOW.md, HOUSE-MAP, agent contracts, playbooks), in a delegation brief, or on a dated check-back that a scheduled pass drains. A file nothing is obligated to read is a silo; do not create it, extend a forced surface instead.

**2. The journal is the core, and it is Alyssa's.**
Her entries are the house's primary source and gravity well: referenced, resurfaced, and more connected every day (SOP-040 journal duty; Journal Web; night shift). The boundary is LOCKED (her words, 2026-07-11: "my journal is not written over... that stays Alyssa only"): connections are made FROM Studio files linking IN; nothing under Notebook/ is edited except on her explicit per-case request.

**3. Precedent before invention.**
Before any desk designs, writes, builds, or decides: load your PLAYBOOK, the relevant [[Studio/Team/TEAM-BRAIN]] slice, and the nearest precedent from the index. Larry's brief must bundle them — no specialist starts cold. Re-deriving something another project already decided is a GL-014 violation, not just waste.

**4. Every Alyssa reaction becomes team memory the same session.**
Approval, rejection, correction, preference — one dated verbatim line in the acting agent's PLAYBOOK (cross-cutting -> TEAM-BRAIN) before close. Pre-apply what she has approved without asking; pre-reject what she has vetoed before she sees it. She never repeats herself: a repeat from her means a close-session upstream leaked — find it and fix the leak, not just the instance.

**5. Every session ends compounded.**
The SOP-040 close gate is not optional for any agent, any host, any hat: fates on every insight, died-because on every dropped thread, second-appearance insights drafted for graduation, journal duty receipt, playbook write, hand-off rewritten, NOW.md regenerated. A session that ends without closing is debt the next open must surface.

## What this changes about future decisions

- New workflow or SOP? It must name its forced consumer and its check-back cadence, or it does not ship.
- New agent? Hired with a PLAYBOOK from day one; contract carries the mandatory loads.
- New file? Answers "who is forced to read this, and when?" — otherwise link into an existing surface.
- New automation? Feeds the loop (NOW.md, playbooks, hubs) rather than creating a parallel memory.
- Anything Alyssa reacts to? Captured verbatim, same session, forever.

## Escape hatches

Thresholds and mechanics live in SOP-040 as dated defaults. This Guideline is the WHY and the law; amendments come from Alyssa (GL-013 convention). If a law here conflicts with GL-013, GL-013 wins and the conflict is flagged to her.
