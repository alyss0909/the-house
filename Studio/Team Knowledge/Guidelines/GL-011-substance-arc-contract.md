# GL-011 — The Substance Arc Contract

The carousel arc is the Rung-3 deliverable: the slide-by-slide skeleton that a persuasion-writer (Hermes) writes final voice copy from. This Guideline is the format law for that arc. It was written after Alyssa rejected a Hermes-built arc for being illegible and non-persuasive ("i dont rlly find the arc easy to decipher... it seems like this person doesnt know what substance is or has never been on instagram lol"). Every rule here is paid for by her verbatim reacts. Cassius owns it; any agent building or reviewing an arc reads it.

## Why this exists (the failure it fixes)

A voice-writer hands over a writer's outline, not a substance plan. Substance is a distinct job: **what the deck argues, in what order, with what proof, shifting which belief per slide** — done BEFORE and separately from the voice write. Routing this to the writer collapses the two jobs and produces an outline that reads like nobody knows what substance is. Rung 3 (Cassius) does substance + persuasion strategy; Rung 4 (Hermes) does voice.

## The five laws (Alyssa, 2026-07-07, verbatim-grounded)

### 1. Per slide = ONE plain line

`[what the slide literally shows] → so that [the belief we shift in the reader]`

Concise. One line. NOT a multi-field spec. The 4-field version (on-slide / goal / persuasion / source) was rejected: **"right direction but too much info."** The source lives once, in the arc's `Sources:` line — never repeated per slide.

Her own worked example, verbatim: *"show the math difference between a 3% open and a 40% open so that we persuade the reader that their next buyer is already subscribed we jsut have to get their open rate up"* — with the correction that the low number must be realistic (see Law 3).

### 2. The arc DOES persuasion — it does not defer it

Each slide line carries **actual persuasive copy that names and agitates the reader's current pain.** Not "problem slide here." The real sentence that makes the reader wince and swipe. Her ruling: *"add persuasive copy that acutally nails their current pain point here and agitates it a bit (as of now you arent doing anything persuasive)."*

**This overrules GL-010 Step 3's "strip anything persuasive."** For the substance rung, if the arc isn't doing persuasion, it isn't substance. (GL-010 Step 3 described the OLD two-job split where persuasion was deferred entirely to the writer; that split is superseded for the arc itself — the arc now carries the persuasion strategy legibly, and Hermes writes it into full voice.)

### 3. Real, believable numbers only

Every stat on a proof slide traces to Alyssa's real data. Never invent. Never round away from the real figure.

- **Email open rates:** her real range is **34–57% opens** (`PKM/Second Brain/analysis/subject-line.md`, 351 broadcasts). A real engaged open rate is ~40–57%. A cold/ghost open rate is single digits to low teens — believable.
- **NEVER 3%.** Her exact callout: *"nobody has a fucking 3% open rate."*
- **Offer facts:** the real offer file in `PKM/My Life/Offers/`. **IG benchmarks:** `Deliverables/2026-07-06-instagram-craft-foundation.md` (verified vs [unverified] flagged there — use the verified direction, not tool-blog fake precision).
- If a needed number isn't in her real data: build the slide without it, or flag the gap. Never fake it.

### 4. Attribution header on every arc

The arc opens with:

`Prepared by: [specialist name] · referenced: [key sources — the teaching file + the data file for the numbers]`

Alyssa must see who built the arc and what fed it, and be able to inspect how they work. Non-negotiable.

### 5. Fit the dashboard-parseable contract

The arc still renders on the pitch dashboard, so it keeps the existing shape — the new substance content goes INSIDE the slide-line `text`:

```
Prepared by: Cassius · referenced: PKM/My Life/Offers/email-list-revival-plan.md · PKM/Second Brain/analysis/subject-line.md

### DAYKEY — Title
**Cover:** [cover title as it appears on slide 1]
1. Cover: [what slide 1 shows] → so that [belief] — [the hook/persuasive line on the slide]
2. Frame: [what slide 2 shows] → so that [belief it breaks] — [copy that names + agitates the pain]
3. Proof: [what slide 3 shows, REAL numbers] → so that [belief the proof lands]
4. Step: [what this slide teaches] → so that [capability it gives]
5. Step: [...] → so that [...]
6. CTA: [the one CTA] → [offer]
Sources: [every file the substance + numbers came from — real paths]
Hooks: [2–3 hook options for slide 1]
Note: / Parked: [only if needed — flags for Alyssa in plain words]
```

Required elements: `### DAYKEY — Title`, `**Cover:**`, numbered `N. Role: text` slide lines, `Sources:`, `Hooks:`. Optional: `Note:`, `Parked:`.

**Dashboard render note:** showing the `Prepared by` header on the 02 Arc card is a small design/dashboard add (comparable to the pending WED-chip add). Flag it to Larry/Iris; don't block the arc on it. The arc body already parses under the current contract.

## Worked example (the corrected Monday "200 vs 2,000" slide)

Concept: **The Email That Wakes Up A Dead List** (W29 Mon, freebie: Email List Revival Plan). The rejected version said only *"Proof: the open-rate math stacked plain — 200 people who actually read you beat 2,000 ghosts."* No real numbers, no persuasion. Corrected under this contract:

`Prepared by: Cassius · referenced: PKM/My Life/Offers/email-list-revival-plan.md · PKM/Second Brain/analysis/subject-line.md (real open rates) · Hermes/FUNNEL.md (offer belief)`

> **3. Proof:** shows the math side by side — a 2,000-person list opening at 8% (160 reads) vs a 200-person list of people who actually read you opening at 45% (90 reads today, and climbing every send as you keep showing up) → so that she stops believing "I just need more subscribers" and starts believing the buyer she wants is already on her list, cold, waiting to be reminded she exists. **"You keep staring at that subscriber count like it's the problem. It isn't. The people who used to open every email you sent are still in there — you just went quiet on them, and they went cold."**

Both open rates are real-range: 45% is squarely inside her 40–57% engaged band (`subject-line.md`); 8% is a believable cold/ghost rate — not the banned 3%. The belief it lands is the offer's belief from `Hermes/FUNNEL.md` ("your next buyer is already subscribed"). The bolded line is the persuasion doing its job on the slide, not deferred.

## Reviewer checklist (Larry's mechanical gate)

- [ ] Attribution header present, sources are real files actually opened.
- [ ] Every slide line is ONE line in the `X → so that Y` shape (not a 4-field block).
- [ ] Slide 2 and every payoff slide carry real persuasive copy that names + agitates a real pain.
- [ ] Every number traces to her real data; no invented stats; no 3% open rates.
- [ ] Funnel-fit is on MEANING vs `Hermes/FUNNEL.md`; the CTA belief is earned by the arc.
- [ ] Fits the dashboard contract (DAYKEY, Cover, numbered lines, Sources, Hooks).
- [ ] No re-skin of a published winner; no curriculum/guru vocab.
