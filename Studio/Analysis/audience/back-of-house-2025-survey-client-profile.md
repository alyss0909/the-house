# Back of House - 2025 Survey Client Profile

> Use this when writing Back of House emails, sales copy, content, webinar material, nurture sequences, launch copy, Pinterest/blog angles, or product positioning.

This is the working audience profile for [[Library/Programs/back-of-house/INDEX|Back of House]], derived from the 2025 audience survey.

Raw survey and processed artifacts live in:

- `Team Inbox/2025 Audience Survey_ (Responses) - Responses (1).csv`
- `Deliverables/2026-06-02-2025-audience-survey-Studio/Analysis/`

## Core Read

The BOH audience is not asking for generic productivity, generic second brain systems, or more content pressure.

They are asking for a calmer, more reliable way to get clients, leads, and sales without living on social media.

The center of gravity is:

> I need more clients, but I do not want my business to depend on constant posting, trend-chasing, or being online all the time.

## Audience Snapshot

Survey size: 96 responses

Strongest audience stage:

- 52.1% are "I've been in the game for a while but need more clients."

Strongest content/training wants:

- 70.8% want replacing social content with slow marketing
- 66.7% want more automated sales from email
- 64.6% want more clients
- 63.5% want passive income from existing offers
- 62.5% want growing an email list
- 62.5% want running a cozy and creative business
- 59.4% want more leads from Pinterest

Channel maturity:

- Social media is mostly inconsistent, stressful, or something they want to rely on less.
- Email marketing is desired, but underbuilt and inconsistent.
- Pinterest is attractive as a calmer discovery channel, but also inconsistent.

## Who This Client Is

This person is usually not brand new to business. They may have offers, clients, skills, ideas, proof, and some audience history. What they do not have is a dependable client-generation system.

They are likely to be:

- Creative, service-based, or online-business owners
- Skilled but under-systemized
- Tired of being told to post more
- Interested in email, Pinterest, evergreen content, and slower marketing
- Overwhelmed by scattered ideas, unfinished courses, and too many possible next steps
- Drawn to cozy, creative, sustainable, behind-the-scenes business building

They want the business outcome of marketing without the emotional and logistical chaos of always-on marketing.

## What They Are Really Buying

They are not only buying email marketing, Pinterest, funnels, sales pages, tripwires, or evergreen content.

They are buying:

- Relief from daily marketing pressure
- A clear order of operations
- A business back end that keeps working when they are not posting
- A way to turn existing ideas and offers into client flow
- Confidence that they can actually implement
- A system they can trust when they are busy, tired, or inconsistent

## Main Pain Points

### Need more clients

This is the loudest business pain. Even when they ask for email, Pinterest, passive income, or systems, the underlying request is usually reliable demand.

Use language like:

- more clients
- lead flow
- client flow
- steady inquiries
- buyers
- sales without being on all the time

### Social media fatigue

They reject advice about algorithms, reels, posting every day, hard selling, and always showing up.

Use language like:

- tired of feeding Instagram
- not living on your phone
- marketing without the daily performance
- slow marketing
- owned-path client flow
- not chasing the algorithm

Avoid making social sound like the primary engine. In BOH, social is a supporting repurposing channel, not the foundation.

### Email is wanted but inconsistent

They want email to work, but many do not have a consistent system.

Use language like:

- your quietest but hardest-working sales team
- nurture people while you are offline
- keep leads warm
- make your inbox the main course
- stop starting from scratch every time you write

### Pinterest feels promising but fuzzy

Pinterest appeals because it feels calmer than social, but they need help understanding how it connects to leads, email, and offers.

Use language like:

- slow burn traffic
- searchable discovery
- fresh leads without fresh posts every day
- traffic pantry
- content that keeps working

### Implementation risk

The biggest purchase barrier is not only money. It is fear of not following through.

Common barriers:

- Overwhelm / too much to do
- Worried they will not follow through
- Cost feels too high
- Sick of courses and groups
- Prefer done-for-you or hands-on help

Every BOH sales asset should answer:

> Why will this be different from the other things I bought and did not finish?

## Messaging Guidance For BOH

Lead with:

- Get clients without living on social.
- Build a calmer client-generation system.
- Turn your existing ideas and offers into email-first sales assets.
- Let Pinterest, email, and evergreen content do more of the heavy lifting.
- Stop starting from scratch every time you need to market.
- Build the back of house that keeps leads warm and sales moving.

Avoid leading with:

- generic productivity
- generic second brain language
- "post more"
- "show up more"
- "hack the algorithm"
- heavy urgency
- hustle language
- purely aesthetic cozy language with no business outcome

Cozy is the atmosphere. Client flow is the promise.

## Email Writing Notes

When writing BOH emails, write to someone who is capable but tired.

Assume:

- She has ideas.
- She has tried advice before.
- She does not want to be scolded.
- She wants a path that feels realistic.
- She wants business growth without losing her nervous system to the internet.

Good angles:

- "You do not need another blank content calendar."
- "The problem is not that you have no ideas. The problem is that your ideas are not connected to a client pathway."
- "Your list does not need more random newsletters. It needs a warm plate method."
- "Pinterest is not another social platform to perform on. It is a pantry."
- "Your sales page, freebie, tripwire, emails, and Pinterest should be one ecosystem."
