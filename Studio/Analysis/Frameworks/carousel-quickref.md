## Agent Workflow

**To generate a new concept:**
1. Take the topic/angle from the user
2. Match to an archetype using the goal → archetype table below
3. Pick a hook formula from [[Studio/Analysis/Frameworks/social-hooks|social-hooks.md]]
4. Fill the brief format (5 decisions below)
5. Get approval on the brief before writing copy

**To execute copy from a completed brief:**
Treat the brief as the full spec. Write directly from it. Do not re-read the full framework. Voice rules are in [[Archive/Studio-Analysis-voice-retired-2026-07-10/voice-profile]] — apply them silently.

## Goal → Archetype

| If the goal is... | Use this archetype | Slide count |
|---|---|---|
| Saves + non-follower reach | Trend Report (series) | 9–10 |
| Shares + brand equity | Manifesto | 9 |
| Maximum cold shares | Provocation / Visual Joke hook | any |
| New followers from cold | Data Argument + 3-punch cover | 10 |
| Comments + warmth | Story/POV | 7 |
| Product conversions | Product Reveal or Step Framework | 7–11 |

## 6 Archetypes — Compressed

**Trend Report** — Stakes frame → 3 trends (problem / implication / silver lining) → freebie CTA. Requires: series Part N label, credibility anchor slide, data point, freebie (not paid) CTA. Credibility anchor: *"I've reviewed my notes (& industry reports) from last year and this is what I'm seeing in my own business and BTS with clients who make their money online but don't want to live online."*

**Manifesto** — Taboo reframe cover → 6 "I" statements → brand slide → engagement question (never a product CTA). Every content slide is first-person present tense. One universal maxim mid-deck. Footer identity statement every slide: "FOR CEOS | REFUSING | TO RUSH" or equivalent.

**Step Framework** — Bold outcome cover → philosophy/context → 4 steps → desire bridge → product CTA. Steps MUST use a metaphor domain (food, architecture, fashion, etc.) — the metaphor IS the step headline, not a label. Reader decodes by reading body copy = the swipe mechanic.

**Story/POV** — Personal decision cover → assumption → complication → tension peak → reframe → appetite CTA → product. 5-beat structure: Assumption → BUT complication → tension → WHAT IF reframe → pivot (3 handwritten lines naming the new model). Does NOT teach. Creates desire only.

**Product Reveal** — SEO cover → philosophy frame → early CTA (slide 3) → 4 features as reader benefits → problem diagnosis → full benefit summary → double CTA (same headline, two backgrounds). Every feature = reader benefit formula: `[reader pain] + "I've got you." → [outcome]`

**Data Argument** — 3-punch thesis cover → 3 proof slides → villain reveal → pivot → 3 solution slides → deadline CTA. Villain reveal = name who profits from reader's continued obedience to the broken system. Converts frustration → righteous anger → action.

## 3 Copy Rules

**Three-layer voice system (required on every slide):**
- ALL-CAPS SPACED TRACKING → authority: data, argument, observations
- Large editorial serif → statement: the memorable phrase, the paradox
- *handwritten/script* → intimacy: asides, CTA whispers, personal notes — max ONE per slide

**Required voice signatures (check each carousel has these):**
- Parenthetical aside doing the intellectual honesty work: "(you've probably answered these questions verbatim already)"
- Personal evidence anchor once per carousel: "I'm seeing this with my own clients every single day"
- Identity mirror phrase in Trend Report / Data Argument: "make their money online but don't want to live online"

**Pattern interrupt rule:** Every carousel has exactly ONE slide that breaks the visual template. Falls at ~75–80% through. Announces the CTA zone. Never the last slide.

## Power Word Clusters

**EASE:** low maintenance, slow marketing, simmer, evergreen, lasts, background, system, automated, steady, compound

**IDENTITY:** smart founders, lazy CEOs with high standards, girlies, service pros, refusing to rush, high standards

**ANTI-HUSTLE:** not living online, intentional, done less, only what lasts, sustainable, once and done

**PERMISSION:** you don't need, you only need, it's easier than you think, I've got you, this is your sign

**FOOD/RECIPE:** bread and butter, key ingredient, serve, recipe, simmer, hot and ready, take reservations, fresh batches

**VILLAIN:** priced out, cooked, tricked, billionaire platforms, disappears after a scroll, not a system

## CTA Format

**Comment keyword mechanic:** handwritten script "Comment" + contrasting bold ALL-CAPS keyword. Example: "Comment PINTEREST"

**Identity qualifier before CTA (strongest format):** Precede the comment trigger with the reader's internal monologue: "FOR THE 'I WANT MY MARKETING TO SIMMER IN THE BACKGROUND' GIRLS"

**Offer framing:** Always "STEAL MY" or "my EXACT" — positions offer as insider access, not a product pitch.

**Final slide:** Never hard-sells. Lands softly: engagement question (Manifesto), emotional close (Story/POV), or gift-framed product reveal (Data Argument).
