## FORMULAS

## BUMP OFFER TEMPLATE:

**SPECIAL OFFER: [ADD / GET / STEAL] [SPECIFIC ASSET]**

Then:

**[Concrete stack of what's inside]**  
Use numbers, speed, and specificity.

Then:

**[Tiny "why this matters" line]**  
Not too much explaining. Just enough to make the buyer go, "ugh yes, add it."

Then:

**[Checkbox CTA with outcome + discount]**  
"ADD [THING] 50% OFF"

## The formula

SPECIAL OFFER: ADD [THE TOOL / VAULT / BOT]

[Number or named asset]  
[What it helps them do faster]  
[What problem it removes]  
[Why it pairs perfectly with what they're already buying]

[Short punchy payoff line]

ADD [SHORT NAME] 50% OFF  
$[PRICE]

## Common traits in the examples

They both do a few smart things:

1. **Specific asset, not vague bonus**  
    "Promo Planner" and "Subject Line Vault" are immediately understandable.  
2. **Numbers create weight**  
    50 prompts, 32 reframes, 28 ideas, 80 subject lines.  
3. **The copy sells the shortcut**  
    It's not "learn more." It's "take the thing you're already doing and make it faster / better / less annoying."  
4. **The bump feels like a tiny strategic add-on**  
    Not a whole second course. More like, "obviously I should throw this in the basket."  
5. **The red headline does the work**  
    It says the offer in one breath. The rest supports it.

## OOPS EMAIL TEMPLATE:

Hey [first name],

I noticed you didn't [specific thing they missed + the most compelling one thing it would have done for them].

In case you missed it, it's called [product name] and it's normally [full price] but you can still grab it for [discount % or $ off ] [timeframe/last chance framing].

[MOCK UP]  
[CTA BUTTON >>]

[One-liner that makes the product feel stupid-easy / obvious]. So just in case you meant to grab it (or are on the fence), [restate the offer simply incl specific features & benefits].

[Reframe the core problem they have], [bust the belief that's stopping them from buying], [simple solution the product provides].

[CUTE SIGN OFF]
