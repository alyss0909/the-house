## The Open (~10 minutes, ~15–20 slides)

Ten components. Keep it brief — move through the open in 10 minutes or less.

### 1. Title Slide
Sexy title + subtitle. Keep the focus on the desired outcome ("Hawaii" = the dream outcome throughout).

### 2. Who It's For
Hype them up and call them forward.
> "This webinar is for [this type of person] who wants to achieve [outcome]."

### 3. What You're Covering & What They Get
What are they getting? Why should they stay?
> "In the next 45 minutes I'm gonna cover how you can get ___ without ever doing ___."

### 4. Your Promise to Them
One sentence tied to the "one thing" of the webinar. Should match registration page bullets.
> "By the end of this webinar you will…"

### 5. The Housekeeping
How to comment, ask questions, navigate the tech, get the workbook. Brief.
> "Okay, now before we get into the goods, let me just check in…"

### 6. Call Them Out (Gently)
Name their current struggle. What's the price of staying where they are? Specific examples.
> "These are just symptoms of a bigger problem."

### 7. The 'Real' Problem
Reframe: introduce "the shifts." Their problems are symptoms of a deeper issue.
> "The problems you're having are symptoms of a bigger problem. The real problem is that you haven't made the shifts. Once you make them…"

### 8. Future Pace
What happens when they make the shifts? 2–4 bullets on what will change.
> "Once you make these shifts you will… [outcome/Hawaii]"

### 9. Flex Your Credibility
What's your specialty? What results have you gotten? Keep short and sweet.
> "Before we go forward, let me introduce myself."

### 10. How You Discovered the Shifts
Your story from the perspective of how it helps *them*. One minute max. Show yourself in the nightmare before → dream results after.
> "There are [number] key shifts you must make to [outcome]. I'm going to walk you through all of them right now."

## The Shifts (Teaching Body)

Each shift = a perspective shift, not a full how-to lesson. Goal: show them WHAT to do and WHY, not the full HOW (that's what the program is for).

### Shift structure
- **Shift 1:** Shift their belief around the problem (you *think* the problem is X, it's actually Y)
- **Shift 2:** Share the solution
- **Shift 3:** Share one fringe benefit of the solution (not only will you get X, you'll also get Y)

### Case Studies
Pair each shift with proof: client results, your own results, or public-figure examples. These prove the shift works with real people — not testimonials for the program.

### Framing rule
> THIS is the old way → HERE is why the old way is BS → HERE is the new way (your way) → HERE is why it's superior.

Focus on getting them to *see differently*, not on teaching everything they need to know.

## The Close (5 components)

Confidence is key. The offer is the logical next step at this point — no apologetic language.

### 1. The Promise
> "Today I promised…" — recap the promise from the open. Prove you delivered.

### 2. The Recap
Quickly recap the shifts.
> "These [N] shifts alone are enough to [outcome they want]."

### 3. Now You Have a Choice
Two paths: DIY with what you just gave them OR get an even better outcome with help.
> "You can DIY this using the shifts I've just shown you OR you can [outcome here] and not only that, but [more outcome]. If you choose option 2, here's what I've got for you…"

### 4. How I Can Help & The Pitch

**If booking calls:** Show how to book, why you take free calls, what they get out of it. Future pace the booking experience.

**If pitching directly:**
- Main outcome of the offer (related to the webinar)
- How it works + what's included (phases, deliverables — keep coming back to what they'll BE/DO/HAVE)
- Program bonuses — lead with the urgency bonus (only available during the webinar)
- Testimonials — screenshots do social selling for you
- Multiple CTAs every few slides — URL, payment options, keep selling
- Future pace — what happens right after they buy? When's the next call? What can they start immediately?
