# Hot Take

## When to choose this framework
Choose Hot Take when the claim needs receipts before the reader will trust the rebuild. The post states something the reader half-suspects, then proves it hard enough that the old belief collapses. Write with market pressure, plus one inner-pressure relief beat so the reader does not feel blamed. Do not confuse it with House Report: House Report teaches what is changing and what it means; Hot Take prosecutes the broken belief before rebuilding.

## Content-role cautions
- Freebie: risky pairing. A small freebie can feel too small after a big prosecution. Only use it if the freebie is the honest first step of the rebuild.
- Product or template: do not reveal early. The receipts must land first.
- Webinar or class: strong pairing. The class can carry the full rebuild the post only sketches.
- Paid offer: this framework's natural home. Earn the ask with stacked proof, then present the offer as the rebuild. Only run Hot Take when the offer can carry a bigger strategic fix, like [[Library/Offers/back-of-house/MAP|Back of House]].
- Waitlist: works if the waitlist is for the rebuild. Sell the world after the collapse.
- Affinity: do not use Hot Take for a no-CTA post. Outrage with no next step leaves the reader worse off.

## Offer and CTA cautions
The ask has to solve the rebuild, not the outrage. If the offer cannot carry a bigger strategic fix, choose a different framework. Kill the obvious escape route before the pivot, and teach the rebuild in proof-backed parts so the reader is not left with only alarm.

## Failure modes
- Concept named as a lesson or clever mechanism instead of a simple promise.
- Curriculum vocabulary anywhere near the copy ("belief move," "reframe," "the formula").
- Re-skin of the Mar 3 winner: "X is dying" with a new noun is a re-skin, not a take.
- Quirky-conceit cover instead of the plain scary claim.
- Framework-specific: outrage with thin receipts. If proof cannot stack until the old belief has nowhere to hide, the post collapses into a rant.
- Framework-specific: skipping the relief beat or the villain. Without them the reader feels blamed for obeying the broken system.
