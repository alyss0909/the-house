# Hot Take

Use this card to write one Hot Take carousel. Do not load the full [[Studio/Analysis/carousel-slide-by-slide]] during normal writing. This card is the compressed source packet.

## When To Use This

Choose Hot Take when the reader will not believe the claim unless the post proves it first.

The job: state the thing the reader half-suspects, stack receipts until the old belief collapses, then teach the rebuild.

This is not House Report. House Report explains a shift. Hot Take prosecutes a broken belief before rebuilding.

## Framework Outline

Fill this before drafting.

1. **Scary or disruptive claim:** the plain claim.
2. **Lived validation:** what the reader is already experiencing.
3. **Proof 1:** first receipt.
4. **Proof 2:** second receipt.
5. **Proof 3:** third receipt.
6. **Escape route to kill:** the obvious objection or "maybe I can still..." move.
7. **Villain:** who benefits if the reader keeps obeying the broken system.
8. **Rebuild:** 3 proof-backed parts.
9. **CTA bridge:** why the offer carries the rebuild.

## Carousel Outline

Map the framework onto the spine.

1. **Cover hook:** plain scary claim, contrarian claim, or provocative reframe.
2. **Slide 2 open loop:** validate the thing the reader already feels.
3. **Old way/problem:** name the belief or system that is breaking.
4. **Proof or validation:** stack receipts until the old belief has nowhere to hide.
5. **Teaching middle:** explain what the receipts mean, then teach the rebuild.
6. **Turn:** villain reveal or system diagnosis.
7. **CTA:** the offer carries the rebuild, not the outrage.

## Pressure And Proof

Pressure choice:

- **Market pressure:** the default. Something outside the reader changed or was built against them.
- **Inner relief beat:** add one so the reader does not feel blamed.

Proof types:

- **Data proof:** stats, reports, platform numbers, benchmark comparisons.
- **Receipt stack:** multiple proof slides before the rebuild.
- **Villain proof:** who benefits from the broken system.
- **Rebuild proof:** the better system solves the problem the receipts made obvious.

## Hook Options

Use one of these shapes:

- Contrarian Claim.
- Plain Scary Claim.
- Provocative Reframe only when the weird frame immediately gets proven.
- Identity Flattery if the reader needs to feel smart for opting out.

No "X is dying" noun swap. That is a re-skin of the source winner.

## Alyssa Move Library

### State The Scary Thing Plainly

What this does: makes the claim impossible to ignore.

How Alyssa does it:

- Uses a short declarative cover.
- Lets the claim be alarming without adding hype.
- Does not soften the claim before proof arrives.

Source pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-03-organic-marketing-is-dying]] starts with alarm, then earns it with receipts.

Write toward: "the system you are feeding is breaking the deal."

Do not write: "the marketing landscape is undergoing a major shift."

### Validate The Lived Experience

What this does: tells the reader they are not imagining it.

How Alyssa does it:

- Names the symptom before the explanation.
- Gives one relief beat before the prosecution starts.
- Does not make the reader the problem.

Source pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-03-organic-marketing-is-dying]] validates the experience before showing the receipts.

Write toward: "if it feels like this got harder, that is not in your head."

Do not write: "you are struggling because you are relying on outdated tactics."

### Stack Receipts

What this does: makes the old belief collapse before the solution appears.

How Alyssa does it:

- Uses more than one proof slide.
- Makes each receipt mean something for the reader.
- Does not jump to the fix after one point.
- Lets the reader feel "oh, so I'm not imagining this."

Source pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-03-organic-marketing-is-dying]] uses proof, proof, proof before villain and rebuild.

Write toward: "here is the receipt, and here is what it means for your next move."

Do not write: "studies show this is becoming less effective."

### Villain Reveal

What this does: protects the reader from blame and gives the outrage a target.

How Alyssa does it:

- Names the system or platform incentive.
- Shows who benefits if the reader keeps feeding the old machine.
- Avoids making the reader the problem.

Source pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-03-organic-marketing-is-dying]] turns frustration into a system diagnosis before rebuilding.

Write toward: "this was built to keep extracting effort."

Do not write: "you need to stop relying on outdated tactics."

### Teach The Rebuild

What this does: gives the reader somewhere to go after the collapse.

How Alyssa does it:

- Teaches a strategic fix in proof-backed parts.
- Makes the offer feel like the full rebuild, not a random product.
- Does not leave the reader with only alarm.

Source pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-03-organic-marketing-is-dying]] moves from alarm to proof to villain to rebuild.

Write toward: "here is the back end that keeps working when the front end stops giving."

Do not write: "here are three ways to future-proof your strategy."

## Content-Role Cautions

- **Freebie:** risky. A small freebie can feel too small after a big prosecution unless it is the honest first step.
- **Product/template:** do not reveal early. Receipts land first.
- **Webinar/class:** strong pairing if the class carries the full rebuild.
- **Paid offer:** natural fit if the offer solves the bigger strategic problem.
- **Waitlist:** works if the waitlist is for the rebuild.

## Failure Modes

- Outrage with thin receipts.
- One proof slide, then a pitch.
- Skipping the relief beat or villain.
- CTA too small for the argument.
- "X is dying" as a noun swap.
- Generic trend language instead of receipts.
