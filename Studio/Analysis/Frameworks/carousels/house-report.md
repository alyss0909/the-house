# House Report

## When to choose this framework
Choose House Report when the idea is stronger as analysis than as advice. The reader needs to believe the market moved before the CTA feels useful. If your idea is "here is what is changing and what it means for your next move," this is your framework. Write slides 2 to 4 with market pressure: shift, proof, cost, implication, better path.

## Content-role cautions
- Freebie: teach enough that the comment-word feels useful, not needy. The final ask can be simple if the deck already taught the first useful move.
- Product or template: reveal early only if the product itself is the teaching surface. House Report usually earns the product late, after the shift is proven.
- Webinar or class: create appetite for the full method. Leave room for the class to carry it.
- Paid offer: earn the ask with stronger belief-building first. The reader must believe the shift before the offer appears.
- Waitlist: sell the world and the future payoff more than immediate action.
- Affinity: skip the forced CTA. End with recognition or a soft invitation.

## Offer and CTA cautions
The ask comes after real analysis. The offer cannot appear as the answer before the reader believes the shift. CTA points to the tool or freebie that helps them take the first smarter move the post named.

## Failure modes
- Concept named as a lesson or clever mechanism instead of a simple promise.
- Curriculum vocabulary anywhere near the copy ("belief move," "reframe," "the formula").
- Re-skin of a published winner: metaphor swapped, skeleton kept.
- Quirky-conceit cover instead of a direct claim the reader suspects is true.
- Framework-specific: trend list with no implication. Every shift needs a "what this means for your next move" beat, and one lower-maintenance alternative so the smarter move feels lighter, not just more work.
- Framework-specific: sliding into Hot Take prosecution. House Report teaches implications; it does not put the old belief on trial.
