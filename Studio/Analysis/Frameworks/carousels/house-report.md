# House Report

## When to use this
Choose House Report when the idea is stronger as analysis than as advice. The reader needs to believe the market moved before the CTA feels useful. If the idea is "here is what is changing and what it means for your next move," this is the framework.

## The forward sequence (this IS the slide plan; fill it, then write)

1. Name the shift the reader can already feel but has not fully sorted yet.
2. Establish why Alyssa is qualified to call the shift.
3. Name the old move that is getting weaker.
4. Show what staying with the old move will cost next.
5. Teach 2 specific shifts, each with a "what this means for your next move" slide.
6. Give one small action that lowers the reader's workload now.
7. CTA to the tool or freebie that helps them take that first action.

The middle requirements are folded into the steps: step 4 is the implication pressure, step 5 carries the proof-and-implication pairs, step 6 is the lighter-alternative beat. Do not restate them as a separate checklist.

Old belief it attacks: the old plan is still the safest plan.
New belief it installs: the market moved, so the smarter move is to build around what lasts now.

CTA rule: the ask comes after real analysis. The offer cannot appear as the answer before the reader believes the shift.

## Content-role cautions
- Freebie: teach enough that the comment-word feels useful, not needy. The final ask can be simple if the deck already taught the first useful move.
- Product or template: reveal early only if the product itself is the teaching surface. House Report usually earns the product late, after the shift is proven.
- Webinar or class: create appetite for the full method. Leave room for the class to carry it.
- Paid offer: earn the ask with stronger belief-building first.
- Waitlist: sell the world and the future payoff more than immediate action.
- Affinity: skip the forced CTA. End with recognition or a soft invitation.

## Failure modes
- Trend list with no implication. Every shift needs a "what this means for your next move" beat.
- Sliding into Hot Take prosecution. House Report teaches implications; it does not put the old belief on trial.
- Concept named as a lesson or clever mechanism instead of a simple promise.
- Curriculum vocabulary anywhere near the copy ("belief move," "reframe," "the formula").
- Re-skin of a published winner: metaphor swapped, skeleton kept.
- Quirky-conceit cover instead of a direct claim the reader suspects is true.
