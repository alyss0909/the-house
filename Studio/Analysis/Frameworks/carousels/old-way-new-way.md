# Old Way / New Way

## When to choose this framework
Choose Old Way / New Way when the reader needs permission to leave something they have been treating as responsible, obvious, or required. The old way made sense once, but it is costing them now. Use market pressure if the old way broke because conditions changed. Use inner pressure if the old way is making the reader blame themselves. Validate why they chose the old way so they never feel stupid, then make it feel too expensive to keep choosing.

## Content-role cautions
- Freebie: the final ask can be simple if the deck already taught the first useful move away from the old pattern.
- Product or template: reveal early only when the product itself is the teaching surface. Usually the product is the exit door, so it lands after the cost is felt.
- Webinar or class: leave room for the class to carry the full new model. The post creates the appetite to switch.
- Paid offer: earn the ask with stronger belief-building first. The reader must feel the cost of staying before the offer makes sense.
- Waitlist: sell the calmer world on the other side more than immediate action.
- Affinity: skip the forced CTA. Relief and recognition can be the whole job.

## Offer and CTA cautions
The ask should feel like the next step after relief, not a product interrupt. CTA points to the offer that helps them leave the old pattern.

## Failure modes
- Concept named as a lesson or clever mechanism instead of a simple promise.
- Curriculum vocabulary anywhere near the copy ("belief move," "reframe," "the formula").
- Re-skin of a published winner: metaphor swapped, skeleton kept. The Feb 24 swap post is published; do not re-skin its skeleton with a new channel pair.
- Quirky-conceit cover instead of a direct identity swap or promise.
- Framework-specific: skipping the steelman. If you never validate why the old way made sense, the reader feels blamed and stops swiping.
- Framework-specific: pitching the new way as more effort in the same place. It has to read as a different system, not harder work.
