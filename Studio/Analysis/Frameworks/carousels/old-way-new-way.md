# Old Way / New Way

Use this card to write one Old Way / New Way carousel. Do not load the full [[Studio/Analysis/carousel-slide-by-slide]] during normal writing. This card is the compressed source packet.

## When To Use This

Choose Old Way / New Way when the reader needs permission to leave something they have been treating as responsible, obvious, or required.

The job: make the old way feel too expensive to keep choosing, then give the calmer or more durable model.

## The forward sequence (this IS the slide plan; pick ONE of her two, fill it, then write)

**The Exit** (use when the reader needs permission to leave something responsible-feeling):

1. Name the old belief the reader already suspects is failing.
2. Steelman why that belief used to make sense.
3. Show why it is breaking now.
4. Name the cost they will keep paying if they stay there: pressure, volume, exhaustion, disappearing content, bad-fit attention.
5. Ask the better question that makes the old belief feel incomplete.
6. Teach the calmer model in 2 or 3 parts.
7. CTA to the offer that helps them leave the old pattern.

**The Pivot** (use when the post can open on identity, a personal switch, a weird claim, or a specific promise):

1. Hook with identity, a personal switch, a weird claim, or a specific promise.
2. Name the false premise or old belief.
3. Agitate the cost of that belief.
4. Validate the reader's experience so they do not feel blamed, and protect them from feeling stupid for choosing the old way.
5. Make the pivot: one line that makes the better way feel obvious.
6. Teach the better model in 2 to 4 concrete parts.
7. CTA to the resource, product, class, or offer that helps them make the pivot.

The middle requirements are folded into the steps (validation is step 4, the pivot line is step 5). Do not restate them as a separate checklist.

Old belief it attacks: the thing they have been trying is the responsible thing to keep doing.
New belief it installs: the calmer, stranger, more specific move is actually the more strategic move.

CTA rule: the ask should feel like the next step after the pivot, not a product interrupt.

## Pressure And Proof

Pressure choice:

- **Inner pressure:** use when the old way makes the reader blame herself.
- **Market pressure:** use when the old way broke because the market, platform, or category changed.

Proof types:

- **Validation proof:** name the feeling so precisely the reader trusts the diagnosis.
- **Lived proof:** Alyssa's own switch, client pattern, inbox pattern, or business evidence.
- **Cost proof:** maintenance burden, pressure, volume, exhaustion, disappearing content, bad-fit attention.
- **Example proof:** concrete before/after or old-way/new-way scenario.

## Hook Options

Use one of these shapes:

- Identity Swap.
- Old-Way Callout.
- Specific Promise without the old maintenance burden.
- Contrarian Claim when the old way is culturally protected.

No cute metaphor cover if the argument is about leaving a costly pattern.

## Alyssa Move Library

### Steelman The Old Belief

What this does: protects the reader before making the old way less desirable.

How Alyssa does it:

- Admits why the old move used to be obvious.
- Makes the reader feel reasonable, not behind.
- Uses the concession to set up the alternative.

Source pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-24-swapped-instagram-for-pinterest]] protects the reader from feeling stupid for choosing Instagram before showing why it got too expensive to keep feeding.

Write toward: "of course you tried it; it was the obvious move for a long time."

Do not write: "you have been wasting your time on the wrong strategy."

### Show What Staying Costs

What this does: makes the old way feel expensive before introducing the better model.

How Alyssa does it:

- Names a cost the reader already feels: pressure, volume, exhaustion, disappearing content, bad-fit attention.
- Makes the old way sound like too much maintenance.
- Protects the reader first, then makes the old way less desirable.
- Does not say "you're stuck because" or "this is why you're failing."

Source pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-24-swapped-instagram-for-pinterest]] makes the platform feel too expensive to keep feeding. [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-20-whats-actually-shifting-in-2026-pt-2]] makes the old marketing move weaker because the market shifted, not because the reader is bad at business.

Write toward: "the old move asks for more than it gives back now."

Do not write: "if you keep doing this, you'll stay overwhelmed and inconsistent."

### Validate Without Blaming

What this does: gives relief before the pivot.

How Alyssa does it:

- Names the exhaustion without making it a character flaw.
- Assigns blame to the maintenance burden, not the reader.
- Lets the reader feel smart for wanting out.

Source pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-24-swapped-instagram-for-pinterest]] uses the platform's high-maintenance behavior as the problem, not the reader's effort.

Write toward: "it makes sense that this started feeling heavy."

Do not write: "you need to stop being inconsistent."

### Ask The Better Question

What this does: makes the old belief feel incomplete.

How Alyssa does it:

- Changes the decision criteria.
- Moves from effort to durability, maintenance, fit, or self-trust.
- Lets the better model feel obvious.

Source pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-20-whats-actually-shifting-in-2026-pt-2]] turns the question from "how do I keep up?" into "what keeps working?"

Write toward: "what if the issue is not consistency, but where the work goes after you make it?"

Do not write: "the real question is how to optimize your system."

### Teach The New Model

What this does: gives relief a practical shape.

How Alyssa does it:

- Teaches 2 to 3 concrete shifts, not a full course.
- Keeps the new way calmer, lighter, or more durable.
- Makes the offer the container for leaving the old pattern.

Source pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-24-swapped-instagram-for-pinterest]] moves from platform exhaustion into a calmer search-based system.

Write toward: "here is the smaller, sturdier move."

Do not write: "now implement a scalable content ecosystem."

## Content-Role Cautions

- **Freebie:** works when the deck gives one useful first move away from the old pattern.
- **Product/template:** usually lands after the cost is felt, unless the product itself teaches.
- **Webinar/class:** creates appetite for the full new model.
- **Paid offer:** the reader must feel the cost of staying before the ask.
- **Waitlist:** sell the calmer world on the other side.

## Failure Modes

- Skipping the steelman.
- Generic cost language.
- Blaming the reader.
- New way is just more effort in the same place.
- Re-skinning the Feb 24 identity-swap skeleton with a new channel pair.
- Framework language in public copy.
