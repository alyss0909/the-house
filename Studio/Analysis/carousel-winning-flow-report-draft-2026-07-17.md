# Carousel Winning Flow Report

Goal: give the content team clear buyer-psychology carousel flows, backed by Alyssa's real examples, so an angle plus an offer becomes a persuasive post instead of a generic summary.

This is the simple version.

The real winners do not work because they follow one master template. They work because each one makes a reader accept one new premise before the ask shows up.

That is the job.

Use this file forward. The examples prove the pattern, but the instructions below are the repeatable jobs for the next carousel. Do not write a historical summary of what an old post did. Choose the flow, then make each slide perform the same persuasion job for the new angle and offer.

## The Flows

### 1. Market Shift Report

Examples: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-15-slow-marketing-trends-pt-1]], [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-20-whats-actually-shifting-in-2026-pt-2]], [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-29-smart-founders-going-low-maintenance]]

Sequence:

1. Name the shift the reader can already feel but has not fully sorted yet.
2. Establish why Alyssa is qualified to call the shift.
3. Name the old move that is getting weaker.
4. Show what staying with the old move will cost next.
5. Teach 2 specific shifts, each with a "what this means for your next move" slide.
6. Give one small action that lowers the reader's workload now.
7. CTA to the tool or freebie that helps them take that first action.

Old belief it attacks: the thing that worked last year is still the safe plan.

New belief it installs: the market has moved, and Alyssa noticed before the reader had language for it.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-15-slow-marketing-trends-pt-1]], [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-20-whats-actually-shifting-in-2026-pt-2]], and [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-29-smart-founders-going-low-maintenance]] all make the reader feel early to a shift, then hand them a lower-maintenance next move.

### 2. Broken Channel Pivot

Examples: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-20-whats-actually-shifting-in-2026-pt-2]], [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-24-swapped-instagram-for-pinterest]]

Sequence:

1. Name the old belief the reader already suspects is failing.
2. Steelman why that belief used to make sense.
3. Show why it is breaking now.
4. Name the cost they will keep paying if they stay there: pressure, volume, exhaustion, disappearing content, bad-fit attention.
5. Ask the better question that makes the old belief feel incomplete.
6. Teach the calmer model in 2 or 3 parts.
7. CTA to the offer that helps them leave the old pattern.

Old belief it attacks: more eyeballs means more sales.

New belief it installs: better-fit attention beats more attention if the current channel is making them work too hard for the wrong people.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-24-swapped-instagram-for-pinterest]] and [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-20-whats-actually-shifting-in-2026-pt-2]] both protect the reader from feeling stupid for using the old way, then make the old way feel too expensive to keep choosing.

### 3. Product Walkthrough

Example: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-06-pinterest-marketing-for-beginners]]

Sequence:

1. Name the concrete result the reader wants.
2. State the philosophy behind the product.
3. Show the product early as the small solution.
4. Turn every feature into an objection-killer.
5. Teach through the product: what to plan, what to use, what to reuse, what to stop overcomplicating.
6. Drop the deeper diagnosis late: the real problem is not the missing asset, it is the missing system.
7. CTA again with the product positioned as the easiest first system.

Old belief it attacks: I need to understand the whole strategy before I can use the tool.

New belief it installs: the tool is the easiest first version of the strategy.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-06-pinterest-marketing-for-beginners]] sells early without feeling thin because the product is also the teaching surface. Copy the job, not the exact structure.

### 4. Recipe System

Example: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-10-get-clients-from-pinterest]]

Sequence:

1. Make a specific outcome promise.
2. Name the hidden opportunity most people are underusing.
3. Teach the method as a named metaphor system.
4. Give 4 steps, each with a job and a concrete instruction.
5. Make the steps feel easier than the reader expected.
6. Ask if they want the full system.
7. CTA to the product that packages the method.

Old belief it attacks: Pinterest is a cute traffic extra.

New belief it installs: Pinterest becomes predictable when the pieces sit in the right order.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-10-get-clients-from-pinterest]] proves a metaphor can carry the teaching order. Future decks need their own metaphor world that makes the method easier to remember.

### 5. Proof-First Plan

Example: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-19-pinterest-sugar-mama]]

Sequence:

1. Use a weird visual joke to stop the scroll.
2. Immediately prove the joke has a real business reason behind it.
3. Name the opportunity the reader has been underestimating.
4. Give a 4-step plan from scratch.
5. Add examples inside the steps so it feels usable.
6. Move from plan to class, product, or resource invite.
7. End with a soft brand close so the CTA does not feel like a hard stop.

Old belief it attacks: Pinterest is for inspiration, not buyers.

New belief it installs: Pinterest already has buyer intent, so the job is to catch it with the right funnel.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-19-pinterest-sugar-mama]] turns a joke into a plan by proving the opportunity before teaching steps.

### 6. Receipts And Rebuild

Example: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-03-organic-marketing-is-dying]]

Sequence:

1. State the scary thing plainly.
2. Validate what the reader is already experiencing.
3. Stack proof until the old belief has nowhere to hide.
4. Kill the obvious escape route.
5. Name who benefits if the reader keeps obeying the broken system.
6. Pivot into what Alyssa would build instead.
7. Teach the rebuild in 3 proof-backed parts.
8. CTA to the bigger offer because the small fix would not be enough.

Old belief it attacks: organic reach is down because the algorithm is being annoying again.

New belief it installs: the platform game benefits the platform, so the rational move is to build a back end that keeps working without feeding it every day.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-03-organic-marketing-is-dying]] shows how to move from alarm to proof to villain to rebuild. Future decks should use the same persuasion job only when the offer can carry a bigger strategic fix, like [[Library/Offers/back-of-house/MAP|Back of House]].

### 7. Clock-In Plan

Example: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-01-80-days-until-summer-90d-clock-in]]

Sequence:

1. Name the time window.
2. Explain why that window creates momentum.
3. Show the payoff if they use the window well.
4. Give 3 operating rules for the season.
5. Break the season into monthly jobs.
6. Make the unfinished work feel like assets, not failure.
7. CTA to the dashboard that helps them clock in and finish.

Old belief it attacks: unfinished ideas mean I am behind.

New belief it installs: unfinished ideas are assets if I clock in for one focused shift and close the right loops.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-01-80-days-until-summer-90d-clock-in]] shows how a date becomes a work container. Future productivity decks should turn time into a focused shift, then attach the offer to finishing.

### 8. Permission Litany

Examples: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-22-wasting-time-for-the-girls]], [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-14-valentines-for-the-girls]]

Sequence:

1. Name the guilt or pressure.
2. Give the reader a more generous interpretation of that behavior.
3. Repeat short permission lines in the same visual world.
4. Include one line that turns the whole post into a principle.
5. Pause on the brand.
6. End with a question, sign-off, or soft invitation.

Old belief it attacks: taking time, changing your mind, or moving slowly means you are lazy.

New belief it installs: the slower choice can be the higher-standard choice.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-22-wasting-time-for-the-girls]] and [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-14-valentines-for-the-girls]] show how to make the reader feel named without teaching a framework.

## Carousel Anatomy

### Cover Page

4 to 10 words in the real winners. New covers should aim for 7 or fewer unless the time anchor earns the extra words.

Forward job: give the reader one clear reason to swipe. A market shift, a time window, a promise, a personal switch, a visual joke, or a permission line.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-03-organic-marketing-is-dying]] is 4 words. [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-15-slow-marketing-trends-pt-1]] is 4 words plus the year. [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-10-get-clients-from-pinterest]] uses 10 words because the promise and time window are doing real work.

Never does: vague topic label. Generic "tips." Question-mark cover. Clean little reframe that resolves itself before the reader swipes.

### Slide 2

Headline: usually 4 to 10 words.

Body: anywhere from 0 to 55 words, depending on the lane.

Forward job: tell the reader why this post matters before the teaching starts. Use credibility, stakes, a false belief, a hidden opportunity, or proof that the claim has receipts.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-20-whats-actually-shifting-in-2026-pt-2]] uses credibility. [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-24-swapped-instagram-for-pinterest]] uses a false belief. [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-10-get-clients-from-pinterest]] uses a hidden opportunity plus an early product seed.

Never does: starts with generic teaching. The reader needs the reason first.

### Problem And Stakes

Usually 1 to 3 slides after the cover.

Forward job: make the old way feel specific, costly, or annoying before the fix appears.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-24-swapped-instagram-for-pinterest]] and [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-03-organic-marketing-is-dying]] both make the current path feel expensive before offering the new one.

Leaves them with: "that is the thing I have been feeling."

### Middle Slides

Usually 4 to 6 teaching or proof slides.

Headline: usually 3 to 7 words.

Body: 25 to 65 words when teaching.

Forward job: teach one named move per slide, with enough detail to use. The slide needs a claim, a detail, and a reason it matters.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-10-get-clients-from-pinterest]] teaches four steps. [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-03-organic-marketing-is-dying]] uses three proof slides and three rebuild slides. [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-15-slow-marketing-trends-pt-1]] teaches two trends with implications and a next move.

Never does: thin label plus filler sentence. Clean parallel slides with no real number, example, screenshot, or named mechanic.

Always leaves them with: one usable thing a non-buyer could act on.

### The Turn

Usually lands around slide 5 to 8.

Forward job: change the explanation. Name the real problem, the real cost, or the better frame.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-15-slow-marketing-trends-pt-1]] uses a bait-and-switch turn. [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-06-pinterest-marketing-for-beginners]] uses a system diagnosis. [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-03-organic-marketing-is-dying]] uses a villain diagnosis.

Never does: relies on a pretty background switch to carry the point. The visual shift helps the reader feel the moment, but the copy has to do the thinking.

### CTA

Usually final 1 to 2 slides.

Forward job: give the next step that solves the exact problem the deck just made obvious.

Proof pattern: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-01-20-whats-actually-shifting-in-2026-pt-2]] moves from owned channels to Comment List. [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-10-get-clients-from-pinterest]] moves from the Pinterest recipe to the planner. [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-03-01-80-days-until-summer-90d-clock-in]] moves from Q2 energy to Comment Clock.

Exception: [[Library/Examples/Carousels/2026-02-06-pinterest-marketing-for-beginners]] sells earlier because the product is the teaching surface.

Never does: asks for a product that the post did not earn.

## Best Practices

- Every education or offer winner teaches one concrete thing.
- FTG posts do a different job: they make the reader feel named.
- The cover gives a clear reason to swipe.
- Slide 2 gives the reason to keep reading.
- The problem comes before the fix.
- The fix comes with proof, not decoration.
- The middle is dense. Real numbers, examples, screenshots, steps, coined terms.
- The offer matches the argument.
- The CTA word is a gift mechanic, not engagement bait.
- The post leaves the old way less desirable than it felt on slide 1.

## External Craft Check

The Tinna Loaiza PDF intake lives at [[Studio/Content/craft-references/2026-07-09-tinna-loaiza-5-carousel-patterns]]. It is useful as a structure check only.

Keep:

- intimacy: the reader should feel specifically seen
- deprivation curiosity: the post should withhold something worth swiping for
- tension: the post should hold two believable paths before resolving
- visual repetition: the deck should look recognizably like itself
- narrative: the slides should move, not dump

Do not use it for Alyssa voice, offer truth, or proof. If it disagrees with [[Studio/Analysis/carousel-slide-by-slide]], the real carousels win.
