# The one-word reply rule

**When someone replies a single word to one of Alyssa's emails, this is what happens.**
Locked 2026-07-20. Built from her Right Time Offer teaching, three of her real past
replies, and her own drafted RTO reply copy.

Related: [[README]] · [[Library/Examples/soft-sundays/3-emails-to-ctrl-c-and-money]] ·
[[Studio/Analysis/boh-teaching-index-phases-3-5]]

---

## 1. What a one-word reply actually is

**It is NOT a request for a freebie.** "Reply VAULT and I'll send you the details"
means details about **joining Back of House**. The Email Prompt Vault was just the
one feature named to make the offer tangible that week.

It is the **second half of a Right Time Offer.** Her own teaching, from the Lazy
Launch material: *"No logistics in the initial email. Save those for after they
reply."* The email deliberately under-sells as an "oh, and btw" moment, so **the
reply is where the selling actually happens.**

Her four stated reasons RTOs work: you slip into a conversation they're already in,
you highlight ONE feature to make it tangible, you give a reason to join NOW, and
the reply-word makes the next step effortless.

**So the reply's content is inherited from whichever email carried the word.** That
is why she pitches something different every time and why a fixed template breaks
the mechanic. Always trace the word back to its broadcast in Kit first.

---

## 2. Look them up BEFORE writing. Every time.

This is the step that makes it feel personal instead of automated. Pull from Kit:

- `list_subscribers` with their email, `include: [tags, fields]`
- `list_tags_for_a_subscriber` — what they've **bought** vs what they've **opted into**
- `list_stats_for_a_subscriber` — open rate, click rate, how long they've been around

Then use it **as a genuine question, never as a receipt.**

> **WRONG:** "you already own half my stuff" / "you've been on this list since 2023
> and open basically everything"
>
> **RIGHT:** "btw i see you've got [thing] — how's that treating you? we go way
> deeper on [that same thing] inside BOH, so if you're [situation]..."

Her ruling, verbatim: *"its ok to mention the things that they have but not in the
way you did you can say more like by the way i see you have X thing how is it
treating you? we go way more into depth on X thing inside boh."*

Reciting their history at them reads as surveillance. Asking how it's going reads as
someone who remembers them. **It also opens a real conversation**, which is the whole
point, and it bridges naturally into what BOH adds on top of the thing they own.

Natural bridges: a freebie or product they hold → the BOH phase that goes deeper on
it. Pinterest Planner → Phase 3, the traffic system. Year of Emails → the sequence
and vault side. Bingeable Newsletters → the simmer.

## 3. The skeleton

Short and sweet. Her real replies run around 110 to 160 words. Long essay-shaped
replies with self-select lists get rejected — that is broadcast furniture, not a
one-to-one reply.

1. **Warm named opener, with the apology tucked into the same line in brackets.**
   Her exact shape, edited by her hand 2026-07-20:
   > `Lily! ok i loooove that you're curious about Back of House 😏 (and sry for the
   > late reply my spam is being SO strict lately, just rescued you from there lol)`

   The lateness never gets its own paragraph. It rides inside the warm line as a
   joke where the spam filter is the villain, not her.

   **⚠️ NEVER use the same excuse on the same person twice. Read the thread history
   first.** The spam line is reusable *across* people, not *with* one person. Caught
   on 2026-07-20: Lily was told "OMGAWD I just found you in my spammmm" on May 11,
   and the new draft told her the spam filter got her again. To one reader that is
   either a lie or someone who never opens her inbox, and it burns the goodwill the
   line exists to create.

   **When it IS a repeat, own the repeat and make that the joke instead:**
   > `ok i just went back through our emails and realised i ALSO told you you were in
   > my spam back in may 🫠 at which point it stops being a spam problem and starts
   > being an alyssa problem. sorry for the wait, again.`

   That still dissolves the lateness (she is the butt of it), but it's honest, and
   naming her own pattern is more charming than repeating a worn excuse.

   **Standing check before any late-reply opener:** search the thread and past mail
   from that address, see how she apologised last time, and use a different door.
2. **A quick rundown of what's inside**, using **✺** bullets — the sixteen-pointed
   asterisk, U+273A. Not ✷, not ✱, not a hyphen. Lead with the thing their
   reply-word was about.
4. **The fork.** Both doors, always. Her exact wording:
   - **If they're ready:** *"if you already know you're in, I'd love to see you on an
     upcoming call - code HUNGRY gets you $500 off:"* — **the invitation leads, the
     discount follows.** Never open on the money. And it's "code HUNGRY", not bare
     HUNGRY.
   - **If they're not sure:** *"if you're not sure, honestly the best thing is a
     quick chat:"* then the link. **The call is the better outcome** and reads that
     way, not as a consolation prize.
5. **The "by the way" aside** about something they own or grabbed (see §2). **ONE
   short line, in brackets, sitting near the call — never a paragraph and never at
   the top.** Her ruling 2026-07-20: *"too much emphasis on the products they already
   have its coming off werid and also too much at the front of the email its more
   like a small aside not this much text."* It is a light touch that proves you know
   who they are, not the subject of the email.
6. **The low-pressure exit.** *"orrrr just reply with any Qs and i'll help you decide
   if it's the right fit, no pressure either way."* Note her two edits: **"orrrr"**
   with the stretched vowel, and a **comma** before "no pressure", not a period, so
   it stays one breath instead of two flat statements.
6. **`Alyssa`** — bare first name.
7. **PS as conspiracy.** *"(PS: pls don't share this secret code x)"*

## 4. The verified facts

Confirmed by Alyssa 2026-07-20. Nothing else may be invented.

| Thing | Value |
|---|---|
| Booking link | `www.alyssacoleman.ca/chat` |
| Coupon | **HUNGRY**, $500 off, confirmed live |
| Checkout with coupon | `https://alyssacoleman.thrivecart.com/backofhouse/?coupon=HUNGRY` |
| Sales page | `www.alyssacoleman.ca/boh` |
| Bullet character | **✺** (U+273A, sixteen-pointed asterisk) |

**Coupon codes are allowed.** An earlier version of this file banned them; that was
wrong and Alyssa overruled it. HUNGRY is hers and she uses it.

**Never invent scarcity.** "A couple seats left", "opening the vaults this week",
"start Wednesday" are only usable when they are true that week. Her own real-urgency
rule: if someone can just join next quarter, they will. Confirm before writing it.

## 5. What gets rejected

Learned the hard way on 2026-07-20, across two failed attempts:

- **Too long.** 250 words with a full essay arc. Her verdict: *"too long and werid…
  way too long… super ai-coded, big x from me."*
- **Marketing vocabulary.** "raised your hand", "here's what you actually raised your
  hand for". Banned.
- **Broadcast furniture in a one-to-one.** Self-select ✺ lists describing a generic
  reader, constructed closing questions. If she already replied, she has selected
  herself.
- **Burying the fork.** No coupon door and no call door means no next step.
- **Inventing her life.** A draft once claimed she "got lost coming home from the
  antique market." Never invent a detail about her.
- **Two drafts that differ only in formatting.** If the options aren't genuinely
  different, it's one option.
- **Bullets that argue.** She cut "the full pinterest system, **not just the
  planner**" back to "the full pinterest system". Name the thing, don't defend it
  against the freebie they already have.

## 6. The follow-up

Her own drafted low-key follow-up, for when a reply goes quiet:

> Hey again [name]! I'm just circling back in case this got buried (or worse, spam!).
> Let me know what questions you have :)
> I have some time today to chat, or if you want me to resend the info, I'm happy to.
> No rush at all, my kickoff calendar is just filling up and I wanted to make sure I
> didn't want to leave you hangin.
> x Alyssa
