# When Alyssa says "riff with me" on carousels, she means short options in her real slide format — not an explained arc

## Context
Asked to riff on middle-slide ideas for two in-progress carousels, I twice missed: first by inventing a full narrative arc with paragraph-length reasoning per beat (and promoting a throwaway spoken analogy of hers into an entire slide), then by pitching abstract "belief" headlines instead of actual middle-slide content. She called the session a failure. The fix only landed once I stopped reading `carousel-quickref.md` (the abstracted framework) and Tinna Loaiza's swipe file, and instead read her actual real slide images in `Library/Examples/Carousels/`.

## What I learned
Her real middle slides follow one exact, terse shape: a 2-4 word metaphor headline (decodable, not literal — "Bread & Butter," "Take Reservations," "The Key Ingredient"), one short subhead sentence, a tracked-caps body of 2-4 sentences, sometimes a "MY ADVICE:" tactical line, and a prop image. Nothing gets a full slide unless it's already that dense and that short. When she riffs conversationally, a casual analogy or comparison ("this is a dinner party, not dinner") is spoken commentary to me, not slide copy — it does not get promoted into a structured beat unless she explicitly says to build it out. "Just riff, don't turn this into a concept" means: give short options in her actual output register, not a consultant's rationale-per-step writeup. The abstracted framework docs (carousel-quickref.md, external swipe files like Tinna Loaiza) describe the pattern in prose, which is not a substitute for reading the real slides when the task is to match her exact register — prose description flattens the thing that actually matters here, which is length and density.

## When this applies
Any time Alyssa references "my carousel frameworks," shows screenshots of carousels she's already building, or asks for "middle slide" ideas. Go straight to the real slide images in `Library/Examples/Carousels/` for a same-format comparable before proposing anything — don't reach for the framework doc or an external pattern file first, and don't build a multi-step explained arc when she's asked to riff.

## When this does NOT apply
Doesn't apply to brand-new concept/angle work at Rung 1 (Angus's remit) before any slides exist — that's angle-level thinking, not slide-format matching, and the abstracted frameworks are the right tool there. Also doesn't apply once she's approved a direction and asked for full slide copy to be written — that's Hermes's rung and a different task shape entirely.
