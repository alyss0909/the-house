# Over-tidiness is what gets me caught — and invented biography is never the fix for it.

## Context
Four rounds of blind lineups on the 6-post sprint (9 judges). Every legitimate catch across all rounds traced to the same family; both writers independently hit the same fact-discipline trap while trying to fix it.

## What I learned
Calibrated judges never caught me on vocabulary or shapes after lint — they caught me on TIDINESS: evenly-weighted slides, coined maxims used as refrains, the same concept restated in elegant variation, matched-grammar triads, arcs that resolve on a moral. What survives is lopsided weight (one idea gets most of the air, one slide gets almost none), a concept said ONCE and left load-bearing, grammar that breaks mid-framework, endings that stop instead of resolve. Second trap: when told to "add mess/texture," my reflex is to invent a plausible personal anecdote — that is fabricated biography and it is banned even when HALT-disclosed. Mess must come from her real source verbatim, or be biographically neutral (reader-directed derailments, self-interruptions that claim no memory).

## When this applies
- Any carousel/caption/email written for a blind lineup or for publish.
- Any rewrite instruction containing "add texture," "add mess," or "break the tidiness."

## When this does NOT apply
- Structural/labeled-steps registers where even lists are her real habit (register-scoped exemptions in ANTI-AI.md).
- Single-line repeatable formats (guest cheque etc.) where understatement IS the mechanic — don't rough those up.
