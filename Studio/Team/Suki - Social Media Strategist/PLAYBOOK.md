# THE PLAYBOOK — v1

*Our house rules for making a great carousel. Built from Alyssa's real winners first, then real Instagram craft, filtered hard, re-pointed at comments-not-shares. Internal build doc, owned by Suki (Head of Content). Committed 2026-07-08.*

---

## THE NORTH STAR (everything below serves this)

**We are not making posts to go viral. We are making posts that turn strangers into subscribers and buyers.**

> A great cover **stops the scroll** → the middle slides **make them believe something** → the last slide asks them to **comment one word** → that word triggers an automatic DM → the DM gets them on the email list or hands them a coupon → the sale.

The whole post is built backwards from **one keyword comment.** Comments are the money. Shares and reach are the amplifier — they widen the top of the funnel so more strangers reach the comment. We never chase shares for their own sake.

**LOCKED — Comment always wins, EXCEPT trend reports.** When "make it saveable" and "make them comment" pull against each other, the comment wins — *except* on trend-report posts (the "What's Actually Shifting in 2026" lane), which are allowed to be **save-first**, because their whole job is to be bookmarked as reference. That's the one lane where a save beats a comment.

**Pre-publish gut check:** "Would a stranger read this, believe it, and comment the word to get the thing?" (For trend reports: "…and save this to come back to?")

## PART 1 — WHAT MAKES A GREAT HOOK (the cover)

The five things true of every winning cover she's made:

1. **It carries a payoff — one of five kinds:** fix ("...And What To Do Instead"), threat ("Organic Marketing Is Dying"), confession ("Why I Swapped Instagram For Pinterest"), series stake ("Part One"), or joke ("...Sugar Mama"). No payoff = it's a label, not a hook. **Non-negotiable, even when "you" is in the cover.**
2. **4–10 words.** Sweet spot 4–9; ten only when a parenthetical earns it.
3. **It never resolves itself.** A cover opens a loop; it doesn't close one. **Reframes ("it's not X, it's Y") are banned on covers** — they answer themselves, leaving nothing to swipe for. Reframes live deep inside a deck, after the reader cares.
4. **Ends with a period or nothing — never a question mark.** Questions belong on the CTA slide.
5. **LOCKED — "You" on covers is fine, and preferred when it's positive/aspirational.** Second-person is welcome ("You're one email away from your next client.") — lean **positive and aspirational, never accusatory** ("You're doing this wrong" = no). First-person ("Why I…"), identity label ("Beginners"), and flat claim ("Organic Marketing Is Dying") all still work. The payoff rule (#1) holds no matter what.

**Vetted tests we ADD (process, not templates):**
- **First-3-words test** — do the first three words alone create alarm/curiosity?
- **"So what?" test** — ask it once after drafting; if nothing answers, it's a label.
- **Dual-word check** — every cover needs one searchable word (so strangers find it) + one coined/emotional word (the part that feels like her).
- **Batch variety** — in a pitched slate, no more than 2 covers share a hook shape.
- **Winning hooks are reusable assets** — re-run a proven cover later with fresh visuals.

## PART 2 — WHAT MAKES A GREAT ARC (the middle)

1. **6–10 slides, one idea each, portrait 4:5.** Every slide ends with a reason to swipe.
2. **Slide 2 re-hooks, it doesn't start teaching** — raises stakes / names the pain. Highest drop-off slide; treat it like a second cover.
3. **Build the problem 3+ slides before the solution.** Every winner confirms the problem before the fix.
4. **The offer arrives after 60%+ of the deck.** Real teaching first; the sell lands late. Selling is an architecture, not a tone.
5. **Exactly one emotional-validation slide** mid-deck — names the reader's feeling, no product, no CTA. "Earns the right to make the offer."
6. **Real numbers only.** Her real engaged open rates run 34–57%; a cold list is single digits to low teens. **Never "3% open rate."** No number in her data → build without it or flag the gap.
7. **Persuasion lives IN the arc**, not deferred — name and agitate the reader's real pain in plain words. That's what makes it substance, not an outline. The writer makes it *sound* like her; the arc makes the *argument*.

**Vetted craft ADDs:** "so what?" on every teaching claim; ~30 words/body slide, ~8/header; one video slide + music where natural (music = free Reels-feed reach, cheapest lever we have).

## PART 3 — WHAT MAKES A GREAT LAST SLIDE (the comment-CTA — the money)

1. **One CTA per post. Ever.** The whole deck drives one behavior: comment the keyword.
2. **The keyword is a natural word, framed as a gift, never a command.** "Comment PINTEREST and I'll send you the plan," not "COMMENT below." The DM delivers something real and immediate (template, freebie, coupon).
3. **Never the literal last slide** — the CTA is followed by a soft closing slide (warm sign-off / identity line). Her decks land soft.
4. **Comment first, share second, save third.** Build the last slides to also be forwardable ("send this to the friend still emailing like a robot") — the forward widens reach so more people hit the comment.
5. **"Comment YES if you agree" is dead** — generic engagement-bait is suppressed. A keyword that delivers a real asset is compliant and fine; manufactured "comment below!" is a downrank risk.

## PART 4 — THE SWIPE FILE (mechanic mining through our filter — never copying)

**We do NOT want her hooks to inbreed on her own 9 posts.** We keep a **curated swipe file of real, working-right-now covers and first-pages** — pulled from the researched accounts and fresh finds — as **fresh stimulus.**

**The rule: swipe = mechanic mining through our filter, never copying.**
- We pull real current-IG covers/first slides that are working *now* into the swipe file as **inspiration.**
- We take **ZERO verbatim text.** We extract the *mechanic* (what makes it stop the scroll) and rebuild it in her voice, under her cover rules (Part 1).
- Anything that survives only as copied text is dead on arrival. If it can't be rebuilt in her voice, it doesn't make the swipe file.
- The swipe file is refreshed on the monthly scrape of the trusted accounts, plus fresh finds. Its job is to keep the input current so her covers stay fresh — not to hand anyone a template.

**Widen the set of HER OWN winners in the reference set too.** The makers currently calibrate on the top handful — widen it. Gather, in one reference file:
- **Every cover + first slide** from her real carousel folders (`Library/Examples/Carousels/` — all posts, not just the ranked 9), transcribed verbatim as they appear.
- Her For The Girls covers/first slides ([[Library/Examples/Carousels/for-the-girls-slide-copy]]) folded in as their own section.
- Each tagged with which of her 7 hook shapes it is + the metric it won on (from the hook forensics), so a maker can see the *range* of her real voice, not just the greatest hits.

So the reference set = **two layers: her widened own-winners (the spin/voice floor) + the current-IG swipe (fresh mechanic stimulus).** Both feed hooks; neither is ever copied.

## PART 5 — THE FOR THE GIRLS RULE (Friday runs on a DIFFERENT format)

**For The Girls is not a normal carousel — stop applying carousel-hook rules to it.** Friday's rejected hooks failed because the writer applied cover-hook rules (payoff, curiosity gap, comment-CTA) to a lane that runs on none of them. Grounded in her real FTG skill file ([[Hermes/craft/for-the-girls-posts]]) and her real FTG slides ([[Library/Examples/Carousels/for-the-girls-slide-copy]]):

**What For The Girls IS:** a Friday identity / permission / brand-world post. Its job is to make the reader feel *"I already know this in my soul, but I forgot I was allowed to live like this."* A tiny permission slip. It sells the *deeper thing* (space, slower marketing, a business that runs while you live) softly — usually with **no CTA at all.**

**The format rules (different from a normal carousel):**
1. **No payoff-hook on the cover.** The cover is a *declaration or a permission slip*, not a curiosity gap. "Wasting Time Is Part of My Process." is the whole move — it doesn't promise a fix or open a loop. The Part 1 hook rules DO NOT APPLY to Friday.
2. **First-person declarations, not teaching.** "I don't need…", "I'm giving myself permission…", "I trust myself…". She states; she never argues. No proof slides, no numbered steps, no "so what?" test.
3. **Address by identity, not demographic.** "TO: the founder thinking about breaking up with social." The reader recognizes herself in a *type*.
4. **The magic formula — most lines do one of:** reclassify something "unproductive" as work ("closing the app / is part of the work"), give permission without advice ("it's ok to stop / before it's perfect"), name the identity to return to ("for the version of me / who got excited before she got strategic"), or make the business point without saying it ("some ideas / need a system / not a spotlight").
5. **Cadence: short lines, line breaks do the work.** Familiar image → quiet reframe → optional tiny twist. Monospace/concrete-poetry layout; copy must survive being broken oddly.
6. **Tone:** calm, sly, intimate, specific, lightly absurd, emotionally restrained. Never motivational, never an affirmation deck, never "5 ways to optimize your creativity," never a sales page cut into pieces.
7. **CTA: none, or the softest possible** — a waitlist mention or an open community question to close ("What are your January reminders?"). **Friday is exempt from the comment-CTA north star.** These posts earn the highest proportional *saves* because they make people feel seen — and that's the win for this lane.

## PART 6 — KEEP / KILL (the rest of the generic craft)

**KEEP:** hook = ~50% of a post's quality; three-part rejection format (quote line → why it hurts → exact rewrite); benchmark only against her own past averages; odd/specific numbers beat round; music-for-Reels + mixed media; for the future quality gate — subtract-points scoring + run her real posts through to make sure it never flags her own work as fake.

**KILL:** "STOP scrolling / the secret nobody tells you / read this before you post"; "X mistakes killing your Y" listicle-fear (keep the threat, kill the countdown); blanket "cut all adverbs / no passive / short-sentences-only" (**LOCKED — these would flatten her long winding spoken voice; we protect it**); "two beats three"; pure-SEO stuffed titles; "wait for it / POV:" TikTok conventions; "comment YES if you agree" bait; generic SaaS headline formulas; hashtag strategy (dead for reach — keywords in caption replaced it).

*(The old "kill the swipe libraries" line is removed — see Part 4. We keep a filtered swipe file; we just never copy from it.)*
