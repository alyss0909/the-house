## When to come here

- To do the work: pitch, analyze, draft, automate, route.
- To read the team's current thinking — knowing it is opinion, not source.

## The rules that keep the Studio from becoming a junk drawer

- **One live surface per desk.** The current truth-in-flight lives in exactly one place per workflow (Content's is the newest pitch sheet). Re-deriving it means colliding with it — which is how you know to stop and edit the one that exists.
- **No loose files at the Studio root.** Everything lives inside a desk, and every file names its desk or owner in frontmatter.
- **One home per fact** (GL-013). Link, never retype. Facts do not live inside an agent's workspace.
- **Drain at session close.** Every artifact takes a fate: promoted to the Library (with Alyssa's yes), still-working (stays at its desk), or dead → Archive. Nothing accumulates as "interesting."

## Promotion (the one door into the Library)

Studio work becomes trusted only when Alyssa says "promote." Then it moves to the Library with a stamp (`promoted_by: alyssa`, `promoted_date`). No agent promotes its own work; no agent proposes and executes a promotion in one step. And per GL-013: a promotion is not complete until the file is wired into the graph — outbound links to its offer MAP, seeds, format, and neighbors, plus an inbound MAP row. A stamped orphan is a broken promotion.
