### WED — Nobody Opens Your Emails Because Of The First 5 Words
**Cover:** Nobody Opens Your Emails Because Of The First 5 Words
1. Cover: title + hook — "the subject line is the whole email. if it flops, the best email you ever wrote dies in the inbox."
2. Frame: sets up the post — most people write the subject line last, in ten seconds, as a summary of what's inside; a summary is the one thing that never gets opened
3. Proof: three of her real highest-open subject lines with their actual Kit numbers, next to the boring version most people would've sent — "Why I burned down a perfectly good business (on purpose)" 56% opens · "I failed" 45% opens · "re: tmrw's meeting" 57% opens · vs "5 email tips for coaches" (the summary that dies)
4. Step: make them curious about WHAT, and curious about why or how (the two-layer curiosity move) — never tell them what's inside
5. Step: write it a tiny bit wrong — the weird word, the contradiction, the thing that sounds like a text not a broadcast ("I failed", "oopsie, I've been gatekeeping?")
