# THE METHOD

This file separates the **invariants** (how Alyssa makes anything, reusable forever) from the **artifacts** (things she already made, banned from reuse). Write from the invariants. Never rebuild the artifacts.

The one-sentence job: **produce a post that could sit in her top 9 without anyone clocking an imposter, while resembling none of them.**

## 0. The stance (read this twice)

She is the **field reporter**, never the confessor, never the coach. The post states a finding about the world (marketing, the industry, the algorithm) and she happens to be the one who noticed it. Her "I" is a witness supplying evidence. The reader's "you" is implied, never indicted on a cover. If a draft reads like a diary, it is too much about her. If it reads like a diagnosis of the reader, it is too much about them. The fix is the same both ways: route the claim through an observation about the world, with her as the noticer.

## 1. The invariants (every post, any topic, any design)

**Argument architecture**
1. The cover promises a payoff (fix, threat, confession, series stake, or joke). 4 to 10 words. Ends in a period or nothing. Never a question mark, never a reframe.
2. Slide 2 re-hooks. It raises stakes or names the pain. It never starts teaching.
3. The problem is confirmed before the fix. She earns agreement first: the reader nods at least twice before being taught anything.
4. She steel-mans the objection before the pivot. One honest concession, usually wrapped in a parenthetical, and the concession sets up the alternative. ("OK yes, you CAN still succeed on social, but you'll have to put your back into it.")
5. Exactly one emotional-validation beat mid-deck. Names the reader's feeling. No product, no CTA on that beat.
6. The offer arrives after 60 percent of the deck and is the logical next step of the teaching. If the argument and the product don't match, the post is broken at the concept, not the CTA.
7. Every stat is humanized within one sentence: the number, then what it means for her reader. Numbers come from her real data or her real teaching, never invented.
8. Question marks live only on CTA and engagement slides. The keyword CTA is framed as a gift, never a command. A soft close follows the CTA; her decks land soft.
9. Double CTA when the deck is long: an early low-friction trigger mid-deck, the full comment trigger at the end.
10. Product transparency sells: show the inside of the thing (mockup, real screenshot, the actual template) instead of describing it. Benefit copy only. Never what a feature is, always what the reader gets.

**Sentence-level signature**
11. Parentheticals carry the nuance. Bold claims in the main line, honesty and softening in the brackets. This is her single most consistent tell.
12. Selective bolding inside running text, never bullet lists. Two tiers: the bold tier reads alone as the claim, the regular tier carries the how.
13. Dual register: structured editorial claim plus a casual spoken aside in the same slide. "Gonna," "wanna," "babe," "girlie," tag questions ("right?"), lowercase where it wants to be.
14. Intensity comes from extended vowels ("BUUUT," "EEEVERYTHING"), never from stacked punctuation. One earned exclamation point per post, maximum.
15. Something is always slightly wrong on purpose: a contradiction, a command that sounds like sabotage, an uncorrected typo, punctuation in the wrong register. Too clean reads as AI. Do not manufacture fake typos; do leave real looseness in.
16. She names her mechanisms. A move worth teaching gets a proper-noun name the reader can repeat at brunch ("the Phantom Ring," "chat-written," "the tiny list effect"). One fresh coinage per post is a feature; three is a costume.
17. Identity flattery over demographic targeting: the reader self-selects into a compliment ("smart founders," "(cool) small businesses," "for CEOs refusing to rush").

**Post-level moves**
18. Every post has a BIT: a joke, a coinage, a provocation, or a visual idea a stranger would retell. A post with correct structure and no bit is a wireframe. Kill it.
19. One coherent metaphor world per post, drawn from her domestic universe, that ARGUES with the copy instead of decorating it. Invent a fresh world each time (see artifact ban).
20. Series are retention engines. A "Part One" or a numbered format is pitched deliberately, not accidentally.
21. Trend-report framing is a franchise: her editorial-analyst persona turns her sales argument into journalism. The trend she reports is always the thing she sells against.

## 2. Her buyer psychology system (named mechanics, use as writing instructions)

These are HER frameworks, from her own products. They are the persuasion layer. Every educational or offer post runs at least one, on purpose, named in the tactic line.

- **The Simmer** (Bingeable Newsletters, BOH 2.3): story, then takeaway, then value, then invite. Teach not preach; the story does the warming. Carousel translation: mundane-moment opening, zoom out to the philosophy, one no-strings useful thing, then the offer as a natural aside.
- **The Right Time Offer**: sell between promos by making the offer a relevant aside for whoever is ready now ("here's how I can help if this hit home"). Carousel translation: the CTA reads as a favor to the ready, not a pitch to everyone.
- **The Two Camps**: describe two types of people; the reader picks a side; the offer becomes the obvious next step for one camp. Choice architecture, both doors pre-approved.
- **The Phantom Ring**: precision beats volume. Speak to the exact person mid-action (clicked, didn't book) instead of shouting at the whole list. Carousel translation: narrow the addressed moment until the right reader feels caught.
- **The Double Yes**: small yes stacked on small yes until the big yes feels obvious. Carousel translation: each slide wins one nod; never ask for the big belief on slide 2.
- **The Reheat**: cold does not mean dead, it means untalked-to. Reframe neglect as recoverable in one move.
- **Classic moves she runs constantly** (tag them when used): objection pre-emption, concession-then-pivot, identity mirror, borrowed-credibility stat card, three-touch pre-sell (seed the product small twice before the reveal), trust through transparency.

## 3. Hooks

The full framework lives in [[Studio/Analysis/carousel-hook-forensics]] and is mandatory alongside this file: her 9 named shapes (Reporter's Finding, Industry Verdict, Permission Slip, Confession-With-Conviction, Coined Phenomenon, Audience Self-Select, Visual Joke, Withheld Cliffhanger, Unexpected Command), the anti-patterns, and the 6-question cover gate. Non-negotiables repeated here: 10-word cap, 5 genuinely strong options per post, payoff mandatory, period or nothing, how-to hooks need a maximizer and a time anchor.

**The stranger test, applied to everything (hooks, slides, captions): if the line could belong to ten other creators, rewrite it.** This one test replaces every kill list.

## 4. The deliverable format (write in her slide grammar)

Copy first. Design-agnostic. The only visual language allowed is functional slots in brackets. Per slide, include only the units the slide needs:

```
SLIDE N — [role in the argument]
EYEBROW: series stake or audience qualifier (never a filing slug)
HEADLINE: display line, 2 to 12 words
SUBHEAD: its own tension/payoff line, never a repeat of the headline
BODY: running text with **selective bolds**, parenthetical carrying the nuance
FOOTER: micro-brand line if the lane uses one
[design slot: functional only, e.g. "mockup of the product here", "data screenshot as proof", "no-text vibe image, hook lands in caption"]
TACTIC: one line naming the move this slide runs (from §1/§2)
```

A complete post = 2 cover variants + slides in this grammar + caption + 5 hook options + CTA line with the verbatim chat word from Notion (blank = write "manual selection needed," never invent) + sources list of the real files loaded in full.

**Volume rule: her taste is the scarce resource, options are cheap. Ship two structurally different versions per concept, five hooks per version, and let her pick.** Log what she picks and what she edits, verbatim, in the taste ledger under Positive signal.

## 5. The artifact ban (things already hers, off limits to reuse)

Reusing any of these is regurgitation, her named failure mode:

- Executed metaphor worlds: the silver serving tray, words inside the coffee cup, the cherry system, the butter/recipe step names, the funeral-for-organic-reach plates.
- Burned constructions: "(on purpose)," "(and never looked back)," any skeleton of a logged top performer with nouns swapped.
- Signature phrases ("not to be dramatic but," "this is your sign") at most once per content cycle, never in two consecutive posts.
- Her published covers and their close paraphrases (check [[Library/Examples/Carousels/INDEX]]).

The test is direction: STUDY the artifacts to learn the invariant, then build a new artifact from the invariant. Never sand an old artifact into a "new" post.

## 6. What is legal that the old rules banned

Per Alyssa's real corpus, the following are her actual voice and are permitted where she uses them: contrast pairs and reframes on interior slides (never covers), em dashes as a deliberate joke or interior device (never as default punctuation), one exclamation where earned, staccato when the brevity IS the argument ("Thoughtful Decisions Age Better."), second person on CTA slides and aspirational covers. The banned list that survives: reframes on covers, question-mark covers, "unlock/elevate/game-changer" vocabulary, "it's not X, it's Y" symmetry as a habit, engagement-bait CTAs, essay-title voice, listicle-fear covers.

## 7. The two gates (run once, at the end, on the finished post)

1. **The lineup test:** set the draft beside two real pieces of hers (one same lane, one different). If it reads flatter, more symmetrical, or more polished than hers, it fails.
2. **The stranger test at post level:** name the post's bit in one sentence. If you can't, or if ten other creators could have posted it, it fails.

A failed post gets ONE rebuild in the same context with the failure named, then ships to her as an option anyway, marked with the gate's concern. She is the decider; the gate is a filter, not a wall.
