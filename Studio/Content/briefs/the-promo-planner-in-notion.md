# The Promo Planner in Notion — low ticket, $59

## What this is
A Notion dashboard (say the thing: a Notion dashboard, $59) that turns one signature offer into 10 seasonal promotions: 50 email prompts, 32 reframe angles, 28+ bonus/incentive ideas, swipe vaults, and performance tracking — mapped by season.

## What it sells
Booking clients during the biggest buying windows of the year without launching anything new. One signature dish, plated for each season, planned once and recycled forward.

## Buying beliefs (what someone must believe before the CTA makes sense)
1. They don't need a new offer — the same signature offer can be repositioned fresh for every season.
2. Buying windows are real: seasonal buyer psychology decides when clients book, and promos should be aligned with it, not improvised.
3. A promo can be irresistible with a reframe and a bonus, not a slashed price.

## Approved argument lanes (one lane per deck, never mashed)

### Lane A — One offer, 10 fresh promos (the reframe mechanic)
Point: they don't need new offers or big launches — the same signature offer, reframed per season, is 10 completely fresh promotions; drop it in and follow the prompts.
Villain: reinventing the wheel — building something new for every promo.
Promise: your signature dish, plated for each season; copy the whole thing forward next year.
Best frameworks: How To (teach one seasonal reframe free on the slides), Old Way / New Way (new launch every time vs one offer recycled).
Use when: the reader's pain is launch fatigue or "I have one offer and nothing new to say about it."
Do not mix with: Lane B. This deck is the mechanic; it never leads with the calendar/buying-windows argument.
Verbatim source lines: "The Notion dashboard designed to turn your one signature offer into 10 completely fresh seasonal promotions without reinventing the wheel." · "Low Maintenance: No big launches, or new content, just your signature dish, plated for each season" · "Repeatable: Once you run it once, you can copy it forward for next year (bonus points for recycling the entire thing!)" · "32 Reframe Angles: Position your (same) offer from New Year to Black Friday, and everything in between" · "All you have to do is drop in your offer and follow the seasonal prompts. You'll walk away with mapped-out campaigns, ready-to-send emails, and a year-end sales plan that actually feels doable."

### Lane B — Plan by season, sell in the buying windows
Point: clients book in predictable seasonal windows — planning offers, promos, and emails by season keeps marketing low-maintenance and aligned with buyer psychology instead of scrambling last-minute.
Villain: the last-minute promo scramble — starting from scratch the week the buying window opens.
Promise: stay offline, keep marketing low-maintenance, and still book clients during the biggest buying windows of the year.
Best frameworks: Old Way / New Way (scramble vs planned season), House Report (the seasonal buying-windows read on the market).
Use when: the reader's pain is missed windows, panic promos, or discount-slashing to catch up.
Do not mix with: Lane A. This deck argues the calendar; it doesn't teach the reframe mechanic.
Verbatim source lines: "Keep your marketing low-maintenance, and still book clients during the biggest buying windows of the year." · "Map out your sales strategy : Plan your offers, promos, and emails by season, so you're always aligned with buyer psychology" · "So you can stay offline, keep your marketing low-maintenance, and still book clients during the biggest buying windows of the year." · "Swipe Vaults: Save subject lines, CTAs, and seasonal hooks so you never have to start from scratch" · "28+ Bonus & Incentive Ideas: Make your promos irresistible without slashing your prices (& profit margins)"
