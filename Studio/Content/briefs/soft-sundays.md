# Soft Sundays — freebie, free (weekly newsletter)

## What this is
A free weekly email newsletter (say the thing: her Sunday newsletter). The weekly productivity report: pep talk, house report intel, check-in ritual. The sales page is short; the deeper voice well is her real issues at [[Library/Examples/soft-sundays/INDEX]] — they are the product itself.

## What it sells
A soft start to the week: a check-in ritual plus real what's-working intel, for people leaving the girlboss era. The sell is the identity and the ritual, not email tips.

## Buying beliefs (what someone must believe before the CTA makes sense)
1. The girlboss/hustle version of productivity is over for them — soft is the upgrade, not the retreat.
2. A weekly check-in ritual is worth protecting (they're the kind of person who loves a good check-in).
3. Intel from real client results beats recycled guru advice in a changing space.

## Approved argument lanes (one lane per deck, never mashed)

### Lane A — Goodbye girlboss, hello softceo (the identity)
Point: there's a post-girlboss era and it has a name — softceo — and a Sunday ritual: candle lit, tone set, week planned soft.
Villain: girlboss-era hustle productivity (the Monday-morning grind identity).
Promise: a Sunday-morning feeling for your business week — a soft start, every week, free.
Best frameworks: For The Girls (the softceo identity IS the sell), Hot Take (girlboss era is over).
Use when: the reader's pain is burnout on hustle culture; the deck is a vibe and an invitation.
Do not mix with: Lane B. This deck never argues from data or "what's working" — it's the identity.
Verbatim source lines: "goodbye girlboss." "hello softceo." · "Come Over to the Soft Side" · "Let's light a candle and set the tone for our soft ceo week ahead." · "The weekly productivity report for daydreamers, creative thinkers, ADHDers, and anybody who loves a good check-in." · "👋 Former procrastinator turned productivity strategist for s ervice providers entering their post-girlboss era ☁️ ."

### Lane B — The intel report (what's working, from BTS of real clients)
Point: the online service space is changing, and every Sunday she reports what's actually working from behind the scenes of real clients and students — what's in, what's out — so readers stop guessing.
Villain: guessing (and recycled advice) in a space that's actively changing.
Promise: real client results, audience research, and buyer data in your inbox every Sunday — free.
Best frameworks: House Report (the newsletter's own "what's in / what's out" section is the format), How To.
Use when: the reader's pain is not knowing what's working anymore; the deck teases one real piece of intel.
Do not mix with: Lane A. This deck earns the sub with substance, not the softceo identity.
Verbatim source lines: "Things are changing in the online service biz space , so I'm sharing what we've learned from real client results, audience research, and buyer data 🤓." · "Plus how you can use the intel to automate more, and convert more of your community using 👯‍♀ Email Marketing & Pinterest 👯‍♀" · "_✍️ Some Inside Intel on what's working, and what's not in various service-biz niches. Reported from BTS of private clients and our students inside Back of House ✍️_" · "**📉 WHAT'S OUT 📉**" "**📈 WHAT'S IN 📈**"
