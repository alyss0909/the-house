# 90d Clock In — low ticket, $19

## What this is
A Notion dashboard (say the thing: a 90-day sprint dashboard in Notion, $19): shift dashboard, idea unload + department tagging, shift queue with smart weekly views, monthly/weekly shift pages, an Idea Pantry for stray ideas, and a Closing Shift Log. The metaphor world is cozy diner shifts, not grind.

## What it sells
90 focused days that turn the offers, leads, and ideas they already have into finished assets and booked clients — work that compounds long after the sprint. Clock in now, let future you collect later.

## Buying beliefs (what someone must believe before the CTA makes sense)
1. They don't need new ideas — they need to finish the (good) ones already lying around.
2. Open loops are the real leak: unfinished projects, open tabs, ideas that never became assets.
3. One department at a time, for one season, beats doing everything at once forever.

## Approved argument lanes (one lane per deck, never mashed)

### Lane A — Close the open loops (finish what you already have)
Point: the business doesn't need more ideas — it needs 90 days of turning existing offers, leads, and ideas into finished assets, one department at a time, with a system that keeps track so their brain doesn't have to.
Villain: open loops — the unfinished projects, stray ideas, and open tabs that never become assets.
Promise: loose ends cleaned up, loops closed (finally), and work that turns into assets and systems that keep working after the sprint.
Best frameworks: How To (the shift/sprint mechanic), Old Way / New Way (keeping track of literally everything vs the system doing it).
Use when: the reader's pain is scattered unfinished projects and idea overwhelm.
Do not mix with: Lane B. This deck is the mechanic; it never leans on the seasonal-arc mood.
Verbatim source lines: "The notion dashboard designed to turn your existing offers, leads, and ideas into finished assets, booked clients, and time and a half paycheques." · "A system that doesn't rely on you keeping track of...literally everything. Designed to clean up loose ends, close open loops (and open tabs)..finally" · "Choose ONE department at a time and go all in... you know exactly what you're doing so you just show up and follow through" · "Unload all your (good) ideas, sort them into the right department, and assign them to the right month" · "Compounding: The work you're doing turns into assets and systems that keep working (for you) long after your 90 day sprint"

### Lane B — The seasonal arc (clock in for the next 90 days)
Point: the next 90 days decide how the business feels all next year — so declare the arc, pick one focus per month, and lock in with the season as the container; her real winter-arc post and "X days until…" framing are the shape.
Villain: drifting through a season with no arc (starting the new year with the same open loops).
Promise: a locked-in seasonal sprint — one focus per month, always closing loops, assets over ideas.
Best frameworks: For The Girls (winter-arc / seasonal-sprint mood — her real post 2026-03-01-80-days-until-summer-90d-clock-in in Library/Examples/Carousels is the reference), How To.
Use when: a season boundary is near (new year, summer countdown, Q4) and urgency is honest.
Do not mix with: Lane A. This deck rides the season's mood; it never opens with the open-loops pain inventory.
Verbatim source lines: "Double down on your good ideas, pick up the right shifts, clock in now, & let future you collect later" · "I'm holding myself, BOH students, (and hopefully YOU) to showing up & **closing loops to determine how your business will feel all of next year**" · "**The rules are simple:** 🤎 **One focus** per month 🤎 Always be **closing (loops)** 🤎 **Assets** > ideas" · "_We're lighting candles, grabbing our softest sweatpants, and actually finishing what we started 👏_" · "Winter Arc Era"
