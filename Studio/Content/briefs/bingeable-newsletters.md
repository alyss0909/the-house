# Bingeable Newsletters — freebie

## What this is
A free guide (say the thing: a free downloadable guide). It teaches her 5 Part Simmer Email Framework end to end, plus the Right Time Offer Method and Inbox VIP, and its own last page bridges into The 5 Minute Newsletter planner.

## What it sells
Nothing, it is the gift. It converts a stranger into a subscriber by teaching the full simmer method for free. The deck's value IS the teaching.

## Buying beliefs (what someone must believe before downloading makes sense)
1. Their boring week already contains the raw material for a great email.
2. A newsletter is a repeatable routine, not a performance to summon.
3. Story-first emails warm people up AND sell, week after week.

## Approved argument lanes (one per deck, never mashed)

### Lane A — One boring moment becomes a bingeable email
Point: the mundane thing that just happened is the email; the 5 parts turn it into something people binge.
Villain: the blank doc and the overthinking.
Promise: a bingeable weekly email in under 30 minutes.
Best frameworks: How To (Metaphor Plan, the simmer kitchen).
Use when: the reader wants to write but never knows what to say.
Do not mix with: Lane B, and never with the 5MN gourmet lane.
Verbatim source lines: "Steal my repeatable structure that turns your messy thoughts into bingeable emails (fast)" · "the best stories aren't Oscar worthy tales, they're what *just* happened." · "even if your life is boring, promise" · "It's time to stop overthinking your newsletter, and start sending"

### Lane B — The routine, not the performance
Point: finding a repeatable routine is the difference between a marketing strategy you avoid and the easiest way you sell weekly.
Villain: the avoid-then-guilt cycle, email cast as a performance.
Promise: a simmering community that opens everything and inches closer to buying.
Best frameworks: Old Way / New Way (Exit or Pivot).
Use when: the reader's pain is avoidance and guilt, not idea-lack.
Do not mix with: Lane A.
Verbatim source lines: "Finding your repeatable newsletter routine is the difference between a marketing strategy you avoid, and one that becomes the easiest, most natural way you sell every week." · "This isn't just a warm up strategy, it's a make sales every week strategy." · "after studying email marketing for over 10 years, here's the TLDR: The emails that make the most money.. are the ones you ACTUALLY SEND!"

## Verbatim copy bank (assemble slide bodies from these; never paraphrase)

> Hook: "The moment someone opens your email, you're on the clock." · "Your hook is what builds thumb momentum inside the email."
> Story: "The easiest way to write regular simmer emails is simple: tell stories." · "It's the mundane update you're texting your friend about, the voice memo you're about to delete, a half-overheard convo you chuckled at, or the (weirdly specific) thing your toddler said."
> Story stash: "I HIGHLY recommend starting a story stash spot to pull from when you need it."
> Takeaway: "So What? It's time to make your point, and zoom out to make your story MEAN something." · "think of it like sending a friend a weird little thought that shifts how they think."
> Value: "No, valuable emails aren't "dead" (and who cares if they were) write something worth remembering." · "Get this RIGHT and ppl will start opening every single one of your emails (regardless of the subj line)."
