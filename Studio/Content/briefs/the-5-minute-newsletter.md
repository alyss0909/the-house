# The 5 Minute Newsletter — paid, $11

## What this is
A Notion planner (say the thing: a Notion planner, $11). A newsletter home base with a template and writing helper, an email angle database of 10 curated newsletter types, 25+ prompts and 15+ real examples baked into every section, and a board plus calendar that moves an email from writing to scheduled.

## What it sells
Actually sending one really good weekly newsletter. Not better writing, not more ideas: the SEND, made so easy it happens every week in about five minutes.

## Buying beliefs (what someone must believe before the CTA makes sense)
1. One really good newsletter outperforms scattered content, but only if it goes out.
2. Everyday benign moments are raw material; the recipe does the lifting.
3. Writing it can take about five minutes when the structure already works.

## Approved argument lanes (one lane per deck, never mashed — the julia v1 failure was mashing A and B)

### Lane A — Gourmet email is out
Point: overbuilt, over-drafted "gourmet" email is why the newsletter never ships; the one-pan simmer is in.
Villain: gourmet email marketing (overthinking drafts, fancy production, emails that lead nowhere).
Promise: one irresistible weekly newsletter, on the table quick.
Best frameworks: How To (Product Plan), Old Way / New Way (the Pivot).
Use when: the reader's pain is the unsent, overworked draft.
Do not mix with: Lane B. One deck, one villain.
Verbatim source lines: "Sorry Julia Child, gourmet email marketing is out" · "Introducing your new one-pan recipe for newsletters on the table quick!" · "Simple ingredients, on the table quick, with connection, value, and sales strategy already baked in. One irresistible weekly newsletter your audience will be craving like its taco tuesday" · "instead of trying to "just write consistently" we follow an email framework that already works, quickly."

### Lane B — Six burners going when one will do
Point: service providers exhaust themselves across reels, carousels, stories, and content pillars just to stay visible, when one really good newsletter outperforms all of it. Her confirmed lead reframe of the offer (2026-07-17).
Villain: the six-channel content grind.
Promise: the lowest-maintenance, highest-return channel done really well once a week.
Best frameworks: House Report (market shift toward fewer, sturdier channels), Old Way / New Way (the Exit).
Use when: the reader's pain is being everywhere and exhausted, not the email draft itself.
Do not mix with: Lane A. And the pain must land on email as the answer, never on teaching Instagram.
Verbatim source lines: "YOU'VE GOT SIX BURNERS GOING WHEN ONE WILL DO" · "Most service providers are posting reels, carousels, stories, and 'educational content pillars' every week just to stay visible…" · "One really good newsletter outperforms all of it... but only if you hit send." · "What if one email did more for your business than a whole week of content?"
