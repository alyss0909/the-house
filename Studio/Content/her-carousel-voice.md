# Her carousel voice

Read this to sound like her on a SLIDE. Her sales-page copy is the substance (what to say). This is the register (how she says it on a carousel). They are different voices. When they conflict, this wins for slide copy.

## The tells (from her real decks, non-negotiable)

1. **Slide headers are SHORT and PUNCHY: 3 to 7 words.** Usually a verdict, a coinage, or an identity claim, ending in a period or nothing. "Organic Reach is Officially Cooked." "The Post-Mortem of Organic Reach." "Marketing Nerd Reporting for Duty." NOT a soft explainer sentence like "Keeping all six lit is the actual full-time job." If your header is a full explanatory sentence, you wrote a sales page, not her carousel.
2. **She reports with receipts.** She is a marketing nerd and insider with notes, not a coach giving a lecture. "I'VE REVIEWED MY NOTES (& INDUSTRY REPORTS)... THIS IS WHAT IM SEEING IN MY OWN BUSINESS AND BTS WITH CLIENTS." Bring her sources into the copy.
3. **Real data, named.** "META HITTING RECORD AD REVENUE IN 2025 WHILE ORGANIC REACH FELL BELOW 1%." Hard numbers and named facts prove the problem (this is spine job 4). Pull her real numbers; never invent one.
4. **Culture-speak pattern interrupts.** "Officially Cooked." "Post-Mortem." "Reporting for Duty." "REHEAT EM QUICK." Slangy, punchy, shareable. This is the bit doing work in the header.
5. **"We," not "you."** Solidarity over blame: "WE'RE BEING PRICED OUT."
6. **Dense run-on bodies in her spoken rhythm.** ALL-CAPS in her real decks, with "&" connectors, phonetic spellings ("em" for them), selective bolds (never bullet lists), and uncorrected typos left in ("IM", "RESUSITATION"). Reads like someone underlining while they talk.
7. **Extended vowels for intensity** ("EEEVERYTHING"), never stacked punctuation. One exclamation per deck, earned.
8. **Parentheticals carry every concession and softening.** Bold claim in the main line, the honesty in the brackets.

## Real exemplar (verbatim, her Jan 20 deck — a House Report; the VOICE is the same across frameworks)

- **Cover:** "What's actually shifting in 2026 (And What To Do Instead)"
- **Slide 2 header:** "Marketing Nerd Reporting for Duty"
  Body: "THE LAST 4 YEARS HAVE CHANGED MORE DRASTICALLY THAN MOST, SO I'VE REVIEWED MY NOTES (& INDUSTRY REPORTS) FROM LAST YEAR AND THIS IS WHAT IM SEEING IN MY OWN BUSINESS AND BTS WITH CLIENTS WHO MAKE THEIR MONEY ONLINE, BUT DON'T WANT TO LIVE ONLINE"
- **Slide 4 header:** "Organic Reach is Officially Cooked."
  Body: "ALGORITHMS ARE FULLY MONETIZED, AND REACHING YOUR OWN AUDIENCE IS HARDER THAN EVER. META HITTING RECORD AD REVENUE IN 2025 WHILE ORGANIC REACH FELL BELOW 1% TELLS US EEEVERYTHING WE NEED TO KNOW: WE'RE BEING PRICED OUT."
- **Slide 5 header:** "The Post-Mortem of Organic Reach"
  Body: "(OK YES, YOU CAN STILL SUCCEED ON SOCIAL, YOU CAN STILL GROW AN AUDIENCE AND MONETIZE - BUT YOU'LL HAVE TO PUT YOUR BACK INTO IT...) So, if you're looking for a slower marketing alternative..."
- **Slide 7 CTA header:** "Comment List"
  Body: "BEEN GHOSTING YOUR EMAIL SUBSCRIBERS? STEAL MY LIST RESUSITATION SEQUENCE TO REHEAT EM QUICK! JUST SAY THE WORD & I'LL SEND OUR NOTION HUB TO YOUR DMS"

## The test
Put your header next to hers ("Organic Reach is Officially Cooked."). If yours is longer, softer, or more explanatory than that, cut it down to a verdict or a coinage. Put your body next to hers. If it reads like a polished pitch instead of her talking fast with receipts, rewrite it.
