# Carousel Hook Workflow

The thing we were missing. A carousel hook is not a reel hook, and it is not a description. This is how we write one.

## 0. The one rule everything hangs on

A carousel cover has to earn a **swipe** and a **save**. A reel hook earns a passive watch, so it can run on pure open-ended curiosity ("wait for it"). A carousel cover cannot. Someone has to decide, with their thumb, that a specific payoff is one swipe away. So every cover does one of two jobs:

- **Opens a loop** the slides visibly close (withhold the answer, the deck delivers it), or
- **Promises a contained, save-worthy payoff** (a plan, a list, a stealable thing).

Never a flat verdict that resolves on the cover. "Organic Marketing Is Dying" only worked because the subhead withheld "the real reason might piss you off." The withhold is the hook.

## 1. The angle engine (this is how we stop repeating her own hooks)

Do NOT start from her past covers. That was the circular trap. Start from two places instead:

1. **Emotional Mirror.** Name the 3 things her audience secretly stresses about this topic at 11pm (women who make money online but don't want to live online). Write toward the unspoken frustration. This produces angles that have nothing to do with her old hooks.
2. **The deck's real payoff.** What do the existing slides actually deliver? (See the swap-safe step.)

The hook lives where an 11pm stress meets what the deck can pay off.

## 2. The shape menu

Pick the shape that fits the angle. All drawn from Tinna's grid, her own sheet, and sociyell (see [[2026-07-22-carousel-hook-intel]]).

| Shape | What it does | Example lineage |
|---|---|---|
| **The Versus** | Two-panel contrast; the loop is "which one, and how" | Tinna "Fast Brain vs Slow Brain"; sociyell Contrast Playbook |
| **The Contained List** | Odd-numbered utility you'd save | Tinna "5 Quiet Habits…"; her sheet "4 Types…" |
| **The Contrarian Verdict** | States the belief is wrong, withholds why | Tinna "Carousels aren't trending"; her "Content Pillars Are Overrated" |
| **The Withheld Loop** | Raises a high-stakes problem, pushes the fix inside | sociyell Retention Loops |
| **The Result + Proof** | Boring input, specific outsized result | Tinna "Boring got me 300k…"; deck's "Pins last 1600x longer" |
| **The Identity Read-This** | "I'm [X]. If you're [Y], read this." | Tinna "I'm 38, if you're a woman in your 30s or 40s read this" |
| **The Reframe** | Flips an expectation into a provocation | Tinna "Failing Is Sexy. Own It." |

## 3. The voice gate (every candidate runs through this)

- She is the **witness/reporter**, not the confessor or the coach. Route claims through an observation about the world. (Emotional Mirror direct-address "you" is allowed as reassurance, not as indictment — flag those for her.)
- **10 words max.**
- **Period or no punctuation**, never a question mark, unless the cover is explicitly a CTA/engagement slide.
- **The wrongness test:** something slightly off (a contradiction, an odd-specific number, a word in the wrong register). Too clean reads as AI.
- **Extended vowels** for intensity, never exclamation stacks ("BUUUT," "EEEVERYTHING").
- **Purge the banned list** (delve, optimize, paradigm, utilize, transformative, testament, leverage, seamless, unlock, elevate, level up) and the banned "it's not X, it's Y" shape.
- Cross-check against [[carousel-hook-forensics]] §5 anti-patterns.

## 4. The swap-safe rule (for "new cover, same deck")

We are keeping her slides. So:

1. **Inventory the payoff** first — write one line per slide: what does it actually deliver? Note the CTA word and product.
2. **Only open loops the slides close.** A hook that promises something the deck never shows is a lie the reader catches on slide 2. (This is why "How I Book Clients From Four Posts" failed — the deck shows the system, not a booking walkthrough.)
3. **Escape hatch, used rarely:** if a hook is strong enough to justify one added slide, flag it as an *option* with the exact slide text and where it slots in. Default is: slides stay as they are. Her call.

## 5. Output format per post

Deliver 5 or 6 hooks. For each: the hook, its **shape**, the **11pm stress** it mirrors (if any), and **which slide closes its loop**. That last part is the proof it's swap-safe.

## Worked example — "Organic Marketing Is Dying" (calibration)

**Payoff inventory:** reach is dying (data, slides 2-4) → you've been building free assets for billionaire platforms (slide 5) → the low-maintenance alternative: 4 evergreen pieces on Pinterest feeding email sales (slides 6-9) → comment YESCHEF for a $500 BOH coupon (slide 10). CTA word: YESCHEF. Product: [[back-of-house]].

**11pm stresses (Emotional Mirror):** (1) "I'm posting constantly and it's going nowhere — is it me?" (2) "If I stop posting, my business dies. I can't keep this pace." (3) "Everyone else is growing and I'm shouting into the void."

**Hooks:**
1. "Posting Daily vs Posting Four Times A Month." — *Versus.* Closes slides 6-9.
2. "You're Not Bad At This. The Game Changed." — *Contrarian Verdict + Mirror (stress 1).* Closes slides 2-5. (uses reassurance "you" — flag)
3. "The Reason Posting Feels Pointless Isn't You." — *Withheld Loop + Mirror (stress 1).* Closes slide 5.
4. "Everyone Blames The Algorithm. The Real Problem Is Worse." — *Withheld Loop.* Closes slide 5.
5. "One Pin Outlasts 1,600 Instagram Posts." — *Result + Proof.* Closes slide 7. (hard number on cover — bends her rule, her call)

## Session outcome (2026-07-22 → 07-23) — read before trusting this

This workflow did NOT crack good hook generation. Across the session, every batch was rejected ("meh," "not hitting," "mostly suck"), including ones written by Hermes from her verbatim words. Honest learnings so the next attempt doesn't repeat the session:

1. **The swap-safe trap (Job 1).** "New cover, keep the slides" is a near-empty box. Her decks already carry their best hook, so a swap-safe + open-loop + non-repeat hook tends to collapse into the cover she already wrote. This is structural, not taste. If we do Job 1 again, the only real source of novelty is spotlighting a DIFFERENT buried idea and nudging a slide forward — i.e. accept it's a small deck edit, not a clean hat swap.
2. **Rule/framework generation still reads as slop.** Filling shapes (Versus, Emotional Mirror, Contrast, her own templates) produced technically-correct, voiceless lines. Her own research already says this: exemplars beat rules. The generation step here is rule-based and that is likely the core flaw.
3. **Reel hooks ≠ carousel hooks** and **open loop = WITHHOLD a specific thing** (steps/why/how/list), never state-and-close. Both true, both necessary, neither sufficient.
4. **Process failure worth remembering:** Larry (orchestrator) sat in the writer's chair the whole session. Content writing routes to Hermes, fronted by Suki. Alyssa called this out directly. See [[feedback_larry_is_librarian_not_orchestrator]].

**Where this stands:** good hook generation for her is UNSOLVED. The reusable wins from the session are the intel ([[2026-07-22-carousel-hook-intel]]) and her filed source library, not this workflow's output. Next attempt should try genuine exemplar-dominant generation (her real covers as dominant context, continuation framing, blind imposter gate — the [[exemplar-writer]] method) rather than shape-filling.
