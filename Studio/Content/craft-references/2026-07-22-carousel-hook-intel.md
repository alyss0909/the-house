## Part 1 — @sociyell: "Turn Claude into a CAROUSEL weapon with 5 frameworks"

Verbatim transcription of all slides Alyssa screenshotted, including the "Prompt Blueprint" text in each slide's top-left corner. The prompts are the reusable asset.

### Slide 1 — Cover
**Hook:** "Your carousel feels like a textbook? People swipe past in 0.4 seconds."
**Body:**
- "The algorithm rewards retention and swipe-through rates."
- "The trap: You ask Claude for a script. It gives you a robotic, academic essay chopped into paragraphs."
- "To win: Force Claude to write like a copywriter who understands human psychology and user behavior."
- "Not like a textbook. Like a conversation."
**Visual:** open book inside a life-preserver ring, floating on dark water.

### Slide 2 — Micro Scripting Prompt
**Prompt Blueprint (corner):** "Act as premium social media copywriter. Convert [topic] into 8-slide Instagram carousel. Every slide: maximum 3 bold bullet points. Each bullet: 12 words max. Eliminate all filler and transition words."
**Subhead:** "Instagram slides have tiny real estate. Claude needs visual breathing room constraints."
**Why it works:** "Forces Claude to distill massive concepts into punchy, scannable micro-insights people consume in a glance."

### Slide 3 — Retention Loops
**Prompt Blueprint (corner):** "Structure across 5 slides using cliffhanger framework. Slide N introduces high-stakes problem. Specific execution step pushed to Slide N+1. Keep momentum forward."
**Body:** "The 'Incomplete Information' Loop. Viral carousels don't deliver everything on slide 3. They force users to slide 4, 5, 6 to get the full picture."
**Why it works:** "Gamifies the swiping experience. Sends positive signals to Instagram algorithm about completion rates."

### Slide 4 — Emotional Mirror
**Prompt Blueprint (corner):** "Analyze target persona: [describe audience]. Identify 3 things they secretly stress about regarding [niche] at 11 PM. Write copy speaking directly to that unexpressed frustration. Use raw, conversational human language."
**Body:** "Generic content states obvious facts. Viral content states hidden things your audience thinks but never says out loud."
**Why it works:** "Builds instant authority. Consumer feels like you're reading their mind. Massive saves and shares follow."

### Slide 5 — Contrast Playbook
**Prompt Blueprint (corner):** "Create comparative matrix. Contrast 'The Traditional Way' vs 'The Efficient AI Way' for [topic]. Format as sharp table: 3 rows comparing Time Spent, Financial Cost, Energy Drain."
**Body:** "People process visual contrast faster than text blocks. Claude builds clean, high-impact comparisons your design team can visualize instantly."
**Design Bonus:** "Beautiful structural layout that breaks text monotony."

### Slide 6 — Tone Humanizing
**Prompt Blueprint (corner):** "Rewrite carousel. Banned words: delve, optimize, paradigm, utilize, transformative, testament. Tone: cynical, experienced 30-year-old creative director talking to colleague over coffee. Short sentences. High impact."
**Body:** "AI loves: delve, testament, leverage, revolutionized, moreover, paradigm, seamless. You need: purged completely."
**Why it works:** "Strips robotic footprint. Makes brand voice feel authentic, human, deeply relatable."

### Caption (verbatim, condensed)
Tested with five agencies. Complaint every time: "Our Claude carousels are flat. Generic. Sounding like essays chopped into slides." Claude isn't the problem, the prompts are. Five frameworks force Claude to write like a copywriter, not a robot. Claims 40-60% higher swipe-through rates. CTA: comment which framework you're testing, or save for later.

## Part 2 — @tinnaloaizaofficial: cover hooks + pattern read

Top hooks transcribed off her grid (2026-07-22 screenshot). Reads as low-res thumbnails, so a few are approximate; grouped by the shape they teach.

- "Carousels vs Reels"
- "One Day vs Day One"
- "Being Chosen vs Being Seen"
- "Story Telling vs Telling Story"

- "Slow Brain vs Fast Brain"

- "Not All Carousels Are Built the Same"
- "You Don't Need a Niche, You Need a Playground"

- "3 Types of Carousels You Should Share"

### "How to [outcome]" with spice or an oddly specific number
- "How to Be Dangerously Good at Storytelling" (repeated — her signature phrase)
- "How to Make Your Carousels 12% Better"
- "How to Turn a 'Boring' Week Into Content"
- "How to Create Carousels That Make People Follow You"

### Result + proof number
- "Boring got me 300k new followers in two months"
- "Carousels that get 4,000 views vs Carousels that get 500,000 views"

### Identity + read-this
- "I'm 38, If You Are a Woman in Your 30s or 40s Read This"
- "I Built My Business During Nap Times. If You Have Little Time Read This"
- "I spent 34 days away from social media"

### Reframe / provocation
- "Failing Is Sexy. Own It."
- "Growing your business will make you angry"
- "why talking about your wins feels like bragging"
- "What if you've been selling your products to someone who doesn't exist?"
- "You Need Someone in the Room With You."

### Craft notes (steal these)
- Oddly specific numbers beat round ones for curiosity: "12% better," "34 days," "30 in 30."
- "Dangerously Good at [X]" is her repeatable coined phrase (structural equivalent of Alyssa's "BUUUT" / "EEEVERYTHING").
- Almost none are vague reel-curiosity. Every cover promises a contained payoff or opens a loop the slides close. That is the carousel rule proven on someone else's grid.
- See also [[2026-07-09-tinna-loaiza-5-carousel-patterns]] (intimacy, deprivation curiosity, tension, visual repetition, narrative).

## The merged AI-tell banned list (sociyell + ours)

Purge on sight in any carousel copy: delve, optimize, paradigm, utilize, transformative, testament, leverage, seamless, moreover, revolutionized, unlock, elevate, game-changer, level up. Plus the banned SHAPE: any "it's not X, it's Y" symmetry. Full house rule in [[carousel-hook-forensics]] §5 and the ANTI-AI judgment layer.
