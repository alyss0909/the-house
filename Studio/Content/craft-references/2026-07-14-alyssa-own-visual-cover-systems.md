# Alyssa's Own Cover Copy Patterns

## Who this is for

Concept, Hook, Writer, Trend Scout — the makers who write words. Nobody on the Content OS does visual design; Alyssa has a separate human designer for that. This file leads with copy, because that's the whole team's job. A compressed visual-template note sits at the bottom, clearly marked, for the one scenario where someone needs to brief a designer.

---

## PART 1 — Four real headline formulas (with proof, not hypotheticals)

**1. Confession-format:** first-person, past-tense, specific, slightly shocking admission. Real: "I Literally Yelled At My Client Yesterday." **Already proven live** — this is the exact format Alyssa approved for the toddler pitch, "I Literally Stole My Best Sales Trick From A 3-Year-Old." Use when the post opens on a real, embarrassing, specific moment.

**2. Coined-word:** invents a name for a phenomenon rather than describing it. Real: "Socks & Sandals Marketing," "Procrastavoidance." Confirms and strengthens [[Studio/Analysis/carousel-hook-forensics]] Pattern E ("The Coined Phenomenon" — "the tiny list effect," "Stoner Math") with two new real examples. Test before coining: does this need a NAME because it's a repeatable pattern, not just an adjective?

**3. Stacked declarative:** plain, conversational, present-tense-adjacent claim, no gimmick. Real: "There are 80 Days Until Summer," "How I Stopped Starting From Scratch Every Week." Use for calendar/systems teaching where the claim itself is the hook.

**4. Number+noun:** a concrete, countable asset as the entire headline. Real: "80 Subject Lines." Use when the post's whole value is a deliverable count.

---

## PART 2 — The subhead: a separate copy unit with its own job

Every real cover in this pull carries a subhead line under the headline, and it does a DIFFERENT job than the headline — the headline states the claim/confession, the subhead adds the tension or payoff. Real subheads: "FUN IS A MARKETING STRATEGY AND I WILL DIE ON THIS HILL," "AND I MIGHT NEED TO YELL AT YOU NOW TOO," "HERE'S MY Q2 PLAN," "I SPLIT TESTED THESE SO YOU DONT HAVE TO." Write it as its own line, not a restatement — a writer should always draft a subhead alongside a headline, never leave that slot as an afterthought or a literal repeat.

---

## PART 3 — Kicker phrases as reusable copy IP (not a layout choice)

Recurring branded verbal devices, reused across posts: "THE ULTIMATE / [X] / BIZ MODEL," "THE / MIDNIGHT / MENU." These are WORDS Alyssa owns and reuses — a naming device the writer can pull into captions, series titles, or other formats, independent of any cover design. Treat "THE MIDNIGHT MENU" the same way the team treats a proven hook: a reusable asset, not a one-off.

---

## PART 4 — For The Girls: the copy rule (already known, reinforced here)

FTG headlines are a first-person plain declaration, the whole "headline" IS the statement — no payoff-hook, no CTA. Real: "MY 5 YEAR PLAN IS TO MOVE INTO A MOSSY SEASHELL ON THE OCEAN FLOOR." Full text rules already locked in [[Studio/Team/Suki - Social Media Strategist/PLAYBOOK]] Part 5 — this file just confirms the pattern held in the new pull.

## What's a hard pattern vs. a judgment call

**Hard pattern (real numbers, this pull of 25 posts):** carousels outperform single-image posts on likes — 56–214 likes on carousels vs. 30–43 on the two single-image posts. Default to carousel.

**Judgment calls, not proven — flagged, not adopted:**
- "Secret/insider" framing ("INSIDE INTEL," "vault isn't linked publicly") sits on the two highest-like posts (174, 214) — suggestive at n=2, needs a deliberate test before it's a rule.
- Short meme-caption posts still pulling solid likes suggests the cover may carry the weight over the caption — plausible, unverified; this pull can't isolate cover-effect from caption-effect.

## FOR DESIGN BRIEFING ONLY — NOT THIS TEAM'S JOB

Compressed reference, useful only if Iris or Alyssa's external designer ever needs briefing. No maker on the Content OS should be directing this.

- **Branded Kicker Template** (her default cover): styled real photography background, editorial serif headline, 3-column small-caps kicker, subhead, BOH logo. Prop must argue with the copy, not decorate it (butter knife+toast for "yelling," snarling collared cat for "obsession").
- **Meme/Pop-Culture-Still Repurpose:** a viral meme or cinematic still as background, paired with a coined-word or stacked headline (Part 1 formulas #2/#3). Real: the "exploding woman" meme for "Procrastavoidance"; a cinematic desk still for "80 Days Until Summer."
- **FTG Monospace System:** monospace type, single still-life object (not lifestyle photography), no BOH logo, "FOR/THE/GIRLS" footer instead of kicker.
- **Design-token gap:** no font names or hex codes extracted — an Iris audit would be needed before this briefs a real design job.
