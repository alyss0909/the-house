# Conversion Rate Optimization — Bdow Article

Good reference for teaching conversions, email copy angles, and stats to back up strategy.

---

## The core analogy

> "Every month you grind for more traffic...Then 97 of every 100 of them leave without subscribing. That's not a traffic problem. That's a bucket problem."

This is a great framing for client/student work — most people think more traffic is the fix, but the leak is in the bucket.

---

## The numbers worth keeping

| Tactic | Lift |
|--------|------|
| Benefit-driven button copy ("Make money flipping websites" vs "Join us") | +33% |
| Cut form fields from 4 → 3 | +50% |
| Cut to 1 field (email only) | even more |
| Two-step opt-ins vs direct forms | up to 10x |
| Limited-time shipping scarcity | +226% |
| Adding testimonials | +34% |
| Adding a face/person image to sales page | +102% |

- Industry baseline opt-in rate: 2–5%
- Well-optimized welcome mats / exit popups: 10–20%+
- 92% of people trust peer recommendations; 70% trust strangers' reviews

---

## The five-bucket framework

1. **Offer** — lead magnet, benefits over features, specific results, exclusivity
2. **Copy** — strong headlines, power words, audience language, button copy
3. **Visual design** — action colors, faces, lines of sight, motion, previews
4. **Psychology** — social proof, pre-empting objections, scarcity/urgency, progress indicators
5. **Technical** — page speed, mobile, form simplicity, multiple touchpoints

---

## Copy principles worth using

- **Button copy:** "Get my free spreadsheet" > generic. Specificity wins.
- **The right number of form fields is one.** Just the email.
- **Simplify choice architecture** — too many nav options kill conversion
- **Testing hierarchy:** button color → button copy → headline → form fields (fastest wins first)
- **Faces** capture attention faster than conscious decision-making — heat maps confirm this
