# Welcome Email Synthesis — Wren Read (bdow.com)

**Source:** [BDOW — Welcome Email Examples](https://bdow.com/stories/welcome-email-examples/)
**Action tier:** read + synthesize → completed 2026-06-29

## What's actually useful for Alyssa's welcome sequence

**1. Welcome email has the highest open rate of anything you send — up to 53%**
This is not the place for a generic "thanks for joining." It's the highest-leverage email in the whole sequence and most people waste it.

**2. Pick ONE objective and don't flinch**
Content consumption, email engagement, or product sales. For BOH-adjacent subscribers: the welcome objective is probably *content consumption + relationship* — get them reading, get them trusting, delay the pitch. For a tripwire sequence: the objective is a soft offer within the first email or two.

**3. Deliver what you promised, exactly**
If they opted in for a freebie, lead with it. Don't bury it. Alyssa's opt-ins likely promise something specific — the welcome email exists to fulfill that promise before anything else.

**4. Ask what they want**
One simple question ("what's your biggest challenge with X right now?") does two things: it gives Alyssa intelligence, and it makes the subscriber feel seen before they've been sold to. Fits her voice perfectly — she already talks to one specific person.

**5. Whitelist ask**
First email should ask them to move it to Primary / whitelist her address. Deliverability matters most at the start of the relationship. One line is enough.

## What doesn't apply

Generic e-commerce tactics (discount codes, purchase encouragement) don't fit. Skip those.

## Recommended next step

Wren: apply these patterns when writing or auditing Alyssa's active welcome sequences. The BOH welcome sequence in particular should be audited against #2 and #3 — does it have a single objective, and does it deliver on whatever the opt-in promised?
