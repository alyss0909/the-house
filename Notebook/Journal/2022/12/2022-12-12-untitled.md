What should be included in a contract for a nanny in Canada
A contract for a nanny in Canada should include the following information:

The names of the nanny and the employer
The address of the employer
The starting date of the employment
The hours of work and the schedule
The rate of pay and the method of payment
The duties and responsibilities of the nanny
The vacation and sick leave entitlements
Any additional benefits provided, such as health insurance or a pension plan
The notice period required for termination of the contract by either party
Any other terms and conditions agreed upon by the employer and the nanny
It is also important to ensure that the contract complies with relevant employment laws in Canada. For example, the contract should specify that the nanny will be paid at least the minimum wage, and should include any provisions required by the province or territory where the nanny will be working.
