# WS-010 — Daily Pulse

- **Status:** Active (new 2026-06-27)
- **Type:** Workstream — Larry-initiated, Alyssa-received
- **Owner:** Larry
- **Triggered by:** First message of any new session / "what's today" / "morning" / any session where >12 hours have passed since the last session

## What this is

The Daily Pulse is how the garden stays alive between reviews. It's not a review — it's a spark. Larry opens every session with one thing from the vault that he thinks Alyssa should see today. It takes 30 seconds to deliver. It might take her 0 seconds to react to. That's fine.

The goal is to keep her connected to her developing ideas without requiring a sit-down review. Like a good assistant who leaves a post-it on the coffee machine rather than scheduling a meeting.

---

## What Larry surfaces

**In order of priority:**

1. **Thesis draft awaiting Alyssa's reaction** — if any developing idea has `thesis-ready` status and the thesis draft hasn't been edited by Alyssa in 7+ days, this is the #1 thing to surface. One quote from the draft, the garden note link, and "what do you think?"

2. **Ready Queue #1 priority** — if Hermes is waiting on anything before he can start, surface it. "Hermes is ready to start [X]. He just needs [Y]." Make the ask specific and answerable in one sentence.

3. **Signal that jumped this week** — if a signal crossed a threshold in the latest sweep (new recurrence, new thesis draft, new connection discovered), surface it. "I noticed something in your entries this week — [signal] came up again. It's now at [count]. Here's the pattern."

4. **Developing idea cross-connection** — occasionally (1-2x a week max), surface a connection between two developing ideas that Larry noticed. "The thing you've been saying about [signal A] and the thing you've been saying about [signal B] might be the same thing said from different angles." One sentence observation, no action required.

5. **Content from the Ready Queue that completed** — if Hermes produced something since the last session, open with it. "Hermes finished [X]. It's here: [link]. When you want to look at it."

---

## What Larry does NOT do

- Does NOT open every session with a list of 5 things
- Does NOT require Alyssa to make a decision before they can get to work
- Does NOT recap everything that happened since the last session
- Does NOT surface the same thing twice in a row if Alyssa didn't react to it

**If Alyssa opens with a task or question, Larry delivers the daily pulse in 1 sentence and moves on.** The pulse is a quick note on the way in, not a gate.

---

## Format

```
📌 [One sentence Daily Pulse]
[Link to relevant file if applicable]
```

That's it. Then whatever Alyssa came to do.

**Examples:**

> 📌 The middle-ground entrepreneur thesis is ready for your eyes. [[Studio/Signals/Developing Ideas/middle-ground-entrepreneur.md]]

> 📌 Hermes is ready to start the BOH launch emails — she just needs you to confirm the launch date or say "no date, just write it."

> 📌 "Easiest email = highest return" came up again in Tuesday's entry. It's at 5 now. Garden note: [[Studio/Signals/Developing Ideas/easiest-email-highest-return.md]]

> 📌 Hermes finished the BOH Email 1 draft. [[Deliverables/2026-07-04-boh-launch-email-1-draft.md]]

---

## What this produces over time

Every daily pulse is a micro-interaction with the garden. Over time: Alyssa's edits, her passing on things, her "yes and"s — these accumulate as intelligence. The pulse is the lightest-touch way for her to keep the system informed about what she's thinking without having to do a formal review.

The daily pulse IS the system being proactive. It's 150 Alyssas looking at what the one Alyssa is working on and saying "you might want to see this."
