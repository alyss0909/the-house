# GL-010 — Content Pitch Operating Brain

This is the judgment layer. WS-011 tells you the steps; this tells you how to think while doing them. It was written after a day of four rejected pitch sheets followed by one she called "night and day." Every rule here was paid for.

## 1. The failure taxonomy (recognize these in your own output BEFORE she sees it)

| Failure mode | What it looks like | Her verbatim verdict |
|---|---|---|
| Teaching-first | Concept named after the lesson ("belief move", "reframe", curriculum vocab) | "nobody reads a post about The belief move" |
| Generic-pattern | Hooks/concepts from skill-repo templates, competent but placeless | "the concepts suck the hooks are mediocre" |
| Corpus regurgitation | Her winner's skeleton with the metaphor swapped | "i dont want some regugitation of my 9 posts" |
| Clever-conceit | Mechanism-driven concepts ("Your Marketing Bill, Itemized", "artifact autopsy") | "REALLY REALLY BAD NOOBDY WOULD POST THIS" |
| Rubric-stacking | Writing to checklists (three-checks, forward tests, pattern annotations at pitch stage) | produces all of the above |

**What finally worked:** 10 one-line concepts, each a simple direct promise + who it's for, written by a fresh context that had just LOOKED at her real slide images. Example winners: "The 4 Posts That Could Bring You Traffic All Year", "How To Get Found On Pinterest Without Posting Every Day", "You're Allowed To Build A Slower Business".

**The concept test (apply to every line):** Would this title actually get posted on Instagram by a real account? Is it a plain promise a stranger instantly understands? If the concept needs a mechanism explained, kill it. Simple promise, strong execution — never the reverse.

## 2. Non-negotiable operating rules

1. **LOOK before writing.** The pitching context must view her real slide images (Read tool on the jpgs, in order) that same session. Text about carousels is never a substitute. For broader cover/grid calibration beyond the top performers, also view [[Team Inbox/Recent Instagram Covers]] and treat [[Deliverables/2026-07-08-recent-instagram-covers-suki-intake]] as interpretation, not source.
2. **Short prompts, fresh contexts.** The winning pitch prompt was ~20 lines. Never accumulate rules across revisions — a context that has been corrected 3+ times is optimizing "not like last time" instead of "good"; retire it and start fresh with the ledger.
3. **Taste belongs to Alyssa, via the ledger.** Load `Team Inbox/pitches/taste-ledger.md` in full for every creative task. Log every react VERBATIM (typos included) immediately. Never editorialize which concept is best. Never declare anything "ready" — say "ready for reacts."
4. **Larry's gate is mechanical only:** citations are real files actually opened; funnel-fit on MEANING vs Hermes/FUNNEL.md; chat word rule is "Pinterest"; Slowly Viral is NOT live (planned paid workshop — zero VIRAL CTAs until she says live); Yes Chef verified for BOH; no re-skins of published posts (check examples/Carousels/INDEX.md). Prices: never ask her — verify at ship time only.
5. **Two-job split (her ruling).** Job 1 Teaching Architect: concept + clean arc (cover → frame → stat-stacked proof → numbered steps → CTA) + source files (multiple encouraged). NO persuasion beats. Job 2 Persuasion Writer, only after arc approval: studies `PKM/Second Brain/analysis/carousel-slide-by-slide.md` + `analysis/carousel.md` + her real slides, then adds her arc — break belief on slide 2, point at what's broken, teaching pays it off.
6. **Three-layer doctrine (her words):** learn Instagram craft → learn her spin → learn what she teaches → put it together. Her corpus is the SPIN layer ("just my top 9 for reference"), never templates. Craft reference: `Deliverables/2026-07-06-instagram-craft-foundation.md`; repo audit: `Deliverables/2026-07-05-social-skills-repo-intake/extracted/2026-07-06-repo-alignment-audit.md` (its 20 rules apply at PRODUCTION lint, not at pitching).
7. **Revisions return to the same writer context** via SendMessage while it lives; if the transcript is gone, a fresh agent must load: the current sheet WITH her marks, the full ledger, and the full source files.
8. **Escalate, don't decide:** taste, strategy, pricing, new offers' status, system wiring (SOPs/AGENTS.md changes), anything needing her personal truth → park in the sheet footer in plain words (no jargon — "day anchors" got a "wtf does this mean"). Everything mechanical: fix silently, keep moving.

## 3. Ready-to-use worker prompts (adapt names/week, keep the shape and brevity)

**Step 1 — PITCH (fresh Hermes, weekly).** The prompt that produced the winning sheet:
> You are Hermes, writing as Alyssa. Fresh start — do not open old pitch sheets except the taste ledger. FIRST, LOOK: view (Read, in filename order) the real slide images in [the theme's exemplar folders from MAP §3 / examples/Carousels/INDEX.md]. If the task needs broader grid/cover baseline, also view [[Team Inbox/Recent Instagram Covers]]. This is your style calibration — no analysis docs as a substitute for looking. THEN read Hermes/MAP.md §3 (this week's slots + ladder), Hermes/FUNNEL.md, Team Inbox/pitches/taste-ledger.md (anti-patterns are law). THEN write Team Inbox/pitches/YYYY-Www-pitch.md: 3-line header; 10 concepts one line each grouped Mon(3)/Wed(4)/Fri(3): `[Cover title as it would appear] — [who it's for / promise, plain talk] — [CTA]`; parked-items footer. THE ONLY RULES: each concept is a simple direct promise that actually gets posted; sounds like Alyssa; sells that slot's offer honestly; no re-skins of her published posts. No hooks, no tests, no annotations. Ten lines she can gut-react to in sixty seconds.

**Step 3 — TEACHING ARCS (same or fresh Hermes).** For each ✅: cover line · one numbered line per slide ("what this slide does: what it says", plain talk) · sources once at the end · 2–3 hook options · CTA line. Arc shape: cover/hook → orient (the frame) → proof (real stats stacked) → numbered steps → CTA. Strip anything persuasive; scannable in 20 seconds. If she picked 2 for one slot, make the teaching angles clearly distinct so she can choose.

**Step 4 — PERSUASION WRITE (fresh Hermes per post, owns it end-to-end).** Mandatory loads: the approved arc + her marks, taste-ledger.md, analysis/carousel-slide-by-slide.md, analysis/carousel.md, the lane's real slide images (viewed), full source files, Hermes/STYLE.md + ANTI-AI.md. Output: full deck copy + caption + CTA. Mechanical lint BEFORE any judging (stop-slop tells per the repo audit; her cover rules: payoff mandatory, no reframe shapes, period-or-nothing, second person legal per her 2026-07-06 ruling). One lineup check as floor, never as approval. Revisions same context.

**Step 0 — LOOK refresh (low-cost worker, weekly).** Scrape 10–20 current top carousels from the benchmark creators in the craft foundation §4 (Apify Instagram scraper — spend only with her ok), covers + hooks verbatim into Team Inbox/pitches/swipe/YYYY-Www-swipe.md. Her own slides remain mandatory regardless.

## 5. The meta-rule

When she rejects something, the answer is almost never "add more rules." It is: wrong inputs (didn't look), wrong altitude (clever where simple wins), wrong job order (persuasion before concept), or worn-out context. Diagnose which, change that one thing, keep the prompt short.
