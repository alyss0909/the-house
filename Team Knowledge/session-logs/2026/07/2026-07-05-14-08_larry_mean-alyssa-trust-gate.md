# Mean Alyssa as Trust Gate

## Context

Alyssa clarified the deeper operating insight from the automation and vault-structure analysis. Today's findings are converging on one mechanism: every big build session created knowledge instead of referencing existing source, and nothing consistently reconciled INDEX promises against disk afterward.

## Insight to remember

Mean Alyssa should not be a final review step. Mean Alyssa should be involved at every step of the way as a trust-preservation gate.

Before the team asks Alyssa a question, creates a wiki note, promotes a report, writes output, routes a task, or claims a source gap, Mean Alyssa should ask:

- Would this break Alyssa's trust?
- Would Alyssa reject the idea because it feels fabricated?
- Would she pause and wonder where this came from?
- Would she tell us to read the entire freebie, course training, BOH module, or prior source before proceeding?
- Is this a bad question because the team should already know the answer from past work?
- Should we search prior work, Google Drive, external gold-standard examples, or source exports before inventing?
- Would Alyssa be angry that this was added to the wiki instead of archived, linked, or deleted?
- Did this output lead nowhere because a later output was better?
- Should older created files be updated, demoted, deleted, or replaced by the better synthesis?

## Why it matters

The team is doing real work, and the outputs are not garbage. The problem is that useful outputs are not continuously synthesized into the vault's source architecture. Reports, agent runs, chats, open loops, and partial builds accumulate as bloat unless they are reconciled daily.

The end-of-day question is not "what did we make?" The better question is:

> What should be promoted, referenced, updated, deleted, archived, dispatched, or researched externally?

## Operating implication

Daily synthesis must connect every chat, every agent, every session, every open loop, and every unfinished output back to what Alyssa is ultimately building. The team must find the nuance across outputs, decide what survived, and remove or demote what did not.

Mean Alyssa's role is to protect source trust before source clutter is created.

## Mean Alyssa source corpus

Mean Alyssa should be built from many inputs, but one core input must be the full rejection corpus across the vault:

- every explicit rejection Alyssa has given
- every realignment
- every failed gate
- every "I would never publish this" moment
- every "this is wrong" or "where did this come from?" correction
- every source-trust break
- every instruction not to invent, summarize, over-structure, file incorrectly, or add clutter

The point is not just to know what Alyssa likes. The point is to know what she would hate **while the work is happening**: the bad question, the fabricated premise, the skipped source, the unnecessary wiki note, the too-clean output, the false provenance, the report that goes nowhere.

Mean Alyssa should use those rejections at every step and every job, before real Alyssa has to spend attention correcting the team.
