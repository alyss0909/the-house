## Decisions made

- **Question:** Should the Hermes brain reference the Second Brain vault or hold voice knowledge itself?
  **Decision:** Brain is judgment/routing layer only; every writing task mandates full tier-1 original loading per README recipe. This is now structural, not optional.
- **Question:** Who judges anti-AI-slop — a rubric, or something else?
  **Decision:** Alyssa's own saved anti-AI rules (`ANTI-AI.md`) sit above the TASTE rubric as the senior gate; the rubric alone already produced two false-positive gate passes.
- **Question:** Should final copy ever be written by a delegated subagent?
  **Decision:** No. After two subagent-written batches failed Gate 3, Larry/Fable writes final copy directly; subagents are restricted to research, forensics, and craft-file rebuilds.
- **Question:** What is the Content OS's A/B testing station?
  **Decision:** Not a virality predictor (documented as unreliable even by vendors) — a weighted rubric scorer (DM-shares > saves > swipe-through > comments) calibrated against Alyssa's real post history.
- **Question:** Email reply-words vs. IG comment-words — same rule?
  **Decision:** No. Reply-words are story-invented per send (free to create, e.g. "SHAMPOO"); IG comment-words are Notion product facts and must never be invented.

## Insights

- The single highest-leverage discovery this session was that **Alyssa already has the fix for most of what failed** — her own anti-AI rules note, her own Postmaster bot, her own bot persona/banned-word list — sitting unread in her vault. The lesson for future rebuilds: exhaust her own prior work before synthesizing new rules from scratch.
- A rubric that scores well is not proof of quality — it proved insufficient twice in this exact session (Gate 2 v1 passed its own rubric, Gate 3 failed outright both times). The real gate is a human reading it next to her real copy.
- Recycling a subject's *documented winning construction* (e.g., reusing "(on purpose)" from her highest-performing subject line) reads as more AI, not less — it's pattern-matching wearing a costume of good taste. Coining something new beats imitating a hit.
- Over-correction is its own tell: aggressively choppy, adverb-free, dash-free prose is a different AI template, not the fix. The target is always specifically HER style, not "less style."
- Metaphors fail not from being present but from being over-extended: the "cupcake FLOP" case showed a metaphor should live in the story beat and get one clean callback, not get re-mapped through every subsequent section.
