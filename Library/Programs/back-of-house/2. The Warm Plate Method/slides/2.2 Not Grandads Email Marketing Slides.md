## Email Has Changed (But Not How Everyone Thinks)

Yes — open rates are down, people are clicking less, and people are having more convos in the DMs. But people aren't spending less time in their inbox. In fact they're spending on average MORE time in the last 5 years.

They ARE getting better at ignoring emails that aren't worth their time. And ESPs are making changes to reflect that. (Gmail's "Do you want to unsubscribe from this sender?" pop-up — that's the new standard. Email platforms don't just want NOT spam, they want proof that your subscribers are actually into it. Because their job is to keep people in their inbox — and that's ours too.)

**The stats:**
- 88% of people check their inbox daily
- #1 channel for marketers whose highest ROI is email
- Nearly half are actively looking for offers

So there's clearly LOTS of opportunity.

The more "broey" marketers are just doubling down — emailing every day, trying to code their way out of the promotions tab. But both subscribers and email providers are tuned into something else entirely:

> *Is this actually interesting? Does it belong in my inbox?*

Trends have changed wildly since 2016 (back then we were putting gifs in footers like it was the height of innovation). But the approach hasn't changed much. Because **what actually works hasn't changed since the Mad Men era.**

## The Plan

| # | Step | What It Means |
|---|---|---|
| I | Spark Curiosity | If they don't open, nothing else matters. Subject lines, preview text, and the small tweaks that build "I always open her stuff" trust. |
| II | Hold Attention | Engagement is everything. Hook strategy, story flow, and formatting that helps your message land — even for skimmers. |
| III | Increase Engagement | Clicks are email marketing's love language. Guide readers toward clicking, replying, buying — behavior that builds your list's engagement score. |
| IV | Never Worry About Trends | Frameworks behind what's working. Trends keep changing, but when these are second nature you can apply any trend without changing your strategy. |

## Frequency

- Highly dependent on your community
- Recommendation: more frequently at first (like daily), then space out to every other day, every 3 days, etc.
- Don't recommend a long sequence

---

## Subject Lines

**Curiouser & curiouser:**
- NOT a summary of what's inside
- Use `(download inside)` or `[GIFT]` for higher open rates
- Personalize
- To emoji or not to emoji — your call
- A/B test

---

## Preview Text

- Continue the subject — tease and intrigue
- Short & sweet
- Consider they likely won't remember who you are
- Personalize & emoji & try new things
- Consider mobile

---

## The Hook

> A hook's only job is to open a loop by sparking curiosity or emotion so strong, they have to keep reading.
> The subject line opens the door, the hook is the smell of fresh baked cookies inside.

- Make it short & sweet
- With the right hook, readers can't help beginning, scrolling, and clicking

---

## The Meat

- Hook (taster line — no textbooks)
- Shorter emails tend to perform better
- Your tone + writing style
- Promises
- Layout
- Headline stack

**On visuals:**
- There's a tradeoff between design-heavy and simple
- GIFs, memes, personal updates, screenshots, handwritten notes all work
- Don't overdo it — but pretty emails do get more opens

## Click Tricks and Conversion Candy

### Calls to Action

Every email needs a CTA. Types to rotate:
- Gift
- Redirect
- Offer
- Reply
- CTA trio (above the fold + button + P.S.)

### Click Baits

Open rate is nice, but clicks pay the bills. These are small layout tricks, link tagging, and interesting CTAs that gently guide your reader out of skim mode and into click mode:
- Exit skimmer mode
- Habit forming
- Tricks to slow the scroll & capture the click

## Sale-Making Post-Scripts

If your reader's in a rush, distracted, or plain ol' sleepy — meet them where they're at. That's usually at the bottom of the email.

Structure:
- Something you *almost forgot*
- Reiterate the main point of the email & add the CTA
- `[Attention grabber] that teases [this outcome].`
- `P.S. I almost didn't share this... but here's why it matters to you.`

This adds a little mystery, a little value, and nails your last chance to get that click.

## Deliverability

- Get whitelisted
- Be careful with "spammy" behaviour (too many links, too many images, bit.ly type links)
- Clean your list (bounces ruin your rep)
- Opened, clicked & replied-to emails get delivered & prioritized

---

## The Delivery Email

Your first contact — and your highest open rate email. Use it to:
- Deliver the thing
- Let them know what to expect
- Get into the primary inbox
- Bonus: get a reply
- Surprise & delight
