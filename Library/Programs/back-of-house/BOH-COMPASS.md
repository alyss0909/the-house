## What BOH Is

Back of House is Alyssa's flagship $3,000 course. Four phases: offers + sales pages (P1), email automation (P2), Pinterest traffic (P3), repeatable launches (P4). The restaurant metaphor is structural throughout — it lives in the module names, the copy, and the student experience. One-liner: "The automated sales & marketing department for CEOs with better things to do."

## Key Definitions (read before referencing BOH IP in any copy)

**Email First Ecosystem** — Alyssa's offer architecture principle: design your full offer suite around the inbox, not social. The framework for why email is the primary channel. Module 1.1.

**The Double Yes Method** — Lead magnet philosophy. The freebie prequalifies the buyer: solving one specific problem that creates desire for the main offer. "Yes" to the freebie predicts "yes" to the purchase. Module 1.4.

**The Warm Plate Method** — The full automated welcome sequence system. Uses a "documentary" structure: symptoms → diagnosis → prescription. Alyssa's approach to making automation feel human. Module 2.1.

**The Simmer** — Weekly nurture email. Relationship-maintenance, not a sales pitch. Story stash, cadence, flavour profile. The "keep the pot warm" email rhythm. Module 2.3. Real examples: `Library/Examples/simmers/`.

**Bread & Butter Content** — 4 cornerstone evergreen content pieces per content pillar, each with an upgrade that routes to the email list. Feeds Pinterest and email simultaneously. Module 3.1.

**Slow Pinterest** — Alyssa's approach to Pinterest as a search engine, not a social platform. Keyword-first strategy, no daily posting required. Module 3.2.

**The Batch** — Pinterest pin batching system: ~70 pins per URL, 6 months of content scheduled in a single session. Module 3.3.

**The Lazy Launch** — Repeatable minimal-effort launch: 1 conversion event + 5 promo emails. Real urgency only. The doctrine for how Alyssa thinks about launches lives in the 4.1 transcript — **read this before writing any launch copy.** Module 4.1.

**Sign Off Specials** — Low-key product mentions tucked under the email sign-off. Soft sell at high frequency. Module 0.2.
