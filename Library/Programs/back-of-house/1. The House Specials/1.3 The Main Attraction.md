## Visual Language

Your visuals are…
- Psychological signals
- Subconscious cues
- Identity encoding

**Tone 🤝 Visuals** — When your sales page copy AND the visual language makes them feel some type of way, they're compelled to take action.

### Breadcrumbs

These are little clues your audience can start to recognize anywhere you show up. They're not logos. They're sensory cues. Micro-patterns. Visual déjà vu.

| BREADCRUMB | EXAMPLE |
|---|---|
| Texture | Linen napkin, film grain overlay, glossy plastic, crumpled paper, velvet drapes, terracotta clay |
| Lighting Style | Gritty glam like euphoria, golden hour, paparazzi-style flash, warm candlelight vibes |
| Crop Style | Wide Sofia Coppola style shots, close up influencer girlie crops, lots of white space etc |
| Recurring Object | Leather notebook, velvet curtain, vintage stamps, candle + matchbook combo, analog clock, post it notes, your matcha, specific mug, ipad |
| Symbols | Big windows, gardens, museums, clouds, busy cityscapes, beaches |
| Shape Language | Soft rounded corners, clean minimal lines, organized grids, nostalgic pixelated hard blocky edges, soft blurs, zigzag grid, pixelated 8-bit accents, overlapping blobs |
| Color Signature | Warm rich tones, clean minimalist colours, earth tones, playful bright colours, retro nostalgic, strong bold colours |
| Signature Phrase | Just do it, think different, because you're worth it, sell without selling out, you CAN sit with us, burnout isn't part of the plan, soft ceo, your brand-bestie, backed by data, your wallstreet bff, this is not a drill, anti-boring |

We LOVE patterns. But we HATE being bored. These breadcrumbs let you build repetition that still feels interesting.

This is **Subconscious Symbolism** — when your sales page FEELS like the outcome of your offer (they'll remember how it feels before they remember the details — so use this in your favor):

- If you're selling **clarity** — Use clean spacing, soft gradients, airy design
- If you're selling **energy** — Use pops of bold color, movement, and strong calls
- If you're selling **calm** — Use earth tones, slow scroll pacing, relaxed fonts

**Choose 2-3 subtle visual breadcrumbs that will appear across ALL your digital assets (sales page, emails, freebie, mockups, pins)**

### The Muse

You've already written great hawaii copy, because you know people don't just buy things, they buy who they get to become. GOOD branding continues the hawaii and invites them to imagine this new identity while reading the sales page (and interacting with your entire brand).

Choose a celebrity (or character) that represents your client's evolution, then design visual cues around that. What does the "upgraded" version of your client look like, and can we make your design reflect that transformation?

Your muse isn't for YOU! It's for the version of your client they're trying to become. Whether that looks more like Audrey Hepburn, Julia Fox, Zendaya, Emma Chamberlain, Solange, or Steve Jobs in a turtleneck is your call. But this is your visual cue. Choose wisely.

- My Muse:
- What are 3 visual traits this Muse embodies?

- Describe assets like tangible items ("Steal our exact brand book")
- Create branded graphics for bonuses
- Use visuals of the transformation (e.g., before/afters, graphs, industry data, real results)

## PayPal Pay in Full

If you don't know how you're collecting payment yet, a simple PayPal button works. No setup stress, everyone has it, and you can start selling NOW. Here's how to set up a pay in full and a payment plan using PayPal:

1. Go to PayPal
2. Click on "PayPal buttons"
3. Choose the option: shopping cart
4. Add the price
5. Select your currency
6. Click Create button
7. Click on "email"

That is the link you send to collect payment! And add to your payment buttons on your sales page (feel free to shorten it with bit.ly). Check out the advanced features below for other optional steps.

## PayPal Payment Plan

**STEP 1: CHOOSE A BUTTON TYPE AND ENTER YOUR PAYMENT DETAILS**

1. Go to PayPal
2. Click on "PayPal buttons"
3. Choose the option: subscription
4. Add billing amount each cycle: this is how much money your client is going to pay at each installment
5. Add billing cycle: this means how often your client is going to be charged the amount stated before (either each month or every couple of weeks, your choice!)
6. Add after how many cycles should billing stop: this will depend on your payment plan

**Example:** if your payment plan is 4 monthly payments of $100
- Your billing amount each cycle will be: $100
- Your billing cycle will be: monthly
- Your "after how many cycles should billing stop?": 4

**STEP 3: CUSTOMIZE ADVANCED FEATURES:**

If you want your customers to be redirected into a specific URL after they check out, check that box and add the URL to your "success" page. Once button is all set go ahead and click >> CREATE BUTTON.

**NOW IT'S TIME TO ADD IT TO YOUR WEBSITE OR TO YOUR EMAIL:**

- **OPTION 1:** click "website" and Copy + paste the HTML code so your button will look like the normal PayPal button (but it is kinda' ugly, so we usually go for option 2)
- **OPTION 2:** click "email" and you will see there is a URL you can hyperlink anywhere

## If You Chose "To Book"

**STEP ONE: YOUR CALENDAR:**

Choose your "call scheduler" platform — some popular options are Calendly and Acuity. I currently use Tidycal.

**THE PRE CALL QUESTIONS:**

This form should be accessible in your call scheduler like Calendly or Tidycal, where you can ask all your questions right in there. The pre-call form questions will be carefully worded and focused on:

- What is not working for them in their business, life or health etc?
- Why are they ready for a change?
- What would they like to see happen within 6 months?

This knowledge combined with their website and maybe their social media handles should give you a good understanding of their business / lifestyle and, consequently, the value that you could bring.

**THE POST CALL EMAIL:**

Have an email template ready that you will tweak for each person. The outline of the email will be something like:

- Explain how happy you are that you will be working together
- Reiterate at a "high level" what you'll be achieving together
- Provide them the bonuses/cost information you agreed on
- Give them link(s) to pay
- Outline payment plan as agreed upon
- Send them the link for them to sign up with
- Recap why you're going to be a great fit for one another
