## Thank You Page vs. Tripwire

After someone opts in, choose your follow-up path:

**Choose a Tripwire if:**
- You have a product that solves a specific version of the freebie pain
- You want to start filtering for fast buyers

**Choose a Thank You Page if:**
- You just want to keep moving quickly
- Your dream client wouldn't be ready to buy or book yet

---

### The Thank You Page

This page is about reassuring your dream client their freebie is on the way, and asking them to take the next step if they're ready.

> "Awesome! The [freebie name] is on the way to your inbox. In the meantime… [take this action]"

Actions to offer:
- Book a call (embed your scheduler)
- Join your group
- Follow you on a social platform
- Listen to this podcast episode
- Watch how I use it (Loom with CTA)

Always explain: [Why / What's in it for them?]

**Under no circumstances deliver the freebie here.**

---

### The Tripwire Should

- Solve a super specific problem or nagging pain you know from the freebie
- Be instantly usable (quick win = dopamine)
- Be aligned with your signature dish (get em hungry)
- Overdeliver on value (they should feel slightly guilty for only paying $9)
- Pre-qualify through context and tone (attract and repel)

> What doubt does your dream client need to overcome before saying yes to your main offer?

> This is how you know they're willing to PAY to solve this problem.

**Keep in mind:**
- They haven't even *opened* the freebie yet — let alone gotten the result. This can't be a "next step" that requires them to have done the work.
- How can they get the outcome they clearly want from the freebie — but BETTER, EASIER, SOONER, with LESS WORK from them?
- Make it CLEAR they aren't being scammed (the free thing is still on the way)

## The Five Tripwire Principles

A five-part framework — work through these in order:

### Part 1: The Real Problem — Find the Tension

Don't settle for surface-level problems like "I want more leads" or "I want a cuter website."

Ask: **What's at stake if they don't solve this?**

Use the 5 Whys method to uncover the emotional root:
> Why do they want more leads? → To get revenue up. → Why? → Because I can't pay my team. → Why? → Because I'm scared to let people down.

Pro tip: Lurk in communities, Reddit threads, YouTube comments. Look for emotionally charged complaints. That's gold.

---

### Part 2: Hair on Fire — Why Now?

The exact moment they're super motivated to fix the problem. What just happened that made them go, "That's it. I'm done. I need to fix this now."

Design your tripwire to speak directly to this moment.

*What are they Googling at 2am?*

---

### Part 3: The Micro Fix — Why This?

The best tripwires solve a SLICE of the problem QUICKLY. Done for you — templates, scripts, fill-in-the-blanks.

> If the macro problem is "I have no clients," the tripwire could be "3 scripts to turn followers into paying clients by Friday."

**Test:** Can they see a specific benefit within 10 minutes of opening it?

*What's the unspoken consequence of not solving this problem? Use this in your copy.*

---

### Part 4: Wildly Generous — Why Not?

Get them into a "WTF… why WOULDN'T I get this?" headspace.

Show a price-value mismatch: "This has helped people charge $5K / lose 30 lbs / etc… you get it for $9."

---

### Part 5: Seal the Deal — Why Buy?

- Use real scarcity: "Only available for 7 minutes," or "Only 5 calls per month," or "Only 10 at the early bird price"
- Use social proof: "1,300 people downloaded this last month"
- Guarantees: "Don't love it? Get a full refund"
- CTA buttons should repeat the value: [Yes, I want the 3 scripts for $9 >>]

## Common Tripwire Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Don't Be Informative

More info instead of a quick win. People don't wanna learn — they wanna WIN.

Make it an actionable fix with a clear, visible win. Focus on doing, not teaching.

- ❌ A 32-page ebook on the science of habit building
- ✅ The Daily Habit Tracker Template + 5-Minute Morning Plan. Start reprogramming your brain today in under 5 minutes.

The buyer should feel like they'll win immediately, not learn eventually.

---

### Mistake 2: Selling the Tool Without the Context

Selling a tool without explaining how you use that tool personally to solve their problem. When they can't see themselves using it, they don't value it.

Pair every tool with a use case, walkthrough, and example:
- ❌ Here's a content calendar template.
- ✅ Here's the exact calendar + a video walkthrough + 3 real examples of how to use it to plan 30 days of posts in 45 minutes.

---

### Mistake 3: No Hand-Me-Downs

Your repurposed product (highly recommend) feels repurposed. Buyers have a spidey-sense for recycled content. If it feels like something you pulled from a call or an old blog post, it kills trust.

Make it feel tailored or *unlocked*:
- ❌ A worksheet from my [course name] client portal
- ✅ My private onboarding framework — never released outside my $2K program. Yours today for $9.

Now they feel like they're getting the inside track. The product becomes a peek behind the curtain, not just a way to get their money.

---

### The Biggest Tripwire Sales Page Mistake

**Selling the product, not the relief.**

- ❌ Get my 10-page workbook on productivity systems for solopreneurs.
- ✅ Finally hit inbox zero in 15 minutes/day (without hiring a VA). Steal the exact system I used to reclaim 6+ hours a week.

**The Mad Libs:** Fill in the blanks to get a fully drafted tripwire sales page copy.

## Un-Boring Tripwire Examples

**Graphic Designer:**
"The Client Experience Bundle" — My project kickoff script, onboarding checklist, and 2 pre-written email templates that make you look ultra-pro and high-end (even if you're freelancing from your couch).

**Copywriter:**
"Plug-and-Play Funnel Copy Framework" — 7 fill-in-the-blank templates + 2 example pages + a 15-minute teardown video of what works and why.

**Life Coach combo:**
- Freebie: "7 Prompts to Journal Your Way Out of a [niche specific] Funk (PDF)"
- Tripwire: "The Breakthrough Habit Builder" — The 5-minute daily visualization my clients use to rewire old patterns and stay consistent without needing motivation. ($7)

**Copywriter combo:**
- Freebie: "25 Hook Headlines You Can Steal for Your Next Sales Page"
- Tripwire: "The 60-Minute Sales Page Swipe Pack" — Get my high-converting page outline + 3 done-for-you story arcs + plug-in CTA formulas. ($11)

## Delivery and Oops Emails

Your lead bought the tripwire — now deliver it and begin your email relationship.

**Delivery email:** Deliver the goods and add some positioning breadcrumbs. Hint at results.

**1 day later (Oops email):** Re-offer your call or tripwire one last time. Swap the oops for "hey you missed booking a call" if applicable. Note about tag.

## Design Notes

**Move quick with templates** — Canva lead magnet/workbook/ebook design templates.

**Tripwire sales page design:**
- Make the first part very obvious: the freebie is coming.
- Add a 7-minute countdown timer (Evergreen).
- Add an image making the discount almost obnoxious.

**Thank you page design:**
- Sell them on how incredible your discovery call/group etc. is.
- Embed your calendar so they know when they can book their call.
- Keep it very simple — add a button.
