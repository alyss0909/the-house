## The Rules of an Email First Offer Suite

| THE RULES OF AN EMAIL FIRST OFFER SUITE Rule One: It should sell without over explaining*Your offer needs to be so clear that a few emails and a strong sales page do all the work. If people need to DM you, watch stories, or jump on a call just to understand it, it's not ready for an email-first approach.* Rule Two:It should always be available*An email-first offer is ready when your audience is. It should have natural entry points like after a freebie, a webinar, or during an automated sequence that creates urgency on the buyer's timeline.* Rule Three:It should be easy to re-offer*Your offer should have multiple angles, bonuses, or price points so you can re-offer it without slipping into full launch mode OR feeling repetitive.*  |

## The Re-Offer Strategy

What's the **highest price** you'd confidently charge for your offer?

What's the **lowest price** you'd offer during a special promotion?

What are 1-4 **bonuses** you could add or remove to create genuine urgency?

What is the **\*lowest hanging fruit bonus\*** you could offer to **make buying a no-brainer**?

How could you use automated **re-offering strategies** like **bonuses, early bird pricing, payment plans, or new angles** to **naturally introduce your offer** again without **feeling repetitive**?

How many times per **quarter** will you **promote your core offer** to your FULL **email list**?

## Your Signature Method

**Your Signature Method**  
Having a signature philosophy creates instant TRUST! People are ready to say "**ok they've got a plan & it works**". The key to a good signature dish is having something unique that differentiates you from everyone else in your niche.

### *Audit your offer based on the criteria below* — A REALLY GOOD SIGNATURE DISH SHOULD:

- [ ] Be able to **hit your income goals ALONE** and without any supplemental offer   
- [ ] Be **scalable** ie: if you JUST did this, you would hit your target (even as they grow)  
- [ ] Have **viable profit margins** \- meaning you can spend marketing $$ on it comfortably   
- [ ] Have a clear and **defined BUYER** & target audience who you resonate with  
- [ ] Be **effortless for you to talk about**   
- [ ] Have a **clear outcome,** benefit, or solution (like a **5 year old** can understand)  
- [ ] Your students should be able to **complete it** with relative ease (compliance)  
- [ ] Should have **multiple lead in opportunities** (a freebie, call, webinar, low ticket offer etc)

## Old Way New Way

**OLD WAY NEW WAY:**

When thinking about your ideal client's problem and your philosophy, what is the **mainstream** or "old way" of solving this problem? These might be solutions they've subscribed to before (but aren't working) OR **what they don't want to do** but think is the answer to their problems.

How is your philosophy on solving their problem the "new way"? This should offer them a new perspective for solving the problem.
