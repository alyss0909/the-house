## The Strategy

Pinterest really is the dream combo of Google's SEO (high intent, evergreen lifespan) and social's enjoyable user experience (home feed — interest-based discovery & search-based discovery).

Not just passively scrolling but actively searching for ideas, inspo, solutions to problems they have. Your content gets indexed by Pinterest and can keep showing up in Pinterest's search results for months and years. We're building a LIBRARY not throwing flyers out a window.

Make the content you've already created work exponentially harder for you. Pinterest lets you plant a seed that keeeeps growing over time.

**How we use Pinterest:**
1. Create Bread & Butter Content — strategic "forever" blog posts, podcasts, or videos that answer your audience's biggest questions, optimized for Pinterest search so they get traffic forever.
2. We batch, loop & automate this process so new leads are added to your email list daily — with just a couple hours of work scheduling per quarter (no NEW content required).
3. You set this up once and it keeps bringing in leads forever. No chasing trends. No stressing about the next post.

## Part Two — Keywords

Keywords just tell Pinterest what your content and offers are about. That's it — don't get in the weeds here.

### Keyword Types

| Type | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Problem-aware | They know they have a problem & wanna solve it somehow | "How to be more productive" |
| Solution-aware | They know a specific solution exists | "How to time block" |
| Brand-aware | They know the brand they want to use | "How to time block in Notion" |

### Short-tail vs Long-tail

| Type | Example |
|---|---|
| Short-tail (seed words) | Productivity, Notion, Anti-aging |
| Long-tail phrases | "How to be more productive work from home", "Best Notion Templates", "Best serums for wrinkles" |

Using a mix of keyword types and tail lengths in your pin titles, descriptions, and on the pin graphic itself, as well as on the destination content (like a blog post or landing page), helps Pinterest understand and categorize your content. This allows your pins to show up for a wider range of relevant searches, driving more traffic to your site.

**To do:** Find 10 short-tail and 10 long-tail for each of your pillars & save them.

### Keyword Research Tools

**The Pinterest Search Bar** — one of the best places. Type in a topic and see the auto-complete suggestions. These are actual search terms people are looking for and likely have high search volume. Also, scroll down after a search to see related keyword boxes — Pinterest shows them to people who searched your initial term. Pure gold.

**Pinterest Trends** — see what people are searching for and, importantly, when they're searching for it. Helps with timing your content — post about a trend 2-3 months before it peaks. You can also compare search volume between different keywords.

**The Pinterest Ads Account** — keyword research tool that can show you estimated search volume for keywords. While Pinterest Trends shows relative volume, the Ads tool provides numerical estimates. Go to: Ads → Create Campaign → Manual Campaign → Get Started → Brand Awareness → Continue → Choose Your Own → Interests and Keywords → Add Keywords. (You don't need to turn your ad on — just draft a campaign to get the intel.)

**External Tools** — Semrush for keyword variations and topics. Google Keyword Planner. Specialized Pinterest tools like Pin Clicks for competitive analysis (pin scores and difficulty). Paid tools can accelerate the process.

For each piece of core content (blog, freebie, offer), list primary keywords, secondary keywords, and relevant long-tail phrases.

### Evergreen vs Trends

Pins take time to index — post pre-trend. If it peaks in January, post in October or November. Think: Black Friday, back to school, summer skincare routine.

### Competitor Research

Use Pinterest search to see what's already ranking at the top for your target keywords. Pinterest ranks pins based on what it thinks is the best content for that search term, not chronologically.

Analyze the top 10–25 pins (excluding ads). Look for:
- What do the visuals have in common (colors, fonts, image style)?
- What angles or specific topics are they covering? This refines your understanding of search intent.
- Keywords used in their pin titles and descriptions.
- Click through to see their destination content and offers. Affiliate links? Own products? Lead magnets?

You are analyzing for ideas and concepts, not copying. Take the successful ideas and put your own spin on them.

**On GPT:** GPT does NOT know search volume or have keyword data — use it for analysis or ideation only.

## Part Three — Account Optimization

K so you've got your keywords, now how do we use them to make sure your pins actually come up when ppl search for them. Keywords need to be placed strategically in multiple spots.

---

## The Big 7 Keyword Placement Areas

1. **Profile Name** — 65 characters; include your name OR business name + an identifying keyword about your content/solution
2. **Bio** — 500 characters; who you serve, how you solve their problems, something about you with personality, CTA; 5 keywords; explain more about who you are and what you do in terms of what kind of content you create; include a call to action to follow you or check out your website/services/products
3. **Board Titles** — use keywords as titles; extremely important for indexing
4. **Board Descriptions** — keyword-pack the descriptions; 500 characters, 5 long-tail keywords; hook, solve the search intent, what's in the board, what it solves, keyword-filled details, CTA
5. **Pin Titles** — keyword-rich and descriptive; one of the first things Pinterest looks at to categorize your pin; crucial for ranking
6. **Pin Descriptions** — engaging descriptions that naturally incorporate multiple keyword variations; can add extra keywords (separated by commas) at the end if they don't fit naturally
7. **Image Overlay Text** — use bold text with relevant keywords; include your main keyword or a compelling hook prominently on the image itself; the algorithm reads this too

**Bonuses:** Alt Text (primarily for accessibility but helps SEO; keep it simple and descriptive, naturally including relevant keywords, no keyword stuffing) · File Names (minor impact; format: `[seed-keyword]-pin#-Q1` e.g., `branding-pin1-Q1`)

**Make sure Pinterest knows:** who you are, who you serve, how you serve them, and what they're struggling with now.

### Photos

- Set your profile photo (match your other social media platforms; opt for a photo of yourself over your logo — more personable)
- Upload cover photo (1600 x 900 px; instant visual representation of your brand — collage of past client work, brand photo with text overlay, or graphic promoting your latest opt-in)
- Board covers (clear & searchable)

### Board Strategy

- Create **4 core boards** matching your content pillars
- Create **1 offer-specific support board**
- Add **5–7 niche-specific support boards** that align with your audience's interests (e.g., "Creative Business Tools," "Time-Saving Tips for CEOs")
- Freebie board: "Free Resources for Entrepreneurs" to highlight all opt-ins
- Use keywords in board titles; write keyword-rich board descriptions
- Fill boards with 15–20 initial pins (a mix of your content and complementary third-party content)
- Each blog post is pinned to its most relevant core board first; same pin can be repinned to niche-specific boards for added visibility; freebie-related pins go to both the freebie board and relevant core/niche boards

**On Group Boards:** Group boards used to be the way to grow traffic fast, but Pinterest now prioritizes fresh, relevant content over boards cluttered with random pins. Most group boards have become spammy free-for-alls that hurt your reach instead of helping. If you participate: be selective (look for boards where pins regularly get 10+ saves), contribute thoughtfully, track results after 30 days, and consider starting your own super curated board with trusted collaborators.

### Pin Optimization Overview

*(Full breakdown in next lesson — pin batching module.)* Strategic keyword placement on individual pins:
- Overlay text on image
- Pin title
- Pin description
- Destination URL (include your main keyword in the URL of your blog post or landing page if possible — Pinterest reads the URL)
- The image itself (Pinterest can "read" and understand images; standard pin size is 2:3 aspect ratio, like 1000×1500 pixels)
- Img alt text

Always include clear CTAs on the pin image text overlay (e.g., "Download Free Guide," "Learn More," "Click to Read").

## Part Four — SEO for Destination Content

Pinterest doesn't just look at the pin — it also looks at the content behind it on your website. Make sure your blog posts, landing pages, or other destination content are highly relevant to the pin that drives traffic there.

### Blog Content Optimization

- Use vertical (pinnable) images
- Add keywords in the alt text of images
- Save image files with keywords in the `.jpg` file name
- Optimize blog post titles for search using long-tail solution-focused keywords (keep under 60 characters)
- Use H2s with supporting keywords — break up content into clear chunks with H2 headings that incorporate supporting keywords; Pinterest scans these for context
- Grow your email list with a lead magnet embedded in the content
- Optimize meta description with your primary keyword at the beginning
- Customize the URL slug to include keyword (e.g., `yourdomain.com/email-funnel-tips`)

### URL Optimization

**Opt-in + Tripwire pages:**
- Keyword in the title of the page
- Clear, keyword-rich headline
- Keyword in the URL slug
- Optimized meta description (make the promise clear in the first 75 characters)
- Vertical, pinnable mockup image
- Keyword in the file name
- Keyword in the image alt text

**Sales page:**
- Keyword in the title of the page
- Clear, keyword-rich headline; include benefit keywords and audience keywords
- Meta title should include the offer TYPE & niche keywords (e.g., "notion template course for business coaches")
- Keyword in the URL slug
- Optimized meta description (make the promise clear in the first 75 characters)
- Vertical, pinnable mockup image with keyword in file name and alt text
