## The Evergreen Advantage

This might be the only non-automated thing you do every week. And it's also the most important. Everything else in your Back of House runs itself. This is the ritual you show up for — and when you do, it pays you back in attention, trust, and sales. Every. Single. Time.

If the only thing you have to do to keep your business warm is send one email a week? That's a low-maintenance, high-return setup. That's front-of-house charm.

You're not doing 12 things a week to stay relevant. You're doing one thing really well. That's the power of an email-first business — it's really sustainable.

We asked: how could we create (and automate) content that resonates so deeply that our work stands out to your dream client in their endless scroll of regurgitated same-sameness?

## The 8 Hallmarks of Evergreen Content

1. **Timeless** — answers questions your audience always has, not just seasonal fads
2. **Core to Your Method** — anchored in your signature philosophies and frameworks
3. **Repurposable** — one post fuels emails, social snippets, reels, pins with minimal edits
4. **Clear Message** — just showing up saying whatever can make it confusing what you DO; evergreen content cements your authority
5. **Always Generating Leads** — consistently attracts and qualifies prospects behind the scenes
6. **Assets Prevent Burnout** — critical for early-stage sustainability; keeps you off the content treadmill
7. **Tool to Scale** — your capsule library of 12–20 cornerstone posts becomes the blueprint for group programs, courses, delegated workflows, and SEO-driven growth without doubling your workload
8. **Loved by Search** — when you focus on timeless, problem-solving posts, search engines (and Pinterest's search algorithm) reward you with "legs" in the feed — steady, organic traffic long after trends have faded

## Content Pillars

Your Signature Topics are a handful of big ideas you share about all the time. These core content pillars should support your philosophy, and you should have an interesting point of view on them.

**Recommend 4 pillars total.** Each pillar should include:

- **Offer:** Which of your offers directly relate to this content pillar?
- **Freebie:** Do you have a lead magnet or content upgrade that organically relates to this pillar? If not, ideas for future ones that naturally fit into the conversation?
- **Tripwire:** Any low-ticket offers that feel like a natural next step? Ideas?
- **POV:** What is your point of view, angle, philosophy or voice on this content pillar? Think: your unique way of solving their problem that you teach inside your product or execute when working with clients.

**POV Examples:**
- All my recipes take less than 5 mins to make
- All workouts require no equipment & can be done at home
- SEO copy focused on connection over keywords
- Your relationship isn't the problem, it's your communication style
- The key to scaling your business is nervous system regulation

*(e.g. a health and fitness coach selling 1:1 training might have the pillars: form, nutrition, strength training and mobility)*
