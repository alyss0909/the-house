## My Rules for Organic Marketing

People will tell you Pinterest isn't a social platform, it's a search engine — and that's true. But let's be real: it's just another place you have to make content for, right?

Our goal isn't to swap one platform for another — it's to find a new way altogether.

**My criteria for organic marketing to replace daily posting:**

- **Create once, let it work forever** — once a pin is indexed it's findable; we focus on resurfacing content around timeless problems + sustainable solutions
- **Don't need NEW to "stay relevant"** — Pinterest likes FRESH but doesn't care about NEW
- **Appeals to buyers or can pre-qualify leads** — pinners are buyers & ALL our URLs are funnel forward
- **Focus on impactful > excessive marketing** — we focus on keyword-optimized, well-written content that builds authority over time
- **Can be batched & automated easily** — we're about to batch 6 months of pins
- **Not trend based / dependent** — search intent is fairly stable and predictable (solve the problems your people are searching for)

**The goal of this strategy:**
- Gets consistent eyeballs on your content (think: thousands of impressions monthly)
- Funnels those clicks straight to your lead magnets and offers
- Because Pinterest users are already problem-aware and solution-seeking, they're way closer to converting than random social scrollers

My plan is to buy back 6 months of your time from marketing to spend on anything else.

## Pinterest Best Practices & Compliance

We're not here to get banned. Pinterest has rules and we play nice — no pinning the same thing over and over. Space it out by a week, tweak your designs and descriptions, and you stay in the safe zone.

**The Rules:**
- **Max 5 Boards Per URL** — use 5 highly relevant boards per URL = broad distribution without over-saturating
- **7-Day Spacing for Reuse** — our rotation logic makes sure each board–URL combo is spaced 7 or more days apart
- **Consistent Posting** — 7 fresh pins per day, rather than sporadic bursts
- **Freshness First** — we prioritize metadata strategy & fresh content, generating 1,400 pins that are truly unique in Pinterest's eyes
- **Quality & Keyword Rich** — good design & keyword-rich metadata across the board
- **NO Duplicates** — our plan guarantees NO duplicate pins are ever posted (no repeated image–title–board–URL combinations that could flag your account)

**What you leave with:**
- **Zero spam risk** — never posting the same combination twice within 6 months
- **Daily pins to all your content & offers** — 7 unique "fresh pins" per day, each linking to one of your powerhouse URLs
- **1,260 pins made & scheduled** — over 6 months, crafted to keep your content evergreen and always discoverable

## What Pinterest Defines as Fresh

Pinterest defines fresh pins not just by image uniqueness, but by the total combination of:

- Image pixels (design + overlay + CTA)
- On-image text (OCR scanned)
- Pin title
- Pin description
- Destination URL
- File name (minor impact)
- Board it's pinned to
- Save date (timing + spacing)

They prioritize these "fresh pin" combinations in search results, giving them a visibility boost. Our plan makes sure every single pin you create is a unique combination, to increase your chances of discovery.

**On Re-Pins:** Back in 2018, re-pins were the secret sauce. But in 2020, Pinterest flipped the script and started suppressing re-pins, focusing on fresh content instead. We found a way to satisfy Pinterest's love for fresh content: you can re-pin to multiple boards and still keep things fresh, without violating any rules — which is exactly what the 7×10×4×5 formula does.

## The Master Formula — 7×10×4×5

**This 6-month Pinterest plan posts 7 pins per day** (1 per URL across 4 cornerstone blogs + 3 offer-related URLs: freebie, tripwire, offer), using 10 designs per URL (5 templates × 2 overlay text variations), paired with 4 title+description combos and scheduled across 5 boards per URL — resulting in 1,400 unique pins, each spaced to avoid repeating the same URL–board combo within 5–7 days, fully Pinterest-compliant and 100% batchable.

| Element | Number | How |
|---|---|---|
| URLs | 7 | 4 cornerstone blogs + freebie + tripwire + offer |
| Design templates | 5 | Layouts optimized for Pinterest (2:3 ratio) |
| Overlay text variations | 2 | Per template |
| Total designs per URL | 10 | 5 templates × 2 overlays |
| Title + description combos | 4 | Per design |
| Boards | 5 | Relevant boards per URL |
| Total unique pins | 1,400 | Fully unique combinations |
| Posting cadence | 7/day | Over ~200 days (6 months) |

## Bulk Create in Canva

### Design Checklist

1. **Choose 5 Design Templates** — pick layouts optimized for Pinterest (2:3 ratio); use Canva Design Trends for inspo
2. **Add Branding Elements** — apply your brand fonts and color palette; add your go-to CTA; insert your website URL; add mockups or product images if relevant; format them until they fit 7–12 word overlays
3. **Update File Names** — rename each Canva file BEFORE bulk upload; format: `[seed-keyword]-pin#-Q1` (e.g., `branding-pin1-Q1`)

### Bulk Create Process

1. **Prepare your data sheet** — fill in headlines and points (one row per design); make sure there are no empty cells (Canva will choke)
2. **Import data into Canva** — open Bulk Create → upload your spreadsheet
3. **Connect the data to the design** — link headline to headline field; link point 1 to point 1 field, and so on
4. **Bulk create your designs** — click "Continue" → "Generate all"; wait for Canva to spit out all your slides
5. **Review and make small edits** — adjust text that doesn't fit; tweak spacing, font sizes, image placements if needed; double check for weird line breaks
6. **Export designs** — export at 2x for high-quality PNGs

## Scheduling

### The Spreadsheet & Image Folder

Organize your assets in the pin planner spreadsheet — images, titles, descriptions, boards, and URLs all in one place.

### The Shuffle

Our Custom App Script rotates combinations of your asset titles, descriptions, boards, and images so that:
- Each asset appears once per day across 200 days
- The title/description sets cycle every 4 days
- The board changes daily (avoiding repins to the same board within 7 days)
- The image used rotates every 10 pins

This creates a balanced, varied schedule without manual shuffling — so Pinterest gets fresh, mixed content daily.

### The Auto-Scheduler

Use the auto-scheduler or go manual for uploading. Upload in chunks of 200 (the platform limitation).

**Limitations & Workarounds:**
- Images must be public URLs (hosted on your website) — use the 📌 Prep for Pinterest button in the app to scrub your drive URLs
- No repeat URL → Board in the entire sheet, even if there's a new image
- 200 pins at a time — add a tracking parameter to URLs by adding `?p=1`, `?p=2` (the 🛎️ Schedule Generation button does this for you); then upload in chunks of 200

## The Upkeep

After your batch is live:
- Sit back — you'll be posting all your blogs, freebie & offers every day for 6 months, 200 times each
- Notice which **topics, designs, and overlay text** are most saved & re-assess before the next batch
- Pinterest drives traffic to your website → leads to your opt-ins → onto your list → through your sequence and regular emails → to your offers
