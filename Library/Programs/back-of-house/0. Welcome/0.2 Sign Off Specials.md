## What Sign Off Specials Do

**Every email you send should do 3 things:** Grow, Nourish & Offer.

1. **REAL VALUE** (Grow)**:** Give em some value to take away (education, new perspective, a good LOL) the idea is this gets them Whether it's education, inspiration, interesting perspective or a good laugh, THIS is why they opted in to get your emails) 

2. **DEEPEN THE RELATIONSHIP** (Nourish): The best emails feel like a love note from you, they nurture the relationship, and make your community feel seen.

3. **OPEN A DOOR** (Offer): It could be a click, a reply, or a sale.  The goal is to create an opportunity for your reader to lean in a little closer.

But **not every email is a sales email** so how do we make offers (& a little cash) in the emails designed to nourish? And how do we do it without coming off pushy in EVERY email?

That's where ***The*** ***Sign Off Specials*** come in.

When you're sending those value-packed regular nourishment emails, the Sign Off Specials section is your gentle nudge. Tucked in the little space under your signature is PRIME realestate your specials are riiight where people naturally linger in the email, the perfect spot to offer a gentle invite that aligns with what they just read.

**It's my favourite way to open the door for sales without turning the whole email into a pitch.**

## HERE'S HOW TO CREATE YOUR SIGN OFF SPECIALS:

*It's not just about dropping links here's how to write great sign off specials*

**STEP 1: CHOOSE YOUR OFFERS**   
Pick 2-4 links that make sense with what you've just shared, these should feel like a natural next step with tiny, intentional invitations.

What are the most important next steps you want readers to take? *(think: your signature offer, booking a call with you, freebies, low ticket offer, upcoming webinars, mini workshops etc…)*

**PRODUCTIVE NOTE**: you should have go-to sign off specials, but also leave wiggle room to add things that make sense with the email you just wrote

**STEP 2: SELL 'EM:**   
Use your copy prowess, storytelling and a bit of charm to make each link feel intriguing. Here are some of my go to techniques (and templates)

- **Bring the Drama:** Ever wondered how \[offer\] can help you \[achieve specific benefit\]? Take a peek and see for yourself.  
- **Challenge Accepted**: Think your workflow can't get any simpler? \[Offer\] might surprise you.  
- **Paradox:** Create more content by doing less. Yes, really. Check out \[Offer\].  
- **Bring Recipepts:** The \[offer\] that's helped \[specific type of customer\] \[achieve benefit\] might just become your new favorite thing.  
- **Show & Tell:** See how \[offer\] makes \[specific result\] feel as easy as a Sunday morning.

**STEP 3: ADD A SOFT CTA**   
Instead of a generic "Check out our services" try something intriguing like: *Take a peek, check it out, see it in action, get the details, or learn more.* 

**STEP 4: BEGIN WITH A WARM INVITE**  
These should feel light & easy like a casual recommendation, not like you're sneaking a sales pitch into the chat.

- *If you're looking for more ways to \[achieve a benefit\], here are a few options you might love:*  
- *I didn't want you to miss out on these…*  
- *Before you go, here are two ways we can work together*  
- *PS: wanna REALLY deepen your relationship? When you're ready here's how we can help:*  
- *Just putting this here in case it's exactly what you need today…*

## THE PSYCHOLOGY BEHIND IT:

*If you're nerdy about understanding buyer psychology, here are a few reasons I think these work to make sales in every email.*

🧠 **The Zeigarnik Effect:** People remember unfinished tasks. When your email offers a few thoughtful links, it creates a sense of incompletion. That little "I need to close the loop" nudge can drive clicks without any pushy vibes.

🚦 **Endowed Progress Effect:** If your email already delivered value, these links feel like a natural next step. It's less of a sales pitch and more like continuing the conversation.

🍬 **Low Friction Engagement:** Multiple links mean there's no pressure to make the "right" choice. It feels casual and low-stakes, more like browsing than committing. And that's when curiosity takes over.
