## Who she is

Alyssa Coleman. Productivity strategist, automation enthusiast, antisocial accomplice since 2017.

She teaches creative service providers to build automated email + Pinterest marketing systems so they can stop relying on social media — and get back to the work (and life) they actually care about.

**The one-liner she uses:**
> "Slow marketing for the creatives who'd rather be making dough."

**The brand promise in full:**
> "The automated sales and marketing department for ceos with better things to do."

---

## What she sells

**Flagship:** [[Library/Offers/back-of-house/MAP|Back of House]] — a course/community that walks creative service providers through building their entire back-end marketing system using email + Pinterest. Four phases: offers, email, traffic (Pinterest), and soft sales.

**Brand positioning:** She's not a social media strategist, a mindset coach, or a generic productivity consultant. She's specifically the person who helps you build the behind-the-scenes system that runs while you're offline. The restaurant metaphor is literal: BOH = the Back of House of your business (everything that makes the front-of-house look effortless).

**Audience:** Creative service providers — copywriters, designers, coaches, therapists, nutritionists, professional organizers, "witches." Smart. Good at their work. Slightly burned out by the content demands. Desperately want permission to stop posting daily.

---

## Her voice in five words

*Conspiratorial. Grounded. Dry-funny. Permissive. Precise.*

See [[Studio/Analysis/writing-style|writing-style.md]] for the full breakdown of what these mean in practice.

**What she sounds like:** A best friend who happens to have done the research, gone down every rabbit hole, and came back with the actual answer. She's not a hype person. She's the "k here's the plan" voice note person.

**What she doesn't sound like:** Hustle culture. Corporate positivity. Fake urgency. Generic aspirational lifestyle content. AI-padded marketing speak.

## The food/restaurant metaphor system

This metaphor isn't decorative — it's structural. It organizes her course names, CTAs, section headers, and key copy moments. See [[Studio/Analysis/writing-style|writing-style.md#2-the-metaphor-system]] for the full taxonomy.

Core terms: **Back of House**, **The Simmer**, **Mise en Place**, **The Signature Dish**, **Warm Plate Method**, **prix fixe menu**, **prep once serve again**, **nonna**, **slow cooking vs. fast food**, **cooked up**, **recipe**.

Anti-metaphor (what she's escaping): **feeding the machine**, **feed the content beast**, **always hungry**, **half-baked**.

---

## The origin story

She tells the same story across every major page — edited for context. Always includes:

1. Portugal trip - small fishing village, peaceful
2. Came back and dreaded work for the first time ever
3. "On paper everything was fine" - business was thriving, maternity leave funded, 3-day work week
4. The real problem: she'd systematized and hired a team, then spent all her time managing them
5. "Burned it all down" + hyperfixated
6. Came out the other side with: just Pinterest + Email
7. Built Back of House from that
8. Now teaching others to do the same

The emotional arc mirrors what she promises her audience: *they'll get to the other side too.*

---

## The permission pattern

Her most distinctive structural habit is giving explicit permission to do what the reader secretly wants:

> "So, go ahead, waste your time."
> "Let social media be a creative outlet again."
> "Back of House isn't designed for you to do more."
> "A good idea loses its wings every time a creative is too busy to go down a rabbit hole."

Permission is not just tone. It's the product. The back of house runs itself *so that you can* rest, daydream, go down rabbit holes.
