## The House Report

_✍️ Some Inside Intel on what's working, and what's not in various service-biz niches. Reported from BTS of private clients and our students inside Back of House ✍️_

**📉 WHAT'S OUT 📉**

### Reinventing your offer every season

"September is baaaasically the pre-game to the big buying seasons, and every. year. I see people panic to whip up *Back to School Bundles* or invent *New Year Challenges* from scratch. It looks exhausting, and imo it makes your audience treat your work like fast fashion 😬.

**😏 Instead: we keep selling your signature dish.**
🍝 The SAME meal, just plated a lil differently.

This is the thing you're KNOWN for 👏👏, the steakhouse doesn't start making soup just because fall hits - they just start pairing it with beets (or something more heirloom sounding iykyk)"

---

**📈 WHAT'S IN 📈**

### Leveraging the Buying Seasons (hard)

"_So what ARE the four natural buying windows left this year?_ Here's a little debrief on each of them, each has its own psychology, and if you align with it, sales can (honestly) feel effortless."

### 🍎 O1: Back to School 🍎

"So the first buying season is all about ⚒️ structure ⚒️, everyone's a liiiittle messy after summer, and September makes us crave a routine again. When you're leveraging this one, **position your offer as the thing that makes structure, organization & systems easier.**

💡 What's working right now is bundling a quick win WITH your core offer. Add in a fast win offer like a 'Fall Focus Calendar' or '5 Hours Back Workflow' and suddenly your same offer feels like the cure to their back to the office overwhelm"

_(Source: The Promo Planner (Notion edition))_

---

### 🖤 O2: Black Friday 🖤

"Then we've got the only time people are actually expecting deals. They're ready to weigh their options & make a purchase so you really don't have to force any urgency, it's already there.

Buuuut don't slash your core offer price (PLEASE! 🥺).

💡 Instead, lean on reframes to your offer like **Bonus Stacks** (same offer, just extra goodies) or a **Girl Math Reframe**. Stack bonuses, run **early bird incentives,** or drop a **bonus vault** that disappears at midnight."

_(Source: The Promo Planner (Notion edition))_

---

### ❄️ O3: Holiday Season ❄️

"December starts to split the room: some people are in full spend mode, others are hiding their credit card in the freezer 🙋‍♀️. The trick is to pitch your offer as a gift to their _future self_. You can't sell one MORE thing to do; you're better off selling relief and setting themselves up for momentum in January.

The last few years, I've been loving all the cozy 'gift yourself ease' type campaigns with (very personal) year in review storytelling. My favorite way in is a subject line like: _Gift yourself a softer January._ Inside, you recap your year, remind them of what didn't work, and set up your system as the 2026 solution.

💡 Pair these emails with bonuses that feel cozy but strategic: a **Year in Review journalling prompts,** a **guest training,** or **extended access** so they can start fresh in January."

_(Source: The Promo Planner (Notion edition))_

---

### 🍾 O4: The new year, new me energy 🍾

"Everyone's buzzing with fresh inspo and new goals, they want change (and.. QUICKLY). The smartest move here is to give them the first taste of your bigger system so they believe in the bigger picture.

💡 Offer reframes like **Resolution Shortcut** (buying is fastest way to hit their *new me* goals), hit hard here. Pair that with incentives like a **founders rate** or **lifetime price** to make it an easy yes (even if their pockets are feelin light)"

_(Source: The Promo Planner (Notion edition))_
