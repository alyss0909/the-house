## The House Report

✍️ _Some Inside Intel on what's working, and what's not in various service-biz niches. Reported from BTS of private clients and our students inside Back of House_ ✍️

📉 **WHAT'S OUT**

**Broken Record Emailing**
If you're selling one main offer (which I highly recommend), it's easy to slip into saying the exact same thing over and over again, right? "this is what i do, this is what i offer, this is who its for and DON'T FORGET TO BOOK A CALL!"

If you do that for too long your list is gonna tune you RIGHT out, and honestly are much more likely to unsubscribe.

👀 So hooooow do you keep making the same offer week after week without being so repetitive and blasting your whole list?

The trick is finding ways to keep the invite FRESH, by sending it to the right ppl at the right time.

For example, we have a Follow-up email automation that only goes to people who click the booking link, but never actually book a call with you. It doesn't go to the whole list, it feels personal, and it still books calls every single week without you blasting your list.

**💌 O1. The Phantom Ring Email**
Here's our template:

> Subject: Did you still wanna chat?
>
> Hey, [Name]!
>
> I saw you peeked at booking a call with me but didn't grab a time.
>
> I thought i'd reach out to see if there was a time that worked better for you? Or if you have any questions about what we might cover let me know!
>
> If you're still curious about [result/offer], here's the link again [add your calendar]
>
> If now's not the right time, and you were just creeping then no worries, I'll be here.
>
> xx

📈 **WHAT'S IN**

**Self Selling Emails**
Okay, so this is fun.. 😏 one of the things I'm seeing working really well right now is… not selling at all. Like, you set the scene, give them two doors you'd be happy for them to walk through, and let them decide.

They self-identify, and suddenly you've got people replying "yep, I'm in Camp Two, what's next?"

😀☝️ I got this idea from parenting a toddler (who has a future as a lawyer 🫠), the easiest way to get a yes from her is by not trying to get her to say yes at all! Just offer two snack options that i already approve of and let her "choose" (except in this case, you get paid in the end!!).

So give it a shot & let them sell themselves. Here are two emails we send all the time that do exactly that:

The first one's what I call a Right Time Offer, here's the scoop👇

**💌 O2. The Right Time Offer Email**

🎟️ These are value first emails that end with a little invitation to work together that makes sense within the context of that value email. RTOs tie back directly to the story you're telling in the email, and instead of a hard CTA it's more of an "oh, and btw.." kinda moment, that really will only register to the people who are READY and want more info now.

Here's an outline:

> [Tell a short relatable story here that ties back to your ultimate philosophy, method or core theme of your offer]
>
> Zoom out and share what that moment taught you, or tie the story to some kind of lesson or insight:
>
> And it got me thinking about how [big idea your reader needs to hear]
>
> Add a quick takeaway here that they can use right now like a checklist, journal prompt (or like maybe a nice little email template or something? Wink wink nudge nudge)
>
> Here's [1–3 bullet tips, or takeaways] so you can [result].
> - takeaway
> - takeaway
> - takeaway
>
> One of my [clients/students] just used this exact approach inside [your offer] and [specific mini win they had]. We set it up using [highlight a feature of your offer e.g., "the notion email prompt vault]. Right now, im [insert a reason that they should join this offer right now, like "reviewing emails live this month before the vault goes back into the archives"
>
> If you want to [result you just described], reply [give them a reply-word] and I'll send you the details.

This type of offer works SOOO well and is a key strategy we use inside Back of House to create weekly sales because...

👀 1. You can slip it into the convo that they're ALREADY interested in
🔨 2. You highlight one of the features to make it tangible,
⏰ 3. You give 'em a reason to join NOW to create some urgency, ANDDD
🐌 4. The reply-word makes it effortless for them to take the next step.

When they DO reach out in a personal convo about your offer, shoot them the details and let the connection naturally happen.

**💌 O3. The Two Camps Email**

In this one, you describe two types of people, or mindsets, within your niche. Camp One is the not (yet) ready crowd, and Camp Two is ready to go. The reader self identities and if they're in Camp Two, your offer naturally becomes the next step. ⛺

Here's how to write it:

> In [describe your niche/business], I usually meet two kinds of [people/clients]
>
> ⛺ Camp One: [describe in detail their current habits and problems, call out their patterns so they feel SEEN by you]
>
> ⛺ Camp Two: [describe exactly what they want to have and the results they're wanting (that your offer solves) Paint the "after" picture clearly enough that they feel themselves there.]
>
> If you're in Camp One, here's why you're going to want to [insert thing here] so that [future pace what happens if they don't make a certain change]
>
> [include next step for camp one that indicates the shift they need to make in order to move into camp two]
>
> If you're in Camp two, here's where we go from here…
>
> [include a short, curiosity driven CTA that leads to your offer being that naturally next step]

People loo0ove identifying themselves, and if they see themselves in the "right fit" description, they'll feel like you created your offer just for them (which, you totally did😏) so no need to hard sell. 💸
