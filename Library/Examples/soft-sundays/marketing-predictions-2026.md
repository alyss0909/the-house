## The House Report
_✍️ Some Inside Intel on what's working, and what's not in various service-biz niches. Reported from BTS of private clients and our students inside Back of House ✍️_

"I've been thinking a lot about what's actually shifting as we land two feet into 2026, especially for service providers and **people who make their money online.**

More specifically, **for soft CEOs who want to do marketing in a way that feels a little more analog, a little more grounded, and a lot more focused on building deeper relationships** with their community instead of relying on social media for their next client.

**Here's what I'm seeing, both in my own business and behind the scenes with clients who make their money online but don't want to live online.**"

### 🔮 Prediction O1: The Post-Mortem of Organic Reach

"We've been saying 'ORGANIC IS DYING' for years, but I really think this is the year where trying to find clients on social media crossed the line from frustrating **to a genuine waste of (precious) time for most small businesses**

Algorithms are fully monetized now, and it's getting harder and harder to reach your OWN followers, let alone get in front of new audiences _(Meta hitting record ad revenue in 2025 while organic reach fell below 1% tells us eeeverything we need to know)_.

**It's not a matter of doing more trial reels, being shadow banned or using the wrong hashtags anymore, we're just being priced out.**

_(OK YES, you can still succeed on social, you can still grow an audience and monetize — BUT you'll have to put your back into it. Better editing, more thoughtful copywriting, and greater posting frequency is what's converting…)_

So, if you're looking for a **slower marketing** alternative the shift I'd make here is pretty clear. **Instead of pouring your energy into the feed, I'd start reallocating that time to creating things that LAST.**

📝 **Ask yourself:** How can I create **BETTER evergreen** content that lasts (and you have more control over) like your blogs on your website, pins leading to your opt-ins, long form and **SEARCHABLE** content for youtube etc.

Lastly, If you've been meaning to start a newsletter, **THIS IS THE YEAR!** And if you already have one, you better not be treating it like an afterthought. Your best ideas can be used (and reused) in newsletters, sequences and other emails bc THAT's where things are converting and don't just disappear after a scroll."

---

### 🔮 Prediction O2: SEO Becomes a Girl's Girl

"The second big shift is around SEO and search becoming way more conversational.

Think about it… we used to Google things like **"sushi Toronto"**

Now it's more like, **"Where should I go for sushi in Toronto for an aesthetic 30th birthday party, with good lighting and vegan options?"**

This is **great news for anyone who's been intimidated by SEO**, because you don't have to understand long tail keyword ranking bla bla bla anymore, 😅 you JUST have to **know what your clients are ACTUALLY asking** (you've probably answered these questions verbatim already).

📝 **Tangible shifts I'm prescribing:** Write blog titles like real phrases, add FAQ sections to your offer pages and blogs (LLMs eat those up) and use Pinterest for visual search because rn **data shows 25% of product and service discovery happening through AI (and that's expected to double)**, and Pinterest has majorly stepped up its game, with its AI integration beating out Google…that's kind of a big deal for small creators."

---

### 🔮 Prediction O3: The AI Kiss of Death (& Tiny Audiences)

"**I'm all for being cringe in public buuut** there's something about spotting a chat-written caption passed off as someone's own insight.. that's just unsettling at this point.

Most of us can spot AI content immediately, and even when we can't exactly put our finger on what feels off, we subconsciously clock it **and discount the creator's credibility. 🥀**

What this means is that audiences are naturally gravitating towards things that feel really nuanced, and are happy to sit with smaller creators, or less frequent content when it feels thoughtful and worth their time. _(our collective substack addiction is proving this one)._

I think **tiny lists are gonna continue to outperform big ones** this year because they feel more intimate. I'm seeing this with my own clients every single day! The work that's getting the best response isn't the most polished (or viral) but more unique, or just **having a surprising perspective**.. **THESE are the things that people are clicking on.**

📝 Something we've been trying is adding **more voice notes, personal photos, GIFs, playlists** etc to your newsletters or using actual voice notes as your jump off point when writing a story based email so you're naturally sounding more like YOU.. (imagine that?!)."

---

### 🔮 Prediction O4: Paying for Curation

"**Lets face it, we're all overstimulated AF.**

It's clear that buyers are tired, and decision fatigue is taking its toll, so **the more confusing your offer suite is, the harder it is for someone to say yes.**

My recommendation to almost every client right now is to **have ONE clear offer, and ONE clear way to get the result your client actually wants.**

📝 If I were you, I'd consolidate your offer suite wherever you can, make sure every offer naturally sells the next thing, so you can go deeper with the same audience instead of constantly creating new content (and paying for new leads). This year's hubspot report showed having 1-3 TOTAL offers actually **increased average order value by 27% and lifetime customer value by 45%** and I don't think that's changing anytime soon.

**💻 _THE CLEARER YOUR OFFER IS,_** **🧲 _THE CLEARER YOUR MARKETING WILL BE,_** **💵 _THE CLEARER YOUR CLIENT WILL BE._**"
