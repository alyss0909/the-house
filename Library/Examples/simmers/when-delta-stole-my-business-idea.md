# when delta stole my business idea (kinda) 😒

Did i ever tell you about the time **I almost quit my business because of delta.**

So, a few years ago I **mapped out an entire automated offer funnel on a napkin** during a red eye flight to LA. We're talkin' emails, webinar, lead mag, hooks, post-purchase sequence.

It was **Strategic. Inspired. Beautiful.** (Deranged probably, but BEAUTIFUL).

Buuuut somewhere between wheels down and room service, it disappeared. 🫠

**Gone.**

Just... not in my bag. 👜
**Not tucked in my book.**
Not folded into my passport like I SWEAR TO GOD it was.

---

**🥹 And here's the kicker:**

I COULDN'T.
REMEMBER.
ANY OF IT.

---

Just poof. Like my brain offloaded it onto the napkin and deleted the file.

It was truly *my tragedy* of 2017

But that's the thing about being a creative entrepreneur, right? Our **best ideas are usually trapped in scraps**, scribbled in notebooks, voice noted to your business bestie, buried in a notion folder called 🤯 *brain dump*

**And while I (literally) cried into my room service brownie** about the napkin idea that was going to finally automate my course sales and give me back my ✨**offline fridays** ✨

---

I sat in my robe and fantasized 🧖🏻‍♀️ about the days when I worked a "**regular job**" and someone just…

**told me what to do.**

---

I **desperately wanted a manual, a playbook, a training**, a ~here's how it actually works behind the scenes~ file that I could study and run with.

**You ever wish for that, [FIRST NAME GOES HERE]?**

…that someone would just hand you THE book. The one from the past owner, **the one with the details on what to do and when, what numbers to hit, how to reset when things go sideways.**

**It's the kinda oxymoron creative entrepreneurs eat for breakfast.**
😒 Too stubborn to let anyone tell you what to do
😓 Too swamped to reinvent the ~~napkin~~ wheel again.

_Since then, I've made it my mission to MAKE the manual for service pros. After working behind the scenes in hundreds of businesses **we've stripped down the system and cut everything but the two systems that actually move leads to sales** (pinterest brings the right people in, email seals the deal)._

# O1: Pinterest Traffic Kiosk

_choose one evergreen freebie & create 3 pins for it using keywords your audience is **actually** searching for (not just a catchy hook)_

📌 **Why: People go to Pinterest to find solutions AND it's EVERGREEN**
Pinterest works like a search engine (but you already knew that). Because of that, it can drive traffic to your list for months (or even years) after posting. This checks off my #1 marketing requirement: **EVERGREEN** FRIENDLY.

---

# O2: Email Reception Desk

_create a welcome sequence that ACTUALLY does something (not just clog up their inbox)_

💌 **Your first few emails should feel like you saying: I see you, and here's what I think could help.**

Start with a belief shift or NEW philosophy they need to hear. Then, walk them through a real conversation you've had with a client, a big AHA moment from your method, or a reframe they didn't know they needed. Keep it short, story focused & persuasive.

---

# O3: (Soft) Sales Register

_install "**right time offers**" in your email strategy so people can buy when THEY'RE ready, not just when you're selling (aka sales come in between promos)_

💳 **Why: Email converts 3x better than social media.**
No more keeping up with trends to get eyes on your offers + it doesn't require constant engagement to work. Plus, the ROI? A cool $42 for every $1 spent. **Translation: Email turns that Pinterest traffic into paying clients**
