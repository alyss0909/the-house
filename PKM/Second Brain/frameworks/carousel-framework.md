Do not write from this file alone. The framework is the map; the source examples are the terrain.

## The 6 Archetypes

### 1 — Trend Report
**Goal:** Saves, non-follower reach, authority
**Slide count:** 9–10
**Series potential:** Very high — Part 1/2/3 structure compounds reach

**Arc:**
Cover (series title + audience qualifier) → Stakes frame ("things are changing") → Trend 1 problem → Trend 1 implication → Trend 1 silver lining → Trend 2 with data → Tactical action step → Pattern interrupt (dark bg) → Freebie CTA

**Required elements:**
- Series "Part N" label handwritten top-left on every slide
- Credibility anchor on frame slide (see below)
- At least one named data point with source
- Freebie offer (not a paid product) as CTA

**The credibility anchor — use near-verbatim:**
> "I've reviewed my notes (& industry reports) from last year and this is what I'm seeing in my own business and BTS with clients who make their money online but don't want to live online."

---

### 2 — Manifesto
**Goal:** Shares, brand trust, parasocial deepening
**Slide count:** 9 (6 permission slips + 1 brand slide + 1 engagement question)
**Series potential:** None — standalone emotional moment

**Arc:**
Taboo reframe cover → 6 "I" statements → Brand/logo pause slide → Engagement question (no product ask)

**Rules:**
- Every content slide is a first-person "I" present-tense declaration
- One maxim slide mid-deck (not an "I" statement — a universal principle in 4–5 words)
- The closing slide is always a question, never a CTA
- Footer identity statement on every slide: "FOR CEOS | REFUSING | TO RUSH" or equivalent
- No data, no framework, no teaching — pure identity mirror

**The "I" statement formula:** "I [verb phrase that the reader secretly wants to say about themselves]"
- "I don't need a reason to take my time."
- "Thoughtful decisions age better." ← the maxim slide (only non-I statement)
- "I trust myself to know the difference between moving slowly and being lazy."

---

### 3 — Step Framework
**Goal:** Saves, shares, product priming
**Slide count:** 7–8
**Series potential:** Medium

**Arc:**
Bold outcome promise cover → Philosophy/context with early CTA seed → Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3 → Step 4 → Desire bridge slide → Product CTA

**The metaphor system (required):**
Every step framework carousel needs a recurring metaphor domain tying the steps together. The step headline IS the metaphor — not a plain label. The reader decodes each one by reading the body copy. This is the swipe mechanic.

Example from Feb 10 (food/restaurant domain):
- "The Key Ingredient" = keywords
- "Serve Your Bread & Butter" = evergreen content
- "Take Reservations" = email opt-in
- "Fresh Batches Same Recipe" = repinning strategy

Choosing a metaphor domain: Food works because it aligns with the BOH brand. Other valid options: architecture, gardening, fashion/wardrobe, travel. Pick one and commit — every step headline must fit.

**Step body copy formula:**
- Bold tier: Outcome or counterintuitive insight ("Content that keeps driving traffic for months (or even years)")
- Body tier: My advice: [specific, tactical instruction]

---

### 4 — Story / POV
**Goal:** Comments, community building, follows from warm-ish audience
**Slide count:** 7 — tightest format, no extras
**Series potential:** None — links to offer/training

**Arc:**
Personal decision cover + kicker → Common assumption → Complication → Tension peak → The pivot → Appetite CTA → Product/event reveal

**The 5-beat story structure:**
1. **Assumption** (what most people believe): "Most people think to get more sales you just need more eyeballs."
2. **Complication** ("BUT"): "But social platforms reward volume over value."
3. **Tension peak** (emotional beat, often in handwritten italics): "It's all very high maintenance... *and well, exhausting*"
4. **Reframe question** ("WHAT IF"): "What if you didn't need more eyeballs? What if you needed better ones instead?"
5. **Pivot** (three handwritten lines naming the new model): *email first / evergreen content first / pinterest over instagram*

**This archetype does NOT teach the model.** It names it and creates desire. The training or product is the teaching. Use this as a top-of-funnel POV post, not a how-to.

---

### 5 — Product Reveal
**Goal:** Comment-mechanic conversions, direct product sales
**Slide count:** 10–11
**Series potential:** Medium — often part of a campaign sequence

**Arc:**
SEO-optimized cover → Philosophy frame → Early CTA slide (slide 3) → Feature 1 (as reader benefit) → Feature 2 → Feature 3 → Feature 4 → Problem diagnosis late → Full benefit summary → Brand sign-off slide → Final CTA (same headline, different background)

**The key rule — education IS the product demo:**
Every feature is introduced as a reader benefit, never as a product description.
Formula: `[Reader's objection/pain] + "I've got you." → [Outcome, not feature name]`
Not: "Tutorial: Loom walkthrough of Notion"
Yes: "Been avoiding Notion because you don't know where to start? I've got you."

**The double CTA technique:**
Same headline on slide 8 (one background) AND slide 11 (different background). The background switch tricks the brain into treating it as new. Captures both mid-deck and end-of-deck readers.

---

### 6 — Data Argument
**Goal:** New followers, saves, cold audience conversion, high-ticket launches
**Slide count:** 10
**Series potential:** Low — standalone argument

**Arc:**
Thesis cover (3-punch formula) → Proof 1 → Proof 2 (3 data points) → Proof 3 → Villain reveal → Pivot → Solution 1 → Solution 2 (with testimonial) → Solution 3 (email layer) → CTA with deadline

**The 3-punch cover formula:**
```
Punch 1: [Fear + validation] — "Organic Marketing Is Dying. You're not imagining it."
Punch 2: [Observable symptom] — "Reach is way down. Clicks & engagement are dropping."
Punch 3: [Rage-bait cliffhanger] — "But the real reason might piss you off."
```
Punch 3 must be a cliffhanger or provocation. "But" is the hinge word.

**The villain reveal (emotional peak of the carousel):**
Name who profits from the reader's continued obedience to the broken system. From March 3: "We're creating free assets for billionaire platforms like IG, TT, & Google. The more content you make → the more people want to be on their sites → the more they make."
This converts frustration into righteous anger — the highest-action emotional state. Use once per campaign.

## Copy Rules

### Three-layer voice system

| Layer | Format | Carries |
|---|---|---|
| All-caps spaced tracking | ALL CAPS WITH SPACE | Authority: data, argument, observations |
| Large editorial serif | **Big headline** | Statement: the memorable phrase, the paradox |
| Handwritten/script | *casual, lowercase* | Intimacy: asides, CTA whispers, personal notes |

One handwritten annotation per slide maximum. It's the warmth layer — overuse kills it.

### Required voice signatures

**1. The parenthetical aside**
Main text makes bold claims; parenthetical does the intellectual honesty work.
- "(you've probably answered these questions verbatim already)"
- "(it's hurting conversions)"
- "(at least for me it was)"

**2. The personal evidence anchor — use exactly once per carousel:**
"I'm seeing this with my own clients every single day" or the credibility formula above.

**3. The identity mirror — the brand's most repeated phrase:**
"make their money online but don't want to live online"
Use in the frame/credibility slide of every Trend Report or Data Argument.

**4. Extended vowels for emphasis (never exclamation marks for intensity):**
"BUUUT..." / "EEEVERYTHING" — used once per carousel at peak emphasis.

**5. The rhetorical wink at the payoff slide:**
"How's that for a chill content strategy?" / "(Imagine that?!)" / "Kind of a big deal."

### CTA copy rules

**Comment keyword mechanic:**
Visual treatment: handwritten script "Comment" + contrasting bold all-caps keyword.
Examples: "Comment PINTEREST" / "Comment TEMPLATE" / "comment yeschef"

**Identity qualifier before the CTA — strongest format in the dataset:**
Precede the comment trigger with the reader's internal monologue in their own voice:
- "FOR THE 'I WANT MY MARKETING TO SIMMER IN THE BACKGROUND' GIRLS"
- "FOR GIRLIES WHO'D RATHER BE ON PINTEREST THIS THURSDAY"
- "FOR CEOS REFUSING TO RUSH"

**Offer framing always uses "STEAL MY" or "my EXACT":**
Positions the offer as a gift the audience takes, not a product they're sold. "Steal" implies insider access.

**The final slide never hard-sells.** It lands softly: an engagement question (Manifesto), emotional close + reassurance (Story/POV), or product reveal that feels like a gift (Data Argument).

### Power word clusters

**EASE:** low maintenance, slow marketing, simmer, evergreen, lasts, background, system, automated, steady, compound

**IDENTITY:** smart founders, lazy CEOs with high standards, girlies, service pros, refusing to rush, high standards

**ANTI-HUSTLE:** not living online, intentional, done less, only what lasts, sustainable, once and done

**PERMISSION:** you don't need, you only need, it's easier than you think, I've got you, this is your sign

**FOOD/RECIPE (for Step Framework carousels):** bread and butter, key ingredient, serve, recipe, simmer, hot and ready, take reservations, fresh batches, make it once and make it good

**VILLAIN (for Data Argument carousels):** priced out, cooked, tricked, billionaire platforms, disappears after a scroll, not a system

## Design Rules (for briefing/copy writing context)

### Color → content-type signal

| Background | Content type |
|---|---|
| Deep wine/burgundy | Manifesto, brand identity, emotional posts |
| Warm cream/parchment | Trend Report (series), Step Framework |
| Pale powder blue | Data Argument, evidence-based posts |
| Dark charcoal (one slide only) | CTA/pattern interrupt — never full carousel |

### Prop selection

Every carousel needs one recurring prop character. Rules:
- Photographically repeatable across multiple shots
- Creates visual variety through angle/scale/staging changes
- Metaphorically reinforces the carousel's content

Prop → metaphor examples: serving tray = "serving" information; coffee cup = private thought; butter/food = recipe framework; wine glass = refinement; torn paper cards = personal/handmade.

### Footer watermark (non-negotiable)

A consistent footer tagline on EVERY slide — no exceptions. Choose one per carousel:
- `BACK — OF — HOUSE`
- `THE MOST — AESTHETIC — MARKETING PLAN`
- `FOR CEOS | REFUSING | TO RUSH`
- `PREP YOUR BIZ FOR A NEW MARKETING ERA`
- Or create a new one specific to the carousel's series/campaign

### The Pattern Interrupt rule

Every carousel has EXACTLY ONE slide that breaks the visual template:
- Falls at ~75–80% through the deck
- Uses dramatic background shift OR brand logo fills frame OR full product spread
- Announces the CTA zone
- Is NEVER the last slide — at least one more follows it

### Series architecture

- Part 1: Broadest concept, most accessible, highest discoverability
- Part 2: Deepens one strand — slightly more specific, rides Part 1's momentum
- Part 3: Most product-adjacent — smaller audience but most qualified

Label EVERY slide in the series with `Part N` (handwritten or small caps). The Part 1 cover must stand alone — new viewers shouldn't need prior parts to understand it.
