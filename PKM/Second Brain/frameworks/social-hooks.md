# Social Hooks

> Source-grounded hook patterns from Alyssa's real social content. Use this alongside [[frameworks/carousel-framework|carousel-framework.md]] for archetype selection, then check [[examples/Carousels/captions|captions.md]] and the matching slide folder before writing anything new.

**IG-specific rule:** The hook does two jobs on Instagram: it is the first line of the caption visible before truncation, and it is the on-screen text of the cover slide. Both must land independently. The caption hook earns the tap; the slide hook earns the swipe.

---

## The 7 Hook Formulas

### 1 — The Paradox
**Emotion:** Curiosity + identity recognition
**Metric:** High saves, high shares
**Template:** `[Taboo/negative behavior] + [legitimizing reframe]`
**Rule:** The taboo word must be something the reader already does and feels guilty about. End with a period, never a question mark. It's a statement, not a question.
**Examples:**
- "Wasting Time Is Part of My Process."
- "Why Smart Founders Are Going Low Maintenance"

### 2 — Bold Outcome Promise
**Emotion:** Aspiration + urgency
**Metric:** High shares
**Template:** `How To [extreme outcome] [timeframe or qualifier]`
**Rule:** "All" and "every" are maximizer words. Add a time anchor. Subtitle carries the audience qualifier so the headline stays clean.
**Examples:**
- "How To Get All Your Clients From Pinterest This Year"

### 3 — Personal Testimony
**Emotion:** Curiosity + parasocial intimacy
**Metric:** High comments
**Template:** `Why I [dramatic personal decision] + [confident kicker]`
**Rule:** The kicker carries the confidence. Use "and," not "but" — the kicker is a consequence, not a concession.
**Examples:**
- "Why I Swapped Instagram For Pinterest (and never looked back)"

### 4 — Trend Alarm
**Emotion:** Fear + urgency + FOMO for the solution
**Metric:** High follows from cold audience
**Template:** `[Industry norm] + [threat verb]`, then add the 3-punch formula for maximum follows
**3-punch formula:**
```
Punch 1: [Fear + validation] — "Organic Marketing Is Dying. You're not imagining it."
Punch 2: [Observable symptom] — "Reach is way down. Clicks & engagement are dropping."
Punch 3: [Rage-bait cliffhanger] — "But the real reason might piss you off."
```
Punch 3 must be a cliffhanger or provocation. "But" is the hinge word.
**Examples:**
- "Organic Marketing Is Dying"
- "2026 Slow Marketing Trends (Part One)"

### 5 — Reframe Question
**Emotion:** Curiosity + insider-truth feeling
**Metric:** High views + comments
**Template:** `What's actually [happening] in [year] (AND WHAT TO DO INSTEAD)`
**Rule:** "Actually" signals insider truth and implies others are getting this wrong. The parenthetical must be on the same cover.
**Examples:**
- "What's Actually Shifting in 2026 (And What To Do Instead)"

### 6 — Audience Identity
**Emotion:** Recognition + self-selection
**Metric:** High comments from beginners
**Template:** `The [Named Framework] for [colloquial audience descriptor]`
**Rule:** "Beginners" is the highest-intent search word. "Wanna" signals peer, not professor.
**Examples:**
- "The Pinterest Marketing Plan for Beginners"

### 7 — Visual Joke / Strikethrough
**Emotion:** Delight + shareability
**Metric:** Maximum shares
**Template:** `[Eyebrow-raising phrase with strikethrough] + [handwritten correction]`
**Rule:** The visual gag must be screenshot-worthy before reading any copy. Use maximum once per content cycle. Reserve for cold audience campaigns.
**Examples:**
- "How To Make Pinterest Your Sugar ~~Daddy~~ *Mama*"
