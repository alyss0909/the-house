# Subject Line Operating System

← [[PKM/Second Brain/frameworks/INDEX|Frameworks]] | [[PKM/Second Brain/analysis/subject-line|Subject Line Analysis]] | [[PKM/Second Brain/analysis/writing-style|Writing Style Analysis]]

This is the working system Wren should use when Alyssa says:

> draft subject lines for this email

The evidence lives in [[PKM/Second Brain/analysis/subject-line]]. This file does not replace it. This file turns that analysis into an operating system: inputs, pattern selection, subject options, preview pairings, and a reusable generator.

## What The Second Brain Already Knew

The existing subject-line analysis is already strong. It already contains:

- the "raccoon in a cashmere sweater" north star
- 12 named patterns from the 351-broadcast Kit dataset
- open vs click distinction
- the Three Subject Line Jobs decision framework
- preview text principles
- Soft Sunday, sales, event, and replay formulas
- template banks
- anti-patterns

So the new value is not another generic analysis. The new value is the **production interface**: how Wren chooses the right 10 patterns for a specific email and outputs subject + preview options without flattening Alyssa's voice.

## The Core Rule

Before writing options, identify the email's job.

| Job | Optimize For | Use These Patterns First |
|---|---|---|
| Relationship / trust | opens and affinity | DM-not-headline, Why I, Soft Sunday wrapper, crooked phrase |
| Resource / training | clicks | Anti-advice, High-value noun, I Fixed, useful contradiction |
| Sales / promo | action | Personal update, odd-dollar coupon, reason-why, small-number access |
| Event / replay | link click | re: appointment ping, class link, replay bracket, now/tmrw language |

Wrong job = wrong subject, even if the line sounds good.

## The Top 10 Alyssa Subject Patterns

These are the 10 working patterns Wren should output by default when asked for "10 subject lines."

Each pattern has a distinct job. Do not write 10 versions of the same formula.

### 1. Anti-Advice Reframe

**Use when:** The email teaches a better way, challenges common advice, or contains a strategic correction.

**Core formula:**

```text
DON'T [common advice] — [better move]
```

**Alyssa-shaped variants:**

```text
DON'T "[thing they think they should do]" do this instead...
```

```text
stop [default behavior], try this
```

```text
the [tactic] advice I'd ignore
```

**Performance proof:** The biggest click outlier in the Kit dataset was `DON'T "grow your list" do this instead...` with `1209` total clicks.

**Preview pairing job:** Put the replacement plan in the doorway, not the whole answer.

```text
the slower option is weirdly faster
```

**Wren note:** This is the Back of House worldview in subject-line form. Use it when the email has a genuine reframe, not for fake hot takes.

### 2. re: Appointment Ping

**Use when:** Event reminder, webinar, planning session, live class, replay deadline, or anything that has a real time anchor.

**Core formula:**

```text
re: tmrw's [thing]
```

**Alyssa-shaped variants:**

```text
re: your [month] plan
```

```text
tmrw: [soft specific outcome]
```

```text
details for tmrw, {{ subscriber.first_name }}
```

**Performance proof:** `re: tmrw's meeting` was the top opener in the dataset. Other `re:` variants also dominated opens.

**Preview pairing job:** Immediately honor the premise so it doesn't feel like a fake reply.

```text
your tiny pre-meeting note
```

**Wren note:** Powerful but easy to abuse. Use only when the body opens in correspondence mode.

### 3. Why I Strategic Confession

**Use when:** The email has a story, belief shift, personal decision, or counterintuitive lesson.

**Core formula:**

```text
Why I [did something that sounds wrong]
```

**Alyssa-shaped variants:**

```text
Why I burned down a perfectly good [thing]
```

```text
Why I'm leaving [platform / strategy / identity]
```

```text
Why I stopped trying to [expected goal]
```

**Performance proof:** `Why I burned down a perfectly good business (on purpose)` won both opens and clicks; `Why i'm leaving social media` was a top opener.

**Preview pairing job:** Add the "there's a method to the madness" clue.

```text
it looked dramatic but the data was rude
```

**Wren note:** The confession must be strategic, not diary-only. Alyssa's audience wants the rabbit hole finding.

### 4. DM-Not-A-Headline

**Use when:** The email is personal, casual, relational, or needs to feel low-polish on purpose.

**Core formula:**

```text
[short text-message fragment]
```

**Alyssa-shaped variants:**

```text
thought of you
```

```text
you coming?
```

```text
k embarrassing story from my 20s
```

```text
oopsie, I've been gatekeeping?
```

**Performance proof:** This pattern appears repeatedly among high performers and is one of the hardest for generic AI copy to fake.

**Preview pairing job:** Add specificity or emotional texture.

```text
lemme fix that lol
```

**Wren note:** If it sounds like a newsletter headline, it failed. It should sound like Alyssa sent it while doing three other things.

### 5. High-Value Click Noun

**Use when:** The email contains a concrete asset: plan, checklist, replay, template, class link, swipe, coupon, invite, fix.

**Core formula:**

```text
your [specific useful noun] inside
```

**Alyssa-shaped variants:**

```text
your [month] plan inside
```

```text
the [problem] checklist
```

```text
your [year] MASTER PLAN inside
```

```text
no [thing]? no problem, fix inside
```

**Performance proof:** High-click emails repeatedly include weighted nouns like `plan`, `checklist`, `replay`, `class link`, `fix`, and `coupon`.

**Preview pairing job:** Show why they need the thing now.

```text
for the part you keep avoiding
```

**Wren note:** "Inside" is weak unless the noun has weight. Never write `details inside` when the real noun is `plan`.

### 6. I Fixed Your ___

**Use when:** Alyssa has organized the mess, made the plan, built the shortcut, or created a resource from the reader's chaos.

**Core formula:**

```text
I fixed your [specific mess]
```

**Alyssa-shaped variants:**

```text
I FIXED your email list
```

```text
I fixed your "idk what to send" problem
```

```text
I fixed your Q4 plan
```

**Performance proof:** `I FIXED your email list (the plan inside)` was a strong full-list click performer and fits Alyssa's "k here's the plan" identity.

**Preview pairing job:** Make it feel concierge, not bossy.

```text
put it on your little tray
```

**Wren note:** This pattern is Alyssa as strategic best friend. It should feel like help already happened.

### 7. Personal Update + Specific Payload

**Use when:** Something actually changed: offer, bonus, deadline, pay plan, exception, behind-the-scenes decision.

**Core formula:**

```text
Personal Update & a [specific payload]
```

**Alyssa-shaped variants:**

```text
personal update & a weird little coupon
```

```text
a small update and a big coupon
```

```text
ok, tiny plot twist
```

```text
this changed the plan a little
```

**Performance proof:** `Personal Update & a $12,212 coupon` and similar exact-number subjects produced major click volume.

**Preview pairing job:** Explain the reason without flattening the curiosity.

```text
I did the math and got annoying about it
```

**Wren note:** Do not waste `Personal Update` on normal promos. It only works if something is genuinely updating.

### 8. Concierge Personalization

**Use when:** The email delivers a link, plan, invite, resource, or hand-picked recommendation.

**Core formula:**

```text
{{ subscriber.first_name }}'s [specific thing]
```

**Alyssa-shaped variants:**

```text
{{ subscriber.first_name }}'s Custom Pinterest plan
```

```text
Class link {{ subscriber.first_name }}!
```

```text
thought of you
```

```text
made you a tiny plan
```

**Performance proof:** Merge-tag and implied-personalization subjects repeatedly performed, especially around class links, plans, and high-intent audiences.

**Preview pairing job:** Make it feel prepared for their table.

```text
hot and waiting quietly
```

**Wren note:** Personalization works because it matches the restaurant/concierge brand, not because merge tags are magic.

### 9. Soft Sunday Weird Wrapper

**Use when:** Personal, reflective, cozy, story-driven, belief-driven, not a hard sell.

**Core formula:**

```text
[2-6 word weird/emotional phrase] |🍦Soft Sunday
```

**Alyssa-shaped variants:**

```text
I failed |🍦Soft Sunday
```

```text
Stoner Math |🍦Soft Sunday
```

```text
I'm Leaving |🍦Soft Sunday
```

```text
socks & sandal marketing |🍦Soft Sunday
```

**Performance proof:** Soft Sunday wrapper has brand equity and appears among top openers when the front phrase stays short and emotionally crooked.

**Preview pairing job:** Add a tiny clue or aside, not the moral.

```text
girl math's chill cousin
```

**Wren note:** Keep the wrapper, but do not make the front phrase too helpful.

### 10. Conceptually Crooked Phrase

**Use when:** The email needs curiosity but not clickbait; the idea itself has a weird angle.

**Core formula:**

```text
[specific phrase that should not quite make sense yet]
```

**Alyssa-shaped variants:**

```text
Stoner Math
```

```text
socks & sandal marketing
```

```text
This isn't about email (but it kinda is)
```

```text
ur (not)NEW march plan inside
```

**Performance proof:** Odd phrasing is a confirmed source of opens because it creates a solvable contradiction.

**Preview pairing job:** Stand in the doorway with a clue.

```text
ready to send babe
```

**Wren note:** Crooked is not vague. The reader should know the category but not the resolution.

## The 10-Pattern Generator

When Alyssa asks for subject lines, Wren should collect or infer:

| Input | Question |
|---|---|
| Email type | Soft Sunday, Simmer, sales, event, replay, resource, delivery? |
| Primary job | Open, click, action, link click, relationship? |
| One-line premise | What is the email actually about? |
| Concrete asset | Is there a plan, checklist, replay, coupon, class link, template, invite, or fix? |
| Real urgency | Is there a real deadline, event time, scarcity, or consequence? |
| Personal angle | Is Alyssa confessing, deciding, changing, noticing, fixing, or handing something over? |
| Audience temperature | Full list, small segment, warm event registrants, buyers, clients? |

Then output one option per pattern:

| Pattern | Subject | Preview | Why this works |
|---|---|---|---|
| Anti-Advice Reframe |  |  |  |
| re: Appointment Ping |  |  |  |
| Why I Strategic Confession |  |  |  |
| DM-Not-A-Headline |  |  |  |
| High-Value Click Noun |  |  |  |
| I Fixed Your ___ |  |  |  |
| Personal Update + Payload |  |  |  |
| Concierge Personalization |  |  |  |
| Soft Sunday Weird Wrapper |  |  |  |
| Conceptually Crooked Phrase |  |  |  |

If a pattern does not fit the email, Wren should still output it but mark it as `stretch` or `do not use`. This teaches the team which patterns are wrong for which email type.

## Preview Text Pairing Rules

Preview text is not a summary. It is a second move.

| Subject Type | Preview Should | Example Move |
|---|---|---|
| Weird phrase | give one clue | `girl math's chill cousin` |
| Personal confession | imply the lesson | `it looked dramatic but the data was rude` |
| Resource noun | sharpen the use-case | `for the part you keep avoiding` |
| Event reminder | give link/context | `your tiny pre-meeting note` |
| Promo/update | give reason | `I did the math and got annoying about it` |
| DM subject | add intimacy | `lemme fix that lol` |

## The Drafting Prompt

Use this prompt when asking Wren for options:

```text
Wren, draft subject lines + preview text for this email using the Subject Line Operating System.

Email type:
Primary job:
Audience:
Email premise:
Concrete asset/link/offer:
Real urgency or deadline:
Personal angle:
Anything to avoid:

Return:
- 10 options, one per top pattern
- subject + preview for each
- label each option as open-optimized, click-optimized, action-optimized, or relationship-optimized
- mark any pattern that is a stretch for this email
- pick the top 3 and explain why
```

## The Quality Gate

Before delivering options, Wren must check:

- No generic urgency unless urgency is real.
- No `details inside` if a weighted noun exists.
- No subject line that fully explains the email.
- No preview text that merely summarizes the subject.
- At least three options should feel like a text from Alyssa, not a newsletter.
- At least one option should be conceptually crooked.
- If the email is a resource/training email, at least one option must use Anti-Advice Reframe.
- If the email is a story email, at least one option must use Why I Strategic Confession.
- If the email is an event email, one `re:` option must be included unless the email body cannot honor it.

## The Strategic Split

Alyssa's audience opens for Alyssa the person.

They click for Alyssa the useful strategist.

So do not ask one subject line to do everything every time.

Use:

- relationship patterns when the email deepens trust
- click nouns when there is something to grab
- anti-advice when there is a real reframe
- event pings when there is a real event
- weird wrappers when the email is meant to feel like a Soft Sunday, not a sales page
