## The Double Yes Method

**How to create lead magnets that seduce dream clients, expose real problems, and start the sales momentum for free.**

The first yesses are the hardest. But once you get 'em, you've got momentum — so we create low-stakes opportunities to say YES before we get to your main thing.

- The **freebie** is the first step (and the most important one) to take someone from window shopping or lurking to actually walking in. We only want cool buyers in your store.
- The **tripwire** acts as your second yes. If you get this one, they're 70% more likely to say yes to something again. By the time they hit your main offer, they trust you.

**The analogy:**
- The freebie is ladies night — free margs for the girls every Thursday. No commitment, high satisfaction, and somehow *just* what they needed tonight.
- The tripwire is half-price chips and guac. Obviously yes. And lemme add queso while we're at it.
- Now they're IN. The marg's going down easy, the music is good, and they're not leaving before dinner and churros.

## Why Most Freebies Fail

> The idea that your freebie should be helpful is why it's not converting. We've been lied to.

Just being useful ≠ conversion. There are tons of free helpful things no one signs up for. We need psychology, positioning, and desire.

This is not a random act of kindness — it's a strategy. Shot and chaser. Give away the boat, sell the island.

**The 3 most common sins:**
1. Too general → Doesn't feel urgent
2. Solves everything → No need to buy
3. Doesn't reveal a gap → Doesn't lead to the next step

**The power of a well-executed freebie:**
- List growth
- Law of reciprocity
- Creates authority
- Beginning of the documentary
- Builds trust

**The ultimate list builder:**
- Attraction Method: People are coming to you because you're attracting them outright
- Quick win and dopamine hit
- Aligned with your signature dish
- Make it so good that your followers might have *paid* for this from someone else

> "If this is free… what else do they know?"

"If this is free…" moments happen when you:
- Give access to something you normally keep private (a script, a tool, a BTS breakdown)
- Solve a pain point they didn't know was solvable
- Make something they thought was complicated feel stupid simple

People buy when they believe you know something they don't — and your freebie should prove that.

## The Goals of a Great Freebie

1. Solve a super specific problem
2. Shift how they see that problem
3. Reveal what's (really) missing
4. Tease your signature dish (Costco samples)
5. Pre-qualify through context and tone (attract and repel)

> Solve a problem they know they have, and reveal the problem they don't see yet — that your offer solves. This is the app that makes em hungrier.

## Part One: The Source (Find the Known Problem)

Sometimes it's about meeting them where they are. If they're "solution aware" — you might be inspired by it but you haven't done the market research that makes it exciting for your dream clients.

**Use a poll to create something your audience actually wants.**

Poll methods:
- **Option 1:** Go live with a couple titles ready and ask people 1–10 how exciting is this for you and why/why not?
- **Option 2:** Go to other people's audiences where you think your ideal client is
- **Option 3:** Use IG Stories with the meter
- **Option 4:** If you have an audience anywhere, ask "What's ONE thing you want me to make free content on, leave it below"

**Use their words.** Let your dream client do your copywriting for you. Use their words in your title so they feel like you're reading their mind. Keep your finger on the pulse of your dream client.

> You're not just listening for ideas — you're listening for misunderstandings. That's where the gold is.

We use their words to reverse-engineer the gap. They think it's content planning, but you know it's fear of visibility.

## Part Three: The Outcome (The First Yes)

**What's the outcome of this freebie?** If you can't answer this in 1 sentence, you're not clear enough.

- Will this outcome make them feel different in 10 minutes (almost instantly)?
- And will it leave them wanting what your paid offer gives?

**The Fridge Test:** Would your dream client print this out and slap it on their fridge?
- Screenshot it?
- Save it to their Notes app?
- Make it their phone background?
- Keep it open in a tab?
- Quote it to their bestie (while crying about this problem)?

**Non-boring freebie formats:**
- ClickUp/Notion dashboard that simplifies their chaos
- "Steal my process" walkthrough workshop
- PDF of boundary scripts, client convo lines, or response templates
- Idea bank for the thing they're stuck on (content, offers, emails, etc.)
- Calculator or self-scorer that gives them a number to obsess over
- Screenshot breakdown of how you actually do what you do
- Quiz that reveals what's secretly holding them back
- Notion vault with 100 swipeable examples they're always Googling
- Private podcast feed with the stuff you only say to paying clients
- Template pack for the thing they're overthinking
- Audit worksheet to help them spot what's not working (yet)
- Decision guide to help them choose the right path/tool/option

## Part Four: The Process (Your Secret Sauce)

Teach something unique. Give them a taste of your thinking, method, and style. Bonus points if this hints at your exact method/phases. You're also filtering for people who resonate with *how* you work.

**Foolproof framework for a freebie PDF:**
- Page 1 — Cover page
- Page 2 — About You (½ page) + How This Works (½ page)
- Middle Pages — The Big Things, each formatted as:
  - What it is
  - Why it's important
  - How to take action
- Last Page — Congrats & Next Steps + CTA

## Part Five: The Sexy Title

No more "Ultimate Guides."

**Formula 1:**
> [Desire] in [time] even if [reason they think they can't]

*Example: Map out your next 90 days of content in one afternoon — even if you still don't know your niche*

**Formula 2:**
> [Number + adjective + strategy] to help you [desire 1 & desire 2]

*Example: 7 permission-soaked scripts to help you parent with peace and stop yelling before breakfast*

## The Freebie Checklist

A high-converting freebie does all five of these:

**1. Gives them a quick win (but not the whole curriculum)**
If your freebie solves the whole problem, there's no reason to keep going — you've given away a textbook. But if it gives them clarity or momentum while showing them what they don't know yet… that's powerful.

*Example: Nutritionist — "What I Eat When I'm PMSing, Melting Down, and Craving Bread Like It Owes Me Money" → Finally a plan that gets them. Now they want 30 more days of this exact energy.*

**2. Reframes how they see the problem (subtly)**
Your freebie should shift *how* they think about their situation. Maybe they realize they've been asking the wrong questions. Maybe they see a pattern for the first time. These little lightbulbs create authority without you needing to brag.

*Example: Copywriter — "It's Not Your Offer, It's Your Vocab: A Glossary of Words That Kill Conversion" → One read-through and they're deleting 80% of their site. Time to hire you.*

**3. Reveals a gap they now want to close**
Your freebie should plant the seed that there's more here. You're not stringing them along — you're solving one problem at a time.

*Example: Organizer — "What Your Closet Says About Your Nervous System" → Suddenly they wanna organize AND heal.*

**4. Positions your method as the obvious next move**
Share your disruptive philosophy or drop some signature phrases (breadcrumbs). "I like how this person thinks about this."

*Example: Nutrition — "My Triple-Triage Framework for What to Eat When You're Anxious"*

**5. Filters out the wrong people**
A good freebie repels. Let people bounce themselves if the vibe or process isn't for them. Speak directly to the people you want to work with (and who are most likely to buy).

*Examples: "You Might Be Too Bland For This Brand Guide" / "The 10-Minute Nap Time Room Reset Routine" / "ClickUp CRM for ADHD-ers"*

> Stop making freebies for everyone and start solving something for one real person.
