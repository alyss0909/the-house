## Step Two: Repurpose & Create a Workflow

How can you take your main content piece and repurpose it to be used in the other content forms?

For example if you've chosen a live video you can then turn that video into a blog post, and also share that video on IG video, and YouTube! Start thinking of creative ways to repurpose your content. In an ideal world by the end of the week you will have one piece of video content, one piece of content on your blog (that you can then share on Pinterest), one email, and one piece (or more) of social content.

**Here is an example workflow:**

1. Go live on IG
2. Download the live video and upload it to YouTube
3. Embed the youtube video in your blog, and write a short summary to correspond
4. Add a content upgrade to your blog post
5. Share video on socials
6. Write an email to your list about your new blog post and video
7. Share about your new blog post to all socials
8. Share your new blog post on Pinterest

## Step Three: Create Your Editorial Calendar

### 1: Identify Your Four Pillars

Start to think about your content in four signature topics, or content pillars. These are the four main things you want to be known for so make them count! Let's say, for example, that Nina is a health and fitness coach. She might want to talk about:

- Self Care
- Nutrition & Supplements
- Recipes
- Exercise

Brainstorm your topics and envision these as the four main sections of your content. Each section you'll be talking about something new (and relevant) and you'll launch or approach your services according to these pillars.

**Each pillar should:**
- **Relate to Your Offer:** Which of your offers directly relate to this content pillar?
- **Lead to a Freebie:** Do you have a lead magnet or content upgrade that organically relates to this content pillar? If not, do you have any ideas of lead magnets or content upgrades that you can create in the future that will naturally fit into the conversation around this pillar?
- **Have a POV:** What is your point of view, angle, philosophy or voice on this content pillar? Think: your unique way of solving their problem that you teach inside your product or that you execute when you're working with clients?

**POV Examples:**
- all my recipes take less than 5 mins to make
- all workouts require no equipment & can be done at home
- seo copy focused on connection over keywords
- your relationship isn't the problem, it's your communication style
- the key to scaling your business is nervous system regulation

---

### 2: Build Out Your Pillar Content

Use a google doc, this notion board, or a fancy journal to write down your 4 main pillars with 13 post ideas under each. Each section represents one week's blog post topic.

For example, when Nina's filling out content for her Nutrition Tips pillar, she might choose topics like "One Thing You Should Never Buy From The Grocery Store" or "How Sourdough Saved My Life."

So, under each pillar write 13 blog post topics that relate to that pillar topic in your business.

After you've done that make sure to have a workflow checklist ready for each one of your sections, so you know clearly what needs to happen to each of those brilliant ideas.

**Here's a sample checklist for each section.** Adapt this to fit your specific biz needs:

- [ ] First draft of blog post completed
- [ ] Draft corresponding email
- [ ] Email scheduled (mailchimp, kit, etc.)
- [ ] Images created
- [ ] Add content upgrade
- [ ] Interlink with old posts
- [ ] Live topic and talking points created
- [ ] Pinned to Pinterest
- [ ] Scheduled into Threads and FB
- [ ] IG related post scheduled
- [ ] IG related story scheduled

Next, start brainstorming your freebies. Do you have a freebie that relates to each of your four pillars and leads into your signature offer? (you could have 1 freebie for EACH pillar OR have ONE freebie that relates to ALL pillars). This is how you can include offers into your content seamlessly.

---

### 3: Create Inspired Content

Now that you're free from the stress of what to write, choose your 4 BEST & start writing your cornerstone content from a place of inspiration.

**Here are a few things to keep in mind while creating each content piece:**
- Think about titles that address a need or explain the benefits.
- Use strong headlines and switch up the formatting — use bold and italics to visually break things up (and only if it makes sense!)
- Use lists or numbered points.
- Include fun images that relate to your brand.
- End with an action for your audience to take. Think, what's the next natural step?

## Step Four: Content Upgrades

So, you've committed to content marketing, but now you want to make sure you're using this incredible free content to GROW your community.

When someone organically comes across your content, that is the BEST time to turn them into a loyal email subscriber because you've already warmed them up with your content and it's a natural time to take your relationship to the next l-e-v-e-l

I have seen content upgrades (paired with pinterest and my email sequence) grow my email list exponentially over the last 12 months. Once you start implementing them you will see how simple it becomes and how natural it is for your readers to join your community and continue building that relationship together.

Content upgrades position you as a trustworthy and credible source for the information they're looking for.

This is how you take someone from cold to hot really quickly! Content upgrades do NOT have to be anything big or fancy, I actually find the simpler you keep them the better!

They should be the logical UPGRADE to the blog post, podcast or video you've just written, if your lead magnet is not the logical next step just create a quick download for your audience.

**Simple examples of bomb content upgrades:**
- worksheet
- checklist
- video course
- email course
- free trial
- swipe file
- notion dash
- custom bot
- step by step tutorial
- meditation
- private podcast
- workshop
- cheat sheet
- template
- quiz
- prompts
