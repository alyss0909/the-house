## Content Styles

[00:22:00] So there are four core content styles. And then two sort of bonus ones. The core ones are: the hot take, the how-to, the bridge builder and the proof. The bonuses are the listicle and the BTS.

**Hot Takes** — your disruptive message. Say the opposite of what everyone else in your industry is saying. These are so good for cutting through the noise on Pinterest and just getting people to your blog because it peaks their curiosity. Examples: "Why journaling every morning is actually making your anxiety worse" or "Five myths about intuitive eating."

**Bridge Builders** — so important for taking people from just like straight up readers on your website to understanding your philosophy and method. This is like three step processes that you use to get a certain result as it relates to that topic. This is how to go from here to here. Examples: "Three things that changed everything for my resume clients," "The three part process that I teach to clients to rebuild trust after a toxic relationship."

**How-To** — simple steps to get an actual, tangible, quick result. Tutorial style. Examples: "Exactly what I do when a client ghosts me so I don't spiral into self doubt," "Six steps that everyone needs to take as soon as they hit publish on their blog post."

**Proof** — how I get X result, how my client went from here to here, how this pop culture figure went from this to this. Data, facts, client results or pop culture references that prove your thesis.

**Bonus — Listicles** — the algorithm loves them. Five weird ways to do this, six tools for this niche. Quick wins, great for SEO, but they don't go as deep.

**Bonus — BTS (Behind the Scenes)** — letting people in to your world, showing them behind the scenes of what you do, sharing confessions, sharing what you actually do in a day.
