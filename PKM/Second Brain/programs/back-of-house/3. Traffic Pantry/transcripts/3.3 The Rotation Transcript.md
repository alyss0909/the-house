## Two Ways to Schedule

The original plan we use with clients used a software that recently raised its price to $500/year. That is against everything Back of House stands for. So we've been on a journey to find new software — and along the way, I may have created my own.

**Option 1: Notion Social** — easiest, uses a Notion database to schedule your pins, affordable pricing, has an affiliate link. We're also excited to use it for other socials.

**Option 2: Pinterest native bulk upload** — free, some workarounds required. I built those workarounds right into the pin planner spreadsheet.

Both are covered below.

---

## Pin Planner Setup — Before You Schedule

Make sure your pin planner spreadsheet is completely filled in before generating a schedule. Key fields:

**Keyword planner section:**
- Seed keyword per board
- Board title written exactly as it appears in Pinterest (the script uses this to match boards)

**Pin images section:**
- All 70 image Google Drive URLs pasted in
- Asset type assigned to each image (which URL does this image go with — blog 1, blog 2, freebie, etc.)
- The planner needs exactly 10 images per asset; if you have 9, the scheduler will detect that

**Pin copy section:**
- 4 titles + 4 descriptions for each of your 7 URLs
- Title under 100 characters (shows red if over)
- Description under 500 characters (shows red if over)
- These length checks are built-in — fix any red cells before generating

**Batch prep checklist:**
- [ ] All 5 Pinterest board names set exactly as in Pinterest
- [ ] Each board has a seed keyword
- [ ] Each asset URL is correct
- [ ] Google Drive folder is set to "Anyone with the link can view"
- [ ] All 4 titles + descriptions finalized per URL
- [ ] Start date chosen (must be in the future)

## Image Folder Setup

After downloading from Canva:
1. Extract the zip file
2. Upload all images to a Google Drive folder
3. **Share the folder → Anyone with the link can view** (critical — without this, images will not be pulled into the scheduler)
4. In Drive, switch to list layout, sort by name (helps you match images to their URL easily)
5. Select all 70 images → Copy → Paste into the pin planner's image section as URLs
6. **Problem:** Google Drive links are hyperlinked in Google Sheets and don't paste as raw URLs
   - **Fix:** Paste into Notepad first → copies as plain text URLs → then paste into the spreadsheet

7. After pasting, open one of the URLs in an incognito window to verify you can see the image (public access confirmed)

## The Schedule Tab and Rotation Logic

Once all your data is in, use the custom menu in the pin planner: **Pinterest Lounge → Generate My Pins**

The script runs in the background (4–30 seconds depending on your computer). You'll see "Running script" at the top. Get yourself a drink.

**What gets generated:**
- 200 days of scheduled pins × 7 pins per day = 1,400 rows
- Each row has: pin title, pin description (with URL embedded), asset number, destination URL, Pinterest board, media image URL, alt text (auto-generated from board seed keyword + blog title), scheduled date + time, status (Done), pin ID

**The rotation logic:**
- Titles and descriptions cycle every 4 days
- Boards change daily
- Images rotate every 10 pins
- Never posts the same URL to the same board within 7 days
- Posts your URL to its most relevant board first

You don't need to understand all of this. Just trust it. The rotation was months of testing to work out. Follow the spreadsheet.

**What the pin ID means:** Format is 1.1.0.1 = [asset].[image].[description combo].[board]. Used internally to verify no duplicates.

## Option 1: Notion Social

**Setup:**
1. Get a Notion Social account (affiliate link in the course)
2. Export the schedule tab as CSV (File → Download CSV)
3. In Notion: import → CSV → choose the file
4. When it asks for property types: make everything **Text** (especially the URL field — setting it as URL type caused issues)
5. It imports all 1,400 rows into a Notion database

**Connect to Notion Social:**
1. Log in to Notion Social
2. Connect to Notion → allow access to your workspace
3. Search for the database you just created
4. Under Manage Properties → verify all columns are linked correctly (the only one that sometimes gets weird is the media/image column)
5. Connect your Pinterest account under Settings → Social Accounts
6. Sync your Pinterest boards

**Assign platform:**
1. In your Notion database, add "Pinterest" to the Platform property for all rows
2. Set up a filtered view: filter by Platform → does not contain Pinterest → select all → edit property → add Pinterest to all
3. Or: group by platform, select all unpublished, batch-assign

**Once synced:** Notion Social will start publishing scheduled posts automatically. You can see what's been published and what's scheduled. Published posts appear on your Pinterest profile with image, full caption, alt text, and correct URL.

## Option 2: Pinterest Native Bulk Upload

Pinterest has a bulk upload feature. Three limitations we had to work around:

**Limitation 1 — Image URLs must be public (not Google Drive links)**
Google Drive URLs, even "anyone with link can view," are not technically *public* URLs. Pinterest can't use them.
Fix: Click **Pinterest Lounge → Prep for Pinterest** in the pin planner. This script scrubs your image URLs and rewrites them in a format that makes them direct, publicly accessible image links from Google Drive. After running this, copy the URL and test it in an incognito window — you should see just the image, no Google interface around it.

**Limitation 2 — No repeat URL to the same board (even different images)**
Pinterest's bulk upload will reject any row where a URL+board combo appears more than once in the sheet — even if the image and title are different.
Fix: Every URL in the schedule gets a unique tracking parameter appended: `?p=pin1`, `?p=pin2`, etc. Pinterest sees these as fresh, distinct URLs. This is technically legitimate (similar to UTM parameters). It may also give you one more layer of freshness that Pinterest rewards.

This tracking parameter is added automatically when you click **Prep for Pinterest**.

**Limitation 3 — Maximum 200 pins per upload**
Our schedule has 1,400 pins. You can't upload them all at once.
Fix: The Prep for Pinterest script automatically chunks your schedule into groups of ≤200, each formatted exactly as Pinterest expects. You download each chunk as a CSV and upload them one at a time.

**Pinterest bulk upload process:**
1. Click **Pinterest Lounge → Prep for Pinterest**
2. Script runs, creates upload sheets (chunk 1 = rows 1–200, chunk 2 = 201–400, etc.)
3. Download each chunk: File → Download CSV
4. Go to Pinterest → Create → Publish Later → Upload CSV
5. Upload chunk 1, verify it looks right, then upload all remaining chunks back to back

Pinterest doesn't care if you upload all 8–9 chunks in one sitting. Just test the first 200 first to make sure everything is working before you upload 1,400 at once.

## Error Checking

The pin planner checks for errors after generating your schedule.

**Errors it catches:**
- Missing images (if you have 9 instead of 10 for an asset)
- Blank URL for an asset
- Missing board name
- Missing seed keyword
- Blank title or description

Any errors are highlighted red with an error message. Corresponding rows in the schedule are also highlighted red.

**Fix:** Go back to the source tab (keyword planner, pin images, or pin copy), make the correction, then click **Generate My Pins** again to regenerate from scratch.

Don't try to manually fix errors in the schedule tab — the script will overwrite your changes anyway. Fix the source, regenerate.

## Upkeep and Analytics

Once scheduled: your 7 URLs go out to all 5 boards, 200 times each, over 200 days. There's nothing more you have to do.

But optionally, once a month or so:

**Check analytics:**
- Pinterest for Business → Analytics Overview
- Look at engagement AND outbound clicks (actual clicks to your website)
- Note which topics, designs, and overlay text are most saved and most clicked
- If 10 pins are head and shoulders above the rest and they're all the same design → do more of that design next batch
- If one topic is consistently outperforming others → consider making it a bigger part of your content mix or creating a sister content piece

**With Notion Social:** You can see metrics right in the Notion Social dashboard alongside your other social platforms — no need to go into Pinterest analytics separately.

**When your 6 months is up:** Go back to your pin planner, update your start date, and regenerate. Your overlay text, titles, descriptions, and images can be mostly or completely reused — especially while your list is growing and new people are seeing them for the first time. Or: refresh your designs with Canva's current trending templates, update your overlay texts with any better-performing angles you noticed, and add any new URLs.

## A Note on Re-Pins

Re-pins used to be the secret sauce in 2018 — we built entire strategies around pinning something to your board and then re-pinning it multiple times. Pinterest saw that as social proof.

That all changed in 2020. Pinterest switched to the fresh pin preference and started suppressing re-pins. After lots of testing: it's better to remain neutral on re-pins. We don't use them as part of our core strategy anymore.

If you want to re-pin something occasionally — an old pin you love, someone else's content that's truly aligned — that's fine. Just don't rely on it. And be thoughtful about which boards you're in, because Pinterest is learning about you based on where you're pinning.
