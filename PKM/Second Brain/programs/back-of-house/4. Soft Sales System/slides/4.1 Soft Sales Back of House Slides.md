## Part One — The Framework

They've been simmering — now we're just gonna add a little STIR and shake things up a bit. See what happens.

**The 3-Part Anatomy:**
- **One Offer**
- **One Event**
- **Five Emails**

Launching is just remixing what you've already said. Same ingredients, new plating, delivered at the moment your reader's actually hungry.

If you've created your back of house correctly you already know all these things — you'll notice in this module I'm referencing other parts of this course over and over again because the same persuasion tactics you've used to create your sales page and your impact sequence and every single simmer email are the same tactics you're going to use to create your conversion event and your launch emails.

The whole point of your back of house is that you do the work once and recycle it over and over again — repurpose it, reshuffle it in new ways, to make the same point from a different angle. To capture your audience based on what stage in the buying process they're at, what type of buyer they are, and what their awareness level is.

Launching doesn't have to be a big deal, especially when you have your back of house set up properly. Three things you really need:
1. Your offer (check)
2. A really cool conversion event
3. Some launch emails

And a bonus is a way to recycle and repurpose those emails so that every launch is more effective and more effortless as time goes on.

### Prerequisites

- Is now the time to launch? Or keep simmering?
- 300–500 people on list (recommended)
- Regardless: hot audience — you've been simmering them, they've been raising their hand along the way

### Why Add a Stir?

You've set yourself up to have an email-first business model, which means your email list is freakin hot at this point. In most cases there are people who have been raising their hand for your Right Time Offers then never took you up on them — and they're ready to open their wallets as soon as you create a little bit of urgency. And THAT'S ALL A LAUNCH IS.

### Urgency & Incentives

- **Coupons** — use that built-in buffer you created in your pricing to offer a coupon code, extended payment plan, or tiered price increases
- **Bonuses** — highlight something really cool you're adding or already have inside your course; add a value to it and make it LIMITED
- **Urgency mechanics:** countdown timer, disappearing bonuses, coupon expiring, prices increasing daily or as spots are filled, limited availability or spots

## Part Two — The Conversion Event

You're going to host some kind of conversion event. Could be a (free or paid) workshop, private podcast, pop-up, zoom open house, hot seats, challenge, hackathon, "waitlist candy" week, virtual summit or giveaway (don't really rec), video series or masterclass, or a webinar.

Doesn't matter what it IS. What matters is what it does.

### What Every Conversion Event Must Do

1. **Call out the problem** — the thing they're sick of, still stuck in ("Lemme Guess...")
2. **Reframe it gently** — show them the actual reason things haven't been working ("The REAL Problem is...")
3. **Reveal your better way** — the philosophy, system, method, or mindset that shifts everything ("The Old Way / New Way")
4. **Invite action** — whether it's to buy, book a call, or reply with a word

### Getting the Buy-In

No matter what happens in your event you HAVE TO get their buy-in on your philosophy. They have to shift their belief from the old way to YOUR new way. In the middle of the event, you NEED them to see the real problem they're facing — once they agree with your diagnosis, you really don't even need to sell.

**The belief-building arc:**
1. Disrupt the industry norms (why the old way is b.s.)
2. Validate their nightmare & dream (usually a dream of wanting "an easier way")
3. Reveal the philosophy (your new way)
4. Teach the method (back up your philosophy with HOW)
5. Inspire action & conversion — give them QUICK WINS here, then paint the picture of outcomes & dream transformation

There should be a turning point in the class where they feel like you just handed them a secret code. This is the missing link between their current frustration & what they really desire.

### The Calls to Action

- ASK FOR THE SALE — tell them to take action
- Add a bit of REAL scarcity or urgency
- Add a "cart close," a price increase, or a disappearing bonus (essential even if you have an evergreen offer)
- A great CTA includes urgency + price + button somewhere
- Use first-person language: "I'm ready to _____," "I want the course," etc.
- **Buy Now** for offers under ~$997 | **Book a Call** for offers over ~$499

## Conversion Event Types

### Hot Seats
- **Kickoff:** Share the one pattern you're seeing over and over (call out the problem)
- **Session:** Fix it in real time with someone — crowd learns too (reveal better way)
- **Close:** Offer to help them do the same thing behind the scenes (invite action)

### Waitlist Loot Bags
- **Drop 1:** Loss Calculator — shows how much $ they're leaving on the table (calls out the problem)
- **Drop 2:** Freebie Red Flag Checklist — reveals why their current opt-in is attracting the wrong people (reframe gently)
- **Drop 3:** Quiz Funnel Planner in Notion — sneak peek of your actual method/system (reveal better way)
- **Drop 4:** Segment Automation Template — 3 example quiz segment templates & automation (invite action)

### Paid Workshop
- **Open:** Why we're doing this thing differently (call out the problem)
- **Teach:** Show the bird's-eye view of your new way & show exactly why it's working way better (reframe gently)
- **Core Workshop:** Guide them through one powerful piece of your method with live implementation (reveal better way)
- **Close:** Invite them to build the full system with you inside the paid offer (invite action)

## Part Four — The Hot Lead Loop

Give your audience plenty of time to WARM up before you launch again. Here's how it works:

After each launch, take 1 entire month to focus on **growth**, and 1 month to focus on **nourishment** before you launch again.

**Grow → Nourish → Launch → repeat**

No matter which month you decide to launch, you'll use the same grow/nourish/launch loop each quarter.

## Part Five — Mini Promos & Right Time Offers

### The 72-Hour Mini Promo

Maybe you're not ready to launch right now — maybe you want to wait until you have a few more people on your list, or you want to take the temperature of your audience before committing to a big launch — but you want to get a little cash in the door. Let's do a mini promo: a 72-hour launch.

Whenever I need a little cash injection I add a 72-hour promo — and we also have these automated in our sequences.

**Here's what you're going to do:**
1. Email your list and let them know you're not promoting this anywhere else
2. Highlight a feature or benefit from the vault
3. Offer a reason to join for 72 hours
4. Soft hand-raiser CTA

Go through your Right Time Offers → choose a feature or benefit and the reason they should take action now → go all in. Say: for the next 72 hours, anybody who joins gets this. Basically, use something from the Right Time Offers but build it out more and make it only available for 72 hours. (If these do well, add them to your follow-up sequence.)

### Right Time Offers

This is how we find out who's ready NOW — without tagging, segmenting, or surveying.

Right Time Offers are a staple of an email-first business model because they let you sell behind the scenes, seed your offers organically, and move leads forward without having to whip up a whole sale. These invites also require almost NO work from you OR them — just add a friendly reply with "INFO" or "YES" and send a personal follow-up.

When you do them well, your weekly newsletter becomes your most consistent sales system.

**Benefit Highlight Options:**

*The Cool Template* — highlight a template or swipe file
*ie: We clean up your welcome sequence and plug in 2 new click-to-segment email templates from the Simmer System vault*

*The New Thing* — mention a new resource you've just added
*ie: This week I put the finishing touches on the bonus Swipe Vault, loaded with onboarding sequences, weekly newsletter layouts, and re-engagement copy, and one student wrote her whole sequence in less than 3 hours using it.*

*The Quickie* — preview a quick win or mini transformation
*ie: I'll help you refine your offer angle, write a scrappy sales page, and launch it in a single weekend — no ads or audience required.*

*The Set Up* — describe a specific thing you help them set up
*ie: We map your client journey on call one, then I build the back-end automations with you inside Kit in real time.*

*The Deliverable* — feature a tangible deliverable
*ie: You'll walk away with a set of 3 branded templates, a mini color system, and an editable asset library for future launches.*

*The Experiment* — show what you test, workshop, or iterate together
*ie: We A/B your PS clicks live during the session and tweak your subject lines to get more replies (you'll see results by next week).*

**Reason to Join:**

- Special price or coupon code
- Secret bonus (not on the sales page, won't be repeated)
- Limited spots ("I'm only reviewing 5 client workflows this round" / "Two spots left")
- Seasonal energy (back to school, Black Friday, tax season, new year, before ad costs skyrocket in Q4)
- Upcoming event (join before Tuesday for the live portfolio review / guest speaker)
- Bonuses — new and fun to talk about, or only available if they join now
- Collective energy — group momentum

**The Magic Word CTA:**
- Reply [this word]
- Reply your city and I'll...
- Reply X if you wanna lock in one of the bonus spots

**When they reply:** A Loom, a checkout link, a simple Google doc — it all works. The more personal the better.

**The follow-up:**
- "Have you had a chance to check this out?"
- "Hey [name], lmk what questions you have"
- "Wanted to remind you that coupon page won't work after today — any questions after looking at the x page?"
- "Hey! We've got one bonus spot left, lmk if you want one and what questions you have while it's still available x"

Add well-performing RTOs to your nurture sequence for built-in cash injections.

## The Organized Launch

One of the great things about repurposing your launch is that you can build on this model over and over again. For the first few launches, your list will be growing rapidly and NEW people will be seeing this same launch copy. Repurposing is GOLDEN during this time.

Set up a Google Drive system to keep yourself organized so you can go back and review the copy you've used before and REUSE a lot of the stuff that you've worked so hard on.

**Take it up a notch (long game):** Have 4 "launch themes" per year (one for each quarter) with a different webinar or "copy theme" for each launch. This allows you to repurpose that content every January, every April, every July, etc. — so you're not using the same exact content 4x per year (although this is TOTALLY okay to do in the beginning).

**What to include in each launch folder:**
- Social graphics folder
- Launch copy doc
- Launch social copy doc
- Pre-launch hype doc
- Webinar slides
- Ad copy doc
- Launch debrief doc

(Plus any extras that change launch to launch — guest podcast schedule, affiliate emails, etc.) Keep all of these together so they're super easy to find in the future — or duplicate the entire folder to reuse for another launch.

## Launch Debrief

After each launch, always debrief and learn.

| Section | Key Questions |
|---|---|
| **Sales Page** | Any tweaks needed? Add new screenshots, testimonials, or details now that you've been through the launch. |
| **Freebie** | How did it perform? Any edits before continuing to promote? |
| **Ads** | Keep running? Increase daily spend? Add new images to keep things fresh? |
| **Launch Copy** | Which subject lines performed best? Which will you keep / change? How much copy will you maintain? How can you freshen it up? |
| **Socials** | Which socials will you keep? Any repurposable slides? What changes for next time? |
| **Conversion Event** | Based on attendance rate, engagement, and close rate — put a date in your calendar to make edits. |
