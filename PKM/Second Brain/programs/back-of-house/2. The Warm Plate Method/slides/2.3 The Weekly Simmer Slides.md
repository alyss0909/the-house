## What the Simmer Is

> This is why BOH thinks FOH has it easy. Everything's prepped behind the scenes. You're just here to charm the room once a week.

This might be the only non-automated thing you do every week. And it's also the most important. Everything else in your Back of House runs itself. This is the ritual you show up for — and when you do, it pays you back in attention, trust, and sales. Every. Single. Time.

> Your regular emails should simmer in the background of your business like a good sauce. Low heat, low effort, big flavour payoff.

The Simmer Strategy is how we really anchor an email-first business model. It's about sending one very human message a week that keeps your audience warm, offers real value, and gently invites the right people to go deeper. That's it. When you get this right you can rely less on referrals, socials, or putting all the pressure on each launch.

## Frequency

A sustainable rhythm your community can count on:
- Weekly or twice a week is the norm right now
- Some big names recommend daily (not there yet — prefer one more thought-out email + something chill)

**What simmer emails ARE:**
- A way to stay present in their inbox
- Top of mind when you're NOT launching
- How they know your philosophy in their BONES — because you've come at it so many different ways
- Connection
- Your cozy little corner of the internet
- A reminder: *I'm still here, and this is still right for you.*

**What simmer emails are NOT:**
- A sales email
- Something to be a perfectionist about (been there)
- Trend-chasing content with graphics

## Part One — Start With Flavour

### Flavour Profile

Before you write anything, you need to know what your newsletter tastes like. The tone, cadence, and point of view of your emails shape how your audience feels about hearing from you — and whether they keep coming back.

A strong Flavour Profile means your readers know what kind of emotional aftertaste to expect: cozy and insightful? Bold and irreverent? Tactical but warm?

Key ingredients:
- Tone and cadence = emotional aftertaste
- Your POV is the flavour — say it YOUR way
- Your email's unspoken promise
- Your sacred writing window & habit

When your flavour is dialed in, your emails stop feeling like a chore. They become something people seek out — because your voice isn't generic. It's familiar, distinct, and sticky in the best way.

**Finding your concept:**
Every cool (& open-worthy) newsletter has a tone you can taste before you even finish the first email. It comes from knowing who you're writing for, what's missing for them, and the kind of space you want to create in their inbox.

> Community + Gap + Vibe

## Part Two — Tell Better Stories

> The easiest way to write regular simmer emails is simple: tell stories. Stories let your people in, teach (not preach) and sneak your philosophy into conversation without even talking about your offer (unless you wanna). PLUS they're the most sustainable way to create high converting content week after week with very little effort from you — even if your life is boring.

### Simmer Stories

Stories don't need to be profound. "This is random but…" They can be:
- About you
- A client
- Something you overheard
- Something you've noticed lately
- A meme you saw recently
- Something from your real life
- From pop culture

**But your boring day-to-day is really the sweet spot.**

> Because the best stories aren't epic, they're what just happened. It's the chaos you're texting your friend about, the voice memo you're about to delete, a half-overheard convo, or the (weirdly specific) thing your toddler said. It doesn't have to be profound, but you DO have to notice it.

If it feels like you might get an unsubscribe, it's probably perfect. You're not writing a masterpiece. You're sending a friend a weird little thought that shifts how they think.

**Story prompt starters:**
- The weirdest thing I saw this week was _________
- I once made a business decision based on _________
- I accidentally did _________ in front of a client
- The most unhinged [DM/email/question] I've received was _________
- I knew I had to change __________ when __________ made me cry on a Tuesday

### Story to Point

> **Your story isn't the point. Your point is the point.**

The formula:
1. What happened?
2. What idea or lesson came out of it?
3. What's one piece of value you could give from this?
4. Is there an offer (or resource in your offer) you can naturally connect it to?
   - If yes: make a right time CTA
   - If no: end on the value

**Two ways to land the plane:**

**The Value Drop** — End with something they can do. This is where you become useful, not just interesting. Think: *If my best client was stuck here, what would I say?*

**The Right Time Offer** — If your story naturally tees up your offer, drop an invite. Simply: *here's how I can help if this hit home.* This is how you find out who's ready NOW.

## Part Three — The Right Time Offer

### What Right Time Offers Are

Some weeks, your story and value naturally tee up your offer. When that happens, slide in a Right Time Offer — a low-key CTA that turns readers into buyers without a promo.

Right Time Offers:
- Let you sell an existing offer anytime by sharing it in a human, relevant way
- Find the portion of your community who are READY NOW — without surveying, segmenting, or any other high-maintenance to-dos
- Require almost NO work from you OR them — just ask them to reply with a word for more info
- Make your weekly newsletter your most consistent sales system

> Remember — these aren't sales emails in disguise. They're just timely, human invitations.

### Right Time Offer Example

**The story:** shared a relatable story about cleaning up my messy desktop folders & how I found an old gem of a workbook that I'm turning into a whole course

**The point:** you're probably sitting on your next best-selling product

**The value drop:** my 3-step hidden asset finder

**The breakdown of the offer:**
- Clarified the offer — walking a small group through beta versions of their quiet funnel setups this month
- Tied it into the story — her mini course had been sitting in Notion for a year. Beautiful branding, but zero sales.
- Highlighted a specific feature — testing CTAs, refreshing lead magnets, building a repeatable rhythm with soft launches and click-to-reply nudges
- Gave a reason to join now — that's the kind of messy, in-progress, high-reward work we're doing this round
- Invited the reply — *Want in? Reply with info and I'll send you the scoop.*

### Feature Highlights (Rotate These)

| Template | How It Works |
|---|---|
| The Cool Template | Highlight a template or swipe file — e.g. *We clean up your welcome sequence and plug in 2 new click-to-segment email templates from the Simmer System vault* |
| The New Thing | Mention a new resource you've just added — e.g. *This week I put the finishing touches on the bonus Swipe Vault, loaded with onboarding sequences, weekly newsletter layouts, and re-engagement copy* |
| The Quickie | Preview a quick win or mini transformation — e.g. *I'll help you refine your offer angle, write a scrappy sales page, and launch it in a single weekend* |
| The Set Up | Describe a specific thing you help them set up — e.g. *we map your client journey on call one, then I build the back-end automations with you inside Kit in real time* |
| The Deliverable | Feature a tangible deliverable — e.g. *you'll walk away with a set of 3 branded templates, a mini color system, and an editable asset library* |
| The Experiment | Show what you test, workshop, or iterate together — e.g. *we A/B your PS clicks live during the session and tweak your subject lines to get more replies* |

### Reasons to Join (Rotate These)

- **Special price** or coupon code
- **Secret bonus** — not on the sales page, won't be repeated
- **Limited spots** — *I only have 3 spots. Two spots are left. Once they're gone, I'm backlogged until fall.*
- **Seasonal energy** — before back to school, Black Friday, tax season, Q4 ad costs, etc.
- **Upcoming event** — *next week, we're doing a full portfolio review inside the membership*
- **Bonuses** — new and fun to talk about, or only available if they join now
- **Collective energy** — *everyone's setting up their new lead systems right now inside. If you join now, you'll have the group momentum behind you*

### The Magic Word

Ask them to reply with a single word:
- "Reply THIS word"
- "Reply your city and I'll [bla]"
- "Reply X if you wanna lock in one of the bonus spots"

### The Follow-Up

When they reply — a Loom, a checkout link, a simple Google doc. It all works. The more personal, the better.

One day later follow-up (if they haven't clicked or replied):
- "Have you had a chance to check this out?"
- "Hey [name], lmk what questions you have"
- "Wanted to remind you that coupon page won't work after today, any questions after looking at the x page?"
- "Hey! We've got one bonus spot left, lmk if you want one of them and what questions you have while it's still available x"

### The 84 Combinations

> You're not changing the offer or invite structure — you're just changing the:
> - Way you introduce it (tie it to a story)
> - Feature you highlight
> - The Reason to join now (why it's the right time)
> - Tone to match the story & based on what they might be feeling this week

Just by mixing & matching **6 feature highlights × 7 reasons to join = 84 possible combinations** — without needing a new product.

> Rotate these like the weekly specials. The dish is the same, you're just describing different notes.

## Putting It All Together

**Step One — Pick a Story**

Choose a moment that's specific, recent-ish, and not overly dramatic. Small is good, weird is even better.

Prompts:
- What's something that happened this week that made you laugh, pause, or rant to your partner?
- What's something you always forget, mess up, or do differently than others in your field?
- What's something a client or student said that stuck with you?

Examples:
- *Graphic Designer: I tried to organize all my client files by color palette. It was a disaster.*
- *Nutritionist: My client texted me in a panic about a "snack emergency" at a birthday party.*

**Step Two — Find the Point**

Zoom out. What does this moment represent?

Prompts:
- What does this reveal about your philosophy?
- What do you wish your community understood about this?

Examples:
- Over-organizing can kill creativity. Sometimes, a little chaos is part of the magic.
- Willpower doesn't win under pressure, preparation does.

> More teachy, less preachy. Sneak your philosophy into your readers' bones by anchoring your ideas in something real.

**Step Three — Serve Value**

Give something practical, teachable, or encouraging — no strings.

Prompts:
- What's one tool, tip, or trick that solves this problem?
- What would you tell a client in this exact situation?
- What belief shift would change this outcome next time?

Format options: steps, bullets, pep talks, templates, checklists.

Examples:
- Here's my 3-step file sorting system that's loose but actually chaos-proof.
- Here's how I build "snack safety nets" into my clients' routines.
- 3 questions I ask myself now because of this
- What I wish I knew before it happened

**Step Four — Make the Offer**

Now that the point is made, softly invite your reader deeper. It's only possible because of the story — it's not random, and therefore doesn't feel like a hard sell. Just a natural invitation.

Ask yourself: Is this story the perfect excuse to mention something you offer?

Examples:
- *I actually teach this inside my branding intensive. If you want the sorting system, reply "files" and I'll send details.*
- *This is something we build into your daily routine inside Nourish. Want to see how it works? Reply "Flow" & I'll send it.*
- *If you want the emergency launch plan I used, it's inside my prep kit. Reply "SOS" to lock in the bonus spot.*
