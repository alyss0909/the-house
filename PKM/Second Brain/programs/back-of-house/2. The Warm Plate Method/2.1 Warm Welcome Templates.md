## Part 1: Symptoms

### Writing Prompt — Symptoms

**Email 1: The Symptoms** *(Goal: Draw attention to hidden, but compounding symptoms of their true problem)*

Write an email that tells your story of discovering their REAL underlying problem. This story should be highly related TO THE READER, and either be a vulnerable share about you, or a credibility boosting story about how you discovered the real problem when working with your clients. Use this email to paint a picture of what the uncommon symptoms or side effects of the problem are, so that the reader can relate, and begin to imagine what it would look like to overcome those symptoms. Make sure there are some valuable & actionable takeaways.

## Part 2: Diagnose

### Writing Prompts — Diagnose

**Part 2: The Diagnosis** *(Goal: Shift the readers belief to what the REAL problem is and gently introduce your process for solving that real problem)*

**Prompt A:** Write an email that reflects back the symptoms they're experiencing and introduces the root cause. This is where you'll shift their belief around what they THINK the problem is and introduce them to the real problem they are facing. Then, give them some SIMPLE STEPS they can take to get a quick win. The goal here is to have them experience some relief so that they are convinced of your diagnosis. This email should be a value add.

**Prompt B:** Write an email that resolves one specific symptom the reader might be experiencing right now, using your unique approach to solving their problem. Again, this should be a value add and offer 2-4 simple action steps they can take immediately. Share specific outcomes this has had in your life or share case studies with examples of alternative outcomes (paint the picture for them!). The goal of this email is for the reader to have a belief shift around their "real problem." Help them understand that although they might currently believe their problem is THIS, the real problem is THIS and once you address this real problem, you will see THESE results.

## Part 3: Prescription

### Writing Prompts — Prescription

**Prompt A:** Write an email that shows real, tangible examples of your solution in action. This can be through a case study, your own experience, or well known examples from pop culture. Think facts, figures and stats that prove your diagnosis and support your prescription. The goal of this email is to take them through the journey of becoming symptom-aware, to application of your diagnosis, to a clear breakdown of the result they want, and how they can get them by following your prescription. Take the time to SELL the steps of your prescription, how is working with you unique? Then, offer them the next step (either to book a call with you, to purchase your service, etc) and incentivise taking action now.

**Prompt B:** Write an email that answers some frequently asked questions about your service or offer. Don't just copy and paste your regular FAQ, think about some objections your reader might currently have and overcome those with your FAQ along with some key testimonials or case studies that bolster your point. Create urgency and get them to book a call with you or check out your sales page.
