**📉 WHAT'S OUT 📉**

### List Envy

Since I've been an entrepreneur (I won't age myself by telling you since when), people have **clung** to the idea that "bigger is better" (when it comes to email lists 🍆). All I've ever heard in marketing spaces and elsewhere is that **more subscribers = more potential customers, right?**

👀 Mmmm…maybe not.

**Here's why i think that's outdated thinking: 👇**

#### O1: 💩 Shitty Deliverability

ESPs (email service providers) like open and click rates, and **use that data to determine whether your emails are worth delivering.** The more disengaged people on your list, the worse your overall metrics, which means your emails might end up in more spam folders (we've gotta keep the email gods happy, ok.)

#### O2: 📉 You Foot the Bill

I'm not gonna sugar coat it. _**Kit is upping their prices and flodesk is phasing out their unlimited option.**_ Most email marketing platforms charge based on the number of contacts, so having a list full of cold subscribers is like throwing money into a black hole; save the cash and buy yourself that new pretty canva template.

#### O3: 🫠 Kinda a Bummer

It feels good to say you've got a 10,000-subscriber list. But if 3,000 of those people haven't opened an email since your last rebrand? Not great for your ego, or your wallet.

_So whether this info feels like the tough love you needed (or maybe it's a relief) it's time to prune away those old branches (with a strategy below)._

---

**📈 WHAT'S IN 📈**

### Re-engagement Rituals

Here's the deal…cutting cold subscribers doesn't mean you're giving up on them 🥹. You're simply _prioritising_ the ones who want to hear from you and that has so many upsides:

#### O1: 💌 Delivery

Less dead weight means **open and click-through rates** improve, which tells email providers that what you're sending is ✨ valuable✨. **In a nutshell: better engagement = better delivery = better engagement = (..you get the idea..).**

#### O2: 🐦‍🔥 Buyers

A cold subscriber isn't a lost cause. **Sometimes life gets busy.** A simple re-engagement campaign can show them the value of staying. You could easily win back someone who's **on the verge of making a purchase,** but just needs that nudge 😉.

#### O3: 📈 ROI

Focusing on re-engaging cold subscribers can be **much more cost-effective** than constantly trying to attract new subscribers. Plus, you've already done the hard work of getting these people on your list in the first place.

### The Re-engagement Menu

K let's get writing! There's no one size fits all type approach to re-engagement, but there are a few strategies that ARE working right now.

---

### 🥹 The "We Miss You" Email

Simple, direct, and often highly effective. Remind them of what they've missed and invite them back. But don't stop there... sweeten the deal with something exclusive. A sneak peek of a product launch, a special discount, or a behind-the-scenes look at something exciting coming up.

_Example:_

_"Hey [name]_

_I haven't seen you around lately! and i want to make sure you're in the right place_

_[tell them a bit about who your emails are for, what problems you solve, what types of things you send to their inbox]_

_Here's a little something special to get back in the groove—an.. [insert super cool thing here that your ideal client would want to pay for and give it to them for free!]_

_[button here that when they click triggers them as re-engaged!]"_

---

### 😏 The Personality Email

Sometimes all it takes is a little laugh. Throw in a funny GIF or a playful subject line. Humour cuts through the noise and gives your subscribers a reason to open that email. This works especially well when emphasizing your brand's voice.

_Example Subject Line: "Have I done something wrong? 😢" or "are we in a fight"_

---

### 👂 The Feedback Email

People love to feel heard especially in marketing. Send an email asking your subscribers for their input on what they want to see in your content. This both re-engages and gives you valuable info on what your audience actually wants.

_Example: What's on your mind? We'd love to know how we can help. [Then, insert a feedback form with an incentive here]_

---

### 🫶 Get Personal

Personalization is huge right now. Use their name in the subject line, reference their past behaviour (like what lead magnet they downloaded), or even call out their inactivity in a gentle way. Show them you know _them_, not just their email address.

_Example: "Hey [NAME], Remember when you signed up for that [resource]? Let's put it to good use with this template coupon!"_

---

### 🚩 The Break Up Email

It sounds counterintuitive, but giving people a way to _opt-out_ often results in more opt-ins! Send a light-hearted series that says, "We're sorry to see you go, but if you ever change your mind…"

_Example Subject Line: "Is this the end? 😢"_
