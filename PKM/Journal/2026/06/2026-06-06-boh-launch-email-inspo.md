## Email 1 — How I'm navigating the new online landscape

*(she knows she hates the word "landscape" — title needs a rewrite)*

The hook: she lives in the middle space between:
- Absolutely hating AI and feeling overwhelmed by the world, feeling disconnected from her own creativity
- Loving a little automation, loving the idea of finally organizing her chaotic brain, having a small team, outsourcing the parts that drain her

She's finding the middle ground. That's the email — what does navigating that actually look like for her?

**Key line:** *"I'm navigating that and I'm finding the middle ground."*

## Email 2 — The New 4-Hour Work Week

The reframe: there's a new version of Tim Ferriss's promise, but it's not about hiring people in Bangladesh. It's about the middle ground a lot of entrepreneurs are standing on right now, even if they don't know exactly where it's going.

Alyssa's version of the 4HWW promise: **spending more time in the realm of your own interests.** More yourself. More obsessed. Less performing marketing literacy.

Story beat: she read the 4HWW at 21, working at a yoga studio for free in exchange for email marketing insight. The Bangladesh thing felt weird and uncomfortable even then. She was never that person. She's still not.

**The real point she's landing on:** I want to be spending more time in the realm of my interests. I want to be more myself. In a world of AI vanilla bullshit, I want to go down the rabbit holes — clip my plants, create a multilayered workbook, become obsessed with a client's funnel, create a world of metaphors, have fun.

## Email 3 — The Auction Story

Inspiration: a woman Alyssa saw at an auction who followed her creative instincts. The art was nothing special. The garden was just fine. Her work was just a job.

But she was clearly a happy woman. A person who enjoyed her life.

*"Maybe she wasn't on the cutting edge and maybe she didn't know the next best fucking thing and maybe she wasn't going to flip her house... but I bet she was a good professor and I bet she told her friends what she learned about indigenous plants and I bet she felt proud."*

Alyssa's self-awareness landing: "I was never an art girl. I was friends with the art girls. I was a history girl. I was a politics girl. I could always understand the past. I could see the patterns. I wasn't the one making the new path — I was the one who cried when she saw the new path."

**The positioning line:** *"I'm not here to tell you what's next. I'm here to tell you what works, and what frees you up to do more of the dumb shit I know you want to do."*

## Who This Is For / Not For

**For:** The one obsessed with parasites or what makes a good supplement. The one who knows the difference between an overphotographed hydrangea and a niche interior flower. The one who knows the exact bilirubin level to look for on a blood test. The one with the secret Notion formula or Photoshop hack. The one who knows the Greek word for something.

**Not for:** *"The girls who like to change their mind."* — mid-thought, needs development.

**The thesis:** We live in a world that tells us the social media literate get the bag. But I've worked with influencers and people with 300 followers. The ones who succeed are the ones who give a shit. What made the difference wasn't understanding social media — it was caring deeply about something specific.

## The Three Things Framework (BOH core)

1. **Growing your audience** — Pinterest is the most low-maintenance way, but she's not precious about it (FB ads, viral Instagram, DMs — not above any of it)
2. **Email marketing** — no options here. Email is king. Writing fucking good emails is something Claude can't teach you, contrary to popular belief
3. **Giving a shit about whatever it is you do** — the secret sauce. Nothing she teaches will work if this isn't there first
