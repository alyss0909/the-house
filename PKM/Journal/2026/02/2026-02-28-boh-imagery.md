# BOH IMAGERY
also see [[2026-02-28-boh-imagery-2]]

Folder manual and stamp are strong because they feel institutional. Not cute. Not crafty. Established.
Now let’s sharpen both so they don’t feel like Etsy props.
You need something that could sit on a stainless prep counter and feel like it belongs there.
Option 1: The House Manual
This is your “book” without being a book.
Imagine:
A thick cream ledger-style folder. Slightly oversized. Matte cover. Minimal serif type centered:
BACK OF HOUSE
Systems & Service
Small line underneath: Email • Pinterest • Infrastructure
No explanation. No bullet points.
Just presence.
Placed:
• On a silver prep counter • In a gray bus bin • Tucked on a cutting board • Behind a bar next to stacked glasses • On a perfectly set table instead of a menu
It feels like the operations manual for a restaurant.
When someone looks at it, the fantasy is: “This place runs.”
Not: “Look at this marketing course.”
Emotion: calm authority.
Option 2: The Stamp
This one feels more process-oriented.
Custom rubber stamp:
BACK OF HOUSE
Est. 2026
Stamped repeatedly on kraft paper, butcher paper, envelopes, or ticket slips.
Stacked neatly.
Placed on stainless steel with the ink pad beside it.
It communicates repetition. Systems. Approval. Execution.
You could do:
• A stack of stamped order tickets clipped on a rail • A pile of stamped envelopes in a bus bin • A cutting board with 20 identical stamped cards
The stamp says: “This has been processed.”
That’s automation without screens.
Now here’s the strategic difference.
Manual = ownership and stability.
Stamp = movement and repetition.
Manual is: I built this once.
Stamp is: this runs over and over.
Manual feels legacy.
Stamp feels active.
You could also combine them.
Image 1: Manual on stainless counter.
Image 2: Stamp mid-action on kraft paper.
Image 3: Stacked stamped papers in a tray.
That creates a narrative:
Build it → Process it → It runs.
And now we get to your breakfast test.
If I’m standing outside a café deciding whether to go in, what makes me want it?
Not the ingredients. The feeling of competence.
You want someone to look at your image and think:
“This place is organized.” “This place is legit.” “This place is calm.” “This place doesn’t drop plates.”
That’s what you’re selling.
