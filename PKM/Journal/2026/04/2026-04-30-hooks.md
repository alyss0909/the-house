## The Science of Great Hooks

A hook passes when it creates genuine cognitive friction for a cold stranger scrolling with no context. Every hook should pass these 5 checks:

1. **Friction** -- Does it create an involuntary need to keep watching?
2. **Strength** -- Is the psychological trigger strong, not just technically present?
3. **Completeness** -- Does it give the viewer enough to feel the friction, or is it half-formed?
4. **Authenticity** -- Does it sound like a real person, not polished ad copy?
5. **Stakes** -- Does it answer "why should I care?" with a personal consequence?

### Psychological Triggers That Stop the Scroll

- **Pattern Interrupt** -- something unexpected that breaks autopilot
- **Identity Call-Out** -- makes a specific viewer self-select
- **Pain Agitation** -- mirrors internal experience so precisely the viewer feels understood
- **Curiosity Gap** -- open loop with real stakes they need to close
- **Contrarian** -- challenges a belief they hold
- **Loss Aversion** -- implies they're losing something or making a costly mistake
- **Social Proof** -- peer validation that makes the viewer want the same outcome
- **Visceral/Taboo** -- bodily or socially uncomfortable language that can't be ignored
- **Relatable Experience** -- universal frustration described with precision

### What Always Fails

- Product/brand before tension
- Benefit-first without pain
- Unfinished thoughts or vague analogies
- Commands without the "why" (e.g. "Stop buying X" without showing the cost)
- Overly dramatic claims that sound manufactured
- Rhetorical questions with obvious answers
