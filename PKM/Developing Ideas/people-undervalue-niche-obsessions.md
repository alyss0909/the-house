# People Undervalue Their Niche Obsessions

## What keeps showing up

Alyssa keeps returning to a specific belief: the people she works with — and the people she *wants* to work with — already have something deeply compelling going on. A parasites obsession. Indigenous plants. Knowing exactly what bilirubin level to flag. A niche so specific that the general internet would find it boring. And they have been told, explicitly or implicitly, that this specificity is a liability in business. That they need to be more marketable. More broadly appealing. More "relatable."

She thinks the opposite. She thinks the obsession IS the business. And BOH is the infrastructure that lets the obsession run without the person having to become a content machine or a marketing expert.

This is distinct from `boh-as-permission-not-product` (which is about how BOH gets positioned and sold) and `middle-ground-entrepreneur` (which is about Alyssa's own identity). This one is about what she believes about her *students* — that they're underestimating themselves because the online world has told them their specificity is a problem.
