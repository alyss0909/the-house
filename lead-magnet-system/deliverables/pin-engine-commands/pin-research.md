## My niche context
I am a [YOUR ONE-LINE NICHE, e.g. "productivity coach for creative service businesses"]. My audience searches Pinterest for help with their business, content, and systems.

## Steps, in order:

1. **Read the content.** If $ARGUMENTS is a URL, fetch it and identify what the piece is actually about, including angles I might not have noticed myself.

2. **Research real keyword data.** Search the web for current Pinterest keyword and trend data for this topic. If I pasted PinClicks results into this chat, treat those numbers as the primary source. Do not invent search volumes. Every keyword must have evidence behind it (a volume number, a published trend report, or repeated appearance across current sources).

3. **Build the cluster, not just the obvious term.** Go past the surface keyword. Find 5-8 long-tail candidates, including at least 2 from an angle I probably would not have searched myself.

4. **Judge winnability.** For each candidate, note: does a new pin have a realistic shot? A term with massive established volume is usually a losing fight for a fresh pin. A specific long-tail with moderate volume is usually the win. Recommend accordingly.

5. **Check timing.** If the topic is seasonal, note the peak window. Pins need 45-90 days of lead time before a seasonal peak.

6. **Write the rows.** Create one row per candidate in my Pin Tracker:
   - Data Source ID: [YOUR DATA SOURCE ID]
   - Fill: Seed Term, Long-Tail Keyword Chosen, Volume / Evidence, Trend Check, Source Content ($ARGUMENTS if URL), Stage = "Researched"

7. **Summarize.** Give me a short table of what you found and why, flag your top 2 picks, and remind me to flip my favorites to "Ready" and run /pin-copy.
